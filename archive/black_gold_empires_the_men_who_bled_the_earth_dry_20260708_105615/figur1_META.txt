TITEL:
Calouste Gulbenkian — The Man Who Owned Five Percent of the World's Oil

BESCHREIBUNG:
He never drilled a well, never signed a treaty, yet he claimed five percent of an empire's black blood and made history bend to a single clause buried in fine print. As the Ottoman Empire collapsed under war and betrayal, one Armenian financier moved through the shadows of Constantinople, London, and Paris, brokering secret partitions that would decide who profited from the earth beneath a dying world. This episode of Black Gold Empires: The Men Who Bled the Earth Dry follows Calouste Gulbenkian from the carving of the Ottoman carcass to the whispered diplomacy of the Red Line Agreement, tracing how ruthless patience and obsessive secrecy built one of history's most staggering personal fortunes. But wealth bought him no peace. Exiled, hunted by war, he retreated into a gilded fortress of stolen masterpieces and mounting paranoia. What remained when the empires fell was not triumph, but silence.
