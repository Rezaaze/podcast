TITEL:
Black Gold Empires: The Men Who Bled the Earth Dry

BESCHREIBUNG:
Oil does not simply erupt from the earth. It is claimed, partitioned, and bled from empires by men willing to gamble their sanity for it. Black Gold Empires traces three such men across one continuous descent into the psychology of petroleum ambition — a reclusive Armenian financier who carved a percentage of the collapsing Ottoman world for himself and vanished into paranoid splendor, a London trader's son who broke an American monopoly with a fleet of tankers only to watch his own creation slip beyond his control, and an American industrialist-statesman who struck deals with dictators and commissars alike, chasing a legacy that oil alone could never satisfy. This is not a history of wells and pipelines. It is a forensic study of obsession — how the pursuit of black gold rewires ambition into something closer to compulsion, and how each of these men, in his own century and his own empire, discovered that the earth extracts a price for everything it gives up. Across three interlocking portraits, spanning the fall of the Ottomans to the twilight of the Cold War, a single continuous thread emerges: fortune built on oil is never truly kept, only borrowed against something far more volatile than money.
