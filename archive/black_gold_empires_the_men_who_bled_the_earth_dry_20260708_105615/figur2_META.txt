TITEL:
Marcus Samuel — He Broke the Suez to Build an Empire That Broke Him

BESCHREIBUNG:
Before he ruled an ocean of oil, he sold seashells from a cramped London shop, learning the value of curiosities long before he understood the value of crude. Marcus Samuel inherited nothing but a modest trading house and an appetite for risk that would reshape the geography of global commerce. When the Suez Canal's rigid rules threatened to strangle any tanker daring to carry bulk oil through its waters, he engineered a vessel built to break them, launching a fleet that would chase John D. Rockefeller's American monopoly across every ocean on earth. This episode of Black Gold Empires: The Men Who Bled the Earth Dry traces the seashell merchant's transformation into an oil titan, from the deadly gamble that built his tanker empire to the financial storm that gathered as his ambitions outgrew his control. Step aboard a story of obsession, engineering audacity, and the razor's edge between empire and ruin.
