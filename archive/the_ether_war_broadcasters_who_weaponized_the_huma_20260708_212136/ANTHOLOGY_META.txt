TITEL:
The Ether War: Broadcasters Who Weaponized the Human Mind

BESCHREIBUNG:
Before the bomb, there was the broadcast. Long before armies crossed borders, voices crossed them first, slipping through static and into living rooms, barracks, and huts, rearranging what people believed to be true. This is the story of the ether as battlefield, and the men and women who turned a microphone into a weapon capable of reaching further than any rifle. The Ether War follows three broadcasters who discovered that the human mind, isolated and afraid, will believe almost anything repeated in the dark. A British fascist adopts an aristocratic sneer to haunt wartime England night after night. A Japanese-American woman, trapped and coerced, becomes the vessel for a paranoid fantasy an entire nation invents about her. A failed radio host in Central Africa turns call-in requests and pop songs into coordinates for slaughter. Each figure believed, at least at first, that they controlled the voice coming through the speaker. Each would learn otherwise. Across one continuous, unbroken descent, this anthology traces how propaganda works on the individual psyche before it ever works on a population, and what happens to the person behind the microphone when the broadcast outlives their control of it.
