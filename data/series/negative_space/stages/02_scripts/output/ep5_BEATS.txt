--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Ethan checks the drive out of habit before he's even fully awake and finds a new image waiting.
2. He recognizes the room in the photo as his own living room, exactly as he left it.
3. The timestamp reads the night before, while he was asleep.
4. He checks his apartment for any sign someone was actually there and finds nothing disturbed.
5. He decides on the spot to downplay this to everyone rather than admit how shaken he is.
6. The audience feels the gap between how calm he will act and how frightened he actually is.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Ethan reports the break-in to the team, framing it as a possible technical exploit rather than a real intrusion.
2. Mei pulls the building's entry log and confirms no forced entry and no unfamiliar key-card use that night.
3. Ethan's flat, procedural tone doesn't match the story he's telling, though no one presses him on it.
4. Mei starts running the new photo's file properties against the drive's earlier images.
5. The audience watches Ethan hide his fear successfully, even from the people closest to the case.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. A certified notice arrives at the print shop addressed to Lina's father.
2. Lina intercepts it before her mother sees the courier.
3. She reads enough to understand the deadline has moved, without yet knowing her father already knows.
4. She hides it in her bag rather than raising it with either parent.
5. The audience recognizes two people in the same family now each sitting on the same secret alone.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Marcus opens a message thread to Ethan and starts typing something close to a confession.
2. He gets several lines in before he stops and rereads what he's written.
3. He deletes it piece by piece, telling himself the timing is wrong rather than admitting he's afraid of the answer.
4. He opens a different thread instead, one clearly meant for his contact, and hesitates there too.
5. The audience sees a man circling an admission he still believes he can avoid making.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Sato reads through Ethan's break-in report line by line.
2. The detail about no forced entry catches on something in her memory.
3. She recognizes it as the same detail from the case she closed years ago.
4. She sits with this for a long moment, not yet ready to say it aloud to anyone.
5. She tells herself the resemblance is likely coincidence, though she doesn't fully believe it.
6. The audience senses her getting closer to a reckoning she's been avoiding all season.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Han sets Ethan up at the enlarger for another old developing exercise.
2. His manner is warm and unhurried, asking after Ethan's week with easy, grandfatherly concern.
3. Ethan mentions the break-in almost in passing, and Han reacts with concern instead of anything else.
4. The familiar rhythm of the darkroom settles something in Ethan he didn't realize was unsettled.
5. Han watches this calm take hold with quiet, private satisfaction.
6. The audience knows what Ethan doesn't: the person soothing his fear is the one who caused it.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Mei finishes her analysis and finds the new apartment photo carries the same anomalous compression signature as every trophy-room image.
2. This confirms all the images, including the one of Ethan's own home, came off the same equipment.
3. She flags the match to the team as significant, without yet voicing her private hunch about what kind of equipment it might be.
4. She holds back the darkroom-adjacent theory, not confident enough yet to say it out loud.
5. The audience understands this links Ethan's violation directly to the killer, even as no one names Han.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Lina's editor calls to move the publication date up, citing competing outlets closing in on the same pattern.
2. She argues for more time and doesn't get it.
3. She realizes she now has to choose between telling Ethan first or losing that chance entirely.
4. She stalls, telling herself she'll find the right moment, though the moment is shrinking fast.
5. The audience watches her run out the clock on a promise she's kept only to herself.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Ethan meets Lina at the promenade, ostensibly for something ordinary.
2. He repeats back a detail she once mentioned that he now realizes she couldn't have known unless she had inside access to his case.
3. Lina freezes, caught between denying it and explaining it.
4. She admits, without giving up her source, that she's been working a story tied to his case.
5. Ethan's hurt tangles immediately with the unspoken fear from the apartment photo, though he can't yet separate the two feelings.
6. He assumes, wrongly, that her source must be someone inside the precinct rather than someone he trusts completely.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The landlord arrives at the print shop with a collections agent, catching all three Chos on the floor at once.
2. The agent lays out the accelerated deadline and the full scope of what's owed.
3. Lina's mother learns of the loan for the first time in front of everyone.
4. Lina's father's silence about the earlier notice is exposed in the same breath as Lina's own year of quiet payments.
5. The family is left standing in the wreckage of two secrets colliding instead of one.
6. The audience sees the moment neither Lina nor her father can control the story any longer.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Marcus learns, through the shrinking publication timeline, that the story is close to breaking wide.
2. He runs through, in his head, exactly how many files he's pulled and how easily they trace to his terminal.
3. He tells himself his friendship with Ethan will matter if it ever comes out, even as the thought rings hollow.
4. The fear that his name could surface lands differently than any warning sign before it.
5. The audience watches the crack from the previous episode widen into something he can no longer contain alone.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Sato reviews the specificity of the reporting circulating about the case and decides she can no longer treat it as background noise.
2. She quietly opens a formal inquiry, keeping it off the wider bullpen's radar for now.
3. She pulls access logs to start narrowing down where the details could have come from.
4. Her working theory points toward a records clerk rather than anyone inside forensics.
5. The audience knows, from Mei's earlier discovery, that her theory is aimed in the wrong direction.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Ethan follows up with Lina at her apartment, unwilling to let the promenade conversation sit unfinished.
2. Cornered and out of room to stall, Lina tells him the truth: Marcus has been her source.
3. Ethan's expression changes as the pieces of the season's leaked details rearrange themselves around a name he trusted without question.
4. Lina watches him absorb it, aware this is the moment she can no longer manage on her own terms.
5. The audience feels both threads — the story and the friendship — collapse into a single confrontation still to come.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Ethan waits for Marcus at the lab after everyone else has gone.
2. He lays out what Lina told him without raising his voice, watching Marcus's face for the denial that doesn't come.
3. Marcus admits it, reaching for the resentment about always being second name as if it explains enough.
4. Ethan hears in it exactly how long this has been going on, longer than Marcus is willing to say outright.
5. Whatever trust existed between them visibly breaks in real time, with neither man able to undo what's been said.
6. The audience recognizes this as the moment Marcus's belief that his loyalty would count for something finally fails him.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Ethan and Lina sit together at the rooftop bar, the day's damage still raw between them.
2. Neither pretends the conversation with Marcus or the confrontation on the promenade didn't happen.
3. Lina reaches for honesty this time, though she still holds back the full weight of what the last months have cost her.
4. Ethan tries to meet her halfway, still not registering that his own unspoken fear from the apartment photo is bleeding into how he's reacting to her.
5. They land somewhere unsteady but still together, neither fully repaired nor fully broken.
6. The audience senses two people trying to hold a relationship together with only part of the truth on the table.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Han stands alone in his shop after closing, the red safelight the only light on.
2. He watches the street through the window for a long, unhurried moment.
3. His expression gives away nothing to anyone who might glance in.
4. The stillness carries the same patient attention he showed Ethan at the enlarger earlier.
5. The audience is left with the unspoken reminder of exactly what that patience is in service of.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Later that night, Ethan's drive updates again on its own.
2. This time it isn't a photo but a short file, a message.
3. It's addressed to him by name, the first time the sender has spoken to him directly instead of through images.
4. Ethan stares at his own name on the screen, the earlier calm from the darkroom gone completely.
5. The audience is left with the season's clearest sign yet that whoever is behind this has been watching far closer than anyone around Ethan realizes.
