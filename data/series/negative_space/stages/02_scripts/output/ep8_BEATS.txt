--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Ethan tackles the fleeing figure among the gantry machinery and pins him down.
2. Backup arrives seconds later, flashlights converging on the two men.
3. The struggle stops entirely once Han stops resisting, going oddly still.
4. Han's calm in that moment reads as strange to everyone but Ethan, who already saw his face.
5. Yuki reaches them and realizes from Ethan's expression that whoever this is means more to him than a stranger.
6. The team begins the standard steps of an arrest, unaware yet whose face is under the light.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Officers move to cuff him, but Han keeps his attention fixed only on Ethan.
2. He ignores the officers' questions entirely, as if they aren't the ones he's answering to.
3. He asks Ethan directly whether he understands yet why it had to be him.
4. Ethan doesn't answer, unable to reconcile the question with the man he thought he knew.
5. Yuki notices the question wasn't procedural and files away that this arrest is stranger than it looks.
6. Han goes quiet again, willing to wait for his answer rather than force it.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Han is brought in and processed like any suspect, calm through fingerprinting and intake.
2. Officers cross-reference his name and find nothing that explains the scale of what's being alleged.
3. Word spreads through the bullpen that the suspect is someone several of them recognize from the neighborhood.
4. The captain pulls Yuki aside, wanting confirmation before the department commits publicly to the theory.
5. Ethan stays visibly rattled, and colleagues start noticing he has some history with the suspect.
6. The department realizes it's about to unravel a case far larger than one night's arrest.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Yuki brings Han into her office alone and sets her old case photo on the table between them.
2. She asks him plainly whether he had any connection to that case.
3. He studies the photo with open interest rather than alarm, admiring its composition instead of denying involvement.
4. He speaks about framing and instinct in a way that mirrors the photographer's habit she noticed earlier.
5. He never confirms or denies the case is his, letting his knowledge of the craft speak for him instead.
6. Yuki leaves still without proof, carrying the same unresolved question she's held alone all episode.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei walks Ethan through the paper stock analysis, showing it matches stock sold at Han's shop.
2. She lays out the key timeline next, confirming the spare key was never returned after Ethan's move.
3. She overlays the GPS trail from the photographs directly onto locations tied to Han's known routes.
4. Ethan watches every thread of evidence close around a man he trusted for nineteen years.
5. Mei states plainly that all three trails trace back to Han alone, with no other explanation left standing.
6. Ethan says nothing for a moment, the confirmation harder to absorb than he expected.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Yuki spreads Marcus's phone records across the table in front of the whole team.
2. She points out the calls timed exactly to nights the storage units were cleared out.
3. Marcus tries to minimize the contact as harmless before the pattern makes that impossible to sustain.
4. Yuki reveals the same contact number matches the anonymous source who's been feeding Lina information all season.
5. Marcus realizes in that instant his carelessness reaches further than he ever let himself consider.
6. The room goes quiet, the full cost of his silence finally visible to everyone at once.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Once the others clear out, Mei stays behind with Marcus.
2. She admits she's suspected him for weeks and chose not to report it.
3. She tells him what that silence may have cost, naming the units cleared out during the delay.
4. Marcus doesn't defend himself, absorbing that her guilt and his failure are now tangled together.
5. Mei doesn't ask for forgiveness, only wants him to understand what her silence protected and what it didn't.
6. Neither of them names what happens next for either of their standing on the team.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Han asks to speak with Ethan alone, and the department allows it under observation.
2. He walks through the photographs one by one, naming families Ethan has only seen as case files.
3. He frames each killing as a step in searching for someone with Ethan's exact mix of skill and hunger.
4. He states outright that mailing the drive and staging the break-in were the final steps of an audition, not a threat.
5. He mentions, almost as an aside, that the print shop's debt was paid the same way — a gift meant to bind Ethan closer before the game's rules were clear.
6. Ethan's certainty that turning Han in would close the matter breaks apart at that last detail, the debt now feeling like something he can't simply hand off to a file number.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Ethan tells Lina the spare key was never lost, that Han kept it deliberately after her family's shop and his own home became something to watch.
2. He describes the audition Han laid out, choosing his words carefully around the parts that still cost him to say aloud.
3. Lina asks what Han actually wants from him now that he's caught.
4. Ethan admits Han still seems to believe an answer is coming, as if capture hasn't changed the terms of the question.
5. He doesn't tell her about the one terrible second he understood why Han thought he'd say yes.
6. Lina holds him without pushing further, sensing there's more he isn't ready to say.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Lina pulls the burner number her source has always called from and runs it against purchase records.
2. The trail lands on a store two blocks from Han's shop, the same stretch of street from the debt payment.
3. She sits with the implication that her exclusive was shaped by someone circling the same man who terrorized Ethan.
4. She hasn't decided whether to tell her editor the source may be compromised before the story runs.
5. She doesn't yet know the source is the same contact who fed Marcus's leak all season.
6. The discovery leaves her questioning how much of her own reporting she can still trust.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Lina and Ethan stand together near the shop after hours, the framed portraits still on the wall behind them.
2. Ethan admits how much it sickens him that even her family's relief turned out to be part of Han's calculation.
3. Lina says she can't bring herself to tell her parents the money came from a serial killer.
4. They weigh whether protecting her parents from the truth is mercy or another quiet debt to Han's design.
5. They agree, without much certainty, to let the shop keep believing in its lucky break a while longer.
6. Neither says aloud that the silence feels uncomfortably close to the secrets they've both been keeping all season.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Ethan gets access to search Han's darkroom as part of the evidence sweep.
2. Under the red safety light, he finds a print already hung to dry, dated the same week as his arrest.
3. The image is of Ethan himself, framed with the same careful composition Han used on every victim.
4. A short note pinned beside it reads like an invitation left for whoever eventually found it.
5. Ethan realizes Han prepared for this exact moment, wanting to be caught precisely this way.
6. He stands with it a long moment before finally logging it as evidence.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Han gives his formal statement, naming locations, methods, and victims with unsettling precision.
2. He answers every question about the drive, the trail, and the break-in without hesitation.
3. When Yuki asks about her old case directly, his precision disappears into vague, deflecting language.
4. He implies familiarity with its framing without ever confirming his involvement outright.
5. Yuki presses once more, and he simply lets the silence answer in his place.
6. The statement closes with that one case still unresolved, exactly as he intends it to stay.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. The team gathers to mark the case closing, the mood muted rather than celebratory.
2. Marcus's empty seat sits between them, his review pending and his invitation quietly withheld.
3. Mei avoids the empty space more than anyone, still carrying what she didn't say in time.
4. Yuki mentions the review process briefly, keeping her own doubts about the department's earlier blind spot to herself.
5. Ethan raises a quiet toast that stops short of pretending everything is fully resolved.
6. The group settles into an unspoken agreement not to name what trust used to look like before tonight.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Ethan and Lina step away from the gathering to the promenade where the operation began.
2. They stand quietly, the fog from that night long since cleared.
3. Lina doesn't mention the source or the story, letting the moment be only about them.
4. Ethan doesn't mention the audition or the print waiting behind the red light.
5. Both leave several things unsaid, choosing the quiet instead of forcing resolution neither is ready for.
6. They stay together anyway, bruised but still choosing each other.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Ethan sits alone with the original hard drive Han once mailed him.
2. He replays the moment he first opened it, back when he thought it was only a puzzle.
3. He admits to himself, and no one else, that some part of him understood Han's reasoning for that one terrible second.
4. He recognizes that understanding something is not the same as accepting it, though the line feels thinner than he'd like.
5. He closes the drive without answering the question Han asked him at the overlook.
6. The episode ends on him alone with that unspoken answer, uncertain how much of it he's already accepted.
