--- SCENE 1 ---
1. The narrator explains that the faded supplier stamp Ethan set aside has been traced through business filing records to a second storage facility, this one on the far side of the city.
2. The trace runs through the same web of shell paperwork as the first unit, not through any name a character would recognize.
3. The narrator notes the team now has two units to compare instead of one, and two is the number where a pattern stops being circumstantial.
4. Ethan has not yet connected the supplier stamp to Han's shop; the audience is reminded of that gap without it closing here.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Ethan and Mei work the second unit methodically, photographing and cataloguing each item before touching it.
2. Mei recognizes more faces from the drive among the framed photographs lining this unit as well.
3. Ethan's flashlight lands on a small object that doesn't match the trophy pattern of the other families' belongings.
4. He goes still in a way Mei notices, though he recovers before she can ask what stopped him.
5. He tells her it's nothing, logging the item under a generic description rather than naming what it reminded him of.
6. The audience understands this is the first time the case has reached inside Ethan rather than around him.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Alone in his apartment, Ethan sets his own copy of the item's photo on the table and stares at it longer than he needs to.
2. He turns over exactly why it resembles something from his childhood home, testing the memory for how solid it actually is.
3. He tells himself the resemblance is a coincidence, that old-fashioned objects like this one were common enough in his mother's era.
4. He decides not to mention how much this rattled him to Mei or Yuki, framing it to himself as not wanting to derail the case with a personal hunch.
5. The audience senses he's arguing himself out of a feeling rather than reasoning it away.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. At Han's Camera Shop, Han greets Ethan and suggests, almost offhand, that they run through an old darkroom exercise together like they used to.
2. Ethan agrees easily, glad for something that feels unrelated to the case.
3. Han frames the invitation as nostalgia, nothing more, and Ethan takes it exactly that way.
4. The audience is reminded, without Ethan knowing it, that Han is deliberately pulling him closer while the case edges nearer to Ethan's own life.
5. Han watches Ethan's reaction to the invitation with more attention than the moment would suggest to anyone but him.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Lina meets her source again at the same foggy promenade spot, the exchange now practiced and quick.
2. The questions this time aren't about the case pattern but about Ethan directly — his schedule, his habits, where he lives.
3. Lina answers a couple of them before she registers how personal the questions have become.
4. She catches herself and stops, but not before something has already been given away.
5. The source reassures her, as always, that this is simply due diligence on background, and she wants to believe it.
6. She leaves with a knot of unease she can't fully name yet.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Walking home, Lina replays the conversation, trying to remember exactly which details about Ethan she confirmed.
2. She realizes she can't be sure how much she gave away versus how much the source already seemed to know.
3. She decides not to tell Ethan any of this happened, telling herself it isn't worth alarming him over yet.
4. The audience recognizes this as the moment her silence stops being about protecting her own investigation and starts being about something closer to him.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. A second notice arrives at the Cho Print Shop, this one harsher in tone than the first.
2. The new deadline is significantly sooner than what the family believed they had.
3. Lina's father reads it alone at the counter, the color draining from his face before he folds it away.
4. He doesn't call Lina or tell his wife right away, buying himself a few hours before he has to say the words out loud.
5. The audience is reminded that Lina still doesn't know the deadline has moved, even as she's already quietly draining her own savings to help.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Alone in her office after hours, Yuki pulls the full case file from her old closed investigation and lays it open on her desk.
2. She rereads her own original ruling with a scrutiny she didn't apply to it the first time.
3. Details she once explained away sit differently now, next to what she's seen on this case.
4. She catches herself hoping to find something that clears her original conclusion rather than confirms her fear.
5. She closes the file without telling Ethan or Mei she's done this, filing it under things she'll deal with once she's sure.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Marcus's contact messages him again, this time asking for more material than the arrangement originally covered.
2. For the first time, Marcus hesitates before answering, the request landing differently than the earlier ones did.
3. He starts typing a refusal, then deletes it, aware of how much faster his contact's help has made him look on the case.
4. He sends a smaller excerpt instead, telling himself it's a compromise rather than a concession.
5. He doesn't register that this hesitation is the first crack in his belief that he can end this whenever he wants.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Mei runs a deeper cross-reference between the unexplained login timestamps and the team's actual work schedules.
2. The pattern narrows sharply, lining up almost exactly with one team member's known hours.
3. She sits with Marcus's name on the screen longer than she wants to admit to herself.
4. She still doesn't tell Ethan, weighing whether to confront Marcus directly first.
5. The audience watches her cross from vague suspicion into something she can no longer dismiss as coincidence.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At the precinct, Ethan and Yuki spread both units' inventories across a table, item by item.
2. The geography of the two locations, plotted together, forms a shape neither of them expected — too deliberate to be random.
3. Yuki feels the pull toward her own old case again but keeps the observation to herself, unwilling to raise it before she's certain.
4. Ethan notices her go quiet at one point but assumes it's just the weight of the discovery, not anything personal to her.
5. Neither of them says aloud that the pattern now looks like it spans years, not just this case.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. At the rooftop bar, the group gathers again, the mood lighter on the surface than the day's discovery would suggest.
2. Marcus makes another joke about being left off the paperwork, and this time it lands with a harder edge that a couple of people notice.
3. Mei watches him a beat too long, her suspicion from the access logs sitting visibly behind her eyes.
4. Marcus catches her looking and covers with another joke, unaware of what she's actually thinking.
5. Ethan senses something has shifted between them but reads it as ordinary friction, not the beginning of something serious.
6. The group disperses without either Mei or Marcus naming what just passed between them.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Later, Ethan gently asks Lina what's been distracting her the past few days.
2. Lina deflects with the shop's ongoing stress, an answer practiced enough to sound completely natural.
3. Ethan accepts it, since it lines up with what he already believes is going on with her.
4. The audience knows the deflection covers something much closer to him than he realizes.
5. Lina feels a small flicker of guilt at how easily the lie worked, and lets it pass without correcting it.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Back in the darkroom, Han walks Ethan through an old exposure technique, adjusting his hands with familiar, teacherly patience.
2. Han frames the lesson as being about patience and control — waiting for exactly the right moment before committing to the image.
3. The language he uses about choosing a subject and knowing when it's "ready" carries a weight Ethan doesn't register as anything but photography talk.
4. Han watches Ethan's focus and ease in the room, privately gauging what it means about how close Ethan might be getting to seeing anything else.
5. Ethan leaves the session feeling closer to Han than before, with no sense of what the lesson was actually testing.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Back at the lab, Mei finds a private moment alone with Ethan and almost brings up what she's found about the access logs.
2. She starts to frame it carefully, wanting to raise it without accusing Marcus outright.
3. Ethan's easy trust in Marcus, visible in how he talks about him, makes her hesitate mid-sentence.
4. She backs off, telling him it's nothing and changing the subject before he can press her.
5. The audience watches her decide, again, to carry the suspicion alone rather than risk being wrong about a friend.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone that night, Lina opens a message to Ethan and starts typing out everything — the source, the questions about him, all of it.
2. She writes further than she has before, the confession taking real shape on the screen.
3. She rereads it, imagining his face as he takes it in, and her nerve falters.
4. She deletes the message line by line instead of sending it, telling herself she'll find a better way to say it later.
5. The audience recognizes this as the closest she's come yet to closing the gap between them, and the moment it slips past again.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Ethan returns home late, the day's darkroom session and the second unit's contents both still sitting unresolved in him.
2. He checks the door locks once, then finds himself checking them again without quite deciding to.
3. He can't explain the habit to himself, brushing it off as tiredness rather than naming what's actually unsettled him.
4. He finally settles into bed, the faded supplier stamp and the childhood-familiar object both still unconnected in his mind.
5. The audience is left holding the two threads he hasn't yet tied together as he falls asleep.
