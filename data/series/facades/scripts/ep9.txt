[SFX: a small intake of breath]

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: quiet, devastated]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: cracking]

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: barely holding]

She said she knew what I'd done to cover the Macau losses, and she said she was keeping the baby, and she said Julian deserved to know all of it before he put a ring on her finger in front of two hundred people.

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: voice like ice cracking]

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: almost inaudible]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: voice dropping to almost nothing]

--- PART 4 ---

[NARRATOR]
Same room, unbroken. Victoria hasn't moved; Konrad is already past the point of stopping.

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: reliving it, breathless]
I grabbed her arm because I wanted her to stop talking, that's all, I swear that's all I wanted in that second. She pulled away hard, harder than I expected, and she stepped back onto the loose board — the one by the railing, the one that had been loose for weeks and nobody fixed because nobody uses that end of the dock. And I reached for her again, and I shoved her. I didn't grab. I shoved. With both hands.

[SFX: a sharp splash, distant, then silence]

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: barely breathing]
Konrad.

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: unraveling completely]

I went in after her. I found her. She wasn't — she wasn't moving, Mother. There was blood from where she'd hit the post, right at her hairline, and she wasn't moving and I pulled her out onto the dock and I performed CPR on my dead pregnant almost-sister-in-law for eleven minutes by my watch, alone, in the dark, screaming.

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: a whisper, almost pleading]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: hollow]

I got the boat weights from the shed. I wrapped her in the tarp we use for the dock furniture. And I put her in the lake, and I told myself it was temporary, that I'd think of something by morning, and then morning came and I still hadn't thought of anything, so I just — kept not thinking of anything. For a year.

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: quiet, deadly precise]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: smallest voice yet]
The railing split when she hit it. A piece of it opened up my palm. I told Isabelle it was a kitchen knife because it was the first thing I thought of and once I'd said it to one person I had to say it to everyone.

[SFX: silence, rain fading to a drizzle]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: spent, hollowed out]
That's everything. That's all of it. There isn't any more.

[VICTORIA_VANDERBURG | style: measured, terrifyingly calm]

[KONRAD_VANDERBURG | style: uncertain]

[SFX: silence, a clock ticking somewhere down the hall]
