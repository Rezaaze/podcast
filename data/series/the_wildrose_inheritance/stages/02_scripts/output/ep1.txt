--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Off-season, the Baltic wind never really stops. Hotel Wildrose has stood on this stretch of coast for three generations, and now it belongs, in equal and uneasy halves, to a brother who has never wanted anything else and a sister who left it behind once already. This is the story of what a house costs when two people love it for entirely different reasons — and what happens when neither of them says so out loud.

[NARRATOR]
The morning after Werner Voss's funeral, in the hotel's empty lobby, his lawyer has just finished reading the will aloud. Tobias, who has run Wildrose's daily operations for six years, stands near the front desk. His sister Lena, who left for China with their mother when she was twelve and now runs a consultancy in Hamburg, sits across from him with the papers still in her lap.

[SFX: wind rattling old windows]

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: flat, still absorbing it]
Say it again. Slower.

[LENA_VOSS | style: quiet, careful]
Fifty-fifty. Both our names on the deed. And if either of us wants to sell — it needs both signatures. Not one.

[SFX: papers shuffling]

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: something like relief, guarded]
Good. Then nothing changes. I keep running it the way I have been, you —

[LENA_VOSS | style: cutting in, low]
Tobias.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: wary]
What.

[LENA_VOSS | style: forcing herself to say it]
I've already been approached. About selling my half.

[SFX: a chair creaking as someone shifts]

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: stunned, then sharp]
Approached. By who. When.

[LENA_VOSS | style: measured, not backing down]
A few weeks ago. Before Dad — before any of this. It's a real offer, Tobias. A serious one.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: voice rising slightly]
He's not even in the ground a full day and you're already — you had an offer sitting in your pocket at the reading of his will?

[LENA_VOSS | style: steady, tired]
I didn't bring it here to hurt you. I'm telling you because you deserve to hear it from me before you hear it from anyone else.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: quiet, wounded]
This hotel is the only thing I've ever wanted, Lena. You know that.

[LENA_VOSS | style: gentler, but firm]
I know what it is to you. I need you to understand it isn't nothing to me either. It's just — not the same thing.

[SFX: gulls crying outside, distant]

[NARRATOR]
Neither of them says the word again. The lawyer's papers sit between them, two signatures required, and only one of the four names on this story has said anything true yet.

--- PART 2 ---

[NARRATOR]
Later that morning, in Werner's study upstairs, Tobias sorts through boxes of his father's papers. Julia Wen, who has run the Wildrose front desk since she was nineteen, has come up to help him.

[SFX: cardboard box lid scraping open]

[JULIA_WEN | style: gentle, practical]
I pulled the guest ledgers first. Thought you'd want those separate from — everything else.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: distracted, grateful]
Thank you. I wouldn't know where to start on my own.

[SFX: a stack of envelopes sliding off a shelf onto the desk]

[JULIA_WEN | style: careful, not lingering]
There's a whole pile of these. Bank, looks like. Want them in the ledger box, or—

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: quick, closing the subject]
Just — set them aside. I'll deal with those later.

[SFX: envelopes stacked, a soft thump]

[JULIA_WEN | style: light, testing]
Later meaning this week, or later meaning the drawer where things go to be forgotten.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: a tired half-laugh]
Fair. I'll open them. Eventually.

[NARRATOR]
Julia lets it go. She has worked here long enough to know when to push and when not to.

[SFX: an old photograph being lifted, paper rustling]

[JULIA_WEN | style: soft, changing the subject]
Look at this one. You can't be more than ten.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: warmer, momentarily elsewhere]
God. Dad hated that shirt. Mum bought it for me anyway.

[JULIA_WEN | style: quiet]
You look happy.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: sincere, unguarded]
I was. This place — it was never complicated back then. It was just home.

[SFX: wind pressing against the window]

[JULIA_WEN | style: measured, careful not to reveal too much]
And now?

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: sighing, honest]
Now I don't know what it's going to be. But I know I couldn't do any of this without you up here. You're the only steady thing in this building right now.

[NARRATOR]
Julia holds the photograph a moment too long before setting it down.

[JULIA_WEN | style: light, deflecting]
Steady. That's a nice word for it.

[TOBIAS_VOSS | style: not noticing the deflection]
It's true.

[SFX: a car door closing somewhere below, distant]

[JULIA_WEN | style: quick, glad for the interruption]
That'll be reception. I should go down.

[NARRATOR]
She leaves before he can say anything else. Below her, on the boardwalk facing the water, Lena is already walking toward a man who has been waiting for her — someone from a life she left behind long before her father died.

--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]
On the boardwalk below the hotel, the wind coming straight off the water, Lena meets David Lin — the investor courting the sale of her half, and someone she knew long before he had any interest in Hotel Wildrose.

[SFX: gulls, waves against the seawall]

[DAVID_LIN | style: warm, careful not to overdo it]
You look cold. You always refused to dress for this coast.

[LENA_VOSS | style: dry, a little disarmed]
And you always showed up ten minutes early to watch me be wrong about it.

[SFX: footsteps on wet boards]

[DAVID_LIN | style: gentler now]
Eight years, Lena. I keep expecting that to feel like a longer time than it does.

[LENA_VOSS | style: guarded, redirecting]
Let's talk about the offer. That's why you're here.

[DAVID_LIN | style: measured, professional again]
Fair. The number hasn't moved. If anything, I pushed for more room on the timeline.

[LENA_VOSS | style: quiet, testing him]
And the building? What actually happens to it.

[DAVID_LIN | style: smooth, softened]
A renovation. Modernized rooms, updated systems. The bones stay — the parts that matter. Nobody's tearing down your father's hotel, Lena.

[SFX: wind gusting, a loose shutter rattling somewhere behind them]

[LENA_VOSS | style: relieved, wanting to believe it]
That's the only reason I can look at this without feeling like I'm selling him out.

[DAVID_LIN | style: gentle, not quite meeting her eyes]
I know what this building means. I wouldn't have taken the meeting otherwise.

[NARRATOR]
Neither of them names what else sits between them — a near-miss from years ago in Shanghai, close enough to still change the shape of a conversation.

[LENA_VOSS | style: almost teasing, undercutting the tension]
You never used to be this diplomatic. Shanghai David would've just told me the number and let me argue with it.

[DAVID_LIN | style: a real laugh, brief]
Shanghai David didn't have a board looking over his shoulder.

[SFX: a bench creaking as someone sits]

[LENA_VOSS | style: softer, more herself]
Is that what this is for you? One more thing to get right for them?

[DAVID_LIN | style: careful, deflecting from the truth]
It's a good deal for you. That's what it is.

[NARRATOR]
Lena studies him a moment, then looks back out at the grey water, choosing, for now, to trust the version of the story he's given her.

[LENA_VOSS | style: resolved, quieter]
Draw up the paperwork. I'll bring Tobias round to it. Somehow.

[DAVID_LIN | style: relieved, warm]
Let me know what you need from me. Whatever it takes.

[SFX: wind rising, gulls calling further off]

[NARRATOR]
That night, alone in his father's study, Tobias finally opens the stack of red-stamped letters from the bank — and realizes the hotel he's spent six years defending has been drowning in debt for longer than he ever knew.
