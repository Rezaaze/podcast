--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is now full dawn, only minutes after Mei-Lin rose in the pre-dawn quiet at the end of the previous night — the same morning continues, unbroken.
2. Liwei wakes to find the unopened seed tin still sitting on his table where he left it, and decides to hold off opening it until he's spoken with his mother in person.
3. In the lobby, word passes that the structural assessment crew is due within the hour, and Iggy is already present, coordinating logistics near the front desk.
4. Mei-Lin intercepts Iggy before he can head toward the glasshouse, asking for a private word "about the building's history" before the crew begins.
5. Declan, having decided overnight to tell the board everything before Iggy's findings force it out of him, asks Mei-Lin to call an emergency session for later that morning.
6. Soo-Jin, still absorbing what she learned about her great-aunt's injury the night before, resolves to gather Liwei and Aiko in Unit 4G and tell the full truth herself before the building's rumors get there first.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mei-Lin walks Iggy to the glasshouse entrance, not toward the southeast pier but toward the room as a whole, and raises the idea of a historic preservation clause protecting the original 1923 structure from invasive work.
2. She frames it as sentiment — old iron, old glass, a building worth saving whole rather than drilled into — and Iggy, who has no reason to read it as anything else, finds the pitch genuinely appealing.
3. Iggy notes a landmark or preservation designation could actually simplify Halloran's liability exposure and speed the deal, and agrees to bring the proposal to legal before the coring crew's equipment is unpacked.
4. The assessment crew, told to stand down pending the preservation review, packs up without ever touching the southeast pier.
5. Mei-Lin feels the closest thing to real relief she's had all season, aware she has bought protection this time instead of another borrowed delay.
6. She tells no one — not Liwei, not Aiko, not the board — what the clause is actually shielding, letting everyone believe it's only about the old ironwork.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Before the emergency session, Declan finds Aiko in her workshop and tells her plainly that he means to confess everything to the board that morning.
2. He asks her, in return, what a proper permitted retrofit of the northwest truss would actually cost, already doing the math in his head.
3. Aiko, caught off guard by his directness, admits she expected judgment from him, not help.
4. Declan says the board owes her the chance to fix what she broke rather than simply removing her, a position he arrives at partly from guilt over how long he let his own failure sit unaddressed.
5. Still unsettled by the specific hazard-report lie she told for Mei-Lin days earlier, Aiko wonders aloud — carefully, without naming it — whether everyone in this building is protecting something at that end of the roof.
6. Declan has no answer for her, having no idea what she means, and the question hangs unresolved as they head toward the office together.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. The emergency board session opens with Declan asking to speak before Iggy's findings are formally raised.
2. He tells the board everything this time — the fabricated Vance Municipal invoices, the six full years of skimmed dues, a true total near $240,000.
3. He explains it went to cover his son Bryan's gambling debts and, more recently, threats from the people Bryan owes money to.
4. He asks the board to let him make it right through repayment rather than hand the matter to the police, though he admits that choice may not be entirely his to make.
5. Liwei, moved by Declan's candor and wary of a criminal case dragging Halloran's due diligence into a courtroom, speaks in favor of a binding repayment plan over prosecution.
6. Mei-Lin, as chair, backs the same approach, aware that keeping this contained internally means fewer outside eyes on the building overall, though she says nothing of her own reasons for wanting that.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. After the session breaks, Soo-Jin gathers Liwei, Aiko, and Mei-Lin into her aunt's front room, unwilling to let the story reach them secondhand again.
2. She tells them plainly: Yoon Hee-ja died five years ago, she never reported it, and she has forged her aunt's signature on ballots and paperwork ever since.
3. She explains she was afraid of losing both her home and her legal standing in the country, with no other family here to fall back on.
4. She doesn't ask for forgiveness so much as brace herself for whatever comes next, watching each face for a verdict.
5. Liwei's shock softens almost immediately into something steadier, the discrepancies he'd been chasing for weeks finally resolving into a truth he can act on rather than just suspect.
6. Mei-Lin says little in the moment, already turning the problem over as a chairwoman looking for a way to keep this, too, from reaching outside scrutiny.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. The board reconvenes formally to decide both Declan's and Soo-Jin's fates, then folds Aiko's own standing into the same session before the day is done.
2. Liwei argues against triggering Halloran's identity verification on Soo-Jin at all, asking the board to let him help her properly this time rather than just having known and said nothing.
3. Mei-Lin proposes the board quietly reclassify the shareholding through a small internal transfer, avoiding the outside notarization that would expose how long the fraud has run.
4. Aiko is the first, and the loudest, to say Soo-Jin should be allowed to stay, her loyalty to Yoon Hee-ja's memory folding easily into fierce protectiveness of the niece who cared for her.
5. When the vote turns to Aiko's own standing, Mei-Lin backs a supervised, fully permitted repair plan that lets her keep her job — real mercy, though she's also quietly aware a superintendent who owes the board her position isn't one who asks twice why one corner still isn't on the repair schedule.
6. Soo-Jin, still furious and grieving over what she's learned about her great-aunt's fall, says nothing against the vote to keep Aiko on, the two feelings sitting uneasily side by side in her.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Soo-Jin finds Aiko alone once the room empties and tells her that Yoon Hee-ja was never someone who believed in throwing people away — the closest she can come, tonight, to forgiveness.
2. Aiko takes it without arguing she deserves it, the guilt she confessed the night before still sitting heavier in her than the board's mercy can fully lift.
3. Declan sits alone in the office afterward, aware that surviving the board is not the same as surviving Bryan or the people he owes.
4. Mei-Lin walks the halls quietly satisfied that both crises stayed inside the building, aware that every scandal kept off Halloran's desk is one fewer reason for anyone to look harder at the roof.
5. Liwei lingers near Unit 4G rather than going home, wanting Soo-Jin to know his support wasn't just for the room to hear.
6. No one in the building yet connects tonight's two confessions to the one the Castellan family has never made.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Back in his apartment, Liwei finally opens the battered seed tin from the potting shed and finds its false bottom.
2. Inside, he discovers an old letter in handwriting he doesn't recognize, mentioning a name — Lai — that means nothing to him yet.
3. He brings it straight to his mother, more curious than alarmed, expecting her to explain it away like she has every other old folder this season.
4. Instead, he watches her face change the instant she sees what's in his hands — a reaction sharper than anything the moment seems to warrant.
5. She takes the letter gently, says only that it's "very old family business" better left alone, and asks him not to mention it to anyone.
6. Liwei, unconvinced for the first time all season, doesn't let it go and starts asking questions she isn't willing to fully answer.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Later that night, Aiko finds Mei-Lin alone in the glasshouse near the southeast pier, ostensibly to report that the preservation clause has cleared the crew for good.
2. Watching Mei-Lin's hand rest against the pier the way it always does, Aiko notices — more sharply now than ever before — that this is the same corner she was asked to lie about, the same corner thirty years of repair crews have somehow always been steered away from.
3. She doesn't ask outright, but for the first time lets herself wonder, in Mei-Lin's presence, whether the chair's caution has always run deeper than a chair's usual caution.
4. Mei-Lin, sensing the shift in Aiko's silence without knowing its cause, deflects with the same practiced warmth she's used all season, and Aiko lets the moment pass unpushed.
5. Elsewhere in the building, Liwei sits with the old letter and his mother's unfinished answer, no closer to the truth than the moment he found the tin.
6. The season closes with the southeast pier still standing sealed and unexamined, its century-old secret intact for one more night, but for the first time held by more people's suspicion than by Mei-Lin's silence alone.
