--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Four days have passed since the shareholder-verification notice went up on the lobby board.
2. Board members gather informally in the lobby as another due-diligence deadline draws closer, tension humming under the surface calm.
3. Liwei, eager to keep Halloran's confidence after the walkthrough's good impression, offers to personally assemble a full shareholder and maintenance archive for the legal team.
4. He frames it as taking pressure off the overworked board, not realizing how much history — and how many secrets — that request will touch.
5. Declan forces an easy nod of approval while privately calculating what "full archive" actually means for his own files.
6. Mei-Lin's smile stays fixed as she agrees too, the word "full" landing in her like a stone.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Back at the apartment, Liwei sketches out which storage boxes the archive project will need, box by box.
2. He mentions he'll start with the oldest crates first, since they're likely the most disorganized.
3. Mei-Lin's hands tighten around her teacup before she can stop the reaction.
4. She steers him instead toward the recent tenant files, citing "sensitive tenant information" that needs a gentler hand than his.
5. Liwei, missing the alarm beneath her words, reads it as simple overprotectiveness about "the old stuff."
6. Once he agrees to start with the recent boxes, Mei-Lin excuses herself early, already thinking of the greenhouse.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Alone in the office, Declan pulls the three Vance Municipal Services invoices from their folder again.
2. He realizes with a cold jolt that Liwei sorting the full archive means these pages will pass in front of the chairwoman's own son, not just a stranger like Iggy.
3. That thought frightens him more than any auditor could — Iggy can be managed with a story, family curiosity cannot.
4. He begins listing which folders he could quietly thin out before Liwei ever reaches them.
5. He considers destroying the invoices outright, then decides a gap in the files would be its own kind of confession.
6. He settles on slipping the most damning pages into a folder Liwei is unlikely to open soon, buying time rather than safety.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. At the corner diner, a longtime resident retells the old story of "the night the roof came down," embellished the way old stories get.
2. He describes the glazing giving way and Yoon Hee-ja's injury with the casual relish of someone who wasn't the one who caused it.
3. Aiko keeps her expression neutral, though the details land closer to what actually happened than anyone at the table realizes.
4. Someone jokes that the super back then must have "done a rush job," not knowing Aiko herself was that super.
5. Aiko forces a small laugh along with the others, the sound not quite matching what's behind her eyes.
6. She leaves the table earlier than usual, the old story trailing her out the door.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Back in the workshop, Aiko pulls out a small private notebook of notes on the northwest truss, kept in no official file.
2. She reviews years of quiet fixes, remembering each one as both relief and reason.
3. She weighs reinforcing the truss once more, quietly, the same way she always has.
4. For the first time in years, she weighs actually telling someone — the board, Mei-Lin, anyone — before an inspection finds it first.
5. She imagines what it would mean for Yoon Hee-ja's injury to finally be explained, and what it would cost her to be the one explaining it.
6. She closes the notebook without deciding, sliding it back into its hiding place instead of an answer.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Liwei arrives at Unit 4G with a checklist, asking Soo-Jin for whatever shareholder documents she can provide for Halloran's file.
2. Soo-Jin hesitates only a moment before handing over what she can — the certificate, a few old forms — holding back what she can't.
3. Their conversation lingers past the paperwork, both of them clearly enjoying more than the errand.
4. Liwei notices himself wanting an excuse to stay a little longer, unaware of what he's stepping toward.
5. Soo-Jin feels the same pull and, underneath it, a colder fear — that the closer he gets to her, the closer he gets to the one thing that could ruin her.
6. She watches him leave with her documents in hand, unsure whether she just protected herself or handed him the first thread.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. That evening, Mei-Lin slips into the potting shed under the pretense of checking on seedlings.
2. She moves aside familiar clutter until her hand finds the false-bottomed seed tin exactly where it's always been.
3. She lifts the false bottom just enough to confirm the 1961 letter is still there, then closes it again without rereading it.
4. She allows herself a private moment of relief that Liwei's project hasn't reached this shed yet.
5. She reminds herself, as she has for years, that this hiding place has survived every renovation and survey so far.
6. She leaves the shed locked behind her, the secret undisturbed for one more night.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Liwei sits with Declan sorting older maintenance ledgers, cross-referencing them against board meeting minutes for the archive.
2. He casually notices one of the Vance Municipal Services invoice dates doesn't line up with the minutes recording its approval.
3. He mentions it to Declan as an offhand nitpick, more curious than concerned, assuming it's just a clerical error.
4. Declan's mind races even as his voice stays light, offering an explanation about backdated paperwork from a contractor known for sloppy invoicing.
5. Liwei accepts the answer without a second thought, moving on to the next folder.
6. Declan, left rattled, makes a private note to double-check every other date on that invoice before anyone else looks as closely.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Later, in the lobby with a stack of ballots spread on a folding table, Liwei cross-references shareholder signatures against the registry.
2. He notices Yoon Hee-ja's signature looks subtly different from one year's ballot to the next.
3. He also notices the dates on a couple of her forms feel slightly off, though nothing he could point to exactly.
4. He chalks it up to an elderly hand growing shakier with age, a small puzzle rather than a suspicion.
5. He sets the file aside to ask Soo-Jin about later, mostly as a reason to see her again.
6. The audience is left watching him hold, without knowing it, the first loose thread of Soo-Jin's five-year secret.
