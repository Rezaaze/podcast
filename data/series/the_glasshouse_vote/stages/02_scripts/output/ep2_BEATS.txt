--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Four days have passed since the tabled vote, and the building's patience is starting to fray.
2. Over jasmine tea, Liwei presses his mother on why the board keeps finding reasons to delay.
3. Mei-Lin answers with the language of "due diligence" and "protecting the co-op's interests," true words aimed at a false purpose.
4. Liwei reads her caution as sentimental attachment to a building she grew up in, nothing more.
5. Mei-Lin tells herself, watching him accept the explanation, that she isn't lying so much as sparing him.
6. Liwei mentions he'll start pulling together old board minutes to help move things along, unaware of what that search will eventually surface.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. A hastily called finance sub-committee meets to prepare for Halloran's due-diligence request.
2. Declan volunteers to organize and "streamline" the co-op's financial records ahead of any outside review.
3. He specifically offers to handle the maintenance and repair files himself, framing it as taking a burden off the board.
4. The board accepts gratefully, relieved someone competent is taking charge.
5. No one asks why he's so eager to be the one touching those particular files.
6. Declan leaves the meeting already mapping which invoices need attention first.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. At dusk, Mei-Lin walks the citrus rows alone under the pretense of checking the fruit.
2. Her path bends, as it always does, toward the southeast corner.
3. She runs a hand along the base of the pier, checking for cracks or disturbance only she would think to look for.
4. She thinks briefly of her grandmother, who walked this same corner for decades without ever explaining why.
5. She notices faint marking flags near the corner that weren't there before, evidence a survey crew has already begun scouting the site.
6. She leaves unsettled, aware her window for "careful caution" has just gotten shorter.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Liwei meets Iggy again at the corner diner, eager to show the co-op is moving.
2. He promises Halloran will have everything it needs — records, verifications, survey access — within the month.
3. Iggy reiterates the two hard requirements: notarized shareholder verification and the structural survey.
4. Iggy mentions a survey crew could be scheduled within weeks if the co-op confirms access soon.
5. Liwei, wanting to look decisive, agrees to a tentative survey date without checking with the board first.
6. Neither of them knows that date now sits on a collision course with two people's secrets.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Aiko stays in the workshop long after her usual hours, waiting until the building has gone quiet.
2. She fits new salvaged brackets directly over the old weld on the northwest truss, disguising rather than replacing the original repair.
3. She reasons privately that a fix nobody asked about can't become a confession.
4. She checks the stress point at the corner where the pier and truss line meet, gauging how much longer it will hold.
5. She logs nothing — no work order, no note to the board — continuing the pattern from years past.
6. Footsteps in the hallway outside make her go still and quickly cover the tools before anyone looks in.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone in 4G by lamplight, Soo-Jin lays out her aunt's old identification documents.
2. She practices Yoon Hee-ja's signature against an old grocery list, chasing the shape of a hand she hasn't seen move in years.
3. She tells herself she still has time before anyone asks for something notarized.
4. She catches herself trying to remember her aunt's face and can't quite hold the image steady.
5. A neighbor's comment overheard earlier — about Liwei promising a survey date — surfaces in her mind and tightens her focus.
6. She puts the documents away carefully, the same habit she repeats every night now.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Residents cluster at the lobby bulletin board, trading theories about why the vote keeps slipping.
2. None of the guesses come close to the real reasons.
3. Someone praises Declan for stepping up to organize the co-op's records, calling it reassuring.
4. Another repeats Liwei's framing of Mei-Lin's caution as old-fashioned sentimentality, and it spreads as accepted fact.
5. Word of a possible survey date circulates, adding a new note of urgency to the gossip.
6. The doorman remarks to no one in particular that the building feels different than it did a week ago.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Alone in the office after hours, Declan pulls the three Vance Municipal Services invoices from the files.
2. He studies them the way a man studies a lie he's about to have to tell better.
3. He rehearses the story he'll use if questioned: a contractor that went under, records lost in the transition.
4. He isn't sure the story will hold up against an actual audit, only that it might survive a glance.
5. He sets aside which documents he'll need to alter or misplace before any outside review begins.
6. He locks the ledger drawer again on his way out, the habit now fully automatic.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Digging through a storage closet for old meeting minutes, Liwei finds a folder marked "ARCHIVE — PRE-1970."
2. He asks his mother, offhand, what's inside it.
3. Mei-Lin keeps her voice light and steers the conversation elsewhere before he can open it.
4. To Liwei, it registers only as old paperwork, a mild curiosity he'll get back to when he has time.
5. He sets the folder aside, unaware he's holding something that touches a fact no one alive is supposed to know.
6. Mei-Lin, watching him move on, feels the margin she's protecting grow one folder thinner.
