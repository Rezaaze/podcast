--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Two days have passed since Mei-Lin's unresolved night at the southeast pier; the coring test is now scheduled for the day after tomorrow.
2. Liwei sits with the shareholder registry and years of ballots, finishing the cross-reference he set aside earlier in the archive project.
3. He confirms what he'd only half-noticed before: Yoon Hee-ja's driver's license expired years ago and was never renewed, and neither was her passport.
4. He lines up five years of ballot signatures side by side and sees the drift isn't just an old woman's shaky hand but a slow, deliberate shift.
5. He tells himself there's an innocent explanation, even as the pattern refuses to resolve into one.
6. He decides, without fully admitting why, that he needs to ask Soo-Jin directly rather than raise it with the board first.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. In the lobby, Declan runs into Aiko carrying a fresh delivery of steel bracing hardware.
2. He jokes, lightly, that she must be planning to rebuild the whole roof at this rate.
3. Aiko forces an easy laugh and deflects with a line about old pipes needing old fixes, giving him nothing to hold onto.
4. Declan, with no idea the joke touches anything real, moves on to his own worries without a second thought.
5. Aiko watches him go, unsettled that even a throwaway comment can graze this close to the truth.
6. She resolves to carry the next delivery up a back stairwell instead of through the lobby.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Liwei brings Soo-Jin tea and the last of the shareholder paperwork under the pretense of finishing the archive file.
2. Their conversation stays warm at first, both of them lingering longer than the paperwork requires.
3. Liwei mentions, as lightly as he can manage, that her aunt's license seems to have lapsed years ago and asks if there's a renewal somewhere he missed.
4. Soo-Jin offers an explanation about her aunt rarely leaving the apartment and letting the small stuff slide, watching his face for whether it lands.
5. Liwei accepts it on the surface, but the unease the registry left in him doesn't fully clear.
6. He leaves without pressing further, telling himself he's imagining a problem that isn't there.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Iggy meets Declan again, mentioning that the assistant's name Declan gave him for Vance Municipal Services turned up no employment record anywhere.
2. Declan improvises further, claiming Vance had actually been subcontracting under a different registered name he can look into for Iggy.
3. Iggy, still outwardly courteous, asks Declan to have that registered name ready within the week.
4. Declan agrees smoothly, already planning to build documentation for a company that was never real in the first place.
5. Internally, Declan recognizes he is no longer managing a misunderstanding but constructing an entire second lie to protect the first.
6. He leaves the meeting for the first time seriously weighing how much time he actually has left before the story collapses.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In the glasshouse ahead of the coring crew's arrival, Aiko inspects the northwest truss one last time and finds a hairline crack in the glazing she hasn't seen before.
2. She traces it back toward the disguised weld and realizes the brace itself may be starting to shift under its own patched weight.
3. Elsewhere in the same room, Mei-Lin notices fresh survey stakes now planted directly beside the southeast pier, closer than the marking flags ever were.
4. She crouches to check the concrete at the base of the pier and finds a fine crack she's certain wasn't there a week ago.
5. Neither woman tells the other what she's found, each reading the other's alarm as ordinary concern for the building.
6. Both leave the glasshouse separately, each certain her own hidden corner may not hold through the coring test.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Liwei returns to 4G that evening, unable to let the license and signature discrepancies rest after all.
2. He lays out what he's found plainly: the expired identification, the drifting signature, the years without a single verified in-person sighting.
3. Soo-Jin's answers come too fast and contradict each other — first that her aunt is simply reclusive, then that she travels often, then neither.
4. Liwei watches the contradictions pile up faster than any explanation can catch them and understands, with cold certainty, that something is deeply wrong.
5. Soo-Jin sees the moment his face shifts from confusion to realization and knows there's no version of this conversation where she still decides when the truth comes out.
6. She finally says it plainly: her great-aunt died five years ago, and she has been living under her name ever since.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Shaken and unable to face Liwei's silence a moment longer, Soo-Jin leaves 4G and goes straight to Aiko's workshop.
2. She confesses what she just told Liwei, braced for shock and the end of whatever trust Aiko had in her.
3. Aiko's face doesn't register the surprise Soo-Jin expects, and she admits she's suspected the truth for at least a year — a woman doesn't simply stop aging in photographs.
4. Aiko says nothing about why she never reported it, only that she has her own reasons not to invite anyone looking too closely at anything in this building.
5. Beneath that, unspoken, sits Aiko's old guilt over the roof collapse that injured the real Yoon Hee-ja on her watch, a debt she has never told anyone about.
6. Aiko offers Soo-Jin the first steady, unpanicked comfort she's had all night, without promising the secret can stay buried.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. In the office, Declan watches Liwei deliver the finished shareholder and maintenance archive box by box, ready for Halloran's legal team.
2. He realizes the invoices he meant to quietly thin out are now bound into a file he no longer controls or can alter unnoticed.
3. He flips through the folder once Liwei has gone, confirming his own fabricated documents sit exactly where a lawyer will find them.
4. He calculates, for the first time in concrete terms, how many days remain before that file leaves the building for good.
5. He considers, again, confessing something small to the board to get ahead of it, and again talks himself out of it.
6. He locks the office that night with the sense that the decision has effectively already been made for him.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Late that night, Mei-Lin sits alone with tea gone cold, still turning over the fresh crack she found at the southeast pier's base.
2. She has no idea that, a few floors below, her son has just learned the truth about Soo-Jin and Unit 4G.
3. She has no idea that Aiko now carries not one secret in this building but two, bound together by an old guilt neither woman has spoken aloud.
4. She thinks only of tomorrow's coring test and how little distance now sits between her and the same reckoning.
5. She rehearses, one more time, the words she might say to Liwei if she ever found the courage, still unable to make them real.
6. She goes to bed with the building around her holding more truth than it has in decades, and no one — including her — yet able to see all of it at once.
