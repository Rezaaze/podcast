--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the same day as the inspector's visit — only a few hours have passed since he knelt at the southeast pier and reached for his testing tool.
2. His reading comes back inconclusive but serious enough that he orders a full structural assessment crew to return within forty-eight hours, buying one more night but nothing more.
3. Word spreads that the board will hold an emergency session that evening to address both the pier finding and Declan's compliance matter together.
4. Mei-Lin understands, hearing the assessment timeline, that her margin has finally run out to nothing.
5. Aiko decides privately that if a confession is coming tonight, it should be one she chooses to give rather than one an inspector's tool eventually forces out of the wall.
6. Liwei, still turning over the discrepancies in Soo-Jin's shareholder file, resolves to find her before the session starts, though he doesn't yet know what he'll say.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The office fills early for the emergency session, tempers already thin from the day's compounding bad news.
2. Soo-Jin slips in at the back, having come looking for Liwei, and stays when no one thinks to send her away amid the tension.
3. Before Iggy's compliance findings can be raised, Aiko quietly asks Mei-Lin for a few minutes of the board's time first.
4. Mei-Lin, assuming this concerns the hazard report she asked Aiko to file, feels a fresh spike of alarm she doesn't let show.
5. Aiko says nothing to correct that assumption, letting Mei-Lin believe whatever gets her through the next few minutes.
6. The board, surprised but curious, agrees to let Aiko speak before anything else.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Aiko tells the board about the storm fifteen years ago, the cracked trusses, and the unpermitted weld she made herself without filing for inspection.
2. She admits the patch was built from mismatched salvaged steel, never meant to last as long as it has.
3. She states plainly that it was her own failed repair that gave way six years ago during the spring gathering, not simple bad luck.
4. Several board members realize for the first time that the old "roof accident" story was never the whole truth.
5. Mei-Lin, as chair, praises Aiko's courage aloud even as she feels the opposite pull growing inside her toward her own far older secret.
6. Aiko admits she has spent six years quietly re-shoring the same corner instead of ever reporting it, and waits to see what the board will do with her now.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Aiko finishes by naming who was hurt when the truss gave way — Yoon Hee-ja — and that the injury led to the decline that killed her the following year.
2. It is the first time Aiko has said that name aloud in front of Soo-Jin, and the room's shock doesn't register with her the way Soo-Jin's stillness does.
3. Hearing the injury and decline described in this much detail for the first time, Soo-Jin absorbs a fuller, harder truth than the one she's carried alone for five years.
4. Beside her, Liwei watches his mother's face during the confession and, for the first time, starts to wonder why she reacts so strangely to any talk of that one greenhouse corner — though he can't yet put the thought into words.
5. Something in him also softens toward Soo-Jin in this moment, the shape of her own secret starting to look less like fraud and more like an inherited weight she never chose.
6. No one in the room yet says anything to connect Aiko's confession to Mei-Lin's silence, though two people have just quietly begun wondering.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Aiko's honesty strips away the last excuse Declan had for hiding his own theft, and he asks to speak before Iggy's findings are even raised.
2. He admits the missing reserve funds go back the full six years of his term, not the single lapse he claimed at the earlier session.
3. He confesses it was to cover his son Bryan's gambling debts, and more recently, threats from the people Bryan owes.
4. The board reacts with a second wave of shock in one night, several looking to Mei-Lin for how to respond.
5. Mei-Lin notes privately that whatever mercy the board found for Aiko tonight is unlikely to reach as far for Declan, whose deception ran longer and touched money rather than grief.
6. The session ends without a decision on consequences for either confession, the board too shaken to do more than adjourn.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. After the session breaks, Mei-Lin walks alone to the glasshouse, drawn again toward the southeast pier.
2. Standing at the crack the inspector marked, she comes closer than she has in decades to telling someone — anyone — what's sealed inside it.
3. She thinks of Aiko's confession and Declan's, both making her own silence feel heavier than it has all season.
4. She rehearses, once more, the opening words of a truth she has never spoken aloud to a living soul.
5. She decides again to protect the family's century-old silence for one more night, telling herself tomorrow will be different.
6. She locks the glasshouse and leaves the pier exactly as unresolved as she found it.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Meanwhile, still unsettled by the evening and needing something to do with his hands, Liwei volunteers to help clear out the old potting shed shelves.
2. Behind a row of clay pots he finds a battered, unusually heavy seed tin that doesn't match anything else stored there.
3. Its weight and hiding place strike him as deliberate rather than simply forgotten.
4. He pockets it, planning to ask his mother about it later, with no idea yet what it might hold.
5. He has no way of knowing it sits only a few feet from the pier his mother stood beside less than an hour before.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Later that night, Aiko finds Soo-Jin alone and, away from the board, says directly to her face what she only gestured at inside the meeting — that she is, in part, responsible for what happened to the woman Soo-Jin loved.
2. Saying it plainly this time, with nothing left to lose, brings Aiko a strange, painful relief she didn't expect.
3. Soo-Jin sits with this fuller truth about her great-aunt, the shame of her own forged signature feeling almost small beside it now.
4. Across the building, Declan waits alone in the office, aware that confessing to the board is not the same as surviving whatever comes next — Bryan, the people he owes, Halloran.
5. Liwei sits with the unopened seed tin on his table, torn between curiosity and a suspicion about his mother he still can't name.
6. Mei-Lin lies awake, aware the structural assessment crew will arrive with daylight whether she's ready or not.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Just before dawn, Mei-Lin rises early, already rehearsing how she'll face the assessment crew due within hours.
2. She has no idea her son now holds the one object that could undo everything she chose to protect again last night.
3. She has no idea that Soo-Jin, having just heard the fuller truth about her great-aunt, may no longer be willing to stay quiet about anything — including things that touch the Castellan family.
4. Unaware any of tonight's confessions took place, Iggy finalizes his memo forwarding the audit findings to Halloran's compliance office, still believing the co-op is hiding rather than confronting its truths on its own.
5. The building around Mei-Lin now holds more truth spoken aloud than in the whole season before it, and still the southeast corner waits, unresolved, for whatever the coming day brings.
