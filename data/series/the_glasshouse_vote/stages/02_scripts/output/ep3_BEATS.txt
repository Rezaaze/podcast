--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Two days have passed since Liwei found the ARCHIVE folder in the storage closet.
2. Iggy arrives at the lobby earlier than the tentatively promised date, clipboard in hand, asking Mei-Lin for "just a look around, nothing official."
3. Mei-Lin, caught off guard by the early arrival, agrees smoothly while mentally scrambling to compress her rehearsed route into far less time than she planned.
4. She quietly signals for word to reach Aiko that Iggy is already in the building, not later as expected.
5. To Mei-Lin, Iggy's easy smile is exactly what makes an "unofficial" visit dangerous — nothing on the record to prepare for, nothing to stop.
6. Declan, spotting Iggy near the elevator, moves to intercept him before he can head straight up.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Aiko gets word that Iggy is already in the building, hours before she expected any visit.
2. She scrambles back to the northwest truss, forced to finish a repair she thought she had another day to complete.
3. Her hands shake fitting the final brace over the old weld — the first time her hands have shaken at this work in years.
4. She second-guesses whether the disguise will survive even a casual glance, let alone a trained eye.
5. The memory of Yoon Hee-ja's injury flickers at the edge of her focus, sharpening her panic without slowing her hands.
6. She hides the tools, wipes away any sign of rushed work, and composes her face before heading up toward the glasshouse.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mei-Lin reaches the glasshouse ahead of Iggy, mentally re-mapping the walking route she has rehearsed for weeks.
2. With less lead time than planned, she trims the route down to only the essentials that keep his feet moving.
3. She runs through her talking points — the citrus, the old ironwork — anything that pulls his eyes up and away from the floor.
4. She checks the sightlines toward the southeast corner, gauging exactly what still needs to be blocked from view.
5. She notices Aiko hasn't arrived yet and feels a spike of worry she doesn't let show on her face.
6. She reminds herself this is procedural caution, the same phrase she has used to steady herself for weeks.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Declan catches Iggy near the elevator with a folder of neatly organized, recent invoices in hand.
2. He steers the conversation toward the newest maintenance records, keeping Iggy's attention on anything less than two years old.
3. While waiting for the glasshouse tour, Iggy idly flips through an older folder Declan hasn't finished cleaning up yet.
4. Iggy's eye catches one of the Vance Municipal Services invoices and he photographs the page out of habit, without any visible suspicion.
5. Declan, mid-sentence about the "streamlined" filing system, doesn't notice the photo being taken.
6. Satisfied he's kept Iggy clear of anything alarming, Declan finally walks him toward the elevator, unaware what has already slipped past him.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei-Lin leads Iggy through the citrus rows exactly as rehearsed, warm anecdotes flowing easily.
2. She narrates the history of the original ironwork, keeping his eyes up and his feet in motion.
3. As they near the southeast corner, Aiko appears just in time and repositions a potted lemon tree to block the worst of the old welds and the base of the pier.
4. Mei-Lin tells Iggy the southeast corner is "the oldest and most fragile part of the room, best admired from a distance" — true in a way she needs him never to fully understand.
5. Iggy reads the whole performance as charming eccentricity: an overly attached chairwoman, a protective super, nothing more.
6. The audience sees both women converge on protecting the very same corner for entirely different, unspoken reasons, neither aware of the other's secret.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. In the background of the day's tension, Soo-Jin opens an email from Halloran's legal team.
2. It requests notarized in-person shareholder identification within thirty days.
3. The deadline lands on her like a countdown she can no longer pretend isn't real.
4. She has no plan yet beyond delay, rehearsing excuses — a lost passport, a slow mail system — that she already knows won't survive a notary's scrutiny.
5. She thinks briefly of asking someone for help, then stops herself, unsure who could help without exposing her.
6. She closes the laptop without replying, the thirty-day deadline now fixed in her mind.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Liwei meets Iggy at the corner diner, buoyant and treating the walkthrough as an unambiguous success.
2. He recaps the highlights — the citrus, the ironwork story — proud of how smoothly it went.
3. Iggy stays pleasant but noncommittal, offering little beyond polite agreement.
4. At one point Iggy glances at his phone, distracted by something from earlier that he doesn't mention.
5. Liwei, hearing only warmth in Iggy's manner, doesn't register the distraction as meaning anything.
6. Iggy confirms the formal structural survey and shareholder verification deadlines are still on track — the real hurdles still ahead, unmoved by today's good impression.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Back in the workshop, Aiko finally exhales, believing she finished the bracing repair with time to spare.
2. She begins putting her tools away, allowing herself a rare moment of relief.
3. Through the doorway she sees Iggy lingering a beat too long near the office maintenance logs on his way out of the building.
4. She freezes, unable to tell if he's simply killing time or actually reading something specific.
5. The audience watches Iggy photograph a page from the log purely out of habit — a discrepancy in the dates has snagged his professional instincts in a way he can't yet name.
6. Aiko can't tell if what she just watched is nothing at all, or the first thread of something starting to come loose.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. At dusk, Mei-Lin watches the glasshouse empty out after the day's strain.
2. She replays the walkthrough beat by beat, checking for any misstep she might have missed.
3. She notes with quiet relief that Iggy never lingered near the southeast corner.
4. She thinks of the potted lemon tree Aiko moved into place, grateful without understanding why Aiko did it or what Aiko herself was protecting.
5. She cannot tell whether she has bought real time or only postponed the moment someone finally looks too closely.
6. She locks up the glasshouse for the night, the southeast corner dark and quiet behind her.
