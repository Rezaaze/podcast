--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Same day as the scheduled coring test — less than twenty-four hours since Mei-Lin's sleepless night at the southeast pier.
2. Word spreads fast through the building that Iggy has called an emergency board session for that afternoon, over "a compliance matter."
3. Declan reads the notice and understands immediately, with sick certainty, exactly what it means for him.
4. Liwei, still absorbed in what he learned about Soo-Jin the night before, barely registers the urgency in the word "compliance."
5. When Mei-Lin insists the session take priority over anything happening at the pier today, her intensity is sharper than any delay she's used before, briefly pulling Liwei out of his own preoccupation to wonder what she's actually so afraid of.
6. He has no room to chase the thought, Soo-Jin still occupying every other corner of his mind, and lets it go.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Iggy's formal report — the result of a deeper licensing check into Vance Municipal Services — arrives in the co-op office and Mei-Lin's inbox at once.
2. It states plainly that Vance dissolved four years before the earliest invoice date, and that the "assistant" Declan named exists in no employment record anywhere.
3. Mei-Lin reads it without yet grasping its full weight, registering only that it concerns Declan and the maintenance files.
4. Declan, reading the same report at his own desk, recognizes there is no further story left to invent.
5. He briefly considers skipping the emergency session altogether, then realizes absence would look exactly like what it is.
6. He locks the ledger drawer one last time, aware for the first time that it won't matter either way.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Before the session, Mei-Lin finds Aiko near the southeast pier, the coring crew's equipment already staged nearby.
2. She asks Aiko, keeping her voice carefully casual, to flag a hazard near the corner — a cracked support, unsafe footing, anything that would push the crew's start back another day.
3. It is the first time in years Mei-Lin has asked Aiko to lie about this specific corner, rather than simply steering people away from it herself.
4. Aiko agrees out of the same loyalty that has held for decades, but the specificity of the request — this corner, not any other — snags at her.
5. She doesn't ask why, telling herself it isn't her place, even as she can't shake the feeling there's something here Mei-Lin has never told her.
6. Aiko heads off to file the false hazard report, now carrying a lie for Mei-Lin on top of the one she already carries for herself.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. In Unit 4G, Soo-Jin has heard nothing further from Liwei since her confession — no reassurance, no ultimatum, only silence.
2. Rumors of "something with 4G" have already started moving through the building, vaguer and more distorted with each retelling.
3. Certain that exposure, and deportation behind it, is now only a matter of time, she pulls out a bag and begins packing only what she can carry.
4. She sets her aunt's old identification documents on top, unsure whether she's keeping them as proof of who she was or evidence to destroy.
5. She keeps glancing at her phone, half-expecting either Liwei or a stranger from Halloran's legal team to call first.
6. She doesn't finish packing, the bag left half full on the bed, unable to commit to leaving before she knows what Liwei will do.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. The emergency session gathers in the co-op office rather than the glasshouse, the mood tighter than any meeting this season.
2. Iggy lays the audit findings out plainly: the dissolved contractor, the invented assistant, the dates that cannot be reconciled with reality.
3. Cornered with the dissolution paperwork in front of him, Declan admits the invoices are false, but frames it as a single desperate lapse rather than what it actually is.
4. The board reacts with shock, several members immediately assuming this is an isolated incident rather than a longer pattern.
5. Mei-Lin, genuinely moved by Declan's visible unraveling but also aware that a deeper investigation could easily reach past him, argues for handling the matter internally rather than looping Halloran's legal team in any further.
6. Iggy stays outwardly agreeable but makes clear, without quite saying so, that this is no longer a matter the co-op gets to keep quiet on its own.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. After the session, Aiko finishes filing the false hazard report on the southeast pier, delaying the coring crew as promised.
2. She returns to the glasshouse to check the northwest truss, the hairline crack from days before still unresolved and, if anything, worse.
3. She realizes she is now holding two separate fabrications at once — her own decades-old repair and the hazard lie she just told for Mei-Lin — with no way to manage either if anyone looks closely at both corners on the same day.
4. She catches herself wondering, for the first time directly, what exactly is buried under Mei-Lin's insistence on that one specific pier.
5. She pushes the thought down, telling herself she doesn't have room to carry a third secret that isn't even hers.
6. She locks the workshop, more exhausted than she has let herself feel all season.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Iggy returns to his temporary office setup and drafts a formal memo to Halloran's senior counsel, attaching the audit findings and a note on the delayed coring test.
2. He treats the fabricated invoices as a serious compliance issue, not co-op incompetence, flagging that it could delay or jeopardize the acquisition's timeline.
3. He mentions, almost as an aside in the same memo, that the structural survey was pushed back again on the co-op's own report of a new hazard near the southeast pier.
4. He has no reason yet to connect the delayed survey to anything beyond routine caution, but the pattern of delays at that exact corner registers as one more thing worth watching.
5. He sends the memo up the chain, aware it will likely bring more scrutiny onto the building than anyone there currently expects.
6. Within Halloran's office, the memo lands on a desk that will, within days, decide whether to send someone of their own to look more closely.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. By evening, the building is thick with fragmented gossip — Declan's name attached to "the invoices," Soo-Jin's name attached to "the papers," neither story quite matching what actually happened.
2. On the stairwell, Aiko intercepts two of the building's oldest residents before they can repeat what they've heard to anyone official.
3. She tells them only enough of the truth about Soo-Jin to make them understand there's more at stake than gossip, calling in decades of trust to buy her a little more time.
4. Liwei overhears a fragment of the stairwell conversation and realizes, with a cold twist, that his own silence about Soo-Jin is no longer just his to keep — the building is already talking.
5. He understands, more clearly than before, that saying nothing is itself a choice he's already making, not a neutral position.
6. No one on the stairs mentions Declan's actual number, or how long it's been going on, the real scope of both secrets still safely below the surface of the rumors.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. The next morning, a city safety inspector arrives at the glasshouse in response to Aiko's filed hazard report, not as part of Halloran's survey at all.
2. Mei-Lin, expecting only a delay, is caught off guard to find an inspector actually walking the southeast corner in person, closer than any Halloran survey has come all season.
3. Aiko realizes, with rising alarm, that a real inspector called to check a fake hazard might look harder at the pier than the fake hazard itself ever warranted.
4. The inspector kneels at the base of the pier, runs a hand along the same fine crack Mei-Lin found days earlier, and asks, mildly, how long it's been there.
5. Neither woman answers immediately, each recalculating in real time whether the lie meant to buy them time has instead delivered exactly the scrutiny they were trying to avoid.
6. The episode ends with the inspector reaching for a tool to test the crack further, the southeast corner now facing more direct attention than it has in over a century.
