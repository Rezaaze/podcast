--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Three days have passed since Mei-Lin locked up the glasshouse after Iggy's walkthrough.
2. Alone in the co-op office, Declan opens a preliminary due-diligence memo Halloran's office emailed to the board.
3. Buried in a checklist of routine follow-ups, one line flags "Vance Municipal Services — contractor licensing verification pending."
4. Declan's stomach drops recognizing the name from his own fabricated invoices.
5. He tells himself it might mean nothing, a bureaucratic box to tick, even as he starts rehearsing an explanation.
6. He deletes the memo from his own inbox after reading it twice, telling no one on the board what he found.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Later that day, Iggy catches Declan near the lobby mailboxes, clipboard tucked under his arm.
2. He mentions, almost offhand, that one of the invoices he photographed listed a contractor he couldn't find in any current licensing database.
3. Declan keeps his voice light, saying the company folded years ago and records from that period are patchy.
4. Iggy nods easily, saying it's probably nothing, just something he needs to note for the file.
5. Internally, Declan recognizes the casualness as exactly what makes it dangerous — Iggy isn't accusing him of anything, which means no one is watching him closely yet.
6. Iggy privately files it away as a minor compliance flag, already planning to request the contractor's licensing paperwork next, with no suspicion of fraud.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. In Unit 4G, Soo-Jin finds a formal letter from Halloran's legal team slid under her door, addressed to "Yoon Hee-ja."
2. It spells out exactly what the earlier email only gestured at: a notarized in-person appointment, a specific date, a list of acceptable identification.
3. She reads the requirements twice, and each one names something she cannot produce for a woman five years dead.
4. She sets the letter down carefully, feeling the whole process suddenly narrowing toward her all at once.
5. She considers, briefly, telling Aiko or anyone the truth, then closes off the thought as fast as it comes.
6. She hides the letter in the same drawer as her aunt's old identification.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. At the corner diner, Aiko notices Soo-Jin picking at her food, clearly somewhere else.
2. Aiko asks gently if something's wrong, and Soo-Jin blames it on "paperwork," a word technically true and completely insufficient.
3. She mentions vaguely that the sale is bringing up "old forms" and "notary stuff," letting Aiko fill in the rest with her own assumptions.
4. Aiko, reading only ordinary immigration-paperwork anxiety common among newer residents, offers reassurance about how the board usually helps with that kind of thing.
5. Soo-Jin lets the comfort land without correcting it, aware the relief Aiko offers doesn't actually touch the real problem.
6. Neither woman realizes the other is quietly protecting a secret of her own just a few floors away.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In the glasshouse, Aiko runs a fresh inspection of the northwest truss brace, still uneasy since Iggy lingered near the maintenance logs.
2. She presses at the disguised weld, satisfied for now that it's holding, though she knows "for now" isn't the same as "safe."
3. Across the room, Mei-Lin lingers near the southeast pier under the pretense of checking the citrus, still unsettled by the marking flags she found and the scrutiny the walkthrough brought.
4. Mei-Lin notices Aiko's focus on the northwest corner and reads it only as ordinary maintenance vigilance, not knowing what Aiko is actually hiding there.
5. Aiko, in turn, notices Mei-Lin's pull toward the southeast corner and privately chalks it up to sentimental attachment to the old ironwork, missing what actually anchors her there.
6. Both women leave the glasshouse having quietly reinforced their own secret, each unaware the other did the same.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Over dinner, Liwei tells his mother the paperwork needs to move faster if they want to keep Halloran's confidence after the walkthrough's good impression.
2. He brings up the ARCHIVE folder again, asking if they should just have it digitized to save time.
3. Mei-Lin deflects with the same "not organized" language she's used for weeks, hearing her own words start to sound thin even to her.
4. Liwei, frustrated but not suspicious, reads her hesitation as an old woman's reluctance to let outsiders touch the building's history.
5. He also mentions, in passing, that Declan seems unusually tense lately about the co-op's finances, more than the sale alone would explain.
6. Mei-Lin notes Declan's tension herself but files it away only as ordinary stress over the acquisition, with no idea what he's actually hiding.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Back in the office that evening, Declan drafts a more elaborate account of a "verbal agreement" he claims to have had with Vance Municipal Services before it dissolved.
2. He invents details — a handshake deal, a discount for cash, no formal contract because the owner was an old friend — specific enough to sound plausible, vague enough to leave nothing to check.
3. He rehearses the story aloud, testing which parts might invite a follow-up question he can't answer.
4. Privately, he knows the story wouldn't survive a real audit, only a polite one.
5. He considers, then rejects, quietly paying back a portion of the missing funds, unsure it would even help at this point.
6. He locks the invoices back in the drawer, the cover story now ready if Iggy asks again.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. A formal notice appears on the lobby bulletin board: every shareholder of record must complete notarized in-person identity verification before the sale can close.
2. Residents cluster to read it, some joking about digging up old passports, others mildly annoyed at the inconvenience.
3. No one in the lobby connects the notice to anything but routine bureaucracy.
4. Aiko passes the board on her way to the roof and reads it without any sense it touches her own hidden repair.
5. Mei-Lin, as chairwoman, forwards it to the shareholder list without registering anything unusual about Unit 4G specifically.
6. The notice becomes, for most of the building, just one more item in an already strange few weeks.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone that night, Soo-Jin spreads her aunt's expired passport, old driver's license, and the notarized 1998 share certificate across the table.
2. She compares the certificate's signature to her own practiced version, gauging how close is close enough to survive a distracted glance but not real scrutiny.
3. She counts the days left before the notary appointment named in Halloran's letter, weighing them against how long she'd need to disappear if she chose that instead.
4. She considers forging a doctor's note claiming illness to buy time, then discards it as only delaying the inevitable by weeks.
5. She thinks, unbidden, of Aiko's easy reassurance at the diner, and how little it actually solved.
6. She puts the documents away in their drawer, no closer to a real answer than when the letter first arrived.
