[SFX: a glass door swinging shut against its frame]

[NARRATOR]

It's the kind of competence people are grateful for and rarely examine.

[SFX: a stack of mail sliding through a brass slot]

[NARRATOR]
Someone else repeats what's become the accepted version of Mei-Lin Castellan's caution — that she's simply an old woman sentimental about a building she's lived in her whole life, in no hurry to let it go. It's Liwei's explanation, passed hand to hand until it sounds like something everyone always knew rather than something one person said once. Nobody in the lobby has a better guess, so the sentimental-grandmother story spreads unchallenged, gathering the small authority that repetition gives a thing.

[SFX: an elevator bell chiming once]

[NARRATOR]
A newer rumor moves through the group too — that a survey crew might be scheduled within weeks, that Liwei all but promised it over dinner somewhere. The word survey lands differently on different people. For most it's just one more sign the sale is finally moving. Two residents drift toward the stairwell still trading guesses, none of them anywhere near the truth of what a crew with proper equipment might actually find.

[SFX: the front door's weather stripping dragging open]

[NARRATOR]

No one answers him. The lobby empties out slowly, the notice still pinned to the board, already curling a little at one corner.
