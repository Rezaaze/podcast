--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the first morning of the season — the offer arrives with no prior warning to anyone in the building.
2. A courier hands the board's outer envelope to the doorman, addressed formally to "The Board of Directors."
3. The doorman mentions the courier's company logo to a resident fetching mail, and the word "Halloran" starts moving faster than the elevator.
4. By midmorning at least three units have heard a rumor of a number before the board has even opened the envelope.
5. Liwei, checking the mailboxes, feels a flush of excitement he doesn't yet know is premature.
6. No one in the lobby yet knows this offer will force four private secrets toward the light at once.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The co-op gathers among the citrus rows for an emergency meeting, folding chairs wedged between planters.
2. Liwei reads Halloran's number aloud, and the room goes silent in a way that feels almost physical.
3. Someone does the quick math out loud — what each unit would net — and a ripple of disbelief follows.
4. Declan says the co-op's finances are "in good shape" to reassure the room, a reassurance he does not believe.
5. Mei-Lin watches the citrus rows rather than the crowd, aware this greenhouse's survival now hangs on a vote.
6. The board agrees to reconvene once the offer's due-diligence terms are reviewed, giving everyone a false sense of more time.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Alone in her apartment, Mei-Lin rereads the offer letter's mention of a required structural and site survey.
2. Her eyes drift, without her quite deciding to look, toward the direction of the greenhouse's southeast corner.
3. She recalls, briefly and privately, the weight of a guardianship she inherited but never chose.
4. She frames tonight's coming objection to herself as procedural caution, not what it actually is.
5. She calculates silently how many more weeks of "careful caution" she has left before someone stops believing it.
6. She tells no one — not even her son — what she is actually protecting.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. In the co-op office, Declan sits alone with the reserve fund ledger spread across the desk before the meeting.
2. He cross-checks numbers only he fully understands, sweating despite the office's mild temperature.
3. He pulls the three contractor invoices — boiler, roof membrane, elevator motor — and studies them like something might have changed.
4. He begins mentally listing which files he could move first if an outside audit becomes real.
5. He rehearses the reassuring phrases he'll use at the meeting, testing how convincing they sound out loud to an empty room.
6. He locks the ledger drawer before he leaves, a habit that started only in the last few months.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In Unit 4G, Soo-Jin hears about the offer secondhand from a neighbor rather than at the meeting itself.
2. Her first thought is not the money but the word "shareholder," and what proving that status would require.
3. She deflects the neighbor's excitement warmly, saying only that she's tired tonight.
4. Alone afterward, she begins counting, almost involuntarily, how many years it has been since anyone actually looked at her aunt's face.
5. She glances at a stack of paperwork on the counter — ballots, renewal forms — that she alone has signed for years.
6. She does not go to the meeting.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. In the super's workshop, Aiko runs her hand along a familiar weld on the northwest truss brace.
2. She notes, privately, exactly how that weld would read to a professional inspector's eye.
3. She recalls, briefly, the incident six years ago involving Yoon Hee-ja, though she does not let herself linger there.
4. She begins calculating how much time she has before any survey crew would reach this corner of the roof.
5. She checks her small private stock of replacement bracing hardware, already thinking ahead to what she'll need.
6. She keeps her expression neutral when a resident passes by, offering nothing about what she's really doing up here.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. At the corner diner, Liwei meets Iggy Chen, Halloran's representative, face-to-face for the first time.
2. Liwei is eager to demonstrate the co-op can move fast, name-dropping the emergency meeting like proof of momentum.
3. Iggy mentions, almost in passing, that the acquisition contract requires notarized in-person verification of every shareholder of record.
4. Iggy also references a standard due-diligence structural survey as a formality, not realizing what that phrase will trigger for two people in the building.
5. Liwei promises full cooperation on behalf of the board, unaware of what "full cooperation" will actually expose.
6. Iggy leaves with a favorable impression of Liwei personally, though the audience senses this friendliness is professional, not personal.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The vote reconvenes among the citrus rows, ballots ready to be counted.
2. Just as the count begins, Mei-Lin raises a procedural objection about the timeline for reviewing due-diligence terms.
3. She frames her objection as caution on behalf of the co-op, careful and reasonable-sounding to every ear but her own.
4. The board, unable to immediately counter her point, agrees to table the count rather than push through it.
5. Declan visibly relaxes at the delay, for reasons that have nothing to do with Mei-Lin's stated concern.
6. Aiko, watching from the back, also seems quietly relieved, though she says nothing.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. The meeting breaks up in confused murmurs, most residents unsure why the vote stalled.
2. Liwei apologizes to no one in particular, embarrassed the momentum he promised Iggy has already stumbled.
3. Mei-Lin walks home alone, carrying the weight of a secret she has now bought a few more weeks to protect.
4. Declan walks home rehearsing which invoices to touch first, grateful for time he didn't expect to get.
5. Soo-Jin, back in 4G, hears the delay reported by a neighbor and feels only a brief, false relief.
6. Aiko heads straight for the workshop, already listing which tools she'll need before any inspection crew arrives.
