--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Three days have passed since Liwei set aside the ballot file, meaning to ask Soo-Jin about the signature discrepancies.
2. Iggy gathers the board and several residents in the lobby to formally announce the coring test at the southeast pier, scheduled for next week.
3. He frames it as a routine technical formality required before closing, nothing more.
4. Most residents react with only mild curiosity about scheduling logistics, not the corner itself.
5. Mei-Lin's polite nod masks a jolt of panic at hearing the exact pier and exact date spoken aloud in public.
6. Declan and Aiko each register the date privately, silently recalculating how much time it leaves them.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Alone in the apartment, Mei-Lin lets her composed lobby face drop the moment the door closes.
2. She runs through every procedural delay she's used this season and finds each one now spent.
3. For the first time, she seriously considers letting the coring test happen and the truth surface on its own.
4. She recoils from that thought almost immediately, picturing her grandmother's decades of silence undone in an afternoon.
5. She rehearses, silently, an opening line for telling Liwei everything, and cannot make herself say it even alone.
6. She decides again to say nothing, not because it feels right anymore, but because she has no better option before next week.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mei-Lin finds Liwei in the glasshouse checking irrigation lines near the citrus rows.
2. She drifts toward the southeast pier under the pretense of inspecting the same line.
3. She opens her mouth to tell him what the corner actually holds, further than she's ever let the words go before.
4. Liwei, misreading her hesitation, fills the silence by promising he'll "smooth things over" with the board so the test proceeds on schedule.
5. His easy confidence about moving the sale forward reminds her exactly what the truth would cost him, and she closes her mouth again.
6. She redirects into a comment about the citrus needing water, and the closest she's come to telling him passes unspoken.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Aiko hears about the coring test date and understands immediately it means a full structural inspection of the roof, not just the pier.
2. She pulls out her private notebook, reading back years of undocumented fixes with new urgency.
3. She rehearses, silently, what confessing to the board would sound like — the unpermitted repair, the mismatched steel, Yoon Hee-ja's injury.
4. She imagines the alternative: an inspector finding it cold, with no context and no chance for her to explain first.
5. She reaches for her phone as if to call a board member, then sets it back down, unable to dial.
6. She locks the workshop early, the decision no closer to made than an hour before.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Iggy meets Declan in the office and mentions he ran Vance Municipal Services against licensing records going back further than before.
2. He notes, evenly, that the company had already dissolved two full years before the earliest invoice date Declan provided.
3. Declan improvises on the spot, claiming the paperwork was processed late by an assistant no longer with the company.
4. Iggy asks, still pleasant, for that assistant's name so he can note it for the file.
5. Declan invents a name in the moment, aware even as he says it that it won't survive a real check.
6. Iggy thanks him and leaves, now privately certain the problem is Declan himself rather than a records error, though he voices this to no one.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Soo-Jin and Aiko end up at the same table at the corner diner, both visibly distracted.
2. Aiko mentions the coring test date, thinking of it only in terms of her own truss and roof.
3. Soo-Jin hears "next week" and privately overlays it against her own notary deadline, the two timelines suddenly colliding in her mind.
4. Neither woman asks the other directly what's wrong, each assuming the other's distraction is just general sale stress.
5. Aiko offers the same generic reassurance about the board helping with "paperwork," unaware how far from paperwork Soo-Jin's problem actually runs.
6. They part with nothing resolved, each carrying her secret a little heavier than when she sat down.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Mei-Lin raises a new procedural objection about the coring test's scheduling, this time with Liwei present.
2. Liwei pushes back openly, pointing out this is the third delay tied to this exact corner.
3. He reminds her, with real frustration, that Halloran's patience isn't unlimited and the board can't keep stalling without explanation.
4. Mei-Lin has no procedural argument left strong enough to counter him, and the pause before she answers shows it.
5. Liwei reads that pause as her running out of legitimate reasons, not as her running out of ways to hide the real one.
6. The board sides with Liwei, and the coring test date stands, unmoved for the first time all season.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Back in 4G, Soo-Jin lays a small bag beside her aunt's documents, an idea she hasn't let herself name until now.
2. She calculates how far her savings would get her, and for how long, if she left before the notary date.
3. She weighs, opposite that, calling Halloran's office herself and telling them the truth before anyone else finds it.
4. She thinks of Liwei, and how either choice — vanishing or confessing — means losing him in a different way.
5. She scrolls to a number she could call and doesn't press it.
6. She puts the bag away unfinished, no decision made, the notary date now closer than either option can outrun.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Late that night, Mei-Lin returns to the glasshouse alone, the coring test now only days away.
2. She stands at the southeast pier the way her grandmother must have stood there decades before her.
3. She runs through every delay and deflection she's used this season and finds none left to try.
4. She considers, one more time, telling the board everything before the coring crew arrives and takes the choice from her.
5. She still cannot make herself take that step, even now, even alone, with dawn and the test both closing in.
6. She leaves the glasshouse without a plan, the southeast corner behind her exactly as unresolved as it has always been.
