--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Night has fully settled over Field Ops, generators humming instead of daylight voices.
2. The team is gathered around the console despite Jonah never having announced the stand-down order to them.
3. The mood in the tent carries a charge of unspoken urgency none of them can fully explain to each other.
4. The audience understands what the team doesn't: a forty-eight-hour clock is already running out beneath this meeting.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Jonah tells the team they need to move on the checkpoint again, tonight, before Old Chen's warning cools into nothing.
2. He frames the urgency around Chen's slip of an engineer's term, not around the deadline he's still keeping to himself.
3. He watches the group's faces for hesitation and finds none, which steadies something anxious in him.
4. He privately weighs how much runway he's burning by not naming the forty-eight hours out loud.
5. The audience recognizes he's spending the team's trust on a partial truth to buy speed.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Wei Zhou announces he's broken into the checkpoint's private badge network overnight.
2. He explains the badge system doesn't run through any government registry he can find.
3. He states plainly that it traces to a private contractor rather than the Department of Defense.
4. Jonah presses for the contractor's name, and Wei Zhou admits he hasn't isolated it yet, only that it's not who they expected.
5. The revelation sharpens the room's sense that something deliberately shielded sits behind that fence.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Yuna argues hard against moving on the checkpoint tonight, citing procedure and risk to the case's legal standing.
2. Her reasoning sounds practiced at first, the same caution the team has heard from her before.
3. Under Jonah's pushback, her arguments start contradicting each other in small, telling ways.
4. Wei Zhou notices the crack but says nothing, still holding his own suspicion of her in reserve.
5. Jonah overrules her, and she backs down faster than her initial resistance would suggest.
6. The audience senses her objection was never really about procedure.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Old Chen appears at the tent's edge, offering to guide the team down a service road himself.
2. He tells them it's a road he designed decades ago, when the facility was still government-run.
3. He frames the offer as simple local knowledge, nothing more.
4. Jonah accepts without pushing on how a caretaker would know a road no map shows.
5. The audience understands Chen is finally acting on the decision he made alone in his cabin.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. In the Trauma Safe Room, Mei Lin works with Milo, gentler than before after his reaction to "ridge."
2. She uses the word "checkpoint" in an otherwise soft, unrelated question.
3. Milo goes agitated immediately, more visibly upset than the ridge moment produced.
4. He refuses to explain the reaction, offering no drawing or words to soften it.
5. Mei Lin logs it as escalating trauma, still not reading it as recognition of something real.
6. The audience understands the word landed because Milo has encountered the checkpoint himself, not because of abstract fear.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. The team moves through the dark along Chen's old service road, following his lead in near silence.
2. Yuna stays near the back of the group, quieter than her usual position in the field.
3. Wei Zhou keeps the Zhao tablet against his body, unwilling to leave it in the tent even for this.
4. Jonah counts the hours in his head without saying the number to anyone beside him.
5. The team reaches sight of the checkpoint fence from an angle the guards don't watch.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. At the checkpoint, Wei Zhou uses his access into the badge network to kill the perimeter cameras.
2. Jonah forces the gate open while the cameras stay dark.
3. The team moves through faster than their last visit, no guards yet aware of the breach.
4. Old Chen hangs back near the fence line rather than entering with the others.
5. The audience notes Chen's hesitation without yet knowing exactly what he's avoiding.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Inside the guard office, the team finds a terminal running the badge system Wei Zhou hacked remotely.
2. The registration on screen names Meridian Sentry Services as the operator, not any government body.
3. Jonah recognizes the name means the checkpoint was never military at all.
4. Wei Zhou searches further and confirms Meridian Sentry Services is listed as a subsidiary entity, though whose remains unclear on this screen.
5. The find reframes everything the team assumed about who has been stonewalling them.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Jonah searches a locked cabinet and pulls out a supply manifest.
2. The manifest lists bulk shipments of a smart-home hub model.
3. Wei Zhou confirms the model matches exactly what's installed in every Amity Hollow home.
4. Jonah understands this ties the checkpoint's operator directly to the town's devices for the first time with hard paper proof.
5. The team moves to photograph and secure the manifest as evidence.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. In the scramble to document the room, Yuna is caught trying to slip the manifest itself into her own bag rather than photograph it.
2. Jonah sees her hand close around the papers a half-second before she can hide the motion.
3. She freezes, the practiced calm from earlier completely gone from her face.
4. The audience recognizes this as the exact document that would tie Halcyon Vantage to Amity Hollow's homes.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Jonah pulls Yuna aside and confronts her in a low, furious voice so the others won't hear.
2. He asks her directly why she was taking the one piece of evidence that matters most.
3. She deflects, framing it as protecting the document from getting lost or damaged in the chaos.
4. She never actually denies that she meant to take it, only reframes her intent.
5. Jonah doesn't tell her what he already suspects from the second phone he saw days ago, choosing to keep watching instead of showing his hand.
6. The audience sees both of them holding back more than they're saying to each other.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. While the confrontation plays out, Wei Zhou works alone at a second terminal, running the manifest's serial numbers.
2. He finds a hub serial number that matches, exactly, the model installed in his cousin Lian's household.
3. He goes still, the case's usual clinical distance gone for a moment.
4. He says nothing to Jonah or Yuna, folding the match away as quietly as everything else he's been hiding.
5. The audience now holds three separate secrets converging on this one room, none of the characters aware how many others are also hiding something.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Back at the Mobile Forensics Unit, the team reviews a fresh thermal drone pass taken during the checkpoint raid.
2. The imaging shows active ventilation exhaust venting from a site marked as undeveloped land on every public zoning map.
3. Jonah connects it to the manifest and the badge system as further proof of something built and hidden beneath the ridge.
4. Wei Zhou notes the vent as a single point, not yet grasping it likely marks only a fraction of what's below.
5. The team marks the coordinates as their next target.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Old Chen leans over the drone map as the team discusses the vent site's coordinates.
2. He goes very still, recognizing the exact location without needing to be told what it connects to.
3. He says nothing about why the spot means something to him personally.
4. Internally, he understands this is the site tied to what happened to Iris, though he still doesn't say her name.
5. The audience recognizes the moment his private guilt and the team's investigation have finally converged on the same ground.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Following the vent coordinates, the team pushes further along the ridge under Chen's continued guidance.
2. They come upon a hatch cut directly into the rock, concealed well enough to pass unnoticed from any trail.
3. Jonah examines the hatch and confirms it aligns with both the thermal vent and the badge system's mapped perimeter.
4. Old Chen stands apart from the group, recognizing construction details in the hatch that only its original builder would know.
5. The team prepares to open it, none of them aware of how much this specific spot already means to the man guiding them.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. The narrator closes on the hatch standing open in the dark, a passage now available to the team.
2. Old Chen's face is held in focus, calm on the surface but carrying the weight of decades of silence.
3. The audience understands he already knows, or fears he knows, exactly what waits below.
4. The episode ends before anyone steps through, the threshold itself the final image.
