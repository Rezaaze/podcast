--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Old Chen reaches the shaft's outer hatch and finds the old mechanism seized with decades of rust.
2. He forces it open with a pry bar and his own weight, joints and hinges screaming against the effort.
3. Beams from the convoy's search lights sweep closer through the trees behind him.
4. The hatch gives way just as the nearest patrol passes within earshot.
5. He slips inside and pulls the door shut behind him, unseen.
6. The audience realizes he beat Halcyon's security to the one entrance they don't know exists.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Down in the sealed observation deck, Jonah's radio crackles back to life mid-static.
2. Old Chen's voice describes a side passage and the shaft beyond it, directions built from memory rather than any map.
3. Wei Zhou repeats the turns back to confirm them as Chen speaks, racing the connection before it drops again.
4. Yuna listens without recognizing any of the route, quietly registering that this exit isn't in anything Halcyon ever gave her.
5. The transmission cuts out again before Chen finishes, but the team has enough to move.
6. The audience understands this shaft is invisible to Halcyon's own system, the one way out the corporation never accounted for.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Yuna hesitates at the corridor junction, one hand drifting toward the pocket where her second phone used to be.
2. She tries her handler's channel under her breath one last time and gets nothing back.
3. Jonah watches her calculate, saying nothing, letting her decide on her own.
4. She turns and leads the way toward Chen's route instead of any direction that would have suited an exit of her own.
5. Internally, she registers that helping them now is the only leverage she has left with anyone.
6. The audience reads her choice as self-preservation, not sudden conscience.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Passing near Chamber 19, Wei Zhou stops and reaches for the manual release, intent on waking his aunt, uncle, and Lian himself.
2. Jonah pulls him back, reminding him none of them know what the sedation or the signal did to the residents' bodies.
3. Wei Zhou argues he can't leave them behind a second time.
4. Jonah tells him waking them wrong could kill them faster than leaving them sedated a little longer.
5. Wei Zhou doesn't answer, but he lets go of the release.
6. The audience feels the cost of the decision without either man calling it the right one.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Yuna checks a nearby panel and confirms the chambers run on a separate power and life-support loop from the rest of the facility.
2. Wei Zhou verifies the readouts himself, unwilling to take her word alone.
3. The loop shows no sign of degrading, independent of the lockdown spreading through the deck.
4. Jonah tells Wei Zhou this buys enough time to get proper medical teams back in, if they can get out and call for them.
5. Wei Zhou accepts it, though his eyes stay on the feed a moment longer before he moves.
6. The audience understands the residents can hold, but only if the team survives long enough to send help.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. One by one, Jonah, Wei Zhou, and Yuna pull themselves up through the narrow shaft.
2. The passage is tight and rust-scarred, forcing them to move slower than any of them want.
3. Cold night air replaces the bunker's stale atmosphere as the hatch opens above them.
4. They emerge into open woods, disoriented by light and space after so long underground.
5. There's no sign of Old Chen at the hatch, only the door left unlocked behind him.
6. The audience registers the emptiness as a sign Chen has already moved on to something else.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Through the trees, the team spots guards spread along the checkpoint fence line in a loose search pattern.
2. Jonah signals for the group to stay low and out of the light.
3. Wei Zhou realizes the direct route back to Field Ops is covered end to end.
4. Yuna recognizes the formation as a full containment sweep, not a routine patrol.
5. The team accepts they'll need another way around, not through.
6. The audience feels the walls closing back in right after the escape.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Skirting the tree line, Wei Zhou spots a satellite uplink van left idling from earlier field work.
2. The keys are still in the ignition, abandoned in the scramble when the lockdown began.
3. He recognizes the rig as the same one used to transmit footage before everything went dark.
4. He points it out to Jonah without needing to explain what it could be used for.
5. The audience recognizes the van as a way to reach the outside world without ever crossing the fence.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Inside the van, Wei Zhou pulls together the corrupted footage he's been carrying — Milo's symbol, the signal data, the chamber feeds.
2. He splices it into a single raw stream meant to play without any editing or explanation needed.
3. Jonah stands at the door, watching the tree line for any guard drifting too close.
4. Yuna feeds Wei Zhou technical shortcuts from her own device knowledge, helping without being asked twice.
5. The upload bar creeps forward while all three wait for something to go wrong before it finishes.
6. The audience understands this raw, unpolished feed is about to become the only proof anyone outside will get.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. In the Halcyon Vantage lobby, a screen flags an unauthorized uplink broadcasting from the restricted zone.
2. Executives scramble between phones, trying to reach the field team to kill the signal remotely.
3. One realizes their kill-switch protocols were never designed for a van operating outside their own network.
4. Panic spreads as the countdown to relay completion keeps climbing instead of stopping.
5. The audience sees the company's control finally slipping past the point it can recover.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Yuna's hand closes around the van's second handset, the one that could still reach her handler.
2. For a breath, neither Jonah nor Wei Zhou can tell if she means to warn Halcyon or stop them from doing exactly that.
3. She keys the channel, then says nothing into it, listening instead to dead air where a handler should be.
4. She lowers the handset without placing the call at all.
5. The audience is left unsure whether she changed her mind or simply had no one left to call.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. The upload bar reaches its final stretch as the last relay accepts the signal.
2. The feed clears every filter meant to stop it and pushes out across every open channel in range.
3. Inside the van, the three of them watch the transfer complete with no confirmation yet of who's receiving it.
4. The audience understands the footage is now beyond anyone's ability to pull back.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Inside the Field Ops tent, screens across the room start looping the same raw footage back from outside news feeds.
2. Officers and staff go quiet as Amity Hollow's own chambers and signal data play out in front of them.
3. Old Chen, having made his way back to Field Ops, stands at the edge of the tent watching the same screens.
4. Pressed by what's playing, he finally says aloud that he built the facility decades ago and that his granddaughter Grace was taken by an earlier version of the same experiment.
5. Jonah, arriving in time to hear it, finally understands why Chen has spent decades watching that ridge.
6. Wei Zhou hears it too, finally understanding why Chen carried this alone for so long.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. As the team reaches the checkpoint, federal agents move in before Yuna can get anywhere near a vehicle.
2. She doesn't resist, though her eyes search briefly for an exit that isn't there.
3. Pressed for her handler's name, she stays silent, holding onto the one thing she hasn't given up yet.
4. She had believed until this moment she might still slip away in the confusion of the broadcast.
5. The agents' presence makes clear that possibility is already gone.
6. The audience watches her run out of leverage in real time.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. In the surface safe room, Mei Lin lets Milo sit beside her as the broadcast plays across her tablet.
2. Milo watches the footage without flinching, without asking a single question.
3. Mei Lin reads his stillness as relief, a sign the worst is finally behind him.
4. Milo gives no indication that what he's watching means anything different to him than it does to her.
5. Internally, he's still counting on this being the end of what was asked of him, expecting to be sent home soon.
6. The audience knows his calm hides something Mei Lin has no reason yet to question.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. A technician combs through the recovered footage from the night of the disappearance for the official record.
2. Frame by frame, the review is routine, almost mechanical after so many hours of the same crowd and same chaos.
3. Something in one frame makes the technician stop and rewind instead of moving on.
4. They isolate the frame and zoom in, unsure yet what caught their attention.
5. The audience senses this is the detail the case has been missing.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. The isolated frame shows a ten-year-old Milo mid-stride in the fleeing crowd, half a second captured before he vanishes back into it.
2. In that half-second, he turns his head not toward the chaos but toward a fixed point off to the side.
3. His hand makes a small, deliberate gesture, brief and precise, toward a camera angle no one flagged as relevant until now.
4. The technician realizes no panicked child moves like that, on purpose, toward a lens nobody else in the frame even looks at.
5. The audience understands this is the proof that Milo's stillness was never innocent, found in the one frame he never knew was watching him back.
