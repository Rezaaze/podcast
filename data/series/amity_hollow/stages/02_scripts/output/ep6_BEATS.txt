--- SCENE 1 ---
1. The team passes through the hatch one by one, headlamps cutting into total blackness.
2. A stairwell cut straight into rock descends far past what the drone's exhaust reading suggested.
3. Old Chen stays above at the hatch mouth, patched into the team's radio channel instead of following down.
4. Jonah orders radio silence beyond essential calls, the air already carrying an old, sealed stillness.
5. The audience senses the descent itself confirming this was built to hide something far larger than a shelter.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The stairwell opens into a cavernous atrium, far larger than anything the surface structures suggested.
2. Rows of dormant monitor banks line the walls, screens dark but clearly built for constant surveillance, not emergency use.
3. Concrete pillars rise well beyond a single story, the space clearly built to last generations, not to be temporary.
4. Wei Zhou notes the layout doesn't match any civil-defense shelter blueprint he's ever seen.
5. Jonah reads the scale as proof this was resourced by someone with far more than a government contractor's budget.
6. The audience recognizes the corporate ambition behind the space before any character says it aloud.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Yuna steps forward and offers to lead, saying the layout matches blueprints she was once shown during a liaison briefing.
2. She frames the memory as vague, something she wasn't even sure was real until seeing the space now.
3. In fact, the blueprints came from an off-book meeting with her corporate contact, who told her at the time they were for "storage."
4. She omits that detail entirely, letting the team believe her knowledge came through normal government channels.
5. The audience now knows her offer to guide is built on a lie of origin, even if the geography she remembers is accurate.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Jonah hesitates, aware Yuna has already produced knowledge she previously claimed not to have.
2. He weighs the risk of following her against the danger of searching this scale of space blind.
3. He decides silently to let her lead while continuing to note every detail that doesn't add up.
4. He says nothing of his private suspicion, choosing distance over confrontation for now.
5. The audience understands he's using her exactly as she's using the team, both sides withholding the same instinct.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Wei Zhou breaks off to check a side corridor and finds a long row of sealed, reinforced chamber doors.
2. Each door has a small viewing window and an external control panel instead of any evacuation signage.
3. He calls the others over, sensing immediately this doesn't match anything a shelter would need.
4. The corridor stretches further than his flashlight can fully illuminate, chamber after chamber in matching rows.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Jonah studies the chamber doors and notes the observation windows all face inward, not outward.
2. Wei Zhou points out the control panels are wired for monitoring, not for emergency release.
3. The team reaches the same conclusion together: this space was designed to hold people under watch, not protect them.
4. Yuna stays quiet, already having half-known this from the moment she recognized the layout.
5. The audience registers her silence as confirmation, not surprise, though no one else reads it that way yet.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Jonah finds a plaque beside one chamber door bearing a resident's name from Amity Hollow's town rolls.
2. Beneath the name sits a status code none of them can decode, clearly internal shorthand rather than any medical or legal term.
3. Wei Zhou photographs the plaque, treating it as evidence rather than immediately grasping its full weight.
4. Jonah orders the team to check the row for more names, sensing this one plaque is not isolated.
5. The audience recognizes the code as the same kind of subject-tracking language used across the facility's other logs, though the team doesn't yet see the pattern.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Wei Zhou moves down the row alone and stops at a chamber logged under the surname Zhang.
2. He reads the plaque twice, his hands going still against the door frame.
3. He says nothing to Jonah or Yuna, stepping back from the chamber before either notices his reaction.
4. Internally, he now has to decide whether to keep hiding his cousin's connection or finally speak it.
5. The audience understands this chamber all but confirms Lian was held in this exact facility.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Wei Zhou pulls Jonah aside, away from Yuna, and finally admits the Zhang household voice match was his cousin Lian.
2. He confesses he ran the comparison off the record and told no one, afraid of being pulled from the case.
3. He explains the two access requests he filed and had denied were both attempts to find her without raising flags.
4. Jonah takes in the confession as one more team member who has been managing him instead of trusting him.
5. Neither of them names it, but this is now the second omission Jonah has had to absorb inside the same building.
6. The audience holds the knowledge that Yuna's hidden stake is still unrevealed, larger than either man in this exchange knows.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Above ground, Mei Lin sits with Milo, still working gently after his earlier reactions to "ridge" and "checkpoint."
2. Without prompting, Milo begins drawing a corridor lined with evenly spaced doors, each marked with a small square.
3. The layout he draws matches, detail for detail, the chamber corridor the team is standing in below, though he has never been told about it.
4. Mei Lin logs the drawing as a striking new development, still working within a trauma framework rather than a literal one.
5. The audience understands Milo isn't imagining or symbolizing anything — he has been inside this exact corridor before.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Wei Zhou reads a chamber's designator code over the radio to Old Chen, hoping he might recognize the facility's original systems.
2. Chen goes quiet on the other end before confirming the numbering is exactly the system he devised decades ago for the original shelter.
3. He doesn't yet say why hearing it aloud unsettles him more than expected.
4. The team takes his confirmation as useful technical context, not realizing what it's stirring in him.
5. The audience senses Chen is now bracing for something he's feared finding since he first approached the tent.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Chen tells Jonah over the radio that he's coming down himself, not staying at the hatch any longer.
2. Jonah objects, citing the unknown risk and Chen's age, wanting him kept safe above ground.
3. Chen doesn't explain his real reason, only repeating that he knows this place better than anyone alive.
4. Jonah relents, reading Chen's insistence as expertise rather than a personal reckoning.
5. The audience understands Chen is walking toward the one confirmation he's dreaded for eleven years.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Once inside, Chen moves through the corridor with unsettling familiarity, bypassing chambers the others haven't checked yet.
2. He stops at one chamber and reads the plaque: Iris Chen, with the same unexplained status code as the others.
3. He stands motionless, the composure he's kept for years finally slipping in front of the team.
4. No one yet understands the surname matches the granddaughter he's never mentioned by name.
5. The audience now holds the full weight of what this specific chamber means, ahead of any character voicing it.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Chen pulls away from the group and tells Jonah, quietly, that Iris was his granddaughter and she never came home.
2. He admits he was the engineer who built the original shelter, and that guilt over that has kept him silent for years.
3. He explains he told himself exposing the place now would give her justice, though saying it aloud, even he hears how thin that hope sounds.
4. Jonah absorbs the confession as confirmation of everything Chen's odd behavior has hinted at since the ridge.
5. Jonah doesn't yet grasp that Chen's grief might push him toward something more reckless than cooperation.
6. The audience understands Chen has finally spoken the truth he's carried alone since Iris disappeared.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Away from the others, Yuna feels the earpiece activate with a short, coded burst from her contact.
2. The instruction confirms what she was told that morning: the order has shifted from monitoring the investigation to containing the investigators themselves.
3. She keeps her face neutral, giving no outward sign to Jonah or Wei Zhou that anything has changed.
4. She tells herself she can still manage the outcome from the inside, even as the instruction narrows her options.
5. The audience now knows the danger to the team is no longer informational — it's physical, and only Yuna knows it.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Yuna redirects the group toward a heavy, reinforced door, telling them it's the fastest route to the control room.
2. She doesn't mention that she now knows this corridor leads exactly where her contact wants the team contained.
3. Jonah accepts her direction, still weighing her usefulness against his unspoken suspicions rather than overruling her outright.
4. Wei Zhou follows without objection, his attention still partly on the confession he just made rather than on her.
5. The audience recognizes she is leading them into position, not toward safety or answers.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. The team reaches the heavy door, its surface marked with the same numbering system Chen designed decades ago.
2. As they stop before it, the corridor's lights begin shifting color one by one, moving down the row toward them.
3. No character reacts yet, none of them recognizing the shift as anything other than old wiring.
4. The audience understands the facility itself has just changed state, the "contain" order now visibly underway.
5. The episode ends on the changing lights, the threshold and its warning arriving in the same instant.
