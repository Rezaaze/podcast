--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Without warning, heavy blast doors slam shut across every atrium exit, sealing Jonah, Wei Zhou, and Yuna inside.
2. The sound is mechanical and deliberate, not the failure of old equipment.
3. Jonah checks the radio and finds it dead past the door, confirming the seal has cut them off from Chen and the surface.
4. Yuna's reaction lags half a beat behind the others, a flicker of recognition she quickly buries.
5. The audience registers that pause as evidence she expected something like this, even though the team reads it only as shock.
6. Wei Zhou immediately starts assessing the door's mechanism instead of reacting emotionally, treating it as a problem to solve.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Jonah and Wei Zhou trace the door's frame for a manual release or override panel.
2. Yuna steps back and says she can "handle it" through her contacts, reaching for her radio.
3. Jonah asks which contacts, and she answers vaguely, citing her liaison channel to local authorities.
4. Internally she is deciding whether to reach her actual handler or maintain the fiction in front of the others.
5. Wei Zhou finds a corroded override box but no working power source behind it.
6. The audience notes Yuna's phrasing carefully avoids naming who she'd actually be calling.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. A bank of monitors along the observation deck flickers to life on its own, without anyone touching a switch.
2. Screen after screen resolves into feeds of the sealed chambers, all forty-one now clearly occupied.
3. Vital-sign readouts beside each feed show steady heartbeats and breathing, not distress.
4. Jonah realizes the residents are not victims of an accident but active, monitored subjects.
5. Wei Zhou stares at the readouts, doing the math on how many faces he might recognize.
6. The audience understands the facility is functioning exactly as designed, the "test" still actively running.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Wei Zhou scans the chamber grid and stops on the feed labeled Chamber 19.
2. He recognizes his aunt and uncle beside Lian, the family he'd already confirmed off the record.
3. In front of both Jonah and Yuna now, he says plainly that this was never just an assignment for him.
4. He admits he requested this case specifically, and that his two denied access requests were both attempts to reach them.
5. Jonah absorbs this fuller confession without surprise, having already heard the core of it privately.
6. Yuna watches with practiced sympathy, giving no sign of her own reason for staying quiet.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. With the moment's urgency briefly parked, Jonah turns the conversation to Yuna directly.
2. He asks her to explain why checkpoint logs show her forwarding his clearance requests to an outside number before filing them through channel.
3. Yuna claims it was a routing error, blaming outdated liaison software for the duplicate log entry.
4. She holds eye contact and keeps her voice level, betraying nothing of the lie.
5. Jonah doesn't accept the explanation but lets it sit rather than push further while they're trapped.
6. The audience already knows the number traces to a private Halcyon relay, making her answer a deliberate lie, not a mistake.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. In an outer stretch of the bunker, Old Chen monitors the team's channel on his old scanner and hears it drop to static.
2. He tries hailing them twice and gets nothing back, confirming the silence isn't just poor signal.
3. He unfolds a set of decades-old schematics and finds the maintenance shaft he personally sealed after the original shelter was decommissioned.
4. He remembers welding that shaft shut himself, the one access point the automated systems might not account for.
5. He decides the surface route to that shaft's outer hatch is now the only way to reach the team.
6. The audience understands Chen now has a concrete plan forming, not just guilt driving him.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. In the surface safe room, Mei Lin sits with Milo as he returns to his recurring drawing.
2. This time he adds a small, precise mark at the center of the corridor symbol, pressing harder than before.
3. Mei Lin gently asks what the new mark means, and Milo stays silent, offering no explanation.
4. She logs the change as a possible breakthrough, reading his focus as emotional processing.
5. Internally, Milo is finishing a detail meant to guide, not express anything, satisfied the mark is complete.
6. The audience understands the drawing isn't approaching a memory — it's approaching a finished instruction.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Above ground, Field Ops loses all radio contact with Jonah's team at the exact moment the atrium doors would have sealed.
2. A junior officer flags the blackout and asks whether protocol requires notifying Halcyon Vantage as the facility's registered contractor.
3. A senior officer hesitates, weighing standard incident procedure against the optics of involving the company tied to the missing residents.
4. They agree to delay formal notification until they've tried other means of contact first.
5. The audience recognizes the irony that notifying Halcyon would mean alerting the very party responsible for the lockdown.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Checking Yuna for a working radio or spare charger, Jonah's hand catches a hard shape stitched into her jacket lining.
2. He tears the seam open and finds a second phone, unlike the one she's used with the team throughout the investigation.
3. Yuna's composure breaks for the first time, her explanations stalling out mid-sentence.
4. Wei Zhou watches the shift, realizing how easily his own clearance was approved earlier suddenly reads differently.
5. Jonah doesn't yet say how long he's suspected her, focused instead on what she'll admit now that the device is out in the open.
6. The audience sees her fear as confirmation her handler's silence over the last hour has finally become real to her.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. While Jonah deals with Yuna, Wei Zhou examines a support beam near the observation deck out of habit, still processing the confrontation.
2. He notices an engineering stamp pressed into the metal bearing a name he now recognizes from the ridge conversations: Chen.
3. He assumes the stamp belongs to a Halcyon engineer who happens to share Old Chen's surname, not grasping the connection yet.
4. He photographs it anyway, filing it as one more detail to raise once they're out.
5. The audience understands this stamp is Old Chen's own decades-old signature, proof the bunker predates Halcyon entirely.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At the surface checkpoint, an unmarked convoy of vehicles arrives without announcing itself over any official channel.
2. The lead vehicle bypasses the checkpoint's stop point and turns directly off-road toward the ridge.
3. Field Ops personnel exchange uneasy looks, none of them cleared to know who authorized the convoy's presence.
4. No one moves to stop it, the vehicles' plates and markings deliberately unreadable.
5. The audience recognizes this as Halcyon Vantage security responding to the breach, arriving faster than any official agency could.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. At his cabin near the ridge, Old Chen packs a canvas bag with a set of forty-year-old keys and handwritten access codes.
2. He writes a short note explaining where he's gone and leaves it on the table instead of radioing anyone directly.
3. He chooses not to ask permission, certain Field Ops would order him to stay put if he did.
4. He shoulders the bag and starts up the old trail toward the ridge alone, moving with more urgency than his age usually allows.
5. The audience understands he is now racing toward the same ground the convoy is heading for, unaware of each other's approach.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Back in the sealed atrium, Jonah presses Yuna harder now that the second device is out in the open.
2. Cornered, she finally drops the pretense and admits Halcyon Vantage calls what happened to Amity Hollow a "trial."
3. She adds, almost involuntarily, that the village was never meant to be the only one it was run on.
4. Wei Zhou goes still, hearing his family's captivity reframed as one data set among others.
5. Jonah absorbs the scale of what she's just confirmed, the case reshaping itself in real time.
6. The audience now has explicit confirmation of the Continuity Trial's true scope, spoken aloud for the first time by someone who knew it firsthand.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Old Chen reaches the trailhead below the ridge, breathing hard but steady on his feet.
2. He pauses to get his bearings against old landmarks he still remembers by feel.
3. Headlights sweep across the treeline behind him, catching his silhouette for a moment before he steps out of the beam.
4. He doesn't yet know if he's been seen, only that he's no longer alone on this approach.
5. The audience recognizes the convoy from the checkpoint has caught up to the same stretch of woods.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Inside the bunker, a new automated sequence begins locking down the observation deck itself, independent of the atrium doors.
2. Ventilation ducts along the ceiling slow, then stop, the air in the room noticeably starting to thin.
3. Jonah notices the change first, feeling the shift in pressure before anyone names it.
4. Wei Zhou checks the vents and confirms they've been shut off deliberately, not failed mechanically.
5. Yuna, still shaken from her exposure, has no further information to offer about this new protocol.
6. The audience understands the system is now actively working against everyone trapped inside, Yuna included.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Jonah tries Old Chen's channel one more time, hoping for any word on an alternate way out.
2. Chen's voice comes through mid-sentence, describing the shaft's outer hatch, before dissolving into static.
3. Repeated attempts to re-establish contact return nothing but dead air.
4. Jonah realizes the one route out he was counting on has just gone silent along with the man who knew it.
5. The audience is left not knowing whether Chen was cut off by terrain, by Halcyon's convoy, or by something worse.
