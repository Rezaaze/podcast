--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Wei Zhou runs the recovered smart-speaker audio through a deeper spectral analysis than the initial sweep used.
2. He isolates a low, repeating waveform hidden beneath the surface layer of ambient "sleep sound" audio.
3. He confirms the pattern repeats at a fixed interval too precise to be background noise or compression artifact.
4. He cross-checks the finding against speaker logs from multiple households and gets the same buried waveform each time.
5. He sits back, quietly unsettled by how deliberate the design looks, before calling anyone else over.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first hard evidence of the signal, though no character has named what it does yet.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Jonah joins Wei Zhou at the monitor and asks what a signal like this could realistically do to someone.
2. Wei Zhou lays out the acoustic facts plainly: it's designed, it's consistent, and it was pushed to every device in the village.
3. Jonah asks whether sound alone could explain forty-one people walking out of their homes.
4. Wei Zhou admits the signal explains exposure but not the specific act of walking into the woods, treating that as a separate open question.
5. Both men agree this moves the case from vanishing to something engineered, without either voicing how much that unsettles them personally.
6. Neither raises the possibility of a second broadcast, though the audience can feel the gap in their explanation.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Jonah asks Yuna to prioritize records tracing who controls the server the firmware update was pushed from.
2. Yuna redirects him toward slower public-records channels, framing it as the more thorough approach.
3. She already knows the shell subsidiary's name from her handler, though she gives no sign of this to Jonah.
4. Once the team disperses, she steps away from camp and places a call on the unlisted number, timing it to when no one is watching.
5. She frames the call to herself as routine coordination, not registering how quickly Wei Zhou's technical trail is closing the gap she's trying to hold open.
6. The audience sees the deflection and the call side by side, understanding the redirection was never about thoroughness.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. The team walks the abandoned square again, plates and mugs still sitting where they were left mid-meal.
2. Jonah lingers at a stove that's long gone cold, noting how ordinary the scene still looks despite what happened.
3. He privately turns over the fact that his initial sweep treated the baby monitor as routine, wondering what else he might have missed as unremarkable.
4. He says nothing to the others about this doubt, keeping it as a private weight rather than a shared concern.
5. The stillness of the square presses on him more than any new evidence has, though he can't point to why.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei Lin sets up another drawing session with Milo, keeping her tone light and unpressured.
2. Milo draws the familiar symbol again, steady and unhesitating.
3. Mei Lin notes the repetition itself as data, comparing it silently to the variation from the previous session.
4. She asks a gentle, indirect question about what the symbol means to him, and he gives no reaction at all.
5. She logs the session as another data point toward a pattern she still can't name, unaware Milo is repeating it deliberately to keep the thread alive.
6. The audience understands Milo's stillness is intent, not absence.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Wei Zhou reviews recovered footage and photographs from the property neighboring the last house.
2. Among the images, he recognizes a photograph of a family he knows personally rather than a stranger's face.
3. He goes still, the clinical distance he's kept all case dropping for a moment before he catches himself.
4. He says nothing to Jonah or Mei Lin, who are nearby, and quickly moves the image aside as though it's unremarkable.
5. The audience learns this is his cousin's family before any character on screen registers what just happened to him.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Jonah notices Wei Zhou's stillness back at the forensics unit and asks directly if something's wrong.
2. Wei Zhou brushes it off as fatigue, keeping his voice even and clinical.
3. Jonah doesn't fully buy the answer but doesn't push further, filing the moment away rather than confronting it.
4. Wei Zhou privately registers relief that the deflection worked, though it costs him to keep hiding it.
5. Neither man says the real question out loud, leaving the audience aware of a gap neither will close yet.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The team hikes to the ridge trailhead to search the maintenance road Jonah found.
2. They discover Old Chen already there, standing near the exact point where the boot prints converge.
3. Chen offers no explanation for his presence beyond saying he walks this ridge often.
4. Jonah and Yuna exchange a look, both registering how implausible the timing is without saying so.
5. The team treats the encounter as suspicious but not yet confrontational, deciding to follow Chen back rather than press him on the spot.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. At Chen's cabin, Jonah and Yuna ask him directly what he knows about the land beneath the ridge.
2. Chen gives a vague account of the old shelter project, describing it as ancient history rather than anything current.
3. He avoids any mention of having designed the original systems himself, letting the team assume he was a minor contractor at most.
4. Jonah notices the story doesn't fully account for why Chen still watches the ridge daily, but can't pin down what's missing.
5. Yuna, distracted by her own concerns, doesn't press as hard as she normally would, letting the interview end without full resolution.
6. Neither investigator realizes Chen is protecting a specific personal loss rather than simply being a private man.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Alone after the team leaves, Chen sits with his ham radio, turning the dial without transmitting anything.
2. He glances toward a drawer where his hand-drawn blueprints are kept, then leaves it shut.
3. He thinks of his granddaughter without saying her name aloud, the old unexamined belief that her disappearance was unrelated tragedy sitting undisturbed.
4. He makes no call, sends no message, and simply watches the ridge through the window as he has for eleven years.
5. The audience senses how close he came to acting and how thoroughly he still holds himself back.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Back at the tent, Jonah asks Yuna again, more directly this time, why the checkpoint access request is still pending.
2. Yuna responds warmly, acknowledging his frustration and promising she's handling it personally.
3. She gives him reassurance instead of any actual information about who holds the lease or why access is delayed.
4. Jonah accepts her warmth on the surface but privately notes it answers nothing he actually asked.
5. Yuna reads his acceptance as the matter being smoothed over, not realizing he's simply choosing not to show his hand yet.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Wei Zhou gathers the full team to formally present his findings on the firmware push.
2. He lays out that the update carrying the buried waveform was transmitted six hours before the disappearance from an external server.
3. He names the transmission as deliberate and traceable, marking the first concrete link between a signal and a specific point of origin.
4. Jonah asks who the server is registered to, and Wei Zhou admits that trace is still incomplete.
5. Yuna listens without volunteering what she already privately knows about the registration, letting the gap in the room stand.
6. The team agrees this is the strongest lead yet, unaware one person in the room already has the missing name.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mei Lin runs another session with Milo, this time asking about the underground more directly than before.
2. Milo adds a new shape connected to the familiar symbol, drawing it with the same deliberate precision as his earlier addition.
3. The moment Mei Lin says the word "underground," Milo stops completely, pencil frozen mid-stroke.
4. He shuts down entirely, refusing to draw further or meet her eyes for the rest of the session.
5. Mei Lin reads this as a trauma trigger rather than recognition, unaware he's reacting to a word that lands too close to something real.
6. The audience understands Milo's reaction confirms a literal underground location, information Mei Lin doesn't yet have the frame to interpret correctly.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Wei Zhou returns to the square alone after dark, telling no one where he's going.
2. He moves through areas the official sweep already cleared, searching with a personal urgency the daytime search didn't have.
3. He orients himself specifically toward the property near 14 Birch Lane rather than searching at random.
4. He works quickly and quietly, aware that being seen would raise questions he isn't ready to answer.
5. The audience recognizes this as a continuation of his private search, though no other character is present to notice it.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Wei Zhou reaches the neighboring property and locates a personal tablet belonging to the Zhao household.
2. He takes it himself rather than logging it through the evidence queue, working outside official chain-of-custody hours.
3. He tells himself he'll process it privately and return it before anyone notices it's gone.
4. He doesn't yet know what's on the device, only that he needs to see it before anyone else does.
5. The audience understands this crosses a clear line in his own protocol, one he's chosen not to examine too closely.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Back at the tent, Jonah confronts Wei Zhou directly about his odd hours and repeated disappearances.
2. Wei Zhou gives a partial answer, citing exhaustion and the pressure of the recovery work, without mentioning the Zhao family or the tablet.
3. Jonah presses gently on the inconsistency between "just tired" and the specific evasiveness he's noticed.
4. Wei Zhou holds his composure but the answer stays incomplete, leaving visible strain neither of them names directly.
5. Jonah decides not to push further tonight, still framing the behavior privately as fatigue rather than a personal connection to the case.
6. The audience holds information neither man voices aloud: a family unnamed, a tablet unmentioned, and a growing risk of concealed conflict of interest.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. The team gathers to plot the waveform's directional bearing using the transmission and reception data collected so far.
2. Wei Zhou walks them through the triangulation, treating it as a technical exercise rather than an emotional one.
3. The bearing resolves to a point none of them expected, aligning with the ridge and the restricted land beyond it.
4. Jonah connects this to the maintenance road he found and the convergence of boot prints at the same location.
5. The team recognizes, for the first time together, that the signal's source and the residents' path lead to the same place.
6. The audience sees the full shape of the mystery drawing closed around a location the characters have only just begun to suspect.
