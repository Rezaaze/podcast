--- SCENE 1 ---
1. NARRATOR describes the Mobile Forensics Unit at dawn, screens humming after a night no one meant to stay awake for.
2. The corrupted file finishes its final render, the frame resolving pixel by pixel.
3. The symbol emerges clearly from the static, identical to the one covering Milo's walls.
4. The audience is reminded this connection exists before any character has acted on it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Wei Zhou gathers the team around the monitor and explains the file contains a hidden digital watermark, not a visible image.
2. He describes it in plain terms: a pattern embedded inside the corrupted data itself, deliberately placed rather than accidental.
3. He shows how the symbol only appears after the recovery process fully decodes the footage.
4. He keeps his tone clinical, giving no sign that the shape means anything to him personally yet.
5. The team registers this as a major break in the case, the first hint of deliberate design behind the vanishing.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Jonah asks pointedly whether this could be a rendering artifact or corruption pattern rather than a real embedded symbol.
2. Wei Zhou pushes back, explaining the pattern's consistency rules out a random glitch.
3. Jonah presses for absolute certainty before he's willing to treat it as evidence, wary of chasing another false lead.
4. Wei Zhou's clinical patience thins slightly under the repeated questioning, though he doesn't explain why this feels urgent to him.
5. Jonah backs off but insists on independent verification before this goes into any official theory.
6. Neither man voices it, but both are privately more unsettled by the finding than they let on.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Mei Lin sits with Milo again, this time avoiding direct questions and instead narrating simple observations about the room.
2. She comments neutrally on the weather, the drawings, ordinary things, watching for any small reaction.
3. Milo remains still, but his eyes track her words more closely than before, a shift she barely registers.
4. She notes to herself that this passive approach seems to ease his guardedness slightly, reading it as a therapeutic win.
5. She has no idea the change in his attention is calculation, not comfort.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Milo is given paper again and draws the familiar symbol, but this time adds a small extra mark at one edge.
2. Mei Lin almost dismisses it as a stray pencil slip before looking closer.
3. She notices the addition is precise, not accidental, though she can't say what it means.
4. She hesitates over whether to log this as a meaningful change or an insignificant variation.
5. She decides to keep watching rather than raise an alarm, unaware the variation is deliberate signaling on Milo's part.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Jonah asks Yuna to pull the full ownership and access records for the restricted military land bordering the village.
2. Yuna calmly redirects him toward the civilian zoning database instead, framing it as the faster starting point.
3. She implies the military records will take longer to requisition, buying herself time without an outright refusal.
4. Jonah accepts the redirection for now, unaware it steers him away from the exact answer he's asking for.
5. Yuna's ease in deflecting comes a little too naturally, though she believes she's only easing friction, not obstructing him.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Jonah walks the abandoned square again and finds a baby monitor still running on a porch table.
2. The device has been transmitting quietly this whole time, overlooked in the initial sweep.
3. He checks it for any recorded audio from the night of the disappearance.
4. He flags it for Wei Zhou to examine, treating it as a minor oversight rather than a major clue yet.
5. The audience senses this device may hold something the team hasn't considered, though Jonah treats it as routine follow-up.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Wei Zhou finishes recovering two more corrupted files, each from a different, unconnected corner of the village.
2. Both contain the same watermark symbol, confirming this isn't isolated to one camera or location.
3. He notes the symbol's placement is consistent enough to suggest a shared source rather than individual tampering.
4. He wrongly concludes a person manually inserted the watermark into each file separately, missing that it was auto-generated by the same firmware push.
5. He decides this finding is significant enough to bring to Jonah and Mei Lin directly.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Jonah and Mei Lin bring Wei Zhou's recovered footage to the last house to compare it against Milo's covered walls.
2. Side by side, the symbol from the footage and the drawings match exactly, stroke for stroke.
3. Mei Lin's earlier trauma read wavers slightly as she takes in the precision of the match.
4. Jonah treats the match as confirmation the symbol was planted as a message or threat by whoever took the residents.
5. Neither considers that the symbol might have been placed there through Milo himself, not just observed by him.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Back at the tent, Jonah asks Yuna directly who the tenant is on the restricted land's private lease.
2. Yuna gives a fluent, well-structured answer that names generic administrative details but avoids any specific company.
3. The answer sounds rehearsed rather than researched, though Jonah can't immediately say why it bothers him.
4. He lets it go for the moment but files away the feeling that her answers are too polished for someone still 'looking into it.'
5. Yuna registers his lingering look and senses she's pushing her luck with how smooth she's being.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Alone in the forensics unit after the others have gone, Wei Zhou pulls up a map of recovered footage locations.
2. He cross-references it against 14 Birch Lane, searching for any file from near his cousin's home.
3. None of the three recovered files come from that part of the village, leaving him with no new answer.
4. He allows himself a private, unconfirmed hope that his cousin's family may have lasted longer than the others.
5. He closes the map before anyone can see what he was really searching for, calling it calibration work if asked.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Mei Lin sits alone in the trauma safe room after hours, reviewing her notes on Milo.
2. She writes down the growing list of details that don't fit a straightforward trauma diagnosis: his composure, the identical drawings, the new variation.
3. She admits to herself, on paper only, that her initial read may be incomplete.
4. She still can't bring herself to say this doubt aloud to the team, worried it will sound like second-guessing without proof.
5. The audience sees her doubt solidifying well before she's ready to act on it.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Jonah returns alone to the ridge trailhead where the boot prints stopped, drawn back by unfinished unease.
2. He clears away brush near the treeline and finds a narrow, deliberately concealed maintenance road.
3. He follows it a short distance and confirms it leads directly toward the restricted zone.
4. He recognizes this contradicts the idea that the prints simply vanished, since a route out clearly exists.
5. He decides to bring this straight to Yuna rather than escalate it through anyone else first.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Jonah returns to the tent and mentions to Yuna that he found a maintenance road hidden near the trailhead.
2. Yuna's reaction is a half-second too fast, brushing past the detail before he's even finished describing it.
3. She immediately tries to steer the conversation toward procedural next steps instead of the road itself.
4. Jonah notices the abruptness and, for the first time, reads it as her hiding something specific rather than general territoriality.
5. Yuna catches herself and privately regrets how obvious the deflection sounded, though she recovers her composure outwardly.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. The team convenes to plan next steps, agreeing the maintenance road needs to be searched.
2. Discussion turns to the clearance required to access land past the treeline.
3. Yuna offers to personally expedite the paperwork, framing it as simply saving everyone time.
4. The team accepts, grateful for her efficiency, unaware this gives her advance knowledge of when and how the search will happen.
5. Jonah, still holding his new suspicion from the tent, watches her volunteer with a flicker of doubt he doesn't voice yet.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Left alone for a moment in the last house, Milo finds a tablet showing the recovered watermark footage on a paused screen.
2. He studies the pattern intently through the window, checking it against what he remembers drawing.
3. His expression carries quiet satisfaction rather than confusion or fear.
4. He quickly resumes his blank, withdrawn posture the moment someone approaches.
5. The audience understands he's tracking the investigation's progress, though no one in the house sees this moment.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. NARRATOR marks night falling over the field ops tent as the team finally settles down.
2. Yuna quietly slips away from camp again, citing permits as she has every night before.
3. For the first time, Jonah notices her leaving and doesn't look away, watching the direction she goes.
4. He says nothing to her, but the audience senses his suspicion has crossed into active attention.
5. The episode ends on Jonah watching Yuna disappear into the dark, the question of where she's really going left hanging.
