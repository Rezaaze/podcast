--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Every device in the mountain village of Amity Hollow is still humming when the digital forensics team arrives — hot stoves, half-eaten dinners, phones charging on nightstands — but every one of its forty-one residents is gone without a single struggle. The only living witness is a ten-year-old boy who won't speak, and the only clue is a symbol he keeps drawing, one that turns up hidden inside the village's own security footage. This season follows the investigators sent to find out where the residents went — and the secrets, rivalries, and quiet betrayals inside their own team that complicate every step closer to the truth.

[NARRATOR]
Dusk, Amity Hollow Square. The forensics convoy climbs the last switchback as the sky bleeds from orange to ash.

[NARRATOR]
String lights crisscross the square overhead, still burning gold against the coming dark. Every door in sight stands open to the evening air, as if the whole village simply forgot to close them.

[SFX: gravel crunching under heavy tires, vehicles slowing to a stop]

[NARRATOR]
Three vans idle at the square's edge. Doors slide open. Boots hit the ground.

[SFX: van doors sliding open in sequence]

[NARRATOR]
No one comes out to meet them. No dogs bark. No lights flick on behind curtains. Just the string lights, swaying faintly, and the low murmur of appliances no one is left to use.

[NARRATOR]
A radio crackles somewhere near the lead vehicle — a voice confirming what the advance drone already found: forty-one residents, zero located. No blood. No forced doors. No sign anyone left in a hurry, or left unwillingly, or left at all in any way the eye can explain.

[SFX: a single radio crackle, then static settling]

[NARRATOR]
The team fans out across the square in practiced silence, flashlights cutting pale circles into the gathering dark. Tables sit set for dinner. Chairs face each other at odd, conversational angles. Nothing broken. Nothing overturned.

[NARRATOR]
Whatever happened here didn't announce itself. It simply arrived, and the village went with it — mid-sentence, mid-bite, mid-breath — leaving only the lights burning to prove anyone had been here at all.

[NARRATOR]
Amity Hollow waits, humming, for someone to ask it what it saw.
