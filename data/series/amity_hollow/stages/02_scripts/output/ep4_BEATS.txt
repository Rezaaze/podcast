--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Jonah steps away from the tent to take a call from his superiors on a secure line.
2. His superior presses him for a timeline on shutting the case down, treating "results" and "closure" as the same thing.
3. Jonah understates how close the triangulation actually is, giving a vaguer picture than what Wei Zhou has already found.
4. He tells himself that once he has hard geographic proof, the pressure to stand down will lose its grip on him.
5. He ends the call without either side naming a deadline outright, though both understand one is coming.
6. The audience senses what Jonah doesn't yet grasp: his superiors already know exactly what lies beneath that ground.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The team reconvenes at the forensics unit to refine the waveform's bearing using overnight data.
2. Wei Zhou narrows the transmission point from a general direction to a specific set of coordinates.
3. Jonah overlays the coordinates against the boot-print convergence map, and the two points align almost exactly.
4. The resolved location sits inside the restricted zone's perimeter, past any boundary the team has legal standing to cross.
5. Yuna watches the coordinates go up on the board without volunteering that she's already seen numbers close to these from her handler.
6. The team agrees the next move has to be the checkpoint itself, whatever waits on the other side of it.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. The team arrives at the restricted zone checkpoint and requests access, citing the coordinates and the case's evidentiary weight.
2. The guards state only that the zone is closed, offering no explanation tied to any agency, project, or authority.
3. Jonah asks who holds the lease on the land and gets a flat refusal rather than a name.
4. Yuna stays quiet through the exchange, aware the answer would come easily if she simply made a call.
5. Wei Zhou notes the guards' unwillingness to even acknowledge a facility exists, reading it as its own kind of confirmation.
6. The team leaves the fence with no new information but a sharper certainty that something deliberate is being protected.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Back at the tent, Jonah calls his superiors again, this time asking outright for more time before the case is pulled.
2. He frames the triangulated coordinates and the checkpoint stonewalling as proof the case is closer than ever, not proof it should end.
3. His superior grants a narrow window, forty-eight hours, without explaining why that number and not another.
4. Jonah reads the quick concession as a win, not registering how easily it came.
5. He doesn't tell the rest of the team how close the shutdown order actually is, keeping the countdown as his own private weight.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. The team hikes back toward the ridge trailhead to look for another way past the checkpoint's line.
2. Old Chen is waiting near the maintenance road, positioned as if he expected them.
3. He warns them off the ridge, and in his urgency names a specific ventilation stack by an engineer's term no local rumor would use.
4. Jonah catches the phrase and files it away without immediately reacting, watching Chen instead of challenging him.
5. Chen sees the flicker of recognition cross Jonah's face and immediately tries to walk the warning back as old superstition.
6. The team lets him retreat, but the specificity of what he said stays with all of them on the walk down.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone in his cabin, Chen sits with what he said at the trailhead replaying in his head.
2. He realizes the term he used for the ventilation stack isn't something a caretaker or hiker would ever say.
3. He understands that Jonah heard it, and heard it land wrong for a man who claims no more than folk knowledge.
4. He tells himself the slip changes nothing yet, that he can still decide later whether to help or stay silent.
5. Underneath that reasoning, he already senses the delay he's been granting himself is running out.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Mei Lin runs another session with Milo, working gently toward the underground shape from before.
2. She asks a soft, indirect question that includes the word "ridge."
3. Milo's hand closes so hard the crayon snaps, the reaction immediate and physical rather than gradual.
4. He goes rigid and silent, offering no drawing, no redirection, nothing to soften the moment this time.
5. Mei Lin logs the reaction as the sharpest yet, still reading it as trauma rather than as him hearing something aimed directly at him.
6. The audience recognizes the word landed exactly where it was meant to, confirming what Milo has known and hidden all along.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Jonah notices Wei Zhou is back at the forensics unit again well after the rest of the team has stood down for the night.
2. He points out that Wei Zhou's hours have gotten stranger rather than easing off as the case load lightens.
3. Wei Zhou deflects with a comment about the triangulation needing more passes before morning.
4. Jonah lets the explanation stand on the surface, but privately notes it doesn't match the specific places Wei Zhou keeps vanishing to.
5. Neither man raises the pattern directly, leaving Jonah's suspicion and Wei Zhou's secret both intact for another night.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Wei Zhou powers on the tablet he took from the Zhao household, working alone and unlogged.
2. He finds a partial voice memo recorded hours before the disappearance, and recognizes his cousin's voice immediately.
3. He hears her say the children won't stop looking at the speaker, a detail that lines up exactly with what the team now knows about the signal.
4. He sits with the recording longer than he needs to, the case's clinical distance gone for the length of it.
5. He understands this is the clearest firsthand account yet of the signal's effect on a listener, and also the last thing he has of her voice.
6. The audience sees him choosing, in that moment, which one of those two facts matters more to him right now.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Back at the forensics unit, Wei Zhou opens the case log intending to enter the memo and stops.
2. He tells himself he needs to verify the recording's authenticity before it becomes part of the record.
3. Underneath that reasoning is a truer one he doesn't say even to himself: logging it turns her voice into evidence instead of the last thing he has of her.
4. He closes the log without an entry, keeping the memo and the tablet both off the books for now.
5. The audience understands he's sitting on the single clearest lead in the case, and that no one else on the team knows it exists.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Yuna slips away from the main tent under the cover of a routine supply check.
2. She retrieves a second phone hidden in the tent's supply crate, one she never uses for official case business.
3. She places a call to the unlisted number, keeping her voice low and clipped.
4. Her handler presses her on how close the team's triangulation has actually gotten, and she gives a fuller answer than she's given the team itself.
5. She still frames the arrangement to herself as legitimate containment, though the specificity of what she's reporting sits uneasily with her this time.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Jonah steps into the supply area to grab equipment, catching Yuna mid-call before she can end it cleanly.
2. She kills the call and pockets the second phone in the same motion, covering the half-second gap with a question about gear instead.
3. Jonah notices the phone isn't the one she carries for official comms, but says nothing about it in the moment.
4. He decides to watch rather than confront, filing the sighting away instead of raising it with the team.
5. Yuna reads his silence as the moment passing cleanly, not realizing he's chosen to hold what he saw rather than let it go.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. The team regroups in the square among the same cold tables and untouched mugs from before.
2. They weigh what ground is left to cover against the forty-eight hours Jonah now knows are ticking down.
3. Jonah doesn't share the exact deadline, framing the urgency in general terms instead of naming the clock.
4. Wei Zhou stays quieter than usual, the tablet and the memo sitting unspoken behind his focus on the search grid.
5. The team settles on pushing toward the ridge access point as the best use of the time they have left.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Alone again in his cabin, Chen takes out the drawer of hand-drawn blueprints he's kept shut for years.
2. He lays them out and studies the access point only he knows, the one that isn't on any public map.
3. He weighs the years of silence against what he saw in Jonah's face at the trailhead, and decides the delay has run its course.
4. He begins copying the access point onto a plain sheet, deliberately leaving off anything that would trace back to him by name.
5. He sets the drawing aside to give the team off the record, choosing to help without yet deciding to explain why.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Wei Zhou reviews the mesh network's activity log and flags an outbound data transmission at an odd hour.
2. The timestamp lines up with a device he half-recognizes as one Yuna claims she doesn't use in the field.
3. He notes it only in a private file for now, not ready to call it more than an anomaly.
4. He tells himself it's more likely a diagnostic artifact from the mesh network than anything deliberate.
5. The audience registers the ping as a second thread, alongside Jonah's sighting, quietly closing in on Yuna without either man comparing notes yet.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. In the dark hours before dawn, the team gathers at the ridge trailhead with Chen's hand-drawn access point in hand.
2. Jonah checks the group's readiness, the forty-eight-hour window now close enough that no one needs it said aloud.
3. Yuna double-checks her gear including the hidden second phone, making sure it stays out of sight for what's ahead.
4. Wei Zhou keeps the Zhao tablet on him rather than leaving it behind, unwilling to let it out of his reach now.
5. The team moves out together toward the access point, each carrying a piece of what they haven't told the others.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Back at the forensics unit before the team fully disperses toward the ridge, Wei Zhou finds a moment alone with the folded map on the console.
2. He watches Yuna across the tent a beat longer than the moment calls for, turning the ping over in his mind.
3. He can't yet convince himself it means what he's starting to suspect, but he no longer entirely believes his own diagnostic explanation either.
4. He says nothing to her or to Jonah, letting the stand-down clock and his own private suspicion run side by side.
5. The audience is left holding two threads closing on the same person from two different men, neither yet aware the other sees it too.
