--- SCENE 1 ---
1. NARRATOR sets the scene: dusk falling over Amity Hollow, a village of forty-one residents gone without a trace.
2. Establishes the string lights still glowing over an empty square and doors left open to the evening air.
3. Notes the forensics team's convoy arriving as the last color drains from the sky.
4. Plants the central mystery: everyone vanished, nothing was taken, no sign of struggle.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Jonah walks the square alone, taking in string lights still lit at full power.
2. He notes dinners left cold and half-eaten on tables, chairs pushed back as if everyone stood up at once.
3. He observes no forced entry, no broken glass, no signs of a struggle anywhere in sight.
4. He forms an early, mistaken read: this looks staged, like a coordinated departure rather than an abduction.
5. He instructs the team to start cataloging before anything is disturbed, masking his unease with procedure.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Wei Zhou sets up the mobile forensics unit and begins logging seized devices: phones, tablets, smart-home hubs.
2. He cross-references the last recorded activity on several phones, noting they all went dark within moments of each other.
3. He flags to no one yet that several devices show identical shutdown timestamps, filing it as a detail to revisit.
4. He keeps his manner brisk and clinical, giving no outward sign that this case is personal to him.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Yuna gathers the team in the field ops tent to brief them on procedure for the case.
2. She explains that the woods bordering the village are a restricted zone requiring special clearance to enter.
3. She presents her own access paperwork as routine, not mentioning how quickly it was approved three years ago.
4. She redirects a question about who actually controls the restricted land toward vague talk of permits and jurisdiction.
5. The audience senses her practiced ease is a little too smooth for someone new to this kind of authorization.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. A search team finds Milo alone in the last house at the edge of the village, unharmed but silent.
2. The walls around him are covered floor to ceiling in the same repeating crayon symbol, drawn over and over.
3. Milo doesn't react to being found, doesn't speak, doesn't reach for anyone.
4. The team logs him as the sole apparent survivor, unaware he was placed there deliberately.
5. Someone remarks on how undisturbed and clean the room feels compared to the wreck of drawings.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Mei Lin sits with Milo in the trauma safe room, trying gentle questions to get any response.
2. Milo stares past her, offering no words, no flinch, no tears.
3. She notes his stillness reads as almost too composed for a child who just lost everyone he knows.
4. She privately diagnoses this as acute dissociation from witnessed trauma, unaware it's a performance.
5. She decides not to push, choosing patience over urgency, and doesn't share her unease with the team yet.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Investigators follow a trail of muddled boot prints leading out of the village toward the ridge trailhead.
2. The prints represent all forty-one residents, overlapping and chaotic but heading the same direction.
3. At the treeline, every set of prints stops abruptly, with no continuation, no drag marks, no signs of a fight.
4. The abrupt end unsettles the team more than a struggle would have, since it makes no physical sense.
5. Jonah notes this detail privately, unaware of how closely it echoes an old case he'd rather not revisit.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Back at the tent, Jonah asks Yuna directly who holds jurisdiction over the restricted land past the treeline.
2. Yuna gives a technically true but incomplete answer, steering around naming any specific tenant or corporation.
3. Jonah presses harder, sensing she's being vaguer than the question calls for.
4. A flicker of friction surfaces between them, though Jonah reads it as her being territorial about her role, not concealment.
5. Yuna closes the conversation by promising to "look into it further," buying herself time.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Wei Zhou digs deeper into the seized smart-home hubs and finds they're all running an identical firmware version.
2. He checks the install timestamp and realizes it was pushed to every hub roughly six hours before the disappearance.
3. He finds no record of who authorized or issued the update, which strikes him as highly irregular.
4. He decides to verify the anomaly himself before flagging it to the team, not wanting to raise a false alarm.
5. He assumes, incorrectly, that the corrupted camera files found elsewhere are an unrelated storage glitch from a power surge.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Alone at an abandoned dinner table, Jonah sits with the wreckage of a normal evening interrupted.
2. He turns over the earlier read he made — mass departure, cult exodus — and starts to doubt his own snap judgment.
3. He recalls, in fragments, a past case with the same eerie stillness that he dismissed too quickly and got wrong.
4. He resolves not to let the team see how much this scene is rattling him, protecting his authority rather than his instincts.
5. He forces himself back into procedure mode, burying the doubt instead of voicing it.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Milo, left with paper and crayons to keep him occupied, draws the same symbol again without prompting.
2. Mei Lin watches and realizes the drawing is identical, stroke for stroke, to ones she's already seen from him.
3. She quietly counts back and recognizes this is not the first or second time — it's become compulsive repetition.
4. She starts to sense something about this doesn't fit the trauma-response pattern she diagnosed earlier, though she can't articulate what.
5. She chooses to keep this growing doubt to herself rather than flag it to the team yet.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Once the tent goes quiet for the night, Yuna slips away from camp under the excuse of checking on permits.
2. She places an encrypted call to the same unlisted number she contacts every night.
3. She gives a brief status update on the investigation's progress, careful and rehearsed.
4. The audience learns, ahead of any character on the team, that this nightly ritual is deliberate and hidden, not incidental.
5. She returns to camp acting as though nothing happened, folding back into the group seamlessly.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Wei Zhou continues logging resident information against the seized devices and reaches the address list.
2. He stops cold at "14 Birch Lane," recognizing it instantly as his cousin's family home.
3. He says nothing to anyone nearby, closing the file and returning to his work as if unaffected.
4. The audience learns, ahead of the team, that the missing include people Wei Zhou loves, not just case subjects.
5. His hands betray a slight unsteadiness that he quickly masters before anyone notices.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Jonah and Mei Lin meet at the last house to discuss next steps with Milo.
2. Jonah argues for more direct questioning, pointing out that time may matter if anyone is still out there.
3. Mei Lin pushes back, insisting Milo needs space and gentleness, not pressure, given his apparent trauma.
4. The disagreement exposes a values clash: Jonah's urgency for answers versus Mei Lin's instinct to protect the child first.
5. Neither of them realizes their entire approach is built on a false read of what Milo actually is to this case.
6. They agree to a compromise — one more gentle attempt tomorrow before considering anything firmer.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. At the ridge trailhead after dark, a technician sets up night-recording equipment near where the boot prints stopped.
2. The equipment begins picking up a faint, steady ambient hum right at the treeline.
3. The team can't identify the source or explain why it's isolated to this one location.
4. The detail is logged as unexplained rather than dismissed, seeding unease for what's beneath the surface.
5. No one present yet connects the hum to anything deliberate or engineered.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Back at the tent, the team regroups to set tomorrow's priorities: the hum, Milo, the firmware, the boot prints.
2. Discussion turns to needing clearance to search the restricted zone near the treeline.
3. Yuna volunteers to expedite that clearance herself, framing it as simply cutting through red tape faster than anyone else could.
4. The team accepts gratefully, unaware her offer gives her more control over what the investigation finds and when.
5. Jonah notes, again, how efficient she is at this — chalking it up to competence rather than familiarity with the land.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. NARRATOR closes the episode past midnight in the mobile forensics unit, where Wei Zhou has stayed long after the others slept.
2. The first corrupted security-camera file finally finishes rendering after hours of recovery work.
3. As the frame resolves, a symbol emerges embedded in the corrupted footage — one the audience recognizes from Milo's walls.
4. The episode ends on this unresolved image, before any character has connected it to what it truly means.
