--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day one of the countdown, six days before Hurricane Odette is projected to make landfall on Halyard Cay.
2. Aldridge assembles the full clinic staff and announces that Odette has been upgraded to Category Two and is still strengthening.
3. He states that the ferry company has confirmed only six guaranteed evacuation seats for clinic patients and staff.
4. Staff react with unease at how small six seats sounds against the number of patients who may need them.
5. Aldridge speaks with practiced calm about fairness and triage, the same rehearsed fairness he has performed for twenty-two years without anyone reading anything into it.
6. The audience sees Aldridge's hand tremble slightly as he sets down the seat-count memo, unremarked by anyone in the room.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Aldridge asks Kimi Sato, as clinic records administrator, to draft this week's evacuation priority list.
2. Kimi accepts the assignment as a straightforward administrative task, flattered by the trust it implies.
3. Aldridge notes that the list should follow the same medical-priority protocol the clinic has always used.
4. The audience recognizes the dramatic irony that this is the same kind of list that once cost Kimi's mother her life, though Kimi does not know it.
5. Kimi mentions she'll want to check past storm-priority records for reference, prompting a flicker of stillness in Aldridge that he covers quickly.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mei Chen tells the records office she needs to pull the clinic's storm files from twenty-two years ago as part of a routine protocol review.
2. She frames the request as standard housekeeping ahead of the coming storm, giving no one reason to question it.
3. Privately, Mei is anxious to see whether Aiko Sato's altered file still reads the way she rewrote it after the storm.
4. The audience understands Mei's true motive is not protocol review but checking on a decades-old cover-up, information the other staff lack.
5. Mei requests the files be pulled quietly, without flagging them to Kimi, who is currently working in the same records system.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Danny Su walks the dock inspecting the ferry, running his hands along the hull and counting seats aloud.
2. He does the math on official capacity versus the six guaranteed clinic seats and how little room exists for anyone else.
3. Danny lingers near the storage shed at the end of the dock, glancing toward it before continuing his inspection.
4. He begins mentally rehearsing an explanation for a mechanical fault that could shave one seat off the official count.
5. The audience senses Danny is planning something with the ferry's numbers, without yet knowing why.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Danny slips behind the crates in the ferry shed to bring food and supplies to someone hidden there.
2. The audience learns this is Marcus Su, Danny's younger brother, who has been hiding in the shed for three weeks.
3. Danny warns Marcus to stay silent and out of sight, treating the visit as routine gear-checking if anyone asks.
4. Marcus presses Danny about how he'll actually get off the island when the ferry leaves.
5. Danny assures Marcus he has a plan involving their late cousin Peter's identification, without yet explaining the mechanical-fault scheme.
6. The audience learns Danny's plan would come at the cost of a seat meant for a clinic patient, though Danny does not frame it that way to himself.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Sophie Kang finds Aldridge alone in his office and notices his hand shaking as he signs a supply requisition form.
2. She asks directly whether he's been sleeping, treating it as ordinary concern about storm-prep exhaustion.
3. Aldridge deflects, blaming the long hours and the pressure of finalizing the seat list.
4. Sophie accepts the explanation at face value, unaware it has nothing to do with sleep.
5. The audience knows what Sophie doesn't: Aldridge was diagnosed with advanced pancreatic cancer six weeks ago and is hiding it.
6. Aldridge quickly changes the subject to logistics, subtly steering Sophie away from further questions.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. At the Saltbox Tavern, islanders add items to a chalkboard list of storm supplies needed before Odette arrives.
2. Talk turns to which residents will need special accommodation on the ferry, including elderly patients.
3. Someone raises Otis Bramwell's name, noting his daily oxygen tanks and the space and priority he'll require.
4. Jonah Bramwell, Otis's grandson, listens in, already uneasy about something in the clinic's paperwork this week that doesn't sit right to him.
5. The audience sees the seed of the collision to come: Otis's guaranteed need for a seat against Danny's hidden plan to cut one.
6. Jonah says nothing about his unease, assuming it's just ordinary supply-shortage worry with no connection to the ferry or Danny.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Kimi boards up the windows of her mother's old house in preparation for the storm.
2. She works beneath a photograph of Aiko Sato watching from the wall, pausing briefly to look at it.
3. Kimi reflects on the story she has always been told: that no doctor could reach her mother in time during Hurricane Marisol.
4. The audience holds the dramatic irony that this belief is false, and that the true cause sits inside records Kimi herself now manages.
5. Kimi finishes the boards and steps back, unaware she has just been handed the very kind of list that determined her mother's fate.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone in the records office after hours, Mei Chen sits with Aiko Sato's altered file in front of her.
2. She hesitates before opening it, having avoided looking at it directly for years.
3. The audience sees her handle the file with the weight of someone protecting a secret, not reviewing routine paperwork.
4. Mei confirms the file still reads 'no bed capacity available,' exactly as she rewrote it twenty-two years ago.
5. She closes the file without updating or flagging anything, choosing to preserve the cover-up rather than reconsider it now that Kimi is nearby.
6. The audience learns Mei's private fear: that Kimi, now trusted with clinic records, might one day look closely enough to see through it.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Sophie finds Aldridge again that evening, still turning over his shaking hands and pallor in her mind.
2. She starts to ask him something more pointed about his health, opening with concern rather than small talk.
3. Aldridge's expression shifts briefly, bracing for a question he isn't ready to answer.
4. Sophie stops herself partway, deciding not to push, telling herself he's just tired and stressed like everyone else this week.
5. The audience recognizes this as a near-miss: Sophie is one direct question away from learning what Aldridge is hiding.
6. Aldridge exhales in private relief once she lets it go, alone with the secret for at least one more day.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. The clinic staff lock up for the night, the seat list and storm prep now set in motion.
2. Aldridge does a final walkthrough alone, pausing at the spot where the evacuation list will eventually be posted.
3. Offshore, Hurricane Odette continues to strengthen, with six days remaining until landfall.
4. The audience is left holding four unresolved threads at once: the buried truth of the Marisol list, Danny's hidden brother and seat scheme, Aldridge's concealed diagnosis, and Kimi's unknowing proximity to her mother's real story.
5. The episode closes on the countdown number ticking forward, the seats still fixed at six, and every character's secret still intact.
