--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Three days have passed since the clinic staff meeting that opened the countdown, one day since day two closed with five days left — it is now day three, four days remaining until Hurricane Odette's projected landfall.
2. The dock crew gathers again around the marine radio as a new bulletin confirms Odette has strengthened to Category Three.
3. The report notes the storm's wind field has widened, and the ferry company warns crossings may need to stop a full day earlier than planned.
4. Danny listens with the others, but his focus narrows on what an earlier cutoff means for the timeline he needs to get Marcus aboard.
5. A crew member says the six-seat count now feels like the least of anyone's worries with a category jump like that.
6. The audience senses Danny's window to execute his plan has just gotten shorter, raising the stakes on what happens next.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Danny radios the clinic and ferry dispatch to report a mechanical fault in one of the ferry's ballast systems.
2. He states the fault reduces safe capacity by one seat, citing weight distribution as the reason.
3. Privately, Danny knows the ferry is sound and the fault is invented, built solely to make room for Marcus under cousin Peter's identification.
4. He frames the report as a safety measure, careful to sound reluctant rather than eager to lose a seat.
5. Dispatch logs the fault without question, trusting Danny's standing as captain.
6. The audience now watches Danny's scheme move from planning into action, with a real patient's seat about to be the cost.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Kimi receives the amended capacity notice and sits down to revise this week's evacuation priority list from six seats to five.
2. Running the numbers against medical priority protocol, she finds Otis Bramwell's oxygen-dependent status now falls just below the new cutoff.
3. Kimi provisionally marks Otis as bumped, flagging him for reassessment if a seat reopens.
4. She has no way of knowing the shortage she's responding to was fabricated, and treats the fault report as simple fact.
5. Kimi feels the weight of the decision but tells herself she is only following the same protocol the clinic has always used.
6. The audience recognizes the collision now landing exactly where episode one and two set it up: Danny's invented seat cut costing Otis his place.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Word of the revised list reaches Jonah before the day is out, and he finds Danny back at the dock.
2. Jonah demands to know how a mechanical fault conveniently costs his grandfather his seat.
3. His anger is sharpened by the shed encounter from the day before, though he still doesn't connect the two.
4. Jonah accuses Danny of not fighting hard enough for Otis, assuming carelessness rather than deliberate manipulation.
5. Danny absorbs the anger without correcting the assumption, letting Jonah believe the more forgivable version of events.
6. The audience holds what Jonah doesn't: the fault is fabricated, and the true reason has nothing to do with negligence.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Jonah presses for details about which part failed and when it will be fixed.
2. Danny gives only vague technical language, citing ballast weight without offering anything Jonah can verify.
3. When Jonah asks whether the fault can be repaired before the ferry's final run, Danny says he can't promise anything.
4. Jonah reads the vagueness as evasiveness but still assumes it's about Danny's competence, not concealment.
5. Danny ends the conversation by turning back to dock work, closing off further questions.
6. The audience sees Danny believe, wrongly, that stonewalling has settled the matter, the same misplaced confidence he showed with Jonah the day before.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Sophie catches Aldridge alone in the supply room during a lull, away from the rest of the staff.
2. She sets aside the earlier deflections and asks him directly whether something is actually wrong with his health.
3. Aldridge goes still for a moment, caught by the directness he's been avoiding since Sophie's first near-question.
4. Sophie says she isn't asking as staff right now, just as someone who's noticed him disappearing into pain more than once.
5. The audience recognizes this as the direct question the previous two episodes have been building toward.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Aldridge answers with a specific, credible story: an old back injury from years ago has flared up under the physical strain of storm prep.
2. He describes it in just enough plausible detail, a fall, a disc issue, that it doesn't invite further medical questions.
3. Sophie visibly relaxes, the explanation accounting for everything she's observed.
4. A small note of doubt lingers in her all the same, though she doesn't voice it or examine why.
5. Aldridge internally believes the answer has fully closed the conversation and bought him more time before anyone looks closer.
6. The audience holds what Sophie doesn't: this is advanced pancreatic cancer, not a back injury, and the lie has landed exactly as Aldridge needed it to.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. At the Saltbox Tavern, the chalkboard supply list is visibly shorter, most items crossed off as shelves empty out.
2. Islanders compare notes on what's left to find, batteries and bottled water now the scarcest items.
3. Talk turns again to the ferry's revised capacity, and word has spread that Otis Bramwell has been bumped.
4. Some islanders murmur sympathy for Otis, others simply note how quickly the math keeps changing.
5. Jonah, present but withdrawn, says nothing about his earlier confrontation with Danny.
6. The audience feels the storm's practical pressure and the human cost of the seat cut converging in the same room.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Late into the evening, Kimi stays behind in the records office, cross-referencing this week's evacuation list against the clinic's older storm-priority archives.
2. She pulls files from Hurricane Marisol twenty-two years earlier, treating it as due diligence on how the clinic has historically handled scarcity.
3. Kimi doesn't yet search directly for her mother's name, but she is now working inside the very archive that holds it.
4. She notes some of the old paperwork looks incomplete or oddly worded compared to more recent records, without knowing what she's looking at.
5. The audience recognizes she is one file away from the record Mei Chen altered, closer than she's ever been.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Mei Chen finds a reason to stay near the records office rather than go home, watching Kimi work from a short distance.
2. She keeps her manner casual, offering to help file or fetch things, while quietly tracking exactly which boxes Kimi opens.
3. Mei's anxiety rises as she sees Kimi's search moving deeper into the Marisol-era archive rather than away from it.
4. She says nothing of this rising fear to Aldridge, holding to her private resolve not to add to his visible strain.
5. Mei tells herself that as long as she keeps redirecting Kimi's questions when they come, the file will stay buried through the crisis week.
6. The audience watches the gap widen between what Mei fears and what she's willing to say aloud to anyone.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Night falls on day three, four days now remaining until Hurricane Odette's landfall, the storm now confirmed at Category Three.
2. The dock crew lashes down loose equipment and double-checks the ferry's mooring lines against the strengthening wind.
3. Danny watches the sea with the added pressure of a shortened timeline now weighing on his plan for Marcus.
4. Every thread from the day remains unresolved: Otis provisionally bumped, Jonah's suspicion redirected but not settled, Sophie's doubt quietly lingering beneath Aldridge's back-injury story, and Kimi drawing steadily nearer to her mother's true file.
5. The audience is left holding all four secrets now measurably closer to the surface than they were the day before.
6. The episode closes on the tightening countdown, one seat already lost, and four days left before the storm arrives in full.
