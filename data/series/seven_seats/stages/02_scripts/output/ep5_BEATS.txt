--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Two days have passed since day four closed with three days remaining; it is now day six, one day before Hurricane Odette's projected landfall on Halyard Cay.
2. Wind and rain from Odette's outer bands reach Halyard Cay hours earlier than forecast, cutting away the last buffer of time everyone assumed they had.
3. Aldridge watches the weather turn from the clinic window, aware his window to speak to Kimi himself has effectively closed.
4. Danny scrambles to secure the ferry against the sudden wind, aware the harbor office will want the mechanical-fault report finalized before conditions worsen further.
5. Kimi returns to Mei Chen, refusing to let the exchange from the night before stay unfinished.
6. The audience feels every deferred confrontation on the island losing its remaining slack at once, forced now by the storm's early arrival.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Kimi finds Mei alone again, giving her no room to retreat behind the storm's urgency.
2. Mei tries once more to redirect with the old line about archival language, but her voice breaks on the first word.
3. Cornered with nowhere left to go, Mei tells Kimi plainly that it was Theo Aldridge and Walter Kessler who swapped her mother off the evacuation list twenty-two years ago.
4. Mei says nothing of her own hand in rewriting the file afterward, letting Kimi believe the concealment began and ended with the two men.
5. The audience recognizes Mei is protecting herself even in the act of finally telling the truth.
6. Kimi goes rigid, absorbing in seconds what twenty-two years of belief cannot immediately hold.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Kimi says nothing in response, her silence heavier than any question she might have asked.
2. Mei reaches for something like an explanation or apology, but Kimi is already turning toward the door.
3. Kimi walks out of the records office before Mei can say anything else, leaving the sentence unfinished between them.
4. The audience notes Kimi now holds two names but not Kessler's pressure on Aldridge, nor that the file itself was altered by Mei's own hand.
5. Mei is left alone with the fear that naming Aldridge and Kessler won't be enough to satisfy what Kimi does next.
6. Twenty-two years of silence ends not in resolution but in Kimi walking into the storm-lit hallway alone.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Sophie finds Aldridge alone in his office as the wind outside picks up against the shutters.
2. She sets the oncologist's letter down on his desk without preamble, done holding it in silence.
3. Aldridge stops mid-motion, recognizing the letterhead before he can form a response.
4. He asks how long she's known, and Sophie admits she's carried it since the day she went looking for requisition paperwork.
5. The audience watches the concealment Aldridge believed was still contained collapse in a single wordless moment.
6. Aldridge sits down heavily, the pretense of the back injury gone between them for good.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Aldridge starts to reach for the mainland-trip cover story, then stops, done rehearsing it.
2. He says the word 'cancer' out loud for the first time since his diagnosis six weeks ago.
3. Sophie asks about the prognosis directly, and Aldridge tells her it's measured in months, not years.
4. He admits he told no one on the island because saying it aloud would have made the rest of the week impossible to get through.
5. The audience hears, for the first time from Aldridge himself, the fact that has shaped his choices since episode one.
6. Sophie says nothing accusatory, only staying present with him in the silence that follows.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Aldridge tells Sophie he's decided to give up his guaranteed staff seat on the evacuation ferry.
2. Sophie pushes back immediately, telling him a diagnosis isn't a reason to stay behind for a hurricane.
3. Aldridge insists the seat should go to a patient, framing the decision as simple math rather than anything more.
4. The audience recognizes what Aldridge doesn't say aloud: the choice is also unspoken penance for the Marisol list, not only self-sacrifice.
5. Sophie asks who the seat should go to, and Aldridge says he'll decide once he sees the final numbers.
6. Sophie leaves the room carrying two secrets instead of one, still telling no one else.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Danny contacts the harbor office again to formally finalize the mechanical-fault report before the storm cuts off communication.
2. He repeats the ballast-weight explanation once more, matching every detail to what he's already told Jonah and dispatch.
3. The harbor office logs the report as final, locking the ferry's official capacity at five seats.
4. Danny allows himself a moment of relief, believing the paperwork is now settled beyond question.
5. The audience knows what Danny doesn't yet: Jonah is already on his way to the shed for rope, minutes from finding Marcus.
6. Danny heads back toward the dock to check on Marcus, unaware the story is about to unravel without any more questions from anyone.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Jonah heads to the ferry shed for rope to lash down loose tavern shutters ahead of the storm's main arrival.
2. He moves past the crates he'd noticed shifted days earlier, this time with no Danny there to redirect him.
3. Behind the diesel drums, Jonah finds a man he doesn't immediately place, thinner and more haggard than he remembers.
4. Marcus, cornered, hesitates a beat too long before offering any explanation, the delay itself giving him away.
5. Jonah realizes this is no drifter, and the footprints, the shifted crates, and Danny's shifting stories all click into place at once.
6. The audience watches the season's second near-miss finally fail to hold, the truth landing with nothing left to deflect it.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Danny rounds the corner toward the shed just in time to see Jonah already standing face to face with Marcus.
2. Danny freezes for a beat, the drifter story dying before he can even attempt it.
3. Jonah looks between the two of them and puts it together: Marcus is Danny's brother, hiding from something on the mainland.
4. Danny starts some half-formed mix of explanation and appeal, but Jonah cuts him off, asking instead about Otis's seat.
5. The audience sees Danny's wrongly-held belief from episode four, that his stonewalling had closed the matter, collapse completely in real time.
6. Jonah's earlier confusion over the shifting mechanical-fault story resolves into cold, clear understanding of exactly what it was covering for.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Back at the clinic, Kimi hears secondhand that Aldridge is giving up his guaranteed staff seat, the news moving fast in the storm's tightening hours.
2. Still reeling from what Mei told her, Kimi pulls the finalized evacuation numbers to see how the vacated seat changes the count.
3. She reconciles Aldridge's forfeited seat against Danny's reduced five-seat total from the fabricated report and finds the number still comes up short against everyone with a rightful claim.
4. Kimi realizes the math doesn't work even with a seat freed up, meaning someone was always going to be left off regardless of Aldridge's gesture.
5. The audience watches the season's two central threads collide in the same column of numbers: the Marisol list's history and this week's ferry shortage now reflecting the same unresolved arithmetic.
6. Kimi sets the list down, unable yet to decide what to do with either truth she's now holding.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At the Saltbox Tavern, the last islanders board the windows for good as the outer bands strengthen into steady wind and rain.
2. Conversation has gone terse, people working in silence rather than trading news the way they did earlier in the week.
3. Word of Jonah finding Marcus in the shed reaches the tavern in fragments, tempers rising as people realize what Danny's fault report may have actually covered for.
4. Someone asks aloud whether Otis's bumped seat was ever really about a mechanical fault at all, and no one has an answer that holds.
5. The audience feels the island's patience running out at the exact moment the storm itself arrives in force.
6. The last board goes up over the tavern window, sealing the building against wind now audibly stronger than an hour before.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Kimi finds Aldridge as the storm noise builds outside, the list from the records office still in her hand.
2. She says it plainly in front of him: whatever happens next, there isn't room on that boat for everyone who has a rightful claim to a seat.
3. Aldridge doesn't ask what she means by 'rightful claim,' understanding immediately that she now knows about her mother too.
4. The audience holds the double weight of the moment: Kimi confronting both this week's crisis and the twenty-two-year-old truth in the same breath, though she still doesn't know the file itself was altered, or by whom.
5. Neither of them says the name Aiko Sato aloud, though it sits plainly between them.
6. The episode closes with the season's two central secrets and its present-day crisis converging on one unresolved sentence, no seats left to spare and no more time to decide who gets one.
