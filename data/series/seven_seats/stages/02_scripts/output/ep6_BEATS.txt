--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Hours have passed since Kimi stood with Aldridge in the storm-lit hallway at the close of the previous episode; Hurricane Odette is now making full landfall on Halyard Cay, the eye still to come but winds already at hurricane force.
2. The ferry crew races to finish loading before the rising surge cuts the dock off from the rest of the island.
3. Danny works the lines and cargo with visible urgency, aware Jonah now knows about Marcus but hasn't yet forced the confrontation into the open.
4. The harbor office radios repeated warnings that the crossing window is closing faster than forecast.
5. The audience feels every deferred secret on the island losing its last slack at the same moment the storm itself arrives in force.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Kimi, Mei, and Aldridge gather over the manifest as the storm intensifies, the official seat count still fixed at five because of Danny's fault report.
2. They find Otis Bramwell's provisionally reinstated name competing against an unfamiliar name, "Peter Su," listed for the same last open seat.
3. None of the three yet knows Peter Su is dead or that his identity is being used to smuggle Marcus aboard.
4. Aldridge sends for Danny to confirm the registration before the list can be finalized.
5. Mei keeps her attention fixed on the manifest work, still holding her own secret while the search for Danny begins around her.
6. Kimi, still absorbing what Mei told her the night before, works the numbers mechanically, treating this as one more crisis stacked on the one she hasn't finished processing.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Jonah arrives at the dock with Danny in tow, having marched him there straight from the shed.
2. Jonah tells Aldridge plainly that Danny has been hiding his brother Marcus in the ferry shed for three weeks.
3. Aldridge connects the name "Peter Su" from the manifest to what Jonah is describing, realizing a dead man's identity was meant to cover a stowaway seat.
4. Danny stands silent for a beat, recognizing there's no version of the drifter story that survives Jonah's presence and Aldridge's authority together.
5. The audience watches Danny's carefully maintained cover collapse in front of the one person he most needed to avoid.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Aldridge asks Danny directly whether the ballast-system fault was ever real.
2. Danny admits it was invented, meant only to justify cutting the official count by one seat.
3. He confirms Marcus was never on any legitimate manifest and was to board using cousin Peter Su's identification.
4. Aldridge and Jonah realize in the same moment that Otis Bramwell's earlier bump was the direct, unknowing cost of Danny's fabrication.
5. Danny doesn't try to soften it further, letting the full shape of the lie stand now that it's finally spoken aloud.
6. The audience sees the season's second concealed thread fully surface, a day after the Marisol List's own near-total exposure.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Danny withdraws any claim to a seat for Marcus, accepting his brother cannot board under a dead man's name.
2. He asks only that Marcus not be turned over to the harbor authorities until the storm has passed.
3. Aldridge agrees to hold that question until the island is safe, putting the immediate crossing ahead of the legal matter.
4. Danny agrees, without conditions, to answer fully for the falsified report once Halyard Cay is through the storm.
5. Jonah watches without satisfaction, the confirmation of deception giving him no comfort about what his grandfather nearly lost.
6. The audience sees Danny's scheme end not in bare exposure but in him choosing to give it up once cornered.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Word reaches Mei that Kimi and Aldridge are now standing together at the manifest, and she goes to them before the moment passes.
2. Mei tells them both, together, that it wasn't only Kessler's order and Aldridge's compliance — she herself wrote the words "no bed capacity available" into Aiko Sato's file twenty-two years ago.
3. Aldridge absorbs the admission alongside his own guilt, hearing for the first time that Mei's hand was in the cover-up as well as his.
4. Kimi finally holds every piece of the twenty-two-year story at once: the swap, the pressure, and the altered record, spoken together for the first time.
5. Mei doesn't ask for forgiveness, only says she's carried it alone since the year Kessler died and could no longer share the blame.
6. The audience watches the file's true origin spoken by the one person who wrote it, closing the season-long concealment in this thread.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Mei steps away, leaving Kimi and Aldridge alone with the full truth between them for the first time.
2. Aldridge confirms directly to Kimi that Kessler pressured him into striking her mother's name, and that he complied.
3. He doesn't shelter behind Kessler's order, admitting his twenty-two years of scrupulous fairness were unspoken penance he never once named aloud.
4. Kimi says her mother's name, Aiko Sato, for the first time in the conversation, finally attaching it plainly to the man she has trusted with the island's fairness for years.
5. Neither offers the other absolution, the silence between them holding both the years lost and the storm still raging outside.
6. The audience sees the Marisol List thread reach the confrontation the season has built toward since episode one.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Word reaches Jonah at the dock that Otis Bramwell has been restored to the confirmed evacuation list, no conditions attached, once Peter Su's name is struck as fraudulent.
2. Jonah allows himself a moment of relief that has nothing to do with any punishment for Danny.
3. He learns Danny will answer for the fabricated report only once the island is safe, an arrangement he accepts given the storm's urgency.
4. The audience sees the collision built across episodes one through four resolved in Otis's favor, restored by the same exposure that ended Danny's scheme.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. In the same stretch of chaos, Sophie finds Mei Chen and tells her quietly that Aldridge has advanced pancreatic cancer with a prognosis measured in months.
2. Sophie explains she's telling her now so Aldridge won't face the last seat decision with no one nearby who understands what it's actually costing him.
3. Mei absorbs this within the same hour her own decades-old secret has just come out to Kimi and Aldridge, with no space left to process either.
4. Neither Sophie nor Mei raises whether Aldridge will take a seat or stay behind, the decision still sitting unresolved between all three of them.
5. The audience holds three secrets now converging on the same small group in the same short window before the ferry leaves.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. With the wind screaming and the ferry engine already running, Kimi stands with the final manifest in hand while Aldridge waits in front of her for the call.
2. The corrected count folds Marcus's phantom seat back to Otis and returns Aldridge's forfeited staff seat to the pool, but seven names remain in claim against six real seats.
3. Kimi runs the names once more, aware that however she decides, someone from Halyard Cay will still be left standing on the dock.
4. Aldridge doesn't ask to be exempted from her decision, standing among the names like anyone else's, his seat forfeited but his place on the boat still unsettled.
5. Kimi makes her call on who boards, but whether Aldridge is among them, or chooses to stay regardless of her decision, remains unspoken between them as the engine idles.
6. The audience is left holding the same unresolved arithmetic from the day before, now forced to its final moment without a clean answer for everyone.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. The ferry casts off from Halyard Cay with the six names Kimi finalized aboard, Danny remaining behind to face the harbor office once the storm passes.
2. Marcus stays on the island as well, his fate tied to whatever accounting Danny agreed to make once conditions allow.
3. Whether Aldridge is aboard or has stayed behind on the dock is left deliberately unclear as the boat pulls into the storm, the question Kimi's list posed to him never answered onscreen.
4. Mei remains on the island too, her confession spoken but its consequences, professional or otherwise, still ahead of her.
5. The audience watches the island's newest secret, Danny's fabricated report, end in confession rather than exposure alone, while its oldest secret, the Marisol List, is finally spoken aloud in full after twenty-two years.
6. Halyard Cay is left in the hands of Hurricane Odette, every thread from the season resolved in truth if not yet in consequence, the boat vanishing into the weather as the countdown finally reaches zero.
