--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is now day two of the countdown, five days before Hurricane Odette's projected landfall on Halyard Cay.
2. The dock crew clusters around a marine radio, listening to a report that Odette's outer rain bands are now visible on the horizon.
3. Danny checks the ferry's mooring lines against the rising chop, his mind more on Marcus than the weather.
4. A crew member remarks that the six-seat count already feels tighter than yesterday, now that the storm feels real.
5. Danny keeps his expression neutral, aware that his planned mechanical-fault story will need to hold up once real urgency sets in.
6. The audience notes the tightening timeline is closing in on Danny's scheme before he's fully secured Marcus's escape.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Jonah walks the dock path again, drawn back to the shed despite telling himself yesterday's unease was nothing.
2. He crouches at the same patch of sand, confirming the footprints are still there, too fresh to be old.
3. Jonah starts to consider explanations beyond a drifter, though the idea of a hidden person doesn't yet occur to him.
4. He notices the shed's crates have been shifted slightly since he last looked, deepening his suspicion.
5. Jonah decides to go to the shed door directly and ask Danny about it rather than keep circling.
6. The audience feels the walls closing in on Marcus's hiding place through no fault of Danny's own carelessness.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Jonah reaches the shed door just as Danny rounds the corner from the opposite side, intercepting him.
2. Danny keeps his tone light, positioning himself between Jonah and the door without seeming to block it deliberately.
3. Inside, unseen, Marcus goes still at the sound of Jonah's voice, seconds from being exposed.
4. Danny steers Jonah away from the door with a question about the tavern's supply list, redirecting his attention.
5. Jonah goes along with it but privately notes how quickly and smoothly Danny moved to head him off.
6. The audience registers this as a near-miss on par with Sophie's near-question to Aldridge in the previous episode.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Kimi brings Mei Chen a mainland insurance form that requires written confirmation of the exact wording in Aiko Sato's old clinic file.
2. Kimi explains it's just a formality for the cross-check, not realizing whose file it originally concerns beyond her own mother's name.
3. Mei recognizes the phrase being requested as the very line she fabricated twenty-two years ago.
4. Mei keeps her voice even, telling Kimi it's simply standard archival language from that era.
5. Kimi accepts the explanation but notices Mei's hands pause slightly longer than the moment should require.
6. The audience watches Mei choose reassurance over honesty, widening the gap between what Kimi is told and what is true.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone after Kimi leaves, Mei pulls Aiko Sato's file again and rereads the exact phrase she wrote decades ago.
2. She traces the words with her finger, remembering the moment she chose them to hide the list swap.
3. Mei considers, briefly, what would happen if she simply told Kimi the truth now, then dismisses the thought.
4. She rehearses in her mind how she'll explain the wording again if the insurance company asks further questions.
5. The audience sees Mei's private fear sharpen: the paperwork trail she once thought was closed is now actively being reopened by Kimi herself.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Sophie finds Aldridge in an exam room gripping the edge of the table, knuckles white, waiting out a wave of pain.
2. She asks if his back has gotten worse, taking the cover story at face value.
3. Aldridge straightens too quickly, insisting it's just a strained muscle from lifting supply crates.
4. Sophie offers to get him a heating pad or cover his afternoon rounds, concern still framed around simple fatigue.
5. Aldridge declines, needing to keep moving so no one has time to look too closely.
6. The audience holds what Sophie doesn't know: this is cancer pain breaking through, not a strained back.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. At the Saltbox Tavern, the storm supply list on the chalkboard has grown longer overnight.
2. Talk turns again to evacuation logistics, and someone asks aloud whether Otis Bramwell will make the cut with only six seats.
3. Another islander mentions Otis's oxygen tanks take up space that could otherwise seat two people.
4. Jonah, still turning over the shed encounter, only half-listens until his grandfather's name catches his attention.
5. The room's casual math about who might not get a seat lands heavier on Jonah now that he's thinking about scarcity.
6. The audience feels the two collision courses converging: Danny's hidden seat-cutting plan and Otis's very public vulnerability.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Danny finds Jonah again before Jonah can return to the shed on his own, aiming to close the matter for good.
2. Danny offers a story that a drifter has been sleeping in the shed, someone he's meant to run off but hasn't gotten around to yet.
3. Jonah asks why Danny didn't mention it yesterday when he first asked about the footprints.
4. Danny covers smoothly, saying he didn't want to worry anyone until he'd confirmed it himself.
5. Jonah accepts the explanation on the surface but files away the inconsistency in timing without naming it as suspicion.
6. The audience sees Danny believe, wrongly, that this story has fully closed the matter with Jonah.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Kimi returns to her mother's house to finish boarding the last windows before the winds pick up further.
2. As she hammers shutters into place, she keeps replaying Mei Chen's oddly careful tone from earlier.
3. Kimi tells herself Mei is just stressed like everyone else this storm week, dismissing the feeling as nothing personal.
4. She glances again at the photograph of Aiko Sato, unaware the file she asked about is the one written to hide her mother's death.
5. The audience holds the dramatic irony that Kimi is one honest answer away from the truth she doesn't know she's circling.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Behind his locked office door, Aldridge waits until the building is quiet before opening a bottle he keeps hidden in a drawer.
2. He measures out medication carefully, timing the dose to get him through the rest of the evening's duties.
3. Aldridge catches his reflection and pauses, aware of how much thinner and grayer he's grown in six weeks.
4. He tells himself he can manage the pain privately for the rest of the week if he's careful about who sees what.
5. The audience is reminded this concealment is layered on top of his older, unspoken penance for the Marisol list.
6. Aldridge steadies his breathing and unlocks the door, resuming his ordinary evening rounds as if nothing happened.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. The clinic goes dark for the night as day two of the countdown closes with five days left until landfall.
2. Every thread from the day sits unresolved: Jonah's suspicion redirected but not erased, Mei's cover-up now under active pressure, Sophie's near-miss on Aldridge's illness, and Kimi's unknowing proximity to her mother's true story.
3. Offshore, Odette's outer bands continue rolling in, a visible sign the countdown is no longer abstract.
4. The audience is left holding all four secrets intact, each one now slightly more exposed than it was the day before.
5. The episode closes on the tightening math of six seats against a growing list of people who may need them.
