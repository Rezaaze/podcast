--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Maya surfaces from anesthesia into a recovery room, disoriented by beeping monitors and the ache of a fresh sternotomy.
2. She becomes aware of her own heartbeat before she is aware of anything else in the room, and it feels louder and steadier than any heartbeat she remembers having.
3. A wave of foreign calm settles over her fear, a stillness she does not recognize as her own emotional register.
4. She flexes her fingers experimentally and notices they move with a precision that feels borrowed rather than practiced.
5. The audience senses, through this wrongness, that something other than Maya's original body is now asserting itself, though Maya herself has no framework yet to name it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. A nurse checks Maya's vitals and praises how "strong" the new heart already sounds, then catches herself and changes the subject.
2. Maya asks directly where the heart came from, and the nurse recites a rehearsed line about donor confidentiality being standard procedure.
3. Maya, still believing this anonymization is routine and not specific to her case, accepts the answer even as something in her gut resists it.
4. The nurse's eyes flick to the door before she finishes speaking, a tell that the audience registers but Maya, groggy, does not.
5. Maya lets the questions drop for now, telling herself she can ask again once she is stronger, unaware the chart itself has already been altered to prevent that.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Daniel arrives at Maya's bedside with visible relief, having spent the surgery hours convinced he might lose her.
2. He takes her hand and talks about the future — home, recovery, ordinary days — entirely unaware of what now occupies her chest.
3. Maya smiles and reassures him she feels fine, already softening the truth of how strange her own body feels to her.
4. Daniel notices a half-second delay in how she looks at him, as if she is arriving in the moment a beat late, but dismisses it as post-surgery grogginess.
5. Neither of them names the small wrongness passing between them, planting the first seed of a distance that will grow through the episode.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. A storm has churned the harbor for days, and receding tide finally drags a body up against the pilings near the docks.
2. Dockworkers discover the corpse in heavy fog, the visibility so poor that at first they mistake it for debris.
3. The body bears distinctive marks — a specific tattoo and old scar pattern — that mark it unmistakably as belonging to one identifiable man.
4. The audience learns the body is Marcus Tan's, surfacing weeks after his actual death, though no one on scene yet has a name for it.
5. Police tape goes up as the workers back away, unsettled by how the corpse looks both recently dumped and strangely wrong for how long it must have been in the water.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Detective Calloway is called to the docks and crouches over the body, running a flashlight over the identifying marks.
2. Recognition hits him hard — he matches the tattoo and scar to a name he has carried in an unclosed file for years: Marcus Tan.
3. He hides from the responding officers just how personally invested he already is, keeping his voice clipped and procedural instead of showing his excitement.
4. He privately assumes this is fresh gang violence, a rival finally finishing what the underworld started long ago, misreading the body's age.
5. He pockets a detail from the scene — evidence of surgical incisions he doesn't yet understand — intending to look into it alone before anyone else claims the case.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Madam Chen sits for her evening tea as a lieutenant reports street rumors that the Quiet Man has been seen, or at least his name has resurfaced.
2. She keeps her composure iron-still, giving nothing away, though the audience can read the flicker of alarm beneath her calm.
3. She privately assumes the rumor must be a rival family staging a message using his reputation, not realizing the corpse just found is literally his remains.
4. She dismisses the lieutenant with instructions to quiet the talk before it spreads further through her people.
5. Alone, she allows herself one unguarded moment, revealing to the audience that she knows exactly why Marcus is dead and that she ordered it — a fact she will protect at any cost.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Sora Kim is summoned to Chen's side and told, in passing, that people are gossiping about Marcus Tan's name again.
2. Sora's professional mask holds for a beat too long, the pause itself betraying how much the name still costs her.
3. She asks flat, controlled questions about the rumor, giving Chen no outward sign of what she feels.
4. Alone afterward, her composure breaks — a private, sharp grief surfacing for a man she still believes simply vanished seven weeks ago.
5. The audience sees, in contrast to her performance in front of Chen, that Sora's love for Marcus never resolved into closure, only silence.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Left alone in recovery, Maya notices her hand resting on the blanket has gone unnaturally still, no tremor at all despite her exhaustion.
2. She watches her own fingers as if they belong to someone else, unsettled by the absence of the shakiness she expects after major surgery.
3. Before she consciously decides to move it, her hand shifts position on its own, settling into a loose, ready-looking curl.
4. A beat passes where she simply stares at her hand, afraid to name what she just witnessed even to herself.
5. She deliberately moves it again, slower this time, testing whether she imagined the first motion, and gets no clear answer.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. A doctor delivers Maya's discharge papers with brisk, reassuring language about her "excellent" recovery trajectory.
2. Maya skims the paperwork and notices the donor field is blank rather than simply redacted, a detail that strikes her as odd but not yet alarming.
3. She wrongly assumes this blankness is just how black-market anonymization works, not evidence that her chart was specifically scrubbed.
4. A staff member hovers a little too closely while she reads, visibly relieved when she asks no further questions.
5. She signs and leaves, unknowingly carrying home a body whose true history has been erased by people who know exactly what they hid.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Daniel insists on carrying Maya over the threshold of their home, a gesture equal parts romance and reassurance that she is safe now.
2. Maya laughs and lets him, performing an ease she doesn't fully feel as her body settles into the familiar space.
3. Daniel sets her down gently, holding on a beat longer than necessary, wanting to ask if she's really all right but choosing not to push.
4. He tells himself not to hover on her first night home, quietly suppressing his own unease rather than voicing it.
5. Maya notices his hesitation and, rather than reassuring him further, changes the subject — a small, first instance of the secrecy that will grow between them.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Daniel's extended family gathers at the Night Market to welcome Maya home with food, noise, and toasts to her recovery.
2. Maya smiles and accepts the attention, but finds the crowd and clatter oddly overwhelming in a way she can't explain.
3. An aunt comments that Maya looks "different — stronger, somehow," meaning it as a compliment; Maya forces a laugh and deflects.
4. Daniel watches this exchange and files away the comment, another small piece of evidence in the growing, wordless unease he isn't voicing.
5. Maya catches herself moving through the crowd with unfamiliar ease and confidence, a steadiness she quickly suppresses so no one else notices.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Back home, Maya reaches into the kitchen's knife drawer to help prepare dinner despite Daniel's protests that she should rest.
2. Her hand closes around a knife handle in a precise, practiced grip a fraction of a second before her conscious mind chooses how to hold it.
3. She freezes, staring at her own hand, aware that the motion felt learned rather than instinctive — and not a skill she has ever had.
4. Daniel, entering the kitchen, sees only his wife pausing oddly with a knife and asks if she's okay; she quickly recovers and brushes it off as tiredness.
5. The audience recognizes this as Marcus Tan's muscle memory surfacing through Maya's body, a fact neither Maya nor Daniel has any way to understand yet.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Late at night, two Clinic staff members work quietly in a back office, pulling up Maya's donor record on a terminal.
2. They delete and rewrite fields in the file, erasing any trace connecting the donor heart to its true source.
3. One of them expresses unease about how thoroughly they're covering the trail; the other insists it's necessary to protect the Clinic's entire black-market pipeline.
4. They discuss, in careful, guarded terms, that the body the heart came from was never supposed to resurface — a line that lands ominously given the corpse just found at the docks.
5. The scene closes on the record showing a clean, false donor history, sealing off the paper trail that might have led back to Marcus Tan.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Calloway returns to the precinct and pulls the dusty, years-old missing persons file on Marcus Tan from storage.
2. He lays the old file beside his new notes from the dock, matching details and confirming the identification to his own satisfaction.
3. He notices inconsistencies in the body's condition — the surgical marks, the wrong signs of decomposition for a "fresh" gang killing — that don't fit his theory, but he brushes past them for now.
4. A colleague suggests handing the reopened case to a fresher detective; Calloway hides how much he wants to keep it, downplaying his interest to avoid losing it.
5. He leaves the precinct that night having quietly claimed the case as his own, determined to close what he never could years ago.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Madam Chen summons her inner circle and demands answers about whose body was found on the docks in her territory.
2. Her lieutenants report only rumor and confusion, no one yet willing to say the name Marcus Tan aloud in front of her.
3. Chen presses harder, her anger a performance calibrated to look like outrage at a rival's incursion rather than fear of her own secret surfacing.
4. She reiterates her wrong belief aloud to her people — that this must be a message from an enemy family — reinforcing the misdirection even as she privately knows the truth.
5. The audience watches her thread a careful line between commanding authority and hiding the fact that she is the one who created this crisis.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Chen calls Sora into her office privately and orders her to find out, quietly, whether the body is really Marcus.
2. Sora keeps her voice level as she accepts the assignment, giving no hint of the personal stakes this investigation holds for her.
3. Chen watches her closely while giving the order, testing for any reaction, but Sora's control holds under the scrutiny.
4. Chen frames the task as protecting the organization's territory and reputation, never once suggesting her own role in Marcus's death.
5. Sora leaves the room already knowing, privately, that she will not be able to treat this as just another job.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. In the middle of the night, Maya lies awake beside a sleeping Daniel, listening to her own heartbeat in the quiet.
2. It sounds steady, strong, almost too controlled — nothing like the anxious flutter she remembers her own heart having before surgery.
3. She presses a hand to her chest, half expecting to feel something answer back, and is unsettled by how normal and yet how alien the sensation is.
4. She thinks of the blank donor field on her discharge papers and, for the first time, lets herself wonder if the silence around it means something more than procedure.
5. She says nothing to Daniel, choosing to carry this fear alone rather than risk sounding unstable so soon after surgery, closing the episode on her isolation with a heart that no longer feels entirely hers.
