--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Maya moves through her first full morning home, determined to treat the day like any ordinary one.
2. She notices the hum of the refrigerator, the drip of the tap, sounds she never registered this clearly before surgery.
3. A car alarm two streets over makes her flinch at a volume that shouldn't reach the apartment.
4. She tells herself this is just post-surgery hypervigilance, a nervous system still recalibrating.
5. Daniel, still asleep, doesn't see the moment she stands very still, listening to the whole building breathe.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. At the kitchen sink, Maya rinses a mug and shuts off the water in one continuous, economical motion.
2. She notices her wrist has turned at an angle that avoids splashing entirely, a small efficiency she never had to think about before.
3. She sets the mug down and deliberately fumbles the next one, testing whether the ease was a fluke.
4. The second motion is just as clean, no fumble at all, and it unsettles her more than the first.
5. She dries her hands slowly, watching them the way she'd watch a stranger's.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Reaching to steady a wobbling chair, Maya's mind flashes with an image that isn't hers: a narrow stairwell, a hand on a railing, tension coiled and ready.
2. The flash lasts less than a second, gone before she can hold onto any detail beyond the feeling of it.
3. She grips the chair edge, disoriented, unable to place the stairwell in any memory of her own life.
4. Marcus's presence recedes as quickly as it surfaced, keeping the rest of the memory — and whose stairwell it was — out of her reach.
5. Maya shakes her head and blames it on leftover anesthesia dreams, filing the image away rather than examining it.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. At the Night Market food counter, Maya moves between orders with a brisk, economical rhythm Daniel has never seen from her before.
2. She sidesteps a hot pan and a swinging door in the same motion, never looking down, never missing a step.
3. Daniel watches from across the stall, momentarily struck by how unfamiliar his own wife's body language looks in motion.
4. He tells himself she's just found her feet again after weeks of convalescence, a good sign rather than a strange one.
5. He adds the observation quietly to a private, growing list he hasn't named as a list yet.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Maya leaves the restaurant at the end of the lunch rush, intending to walk straight home.
2. The next clear thing she registers is her own front door, with no continuous memory of the streets between.
3. She checks her phone and finds far more time has passed than the walk should have taken.
4. Panic flickers, but she quickly reasons that she must have wandered slowly, tired, and simply lost track.
5. She decides not to mention the gap to Daniel, treating it as her own private confusion rather than anything worth alarming him with.
6. The audience is left with no more insight than Maya into where that hour went, only the growing pattern that these gaps are not accidents.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Daniel spots dried dirt caked on the side of Maya's shoe, inconsistent with a route that's all pavement and market stalls.
2. He notices her wincing slightly when she reaches for a high shelf, favoring one shoulder like it's been strained.
3. Neither detail matches the version of her afternoon she gave him at dinner.
4. He says nothing, choosing to watch rather than confront, telling himself it isn't the moment to make her feel accused.
5. He privately starts keeping track of these mismatches, a habit he doesn't admit to himself he's forming.
6. The audience recognizes this silence as the first deliberate brick in the wall being built between them.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Sora sits alone reviewing grainy dockside security footage as part of her ordinary intelligence sweep.
2. A figure crosses the frame for only a few frames, face obscured by shadow and distance.
3. Something about the way the figure shifts its weight before stepping stops her hand on the mouse.
4. She rewinds and watches the same three seconds again, then again, unable to look away from the balance in that stance.
5. She knows, with a certainty she can't yet justify on paper, that she is looking at Marcus.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Sora saves a private copy of the footage rather than logging it in the shared case file.
2. She considers, briefly, walking straight to Chen's office with what she's seen.
3. She stops herself, reasoning that raising Marcus's name on nothing but a feeling could look unstable, or worse, expose exactly how much she still cares.
4. She decides to verify it herself first, quietly, before risking anyone else's eyes on it.
5. She closes the file without telling a single person what she watched.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Late at night, two Clinic staff return to the same back-office terminal from before.
2. They locate a transplant log entry still linked, faintly, to an old shipment manifest.
3. One deletes the log line while the other reroutes the manifest to point to a fabricated, unrelated shipment.
4. They confirm to each other that no thread now connects the transplant record to its true point of origin.
5. One staffer mutters that a body surfacing at the docks makes this cleanup feel less like precaution and more like damage control.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. A junior nurse pulls up an old flagged inquiry about missing shipment paperwork from weeks earlier.
2. She notices the current cleanup overlaps suspiciously with that same shipment number.
3. She drafts a note to escalate the discrepancy to her supervisor.
4. She hesitates, remembering how quickly the last person who asked questions was reassigned to a different ward.
5. She deletes the draft and says nothing, letting the irregularity pass unflagged.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Detective Calloway submits a formal records request to the Clinic, citing the harbor body investigation.
2. A Clinic administrator responds within hours, denying the request and citing patient confidentiality without further explanation.
3. Calloway presses for a legal basis and receives only a form letter restating the denial.
4. He recognizes the response as unusually fast and unusually thin for a routine confidentiality claim.
5. He decides privately that he will find another way into those records, without yet deciding how far he's willing to go.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Madam Chen gathers her lieutenants again, her tea long gone cold on the table before her.
2. She demands they confirm, one way or another, whether the harbor body is truly Marcus Tan.
3. Her people report only that the tattoo and scar pattern match old descriptions, nothing conclusive enough to state outright.
4. Chen presses harder, her irritation calibrated to look like impatience with sloppy work rather than fear of the answer.
5. She dismisses them still without an answer, privately certain of the identity already and only performing the search for the benefit of the room.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Alone at night, Maya feels the now-familiar stillness settle over her, but this time it doesn't fade after a few seconds.
2. A clear, internal impulse rises in her, not a flash of image this time but something closer to direction: move toward the door, check the street below.
3. She resists it for a moment, frightened by how much it feels like being told rather than deciding.
4. She gives in partway, crossing to the window despite herself, and finds nothing there to explain the impulse.
5. Marcus's presence recedes once the small task is done, leaving Maya with no memory of why she felt compelled to do it, and no sense of whose purpose it served.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Daniel asks Maya directly why she got home so late from the restaurant.
2. Maya tells him she lost track of time and needed air after her shift, a story with no relationship to what actually happened.
3. She adds that she's just tired, using her exhaustion as cover for the blank hour rather than admitting she can't explain it.
4. Daniel accepts the answer on its surface, though it doesn't fully match the dirt and stiffness he noticed earlier.
5. Maya feels a small relief at not having frightened him, telling herself this omission protects him rather than deceives him.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Sora reports back to Chen as instructed, keeping her tone carefully neutral.
2. She says only that the rumor about Marcus might have "some substance" worth a closer look.
3. She omits any mention of the footage, the stance, or her private certainty of what she saw.
4. Chen studies her for a reaction, but Sora's control holds through the exchange.
5. Chen tells her to keep looking quietly, unaware she has just authorized Sora to pursue exactly the private investigation Sora already intended.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Sora returns to the dockside alley alone, off the clock and off any official log.
2. She positions herself where she can watch the same stretch of frame the camera caught, waiting for the figure to reappear.
3. Hours pass with nothing but ordinary foot traffic and shifting fog.
4. She tells herself this quiet, solitary watch is the responsible way to confirm the truth before anyone acts rashly on a rumor.
5. She leaves before dawn having seen nothing, but no less convinced of what she recognized on the tape.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Alone in the bathroom, Maya presses her palm flat against her sternum and holds still.
2. She feels the heartbeat underneath, steady and unhurried, indifferent to how unsteady she feels.
3. She asks it, silently, whether there is something else in there with her.
4. The beat continues exactly as before, giving her nothing back, no confirmation and no denial.
5. She lowers her hand and goes back to bed beside Daniel, carrying the question alone into another night.
