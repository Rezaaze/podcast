--- SCENE 1 ---
1. A syndicate enforcer is discovered at the docks at dawn, beaten with precise, targeted damage rather than random violence.
2. The injuries are concentrated on pressure points and joints, the mark of someone trained rather than enraged.
3. Low-level soldiers who find him recognize the method before they recognize the man, trading uneasy glances.
4. Word starts moving up the chain immediately, faster than anyone bothers to check the facts first.
5. No one at the scene yet says the name Marcus Tan aloud, but the shape of the thought is already there.
6. The audience recognizes the beating as the aftermath of Marcus acting through Maya, confirming the ghost is escalating from sightings to violence.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Sora arrives at the dock scene ahead of most of the chain of command, drawn by the first whispers.
2. She examines the enforcer's injuries with clinical focus, reading the damage like a signature rather than a crime.
3. She identifies the exact joint-lock technique as one Marcus taught her personally, one he never taught anyone else.
4. She keeps her reaction contained in front of the other soldiers present, giving nothing away on her face.
5. Internally, the certainty she's been suppressing since the rumors began hardens into something closer to conviction.
6. The audience sees her private notebook theory gain real, physical evidence for the first time.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. A flashback fragment shows Maya emerging from fog on the docks, disoriented, mid-step, as if waking mid-motion.
2. Her knuckles are freshly split and bleeding, the skin torn in a way that suggests recent impact against a hard target.
3. She has no memory of arriving here or of anything that happened in the preceding hour.
4. She looks down at her own hands as though they belong to someone else, uncertain and afraid.
5. The fog and disorientation prevent her from seeing anything that would tell her where she is or what just occurred.
6. The audience is shown, without explanation to Maya, the immediate physical aftermath of the beating from Scene 1.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Maya calls the school to say she's sick, her voice unsteady in a way she has to consciously control.
2. She hangs up and stares at her bruised, split knuckles, turning her hands over as if they might explain themselves.
3. She tries to reconstruct the missing hour and finds nothing, not even a fragment to hold onto.
4. A tremor runs through her hands that she can't fully attribute to nerves or to her recovery from surgery.
5. She hides the state of her hands from herself as much as from anyone else, refusing to name what she suspects.
6. The audience watches her fear outpace her ability to explain it, with no framework yet for what's actually happening to her.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Daniel notices Maya wearing long sleeves indoors, unusual enough that he clocks it immediately.
2. He catches a glimpse of her knuckles as she adjusts her cuff and asks directly what happened.
3. Maya hesitates just long enough for him to notice before answering that she fell.
4. She keeps her tone light and quick, hoping the casualness will discourage further questions.
5. Daniel doesn't challenge her outright, but his eyes linger on her hands a beat longer than the lie deserves.
6. The audience recognizes this as another layer added to the concealment already building between them.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone, Maya feels a settling sensation in her chest, unfamiliar and disturbingly calm.
2. Marcus's presence registers quiet satisfaction at what occurred on the docks, though Maya has no access to why.
3. She feels this foreign calm as an intrusion, something laid over her own anxiety rather than coming from it.
4. She tries to shake the sensation off, telling herself it's exhaustion or medication, and fails.
5. Marcus withholds any specific memory or intention from surfacing, leaving Maya with only the unsettling residue of his mood.
6. The audience understands this is Marcus's consciousness consolidating its hold, a detail Maya cannot perceive or name.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. A lieutenant informs Madam Chen about the enforcer's beating at the Teahouse, relaying it as urgent rather than mere gossip this time.
2. Chen's expression sharpens the moment she hears the technique described, though she keeps her voice level.
3. She demands specifics: which joints, what sequence, whether the man saw anything or anyone.
4. She learns the enforcer answers to Reyes, a detail that visibly unsettles her more than she lets on.
5. She gives no outward indication that she personally ordered Marcus's death two years ago.
6. The audience sees Chen's controlled urgency as the collision of two fears she can't voice: an impossible ghost and an exposed secret.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Chen and the lieutenant debate whether a rival crew is staging an impersonation to destabilize her territory.
2. The lieutenant argues for the simpler explanation, that someone is deliberately invoking the Quiet Man's legend as psychological warfare.
3. Chen pushes the theory further than she privately believes it, using it as the version she needs her people to accept.
4. She never voices the possibility that the ghost might be real, biological, or connected to her own hidden order.
5. She steers the conversation toward finding the impersonator rather than questioning the body she had disposed of.
6. The audience recognizes Chen is deliberately building a false narrative to protect a truth she can't afford anyone finding.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Sora requests to lead the hunt for whoever is behind the beating, framing it as professional initiative.
2. Chen mildly resists, suggesting someone with less personal history with Marcus should handle it.
3. Sora argues her familiarity with his methods makes her the only one equipped to actually catch a copycat.
4. She keeps her real motive, the personal grief and doubt driving her, completely hidden beneath procedural logic.
5. Chen relents, watching Sora a beat too long, as if she suspects there's more to the request than duty.
6. The audience senses Chen may be quietly testing Sora's loyalty as much as approving her competence.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Calloway reviews the updated forensic report on the harbor body at his desk, reading through surgical terminology.
2. The report confirms the organ removal was performed with clinical precision, not the damage of a street killing or dumping.
3. He flags the inconsistency between the body's presentation and every gang-violence case he's worked before.
4. He starts building a mental list of who in the city could perform unlicensed, high-skill surgical harvesting.
5. He keeps his growing certainty to himself for now, not yet ready to say the word "clinic" out loud to anyone.
6. The audience sees Calloway's cold case quietly shift from a homicide to something closer to a trafficking investigation.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Calloway meets an old informant in a low-key location, someone who owes him a favor from years back.
2. He describes the surgical precision of the wounds without revealing case specifics, testing what the informant might already know.
3. The informant grows visibly uncomfortable, clearly recognizing the description but reluctant to engage.
4. Calloway presses carefully, offering reassurance rather than pressure, aware that fear is what's stopping the man from talking.
5. The informant gives a vague acknowledgment that "people like that" exist in the city without naming anyone yet.
6. The audience senses Calloway is one conversation away from a name, though the informant isn't ready to give it here.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. At the Night Market, Daniel and Maya move through the crowd, trying for a normal evening together.
2. A stranger nearby raises a hand suddenly, gesturing to a vendor, nothing threatening in itself.
3. Maya flinches hard, her body bracing into a defensive stance before her conscious mind catches up.
4. She recovers quickly, laughing it off as being startled, but the motion was too precise to be simple surprise.
5. Daniel says nothing in the moment, but the reflex registers as wrong in a way he can't shake.
6. The audience sees Daniel filing this away as further evidence of someone, or something, he doesn't recognize in her.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Later at a food stall, Maya's hand closes around a knife left on the counter for slicing.
2. Her grip settles into a precise, practiced hold entirely unlike how she normally handles a kitchen knife.
3. She catches herself a beat later, staring at her own hand as if it moved without her permission.
4. She sets the knife down quickly, forcing a casual comment to cover the pause.
5. Daniel, a few steps away, doesn't see this moment, leaving Maya alone with the unease it creates.
6. The audience recognizes this as another small surfacing of Marcus's trained reflexes bleeding into Maya's body.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Sora follows Maya and Daniel through the Night Market from a careful distance, using surveillance discipline rather than instinct alone.
2. She isn't tailing Maya specifically, having picked up the trail through the same location patterns tied to the dock beating.
3. She watches Maya's earlier flinch and knife-grip moment from far enough away to see the shape of it without hearing anything.
4. Something about Maya's posture in that instant strikes Sora as familiar in a way she can't immediately place.
5. She doesn't yet connect the woman she's watching to Marcus directly, only that the stance nags at her.
6. The audience registers this as Sora's closest approach yet to the truth, still one inferential step away from Maya herself.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. After the market, Sora sits alone, replaying the stance she saw in her mind.
2. She tries to logically dismiss it, reminding herself that grief can manufacture patterns that aren't really there.
3. She compares what she saw to specific memories of training under Marcus, testing the resemblance against real recollection rather than feeling.
4. The comparison holds up uncomfortably well, refusing to be explained away as coincidence.
5. She doesn't yet consider a transplant or any biological explanation, only that something about this doesn't fit either death or a copycat.
6. The audience watches Sora inch toward the truth from an angle she has no framework yet to fully understand.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Calloway follows up with his informant again, offering a stronger guarantee of protection in exchange for a name.
2. Under that pressure, the informant finally gives up a specific place: an off-the-books recovery clinic known for asking no questions.
3. He warns Calloway that the people behind it are protected, though he claims not to know by whom specifically.
4. Calloway writes down the location without revealing how significant he privately believes this lead to be.
5. He decides not to log the full weight of the tip in his official report yet, keeping it below his captain's radar.
6. The audience sees Calloway's investigation arrive, unknowingly, at the doorstep of Madam Chen's Clinic.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Calloway parks in an unmarked position outside the Clinic as it approaches closing time.
2. He watches staff moving with brisk, deliberate care, more suited to erasing something than closing up for the night.
3. He notices boxes and files being carried out through a rear entrance rather than the front.
4. He resists the urge to approach directly, choosing to observe and document instead of confronting anyone yet.
5. He has no idea the Clinic is financially tied to Madam Chen or that Maya Lin's transplant happened inside these same walls.
6. The audience sees the Clinic thread and the syndicate thread converge visually for the first time, even though Calloway remains blind to the connection.
