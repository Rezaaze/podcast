--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Chen, in her private room at the Teahouse, decides she needs proof beyond rumor before committing men or resources to hunting a ghost.
2. She recalls an old recognition code from Marcus's earliest years under her, a phrase-and-response pattern known only to him and to her.
3. She reasons that no impersonator could reproduce it correctly, making it the cleanest test of authenticity available.
4. She frames the plan to herself as confirmation-seeking, not admitting even privately how much she wants it answered.
5. She calculates where and how to plant the phrase so it reaches the rumored sightings without exposing her hand.
6. The audience sees Chen constructing a trap whose real purpose, protecting her own secret, she conceals even in her own planning.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Sora, brought in to hear the plan, asks what happens to the woman if she simply doesn't answer the code at all.
2. She raises the possibility they might be terrorizing or trapping someone with no connection to Marcus whatsoever.
3. Chen's answer sidesteps the moral question, focusing only on the operational value of a clean result either way.
4. Sora frames her doubt as caution rather than sympathy, unwilling to reveal how much this assignment has already gotten under her skin.
5. The audience senses Sora's professional objection is a thin cover for a personal fear she hasn't named even to herself.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Chen waves off the concern, reminding Sora that caution has already cost them time while the ghost keeps moving.
2. She orders the phrase released quietly through two trusted low-level contacts who won't ask why.
3. She insists on deniability, choosing channels vague enough that the phrase's origin can't trace back to her personally.
4. She tells herself this is a controlled variable, not a gamble, refusing to weigh what it means if the answer comes back correct.
5. Sora logs the instructions without further argument, though her unease doesn't leave the room with her.
6. The audience watches Chen commit to a test she has already, secretly, decided she believes will succeed.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Two of Chen's men arrive at the Clinic in daylight, dressed unremarkably, their manner courteous and unhurried.
2. They speak to the surgeon in measured, almost solicitous tones, framing the visit as a routine check-in about discretion.
3. Beneath the pleasantries, they make clear, without raising their voices, exactly what continued silence is worth to him.
4. They mention no names and give no direct order, letting implication do the threatening for them.
5. The surgeon's forced calm during the visit conceals how thoroughly he understands what's actually being said.
6. The audience recognizes this as Chen's personal cleanup, though her name is never spoken in the room.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone after the men leave, the surgeon's composure breaks into visible, shaking urgency.
2. He pulls Maya Lin's surgical file specifically, along with several adjacent records, and begins destroying them by hand.
3. He works methodically despite his fear, aware that partial destruction would be worse than none at all.
4. He rationalizes the act to himself as protecting his own life, not participating in a murder, even as he understands otherwise.
5. He leaves no copy anywhere, unaware a warrant is already moving toward his door from an entirely different direction.
6. The audience sees the last physical evidence of Maya's transplant vanish just ahead of the law reaching for it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Calloway lays his assembled evidence in front of a judge, the financial trail and harbor-body forensics finally cohering into enough for a signature.
2. The judge questions him briefly on the shell-company angle, testing whether the case will hold up to a later challenge.
3. Calloway answers carefully, aware that overreaching now could sink the whole warrant on a technicality.
4. The signature lands, and relief hits him harder than he expected after weeks of working the case alone.
5. He allows himself a small, private sense that this cold case is finally about to move.
6. The audience knows this legitimate breakthrough is about to collide, by accident, with Chen's parallel effort to erase the same evidence.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Calloway briefs his partner on timing, insisting they move today rather than risk any further delay.
2. He flags his suspicion that the Clinic may already sense exposure, given how carefully its ownership has been hidden.
3. He avoids overstating his case, still framing this to his partner as a trafficking and record-fraud matter, nothing larger.
4. His partner asks a few practical questions about staffing and access, keeping the conversation procedural rather than speculative.
5. Calloway feels the case narrowing toward a name he still can't quite bring himself to say out loud.
6. The audience senses Calloway is one motion away from a scope of investigation he doesn't yet grasp.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. A quiet, unremarkable customer approaches Daniel's counter and orders in a tone indistinguishable from any other customer.
2. Woven into the ordinary phrasing of the order is the old recognition code, delivered so casually it could pass for nothing at all.
3. Maya, working near the counter, hears the words without any conscious sense that they mean anything.
4. Daniel notices nothing unusual about the exchange, absorbed in the routine rhythm of the shift.
5. The customer watches Maya's reaction for a beat too long before turning away, giving no other sign of purpose.
6. The audience recognizes the moment as Chen's test arriving exactly where it was aimed, unnoticed by everyone except the one person sent to plant it.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Something in Maya locks the instant the phrase lands, her body responding before her mind can intervene.
2. Words leave her mouth in the correct cadence and content, an answer she has no memory of learning or choosing to give.
3. For the length of one sentence, her voice carries a flatness and precision that isn't hers.
4. As quickly as it came, the moment passes, leaving her faintly dizzy and unsure what she just said or why.
5. She covers the lapse with a small, automatic customer-service smile, hoping it read as nothing.
6. The audience understands Marcus answered deliberately, choosing to be heard, while Maya remains unaware she was ever anything but herself in that moment.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Refilling a water glass a few feet away, Daniel catches the tail end of Maya's response out of the corner of his eye.
2. Her face, for that one sentence, is utterly still in a way he has never seen on her before, blank and cold rather than distracted.
3. By the time he looks directly at her, she's already smiling again, and the strangeness has nowhere obvious to attach itself.
4. He says nothing in the moment, unwilling to make a scene at the counter, but the image doesn't leave him.
5. He adds it, silently, to the private list he's already been keeping since the knife and the long sleeves.
6. The audience sees Daniel's suspicion sharpening from vague unease into something closer to a specific, unnamed dread.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. That night, Maya finds herself outside, walking with a clear sense of direction she never consciously chose.
2. She doesn't decide to go to the Docks so much as notice, partway there, that this is where her feet have been taking her.
3. She tells herself she needed air, layering a small, plausible reason over a pull she can't actually explain.
4. She doesn't examine why this specific place feels necessary, or consider that anything beyond her own restlessness might be steering her.
5. She arrives at the waterline with no memory of the walk itself, only the fact of having completed it.
6. The audience recognizes Marcus's pull toward Reyes's territory operating again, more efficient and less visible to Maya than before.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Sora is already positioned at the Docks per Chen's standing instructions, watching the location where the code was aimed to draw its answer.
2. She sees Maya approach and feels the same visceral recognition from the Night Market crowd, now impossible to dismiss as coincidence.
3. She reminds herself, deliberately, that her orders are to watch and confirm only, nothing more.
4. She registers a growing conflict between what her orders demand and what her body is telling her to do.
5. She stays in position, watching the trap she helped enable begin to close around a woman she still can't fully name as either target or victim.
6. The audience feels the convergence of the ghost thread and Sora's grief arriving at its sharpest point yet.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Chen's men, stationed nearby, move in around Maya at the waterline the moment she stops moving.
2. Their approach is coordinated and unhurried, treating her the way they'd treat a target already confirmed rather than merely suspected.
3. Maya freezes, disoriented, unable to explain to herself why she's here or why she's suddenly surrounded.
4. For a moment, her stillness and the men's certainty make the scene look, to any observer, like the answer everyone has been chasing.
5. Marcus registers the closing circle with interest rather than fear, recognizing it as movement toward Reyes rather than danger to himself.
6. The audience sees the trap succeed exactly as designed, at the direct expense of a woman who understands almost none of what's happening to her.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Sora steps out of the fog before the men can do more than surround Maya, placing her own body between them and their target.
2. She gives no real explanation for the interruption, relying on her standing and Chen's name to make the men hesitate.
3. She tells the men this one isn't confirmed yet, a claim that is both an order and, privately, a lie to buy time.
4. Marcus, denied the encounter he was moving toward, registers frustration at the interruption rather than any gratitude.
5. Maya, still disoriented, has no idea that the woman who just stopped the men saved her from something she never knew was happening.
6. The audience sees Sora choosing Maya, or perhaps Marcus, over her explicit orders for the first time.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Across town, Calloway's team arrives at the Clinic with the signed warrant, expecting resistance but not quite this.
2. They find staff already mid-scramble, drawers open and papers half-fed into a shredder still running.
3. Calloway reads the scene immediately as confirmation that someone knew he was coming, sharpening his suspicion into certainty.
4. He has no way to know the panic was triggered by an entirely separate visit from Chen's men, not by any leak from his own investigation.
5. He orders the shredder stopped and begins securing whatever fragments remain, aware most of what mattered may already be gone.
6. The audience sees Calloway's cold case and Chen's cover-up collide without either side recognizing the other's hand in the timing.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Maya makes it home still shaking, the walk back doing nothing to steady hands that won't stop trembling.
2. Daniel is waiting, already primed by the counter incident earlier, and reads her state instantly as more than ordinary stress.
3. He asks directly where she's been, and she finds she genuinely has no answer that isn't either a lie or an admission she can't make.
4. She offers a fragment of truth, that she's frightened, without any explanation attached, and it visibly isn't enough for him this time.
5. Neither of them says the words breaking point aloud, but both feel the conversation arriving somewhere new and unrecoverable.
6. The audience watches the marriage thread tighten toward crisis, driven entirely by what Maya still can't tell him.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Later, Sora stands outside the apartment building, having followed Maya home instead of reporting back to Chen first.
2. She tells herself she's only confirming the woman got home safely, a thin justification for a choice that's already gone past her orders.
3. She thinks of the wrist-grab, the gait, the code, and the exact stillness she saw at the waterline, and can no longer file any of it as procedure.
4. Something in her decides, without a fully formed plan, that watching from a distance is no longer enough.
5. She crosses the street and starts toward the door, choosing to close the distance she's maintained the entire investigation.
6. The audience is left uncertain whether Sora is approaching a victim to warn her, a ghost to confront, or a truth she isn't ready to survive.
