--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Maya sits alone and tries slow breathing and counting exercises, attempting to name five things she can see to anchor herself in her own body.
2. The unfamiliar calm from the previous episode's residue has not faded, instead settling deeper into her chest like a second heartbeat.
3. She presses a hand flat against her sternum, over the scar, as though she could physically hold something down.
4. The grounding technique works only partially, quieting her fear but not the sense of another presence waiting beneath it.
5. She tells herself this is anxiety and post-surgical stress, refusing to let the alternative fully form as a thought.
6. The audience sees her fighting a battle she has not yet correctly identified, using tools built for the wrong enemy.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Interior to Maya's body, Marcus registers her grounding attempt as a minor, almost fond irritation, like watching someone bail water from a sinking boat.
2. He tests the edges of her control deliberately, a small pressure against her hands and breath, gauging resistance rather than trying to break through fully.
3. He feels no urgency, certain that time and repetition are on his side and that each cycle costs her more than it costs him.
4. He withholds any image, memory, or intention that would let Maya identify him, keeping himself a sensation rather than a self to her.
5. His patience is framed as strategic rather than passive, a predator content to let the prey exhaust itself first.
6. The audience learns Marcus experiences these moments as a contest with a foregone conclusion, information Maya has no access to.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Daniel tidies the apartment, partly to feel useful and partly to avoid sitting with his own unease about Maya.
2. Reorganizing a drawer, his hand closes on the folding knife pushed far to the back, deliberately out of casual sight.
3. He turns it over, recognizing it isn't a kitchen tool and doesn't match anything either of them owns or uses.
4. He notices small details, dried residue in the hinge, wear on the grip, that suggest it has been handled, not just stored.
5. He sets it on the counter rather than putting it away, choosing to wait for Maya rather than confront the drawer itself.
6. The audience recognizes this as physical evidence surfacing from Maya's blackout violence, unknown to Daniel as anything but strange.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Daniel holds up the knife when Maya returns, watching her face closely before he says anything.
2. Maya's hesitation is brief but visible, a half-second where she calculates rather than simply reacts.
3. She claims she bought it after a break-in scare, describing an incident that never happened and was never reported to police or building security.
4. She adds small, specific details to the fabricated story, hoping specificity will read as truth.
5. Daniel notices the story arrived a beat too polished for something spontaneous, though he can't articulate why that bothers him.
6. The audience knows the entire account is invented, widening the gap between what Daniel is told and what actually occurred.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Daniel doesn't challenge the story outright, choosing to let the moment pass rather than force a fight tonight.
2. His decision to drop it is not belief but exhaustion, a conscious choice to postpone rather than resolve his doubt.
3. Maya reads his silence as acceptance and feels a brief, guilty relief that only deepens her isolation.
4. Something unspoken settles between them, a small crack neither names but both privately register.
5. Daniel files the moment away with the earlier long-sleeves incident, building a private, growing list of things that don't add up.
6. The audience sees trust eroding incrementally, driven entirely by Maya's protective lying rather than any actual betrayal.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Madam Chen summons Sora privately and assigns her to surveil Maya Lin specifically, not the dock rumors in general.
2. Chen frames the assignment as procedural, tied to the enforcer's beating, without explaining her deeper personal fear.
3. She does not reveal why she has focused on Maya Lin by name, leaving Sora to assume this is simply the next lead.
4. Sora accepts without visible surprise, though internally the assignment aligns unsettlingly with her own half-formed suspicions.
5. Chen watches Sora's reaction carefully, still quietly testing her loyalty as much as directing her work.
6. The audience understands Chen has connected Maya to the transplant in some instinctive way she isn't voicing even to herself outright.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Chen gives Sora precise, limited instructions: watch, confirm, and do not engage under any circumstance.
2. She emphasizes discretion, insisting no one else in the organization learns the true focus of this surveillance.
3. Sora asks what exactly she's meant to be confirming, and Chen deflects with a vague answer about the impersonation theory.
4. Chen's carefulness here is really about protecting her own secret, not standard operational caution, though she frames it as the latter.
5. Sora senses there's more beneath the instructions than Chen admits, but doesn't press further in the moment.
6. The audience recognizes Chen is using Sora as a controlled asset while withholding the one fact that would explain everything.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Sora begins tailing Maya during mundane daily errands, groceries, a pharmacy stop, nothing dramatic.
2. She tells herself repeatedly that this is only reconnaissance, professional distance maintained by habit and discipline.
3. She studies Maya's ordinary movements, cataloguing them against nothing in particular yet, just building a baseline.
4. Internally, she is also watching for herself, unwilling to admit she wants confirmation as much as Chen does.
5. Maya remains completely unaware she is being followed, going about her day inside a fragile, private normalcy.
6. The audience sees two threads of the story, the ghost investigation and the marriage, converging without either woman recognizing the other's full stakes yet.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. At the Night Market, Sora watches Maya move through the crowd from a discreet distance.
2. Something about Maya's gait catches Sora immediately, a rhythm and weight distribution she recognizes on a bodily, pre-verbal level.
3. Sora goes very still, gripped by a recognition that arrives faster than her conscious thought can process it.
4. She tells herself it's a coincidence, a trick of crowd movement and stress, even as her body reacts otherwise.
5. She does not yet connect this to any concept of transplant or survival, only that this stranger moves like someone she knew intimately.
6. The audience witnesses Sora's closest and most visceral brush yet with the truth, still one conceptual leap away from naming it.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Sora steps away from her surveillance position, needing distance to steady her breathing.
2. Grief she has been managing since Marcus's death surfaces suddenly and without her permission, sharper than she expected.
3. She grips something solid, a railing or wall, to physically ground herself against the wave of feeling.
4. She questions her own reliability as a witness, wondering if wanting Marcus alive is warping what she's actually seeing.
5. She forces herself back into professional composure before returning to her position, unwilling to let the moment show.
6. The audience sees the emotional cost of Sora's proximity to the truth, a cost Chen never accounted for in the assignment.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Detective Calloway digs deeper into the financial trail from the Clinic, following it through a shell company's filings.
2. He finds the ownership structure deliberately obscured, routed through entities that don't connect to any name he recognizes.
3. He recognizes the sophistication of the concealment as beyond a simple black-market operation, though he can't yet say what that implies.
4. He cross-references dates and incorporation records, slowly narrowing the field of who could plausibly be behind it.
5. He keeps hitting a wall just short of a name, aware he's close but not yet able to prove anything.
6. The audience sees Calloway's cold case inching toward Madam Chen's syndicate without his conscious awareness of the target.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Calloway starts assembling paperwork for a warrant, listing what evidence he has and what gaps remain.
2. He recognizes the current evidence is circumstantial, enough to raise suspicion but not enough to convince a judge.
3. He weighs whether to bring in his captain now or wait until the financial trail yields a name.
4. He decides to wait, wary of the case being reassigned or buried once its true scope becomes visible.
5. He documents everything meticulously anyway, protecting himself procedurally even while working mostly alone.
6. The audience senses the investigation is nearing a threshold where secrecy will no longer be sustainable.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Near the Docks, a stranger bumps Maya's shoulder in a crowd, an ordinary, harmless collision.
2. Before Maya's conscious mind registers the contact, her hand is already locked around the stranger's wrist.
3. The grip is controlled and specific, immobilizing rather than defensive, executed with trained precision.
4. Maya catches herself a beat too late, releasing the stranger and stammering an apology that doesn't match what her body just did.
5. The stranger reacts with confusion and mild alarm rather than recognition, having no idea what almost happened.
6. The audience sees Marcus's control extending further than before, no longer limited to full blackouts but bleeding into her waking reflexes.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Maya looks around and realizes she is at the Docks, with no memory of deciding to come here or of the walk itself.
2. She checks the time and realizes a gap has passed that she cannot account for, though shorter than her full blackouts.
3. Fear spikes as she recognizes this as different from before, not lost time but lost intention, her feet choosing a destination without her.
4. She tries to rationalize it as distraction or autopilot, a story that doesn't hold up against what she actually feels happened.
5. She doesn't yet consider that a specific place, or a specific person, might be pulling her here for a reason.
6. The audience recognizes this as Marcus's pull toward Reyes's territory, a detail entirely invisible to Maya herself.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Sora, tailing at a distance, watches the entire wrist-grab and release happen in real time.
2. The technique lands as unmistakable to her, not merely familiar but exact, a signature she could reproduce blind.
3. She takes an involuntary step out of the shadows, some instinct to approach or confront nearly overriding her discipline.
4. She stops herself just short of being seen, remembering Chen's explicit order not to engage.
5. She stands frozen for a moment, caught between years of training and the emotional force of what she just witnessed.
6. The audience sees Sora arrive at the threshold of the truth, restrained only by orders rather than by doubt.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Maya arrives home still shaking from the Docks incident, unable to fully hide her distress from Daniel.
2. Daniel, already unsettled since the knife discovery, reads her state as confirmation that something is deeply wrong.
3. He pushes harder than he ever has before, demanding a real answer instead of another quick, tidy explanation.
4. Maya tries to deflect with a partial truth, admitting she's scared without explaining why, but it isn't enough for him this time.
5. The argument escalates beyond their usual pattern, both of them raising the stakes past where they normally stop.
6. The audience senses this is the first time Daniel's suspicion has hardened into active confrontation rather than quiet observation.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Mid-argument, something shifts physically in Maya, her posture straightening and her expression going strangely still.
2. Marcus speaks through her, his cadence cold, measured, and utterly unlike anything Daniel has heard from his wife.
3. The words that come out are not Maya's phrasing or tone, carrying a certainty and detachment foreign to her.
4. Daniel freezes mid-sentence, the argument derailing entirely as he registers that he is no longer talking to Maya.
5. Maya has no awareness in this moment of what is happening or what is being said through her.
6. The audience, along with Daniel, hears Marcus directly for the first time, confirming the horror the story has been building toward all episode.
