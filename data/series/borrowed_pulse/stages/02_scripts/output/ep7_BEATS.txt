--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Chen summons Sora to report on what happened at the waterline the night before.
2. Sora tells Chen the target got away before the men could confirm anything, framing it as procedural failure rather than her own intervention.
3. Chen is furious at the lost opportunity and presses Sora for a firm yes-or-no on whether this is Marcus.
4. Sora deflects, saying she needs more time before she can give Chen a confirmed answer.
5. Chen orders Sora to get that confirmation personally and quickly, unaware Sora has already decided to handle this outside her orders.
6. The audience sees Chen's urgency sharpening into something closer to fear than command.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Maya wakes stiff and sore, finding fresh bruising on her wrists and forearms she can't account for.
2. She has no memory of the men closing in around her at the water's edge the night before.
3. She examines the marks in the bathroom mirror, quietly cataloguing them the way she's learned to do with every unexplained mark lately.
4. She tells herself, without believing it, that she must have bumped into something at the restaurant.
5. She hides her arms under long sleeves before Daniel wakes, continuing the pattern of covering rather than explaining.
6. The audience recognizes the bruises as the physical residue of the near-abduction, evidence Maya herself can't read.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. During a slow stretch at the restaurant, Daniel finally says out loud to his cousin what he's been carrying alone.
2. He describes the long sleeves, the blank moment at the counter, and the growing sense that Maya is hiding someone.
3. His cousin asks whether he's actually seen anything or just felt it, and Daniel admits it's only ever felt.
4. Daniel doesn't mention the forty-thousand-dollar payment to the broker, keeping that secret even from the one person he's confiding in.
5. His cousin suggests he just ask Maya directly, and Daniel admits he's more afraid of the answer than the silence.
6. The audience hears Daniel building a private case against Maya using only the pieces that fit an affair, blind to the pieces that don't.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Calloway reviews what his team salvaged from the Clinic's shredder, finding only fragments instead of full files.
2. One surviving fragment shows an unregistered cash transaction, its amount and date suspiciously specific.
3. He checks the fragment against the Clinic's official billing and confirms no listed procedure accounts for it.
4. He drafts a second warrant, this time aimed squarely at the Clinic's financial records rather than patient files.
5. The judge signs it quickly, treating the shredding itself as proof enough that something is being hidden.
6. Calloway still reads the case as organ trafficking and fraud, with no idea the cash trail leads to a private buyer rather than a criminal ring.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Sora positions herself alone at the container line near the docks, away from Chen's men and Chen's knowledge.
2. She's chosen this spot deliberately, close to where the phrase drew Maya out before.
3. She tells herself she's only trying to get closer to the truth before she reports anything, not admitting how personal this has become.
4. She checks a dormant recognition channel Marcus used years ago, one Chen's men wouldn't recognize or think to use.
5. The audience sees Sora quietly building a second, private investigation running parallel to the one Chen ordered.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Sora sends a message through the dormant channel using the exact rhythm and phrasing Marcus once used with contacts.
2. The message reaches a phone Maya doesn't remember acquiring, tucked at the bottom of her bag.
3. Something in Maya answers the pull before she consciously decides to move, already reaching for her coat.
4. She tells Daniel only that she needs air, a fragment of truth layered over what she can't explain even to herself.
5. Daniel, still holding what he saw at the counter and hasn't said, watches her go without stopping her this time.
6. The audience understands this departure is engineered, not restless wandering, and that Daniel's silence is its own kind of surveillance.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Sora steps into Maya's path between the containers, no fog or distance left between them this time.
2. Maya, disoriented and cornered, has no memory of how she got there or why this woman feels familiar.
3. Sora studies her closely, watching for anything that might disprove what she already suspects.
4. Maya tries to explain herself with half-true fragments, unable to offer anything that actually makes sense.
5. Sora doesn't identify herself or her connection to Marcus, keeping her own stake in this hidden from Maya.
6. The audience feels the two threads, Sora's grief and Maya's blackouts, finally standing face to face.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Under pressure, Maya's hand moves on its own, two fingers tapping once against her collarbone.
2. She has no idea what the gesture means or where it came from, barely registering she's done it.
3. Sora's composure breaks for the first time in the whole investigation, the private signal landing exactly as she feared.
4. Sora now has the confirmation Chen demanded, with no room left for coincidence or a con.
5. Maya notices Sora's reaction without understanding its cause, only that she's said or done something that mattered.
6. The audience alone understands that Marcus, not Maya, produced that gesture, and that Sora now knows it for certain.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Sora has every reason now to press further, but instead pulls back and ends the encounter before Maya can ask who she is.
2. She doesn't tell Maya she once loved the man whose reflexes just answered her.
3. She doesn't tell Maya that the order that got Marcus killed was a contract on her own life.
4. She reminds herself she still believes a rival faction killed Marcus, unaware that belief is protecting Chen more than it protects her.
5. She leaves Maya standing confused on the container line, choosing silence over the explanation Maya actually needs.
6. The audience sees Sora carrying two secrets now, one about Marcus's death and one about tonight, telling neither Chen nor Maya the truth.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The surgeon learns through a contact that police recovered fragments from his shredder rather than nothing at all.
2. He realizes the unregistered cash entry he thought was destroyed may have partially survived.
3. He weighs whether to warn Chen's people or try to handle the exposure quietly himself.
4. He decides against contacting Chen directly, afraid that reaching out now would mark him as a liability rather than an asset.
5. He begins quietly arranging to disappear rather than wait for whichever side reaches him first.
6. The audience sees the surgeon's fear curdling into a decision that will make him harder for either Calloway or Chen to control.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Calloway takes the surviving cash-transaction fragment to his partner, proposing they trace the payment through informal broker networks instead of bank records.
2. He starts pulling threads on unlicensed organ brokers operating in the same period as the falsified certificate.
3. He doesn't yet connect the payment to any specific buyer, only to the broker layer between buyer and Clinic.
4. His partner flags that this could take the case well beyond a single clinic if the broker has other clients.
5. Calloway feels the investigation widening past what he originally scoped it as, though he still doesn't know it leads to a private household near the harbor.
6. The audience recognizes Calloway is now one step closer to Daniel, without either of them knowing it yet.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Daniel finally acts on what he's been sitting on, retrieving the bloodstained jacket sleeve from where Maya buried it in the laundry.
2. He takes it to the lab contact he mentioned to no one, including his cousin, wanting hard proof before he says anything to Maya.
3. The test confirms what he already suspected, a blood type that isn't Maya's and isn't his.
4. He reads the result as proof of a man, an affair, a betrayal, the only story his fear can construct from the evidence.
5. He decides to confront her rather than wait for another quiet explanation he doesn't believe.
6. The audience understands Daniel now holds real evidence pointing at something violent and true, filtered entirely through the wrong conclusion.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Daniel corners Maya publicly rather than waiting for privacy, unable to hold the accusation in any longer.
2. He states plainly what the lab found, watching her face for the reaction that will confirm or break his theory.
3. Maya has no explanation that isn't either a lie or an admission of something she doesn't understand herself, and so she offers neither.
4. Onlookers register the scene as a domestic accusation, an affair confronted in public, with no sense of the stranger truth underneath it.
5. Maya's silence, meant to protect Daniel from something worse, instead reads to him as guilt confirmed.
6. The audience watches the marriage thread reach open crisis, built entirely on true evidence and a false interpretation of it.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Sora returns to Chen and confirms that the target responded to the recognition code, exactly as Chen hoped and feared.
2. She withholds any mention of the personal signal, the collarbone tap, framing her confirmation as procedural instead of certain.
3. She doesn't tell Chen she let the woman go a second time or that this has become personal, not just professional.
4. Chen, satisfied with confirmation alone, begins moving toward decisions about what happens next without knowing how much closer this has become for Sora.
5. Sora leaves the report deliberately incomplete, protecting her own investigation into who really killed Marcus.
6. The audience sees Sora managing Chen the same careful way Chen manages everyone else, using selective truth as a tool.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. In a private, unguarded moment, Maya's fear from the day's confrontations settles into something Marcus's fragment can register directly.
2. He recognizes the fear as useful, evidence that Sora's approach has rattled Maya enough to keep her moving toward the docks and away from safety.
3. He has no interest in comforting her, only in what her fear will make her do next.
4. He notes, with something like satisfaction, that Sora came alone and unarmed rather than with Chen's men.
5. The audience learns Marcus already suspects Sora broke from her orders tonight, information Maya herself doesn't have.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone at the harbor's edge after dark, Chen looks out at the water where Marcus's body was disposed of years ago.
2. She allows herself, briefly and only in private, to consider that this might genuinely be him rather than an impersonator.
3. She recalls the refusal that ended his usefulness to her, a memory she has never once revisited aloud.
4. She reminds herself the story everyone believes, a rival faction's hit, has held for years and only she knows it's false.
5. She decides the confirmation changes what she's willing to risk to end this cleanly.
6. The audience senses Chen's guilt and her ruthlessness converging into the same resolve.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Chen calls Sora back in, intending to issue the next order now that confirmation is in hand.
2. She stops short of naming the target directly, testing how far she can push Sora without saying the word kill.
3. Sora waits, giving Chen no help, forcing her to either say it plainly or back away from it.
4. Chen ultimately leaves the order implied rather than spoken, preserving her own deniability even with her most trusted lieutenant.
5. Sora leaves without confirming she'll carry it out, though Chen chooses to hear silence as compliance.
6. The audience closes the episode uncertain whether Sora's next move will serve Chen, Marcus, or Maya alone.
