--- SCENE 1 ---

2. She frames tonight's decision as protecting the whole operation, never mentioning Marcus's name or the heart.
3. She assigns one team to finish sweeping the Clinic's remaining records before Calloway's people can use them.
4. She assigns another matter, unnamed and understood only by Sora, to be handled separately and quietly.
5. Sora watches Chen carefully, noting how much effort Chen spends not naming the actual target.
6. The audience senses Chen managing her own inner circle exactly as carefully as she manages her enemies.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Maya rehearses what she'll say to Daniel, testing which parts of the truth she can survive telling him.
2. She still can't decide whether to give him the donor's identity or keep inventing reasons to delay.
3. She checks her arms and collarbone in the mirror, aware her body might do something she can't control in front of him.
4. She tells herself she's protecting him by managing what he learns, not lying to him outright.
5. She hears him moving in the next room and feels the conversation can no longer be postponed.

--- SCENE 3 ---

2. He assigns priority to securing financial records before anyone inside the Clinic can finish destroying them.
3. His partner points to the earlier shredding as proof the Clinic already expects them, urging speed.
4. Calloway sets a tight window for the raid, wanting to move before word can travel further.
5. He still frames the case as trafficking and fraud, with no sense that a private household sits at the end of this trail.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Sora updates Chen on the plan to intercept the Clinic's records before Calloway's raid.
2. She presents the operation as straightforward, giving no hint of her own hesitation about the larger order Chen wants from her.
3. She doesn't mention how unusually fast and total Chen's urgency to end this has become.
4. She notices herself withholding that observation deliberately, unsure yet what she'll do with the suspicion.
5. Chen, unaware of Sora's private doubt, treats the report as complete.
6. The audience sees Sora's loyalty to Chen thinning in real time, one omission at a time.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Chen's inner circle debates openly whether the target should be killed outright or brought in alive for questioning.
2. One lieutenant argues a living prisoner could explain how any of this is possible; Chen shuts the idea down fast.
3. Chen insists a clean ending matters more than answers, revealing more urgency than the situation seems to justify.
4. Sora stays silent through the debate, aware she's the one most likely to be asked to carry it out.
5. The room settles on elimination as the goal, though the order itself still isn't formally spoken.
6. The audience recognizes Chen choosing to erase the mystery rather than understand it, for reasons she isn't stating aloud.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Daniel lingers at the restaurant after close, choosing the empty space deliberately instead of home.
2. He rehearses how he'll raise the blood test without revealing he already has the results.
3. He decides to let Maya speak first, wanting to see how much she'll admit unprompted.
4. His cousin's advice from days ago, just ask her directly, sits unused beneath a plan built on strategy instead of honesty.
5. He hears Maya approaching before she says anything, and feels his resolve tighten.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Maya tells Daniel plainly that the transplant was illegal, bought outside the legal system.
2. Daniel absorbs this as confirmation of something, though not yet the something he actually suspects.
3. He asks who the donor was; Maya says she doesn't know enough to say, which isn't fully true.
4. He doesn't mention the blood test results, watching instead to see if she'll volunteer anything closer to what he already has proof of.
5. Maya reads his silence as acceptance rather than a test, believing the partial truth has bought her more room than it has.
6. The audience sees both of them holding evidence back in the same conversation, each mistaking the other's silence for resolution.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The surgeon moves through an ordinary morning routine, aware it may be his last one in this life.
2. He finalizes quiet arrangements to leave the city, careful not to alert anyone at the Clinic to his plan.
3. He avoids all contact with Chen's people, still afraid that reaching out would mark him as a liability rather than an asset.
4. He destroys a personal, off-book set of notes he never told the Clinic or Chen existed.
5. The audience sees him erasing his own trail in parallel with, but separate from, whatever Chen's people are doing.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Calloway's team moves on the Clinic simultaneously across its entrances, catching most staff still on site.
2. They secure the financial records area first, per Calloway's priority.
3. Staff scramble to explain incomplete answers about supply orders and patient files that don't match.
4. Calloway notices signs someone had already been through these same records recently, ahead of his team.
5. The raid produces real evidence, but also confirms his earlier fear that someone tipped the Clinic off.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The small team Chen sent to clear the last records reaches the Clinic just as Calloway's raid is already underway.
2. They pull back before making contact, unsure whether they're already too late to finish the job.
3. Sora's earlier stalling means they arrive with less of a head start than Chen believes they have.
4. The team reports back only that they couldn't get inside, without saying how close the timing actually was.
5. The audience understands Sora's small delay may have shaped this outcome more than anyone in the room realizes.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Sorting through what his team recovered, Calloway finds a set of files clearly pulled and reorganized recently.
2. The gap tells him someone accessed these exact records only shortly before he arrived.
3. He compares the timing to his earlier suspicion about a leak, now with physical evidence to support it.
4. He still can't place who did the pulling, only that it wasn't random and wasn't panic.
5. The audience recognizes this as the trace of Sora's aborted intercept, invisible to Calloway as anything but a professional job.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. In a quiet moment, the fragment inside Maya registers the day's converging pressures, Daniel's suspicion, Chen's urgency, the raid's aftermath, as one closing net.
2. He recognizes Chen moving faster than the danger alone would explain, sharpening his own sense that time is short.
3. He has no way to warn Maya directly, only the ability to nudge her body toward caution she doesn't consciously choose.
4. He weighs, coldly, whether protecting Sora is still possible before Chen closes this out.
5. The audience learns Marcus now believes he's nearly out of time, information Maya still doesn't have.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Chen calls Sora in alone, done with implying and hedging.
2. She states the order plainly this time, naming what she wants done rather than leaving it to be inferred.
3. She frames it as necessary after the raid, using the day's chaos as cover for the timing.
4. Sora receives it without visible reaction, giving Chen no read on what she actually intends to do.
5. Chen, satisfied by Sora's stillness, assumes compliance rather than hesitation.
6. The audience hears the order spoken aloud for the first time, with no ambiguity left for Sora to hide behind.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Daniel sits alone in the restaurant's back kitchen after closing, steam still rising from the last of the cleanup.
2. He replays Maya's confession, testing it against the blood test result he still hasn't shown her.
3. He admits to himself the partial truth she gave him isn't enough, whatever she believes it bought her.
4. He decides his next move will be forcing the rest of it out of her, not waiting for her to offer it.
5. The audience sees him choosing confrontation over patience, unaware of how much further from an affair the truth actually sits.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Alone after receiving Chen's order, Sora drafts a message meant to reach Maya through the same dormant channel.
2. She stops short of sending it, unsure if warning a stranger is worth what it would cost her standing with Chen.
3. She deletes the words instead of finishing them, telling herself there's still time to decide differently.
4. She doesn't admit to herself the real reason, that warning Maya means admitting how much this is about Marcus, not duty.
5. The audience sees Sora choosing paralysis over betrayal of either side, for now.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone at home, Maya feels a strange calm move through her that isn't hers, steadying her hands before she notices they were shaking.
2. For a moment she trusts the certainty completely, mistaking it for her own resolve returning.
3. She has no memory of deciding to plan for danger, yet finds herself checking the locks and noting exits.
4. She doesn't recognize this as anything but her own instincts sharpening under pressure.
5. The audience understands Marcus's certainty is steering her, not the reassurance she believes it to be.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Across the city, Calloway's investigation and Chen's syndicate now converge on the same handful of people without either side aware of the other's proximity.
2. Chen's order to Sora sits unconfirmed, its outcome resting entirely on a choice she hasn't made.
3. The surgeon has already begun to vanish, one step ahead of both the raid and the syndicate.
4. Maya moves through her apartment steadier than she has any right to feel, carried by a certainty that isn't her own.
5. Daniel prepares to demand the rest of a truth stranger than anything he's imagined.
6. The audience is left holding every thread pulled taut at once, waiting to see which one snaps first.
