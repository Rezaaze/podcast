--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Chen learns the surgeon has stopped answering contact attempts and reads it as confirmation he intends to talk.
2. She orders a small team to locate him before he can reach anyone outside the syndicate.
3. She tells the team only that he's a liability, without naming what he might expose.
4. She weighs privately whether the order should end in persuasion or something final, and doesn't specify which out loud.
5. She instructs the team to move quietly, wanting no trace back to her if it goes wrong.
6. The audience senses Chen closing every open door at once, the surgeon just one more loose thread among several.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The surgeon arrives at a modest unmarked meeting place, having chosen protection over vanishing entirely.
2. He carries nothing but the drive containing the notes he'd otherwise have destroyed for good.
3. He tells Calloway's team he'll give them everything he knows about the Clinic and the syndicate above it.
4. He asks for protection first and information second, unwilling to hand over leverage without a guarantee.
5. He has no way of knowing Chen's people are already searching the city for him.
6. The audience recognizes he's chosen the one path Chen didn't anticipate, arriving a step ahead of her order.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Maya comes back to herself standing somewhere she doesn't remember walking to, missing time she can't account for.
2. She finds small signs of preparation she doesn't remember making, a bag packed, a route already decided.
3. She recognizes the calm from before, the same borrowed steadiness, and understands it did this without her.
4. She realizes she's no longer just carrying Marcus's instincts, he's begun acting ahead of her, not just alongside her.
5. She has no memory of where he's decided they're going, only that it's already been decided.
6. The audience understands the timeline of tonight is no longer fully in Maya's hands.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Daniel watches Maya dress and move through the apartment with the same unfamiliar purpose he's seen building for weeks.
2. He shelves the confrontation he'd planned, deciding proof will serve him better than another partial answer.
3. He waits until she's out the door before grabbing his own keys, keeping enough distance to stay unseen.
4. He tells himself this is the only way left to actually understand what she won't say.
5. He has no idea what he's driving toward, only that it's finally something instead of nothing.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Sora receives the final details of Chen's order, a time and place instead of just a name.
2. She's told to make it look clean, no loose ends that could point back to the syndicate.
3. She says nothing of her earlier hesitation, giving Chen no reason to doubt the assignment will be carried out.
4. She packs for the meeting the way she's trained to, methodical, without letting herself think past the next hour.
5. She still hasn't decided, even now, whether she'll actually go through with it when she's standing in front of Maya.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Sora arrives at the meeting point ready to carry out Chen's order, finding Maya already waiting, unnervingly calm.
2. Partway through, Maya's posture and voice shift into something Sora recognizes on some level before she can name it.
3. Maya's hand rises to her collarbone in the old two-finger tap, the private signal only Sora and Marcus ever used.
4. Sora freezes, the weapon still in her hand, unable to reconcile what she's seeing with who she came here to kill.
5. Maya's voice steadies into something that isn't quite hers, referencing things only Marcus would know.
6. The audience watches Marcus finally claim the moment he's been steering Maya toward all along.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Marcus, through Maya, tells Sora that Chen's last order to him was a contract on her, not on anyone else.
2. He tells her he refused it, the one order in his career he wouldn't carry out.
3. He tells her he tried to get her out of the city instead, and that Chen answered the refusal within two days.
4. He tells her his death was staged to look like a rival faction's work, a lie Chen let stand for everyone, including her.
5. Sora hears, for the first time, that her survival cost him his life, not the other way around.
6. The audience sees the Vendetta thread's central secret spoken aloud, no longer buried in inference.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Sora lowers the weapon without deciding to, the order she came to carry out no longer making sense to finish.
2. She measures everything she's done for Chen since Marcus's death against what she's just learned it was built on.
3. She doesn't tell Maya or Marcus what she intends to do next, giving nothing away even now.
4. She recognizes that returning to Chen empty-handed will read as failure or, worse, as suspicion.
5. She leaves the meeting having made no promises to anyone, including herself.
6. The audience understands Sora's loyalty to Chen has just quietly ended, whatever she does next.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Chen's team reports back that the surgeon's home and known contacts turned up nothing, no trace of where he went.
2. She presses them on whether anyone official got to him first, and reads their uncertainty as a bad sign.
3. She realizes she has no way to know yet whether he's simply vanished or already talking to someone.
4. She recalculates the damage a cooperating witness could do to the parts of her operation not yet exposed.
5. The audience already knows what Chen doesn't, that he's a step further along than fled, and already speaking.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Calloway secures the surgeon's formal agreement to testify in exchange for protection, the deal now locked.
2. The surgeon lays out the falsified death record and the harvest procedure performed without consent or disclosure.
3. He confirms the unregistered cash transaction Calloway's team already flagged, tying it to an anonymous buyer match.
4. He gives Calloway the first solid thread connecting the Clinic directly to Chen's protection over the years.
5. He says nothing about any specific transplant recipient, having never been told or asked whose heart it was.
6. The audience sees Calloway's case close in on the syndicate itself, still with no sense that Maya sits anywhere near its center.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Maya comes back to herself standing across from Sora, the weapon lowered, neither of them having moved to leave.
2. She has no memory of most of what was just said, only fragments of a voice that used her mouth without her.
3. She realizes, piecing together Sora's stunned expression, that whatever happened here changed something between them.
4. She feels the strange calm draining out of her, leaving only exhaustion and fear of what she doesn't remember doing.
5. She understands, without being told, that Marcus got what he wanted tonight, and still doesn't know what that costs her.
6. The audience sees Maya left holding the aftermath of a conversation she never consciously had.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Daniel loses Maya's trail in traffic and doubles back toward home, hoping she'll have returned before him.
2. He finds the apartment dark and empty, exactly as it was when she left, confirming she isn't coming back for hours yet.
3. He notices the packed bag is gone along with her, a detail that unsettles him more than her absence alone.
4. He abandons the idea of waiting, deciding instead to retrace the direction she'd been driving before he lost her.
5. The audience watches his plan to simply observe curdle into something closer to pursuit.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Sora stands outside Chen's reach for the first time since receiving the order, the decision no longer deferred.
2. She weighs reporting the truth to Chen against protecting what's left of Marcus's last wish, delivered through Maya.
3. She decides not to return to Chen with a completed job, choosing silence over either confession or false success.
4. She resolves to let Chen believe the assignment is still in progress, buying time she hasn't yet decided how to use.
5. The audience sees Sora step, deliberately this time, onto the side of the people Chen wants erased.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Chen, still unaware the surgeon is already testifying, decides she can no longer wait on Sora's silence for an update.
2. She reads the lack of confirmation as delay, not refusal, and decides to send a second, more expendable team as backup.
3. She narrows her own instructions this time, wanting the matter closed tonight regardless of who carries it out.
4. She still frames this only to herself as protecting the operation, never once naming Marcus or what Maya actually is.
5. The audience sees Chen escalating on incomplete information, from every side, at the worst possible moment.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Daniel's car rolls to a stop near a stretch of waterfront he doesn't recognize, following the last direction he saw Maya's car turn.
2. He sits for a moment, uncertain whether to go further or turn back before he sees something he can't undo.
3. He gets out anyway, drawn by the sight of a parked car he's sure is hers, its driver's door still faintly warm.
4. He starts walking toward the nearest structure, no plan beyond finally seeing her face when she doesn't expect him.
5. The audience hears the second car door of the night, Chen's backup team arriving on the same stretch of waterfront.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Daniel steps into view just as Maya and Sora are still standing in the wreckage of what passed between them.
2. He sees no stranger, no evidence of an affair, only his wife looking at another woman like they've survived something together.
3. Maya turns and sees him there, and understands in that instant there's no version of this she can still manage or delay.
4. Sora reads Daniel's arrival as a complication neither Chen's team nor anyone else accounted for.
5. Nobody in the scene says enough to explain any of it to him, leaving Daniel standing inside a truth stranger than anything he suspected.
6. The audience watches the Marriage thread collide head-on with the Borrowed Heart and Vendetta threads, with no way left to keep them separate.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Tonight, a surgeon chose a witness stand over a vanishing act, and gave Calloway the first thread leading straight to Chen.
2. Tonight, Sora heard the truth she was sent to erase, and chose the people Chen wanted gone over the woman she's served for years.
3. Tonight, Marcus said what he died refusing to leave unsaid, and Maya paid for it with an hour of her life she'll never recover.
4. Tonight, Daniel followed the only trail he had, and found himself standing in the exact story he never let himself imagine.
5. Every thread that started separately now shares the same stretch of water, the same night, and no one left standing outside it.
