--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Low-level syndicate soldiers gather during a lull, trading rumors in hushed voices.
2. One claims he heard from a dockhand that the Quiet Man was spotted near the harbor.
3. Another mocks the idea, insisting dead men don't walk, while a third looks genuinely unsettled.
4. They agree, half-joking and half-serious, not to repeat the name where Chen's people might hear.
5. None of them know the rumor already reached higher ranks days ago through separate channels.
6. The group falls silent as a superior passes, the rumor unspoken but not forgotten.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Word of the soldiers' talk reaches Madam Chen through a lieutenant reporting it as gossip worth stamping out.
2. She calls a small gathering and flatly states that Marcus Tan is dead and the subject is closed.
3. She watches her people's faces as she says it, gauging who believes her and who doesn't.
4. No one challenges her aloud, but she can read the doubt in the room.
5. Once alone, her composure breaks slightly, her hand tightening on the edge of her desk.
6. Her private reaction reveals to the audience that her certainty about Marcus's death sits closer to guilt than authority.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Sora opens a blank notebook, not a case file, and begins writing what she actually believes rather than what she can prove.
2. She lists the footage, the stance, and the timing of the harbor rumors, keeping the page for herself alone.
3. She deliberately omits this ledger from any shared or official system.
4. She writes Marcus's name at the top, then hesitates before underlining it.
5. She locks the notebook away, telling herself this is due diligence rather than something more personal.
6. The audience senses she is investigating her own heart as much as the ghost.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Maya wakes before her alarm, disoriented, her knuckles aching in a way she doesn't recognize.
2. She lifts her hand and finds the skin bruised and slightly split, as if she struck something solid.
3. She has no memory of the hours between falling asleep and this moment.
4. She checks beside her and finds Daniel still sleeping, undisturbed, giving her no clue what happened.
5. She flexes her fingers, testing the pain, and feels a flicker of fear she immediately suppresses.
6. She hides her hand under the blanket rather than risk waking him with a question she can't answer herself.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Reaching for her own phone in her coat, Maya's fingers close around a second device she doesn't own.
2. She pulls it out and finds it warm, as though it's just been used, though she has no memory of using it.
3. She checks the pocket again, confused about how something unfamiliar ended up there.
4. She hears Daniel moving in the next room and instinctively closes her hand around the phone, hiding it from view.
5. She slips it into a drawer rather than asking him if he's seen it, already choosing concealment over explanation.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first physical evidence tying Maya's blackouts to a life she doesn't consciously control.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone, Maya examines the burner phone more carefully.
2. She finds no call history, no photos, no apps, only a single saved contact with no name attached.
3. She stares at the number, trying and failing to place it in anything she knows.
4. She considers calling it, then stops herself, afraid of what might answer.
5. She powers the phone down and hides it deeper in the drawer, beneath things Daniel never touches.
6. The audience is left, like Maya, with no idea whose number this is or why it matters.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. During a quiet moment at home, a tightness rises in Maya's chest that has nothing to do with her surgery.
2. It sharpens into something like a destination forming in her mind, an address she's certain she's never seen.
3. She tries to shake it off as nerves, but the pull only intensifies the longer she ignores it.
4. She finds herself already reaching for her coat before she's consciously decided to leave.
5. She tells Daniel she needs air, another half-truth layered onto the growing pile.
6. She steps outside already walking with more certainty than she should have toward a place she can't name.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Maya walks without consulting a map, her feet choosing turns before her mind catches up.
2. She stops in front of a nondescript building in a part of the city she's never had reason to visit.
3. She has no memory of deciding to come here, only the sense of having arrived somewhere important.
4. She searches her own life for a reason the place should mean anything to her, and finds none.
5. A wave of unease passes through her, the sense of being watched by her own body rather than by anyone else.
6. She lingers at the entrance, torn between the pull to go inside and a rising instinct to leave immediately.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Standing at the threshold, Maya's vision blurs for a moment with a feeling of familiarity that isn't hers.
2. Beneath her confusion, Marcus's presence registers the building instantly, the way a man recognizes his own front door.
3. He withholds the memory itself from surfacing, letting Maya feel only the pull and none of the meaning behind it.
4. Something in her hand moves toward a specific ledge before she consciously notices doing it.
5. The moment passes, and Maya pulls back, spooked enough to turn and walk away without going inside.
6. The audience now understands this location matters to Marcus specifically, even though Maya remains completely in the dark.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. At breakfast, Daniel notices Maya's knuckles as she reaches for her cup, the bruising impossible to miss in daylight.
2. He sets his own cup down and asks her plainly what happened to her hand.
3. This is the first time he's asked a direct question instead of quietly filing an observation away.
4. Maya freezes for a beat, caught without a prepared answer.
5. Daniel's tone stays gentle, but his eyes stay fixed on her, waiting for whatever she says next.
6. The audience feels the shift from passive watching to active confrontation, however mild.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Maya recovers quickly, inventing a story about slipping and catching herself against something hard.
2. She delivers the explanation smoothly, surprising even herself with how easily the lie comes.
3. Daniel studies her for a moment, clearly aware the story doesn't fully explain the pattern he's been tracking.
4. He chooses not to press further, telling her to be more careful and letting the subject drop.
5. Maya reads his silence as belief rather than as him quietly filing away one more inconsistency.
6. The audience understands, from his expression, that he believes her far less than she thinks.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Calloway walks the harbor district, showing dockworkers a photo from the body's recovery.
2. Most give him nothing, either genuine ignorance or practiced silence.
3. One older worker mentions offhand that people down here have been talking about someone who "shouldn't be walking around."
4. Calloway presses gently, careful not to spook the man into clamming up.
5. The worker grows vague and uncomfortable, clearly holding something back out of fear rather than ignorance.
6. Calloway leaves with the sense that the rumor mill knows more than the official case file does.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Further down the docks, a second worker gets more specific, describing a nickname used in the old organized crime cases.
2. He starts to say "the Quiet—" before catching himself and shutting his mouth.
3. Calloway asks him directly to finish the name, keeping his tone light rather than urgent.
4. The worker deflects, claiming he misspoke, and finds a reason to end the conversation.
5. Calloway lets him go but writes the fragment down anyway, unwilling to let it disappear.
6. The audience recognizes this as confirmation that the street-level rumor and Calloway's own case are converging on the same name.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Sora's private surveillance work leads her to cross-reference an old address once tied to Marcus.
2. She stakes it out from a distance, applying the same discipline she'd use on any official target.
3. She notices signs that someone has been near the entrance recently, a disturbed detail too fresh to be old.
4. She feels a private certainty tighten in her chest, the sense of closing in on something real rather than a rumor.
5. She approaches the building cautiously, unaware that Maya was here only a short time before.
6. The audience registers this as the first physical convergence between Sora's hunt and Maya's blackouts.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Sora reaches the entrance and finds a small trace, out of place, that only someone who knew Marcus would recognize.
2. She crouches to examine it more closely, her breath catching at the unmistakable specificity of it.
3. She realizes with a jolt that whoever left this was here only minutes ago, not hours.
4. She scans the street in both directions but finds no one, the trail already gone cold.
5. She pockets what she can and stands there a moment, shaken by how close she came to whatever this is.
6. The audience feels the near-miss acutely, aware that Sora and Maya are converging on the same truth from opposite directions.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. After Maya falls asleep, Daniel sits alone in the kitchen, turning the day's inconsistencies over in his mind.
2. He recalls the bruises, the dirt on her shoe, the blank hour, and now her explanation that didn't quite fit.
3. He briefly considers waking her and demanding the truth, then decides against it.
4. He tells himself that watching quietly will reveal more than confronting her would right now.
5. He doesn't yet let himself think the word affair directly, but the shape of the suspicion is forming underneath everything else.
6. The audience understands he is now actively building a case in his own mind, even if he doesn't call it that.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Later, in the dark, the burner phone hidden in the drawer buzzes once with an incoming message.
2. Neither Maya nor Daniel stirs, the sound swallowed by the quiet of the apartment.
3. The message is addressed, in effect, to a man who has been dead for weeks, sent by someone who doesn't yet know that.
4. The phone's screen glows briefly against the drawer's interior before going dark again.
5. The audience is given information neither Maya nor Daniel possesses: that whoever sent this believes Marcus is still alive and reachable.
6. The unanswered message sits waiting, a thread pulled taut between the dead man's old life and the woman now carrying pieces of it.
