--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Night demolition crew works under floodlights inside the gutted asylum, clearing a load-bearing wall marked for removal.
2. The foreman's crowbar catches on something that isn't structural — a hollow behind the plaster instead of brick.
3. He waves the crew back and works the gap wider by hand, uncovering a metal box sealed into the cavity.
4. Dust and light catch on something inside — a stack of photographs and paper, clearly hidden on purpose, not lost by accident.
5. The foreman hesitates, then reaches for his phone instead of his tools.
6. He photographs the find before touching it further, aware this isn't something he's supposed to handle alone.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mira gets the call from her demolition-crew contact before the site can be secured or cleared by the property crew.
2. She arrives fast, wanting eyes on the capsule before it gets reported up the chain and locked away.
3. She asks the foreman to hold the crew off a little longer, buying time without yet explaining why it matters this much.
4. She photographs the cavity and the box in place before anyone moves it, treating the scene like evidence rather than a curiosity.
5. She thanks the foreman but stops short of naming him as her source when she starts talking through next steps.
6. She leaves already reframing this as the season's story, not just a curiosity find.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mira and Ethan lay the sealed box open on the War Room table, cataloguing each item as it comes out.
2. They find a set of handwritten diaries and count fourteen Polaroids, each captioned with a first name and the word 'cured.'
3. They flip through the intake-style handwriting together, treating this as one more piece of institutional history to sort.
4. One Polaroid holds Ethan's attention a beat too long; he keeps his reaction small and moves on before Mira notices.
5. Mira starts grouping the Polaroids by handwriting and date, thinking aloud about how the discharges might map to hospital records.
6. Neither of them yet has any reason to doubt that 'cured' meant what it says.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Nurse Eleanor Voss begins a new diary entry after her overnight shift, describing something on the ward she wasn't supposed to notice.
2. She records a patient's file changing overnight in a way she can't explain through ordinary treatment.
3. She weighs raising it with someone above her, deciding the risk of telling the wrong person outweighs saying nothing yet.
4. She commits the observation to paper instead of conversation, treating the diary as the safer place to keep what she's seeing.
5. She closes the entry without naming what she suspects, only that she intends to keep watching.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mira and Ethan record a short announcement for the show's feed, framing the capsule as a promising new season opener.
2. Mira keeps the language general — a sealed box, old records, a mystery worth digging into — without overpromising what it means yet.
3. Ethan delivers his lines smoothly, giving no sign of the private reaction he had earlier in the War Room.
4. They agree on how much to reveal now versus save for later, treating this as ordinary editorial pacing.
5. The recording ends with both of them sounding like they're starting the same story together, with nothing yet unspoken between them.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. The team lays the fourteen Polaroids in a row and starts cross-referencing them against the asylum's intake ledger numbers.
2. They match names and case numbers one by one, building a working timeline of arrival and discharge for each patient.
3. Mira notices all fourteen discharges cluster inside the same six-month window in 1977 and flags it as worth double-checking.
4. Ethan helps build the same timeline while privately keeping track of where one particular photograph sits in the sequence.
5. The pattern reads, for now, as an administrative coincidence rather than anything coordinated.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Ethan waits until the War Room empties before pulling the one Polaroid back out from the stack.
2. He turns it over and studies the pencil initials on the back, initials he recognizes from a family document he saw years ago.
3. He confirms to himself, alone, that the handwriting matches his grandfather's.
4. He sits with the fact that his grandfather ran the very hospital this season's story is about, and says nothing to anyone.
5. He returns the photo to the stack exactly where he found it, giving no outward sign anything has changed.
6. He tells himself he only needs a little time to understand what this means before it becomes anyone else's problem.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Mira meets a retired local reporter who covered the asylum's closure decades ago, hoping for context the official record doesn't have.
2. He recalls the closure as sudden and oddly quiet for an institution that size, with little follow-up coverage at the time.
3. He mentions that questions about the discharged patients never really got answered, more forgotten than resolved.
4. Mira presses for names or leads he might still have, and he admits most of his old notes and contacts are long gone.
5. She leaves with atmosphere and confirmation of the silence around the closure, but no new hard facts.
6. The gap between how big this story feels and how little was ever reported sharpens her instinct that something was missed.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Eleanor writes a new entry describing how much more carefully she moves around the ward now.
2. She notes that Director Faulkner has started reviewing her paperwork more closely than before, though he gives no reason.
3. She writes plainly that she doesn't believe he knows what she's already seen and recorded.
4. She begins deliberately softening what she writes in her official notes, saving the fuller truth only for the diary.
5. She reminds herself, in her own hand, to be careful about who sees these pages from now on.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The team runs the fourteen names and known aliases through corporate and public filings, looking for any trace after 1977.
2. The first pass turns up nothing conclusive — no clean match, no obvious paper trail under any of the original names.
3. Mira notices a handful of near-misses and coincidences that don't prove anything but don't feel random either.
4. Ethan helps narrow the search criteria while quietly steering away from the entries closest to the photograph he's tracking privately.
5. The team agrees to keep digging next episode, logging what little pattern they have without yet naming a theory.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. The finished episode goes live on the show's feed, opening with the capsule discovery and the fourteen Polaroids.
2. Mira narrates the discovery and the six-month discharge window as the hook for the season.
3. The episode credits the demolition crew generally without naming Mira's contact directly.
4. Ethan's recorded segments play exactly as smooth and composed as they sounded in the booth.
5. Listener reaction arrives almost immediately, several comments asking what happened to the fourteen patients.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Ethan steps into the parking garage to call his mother, out of earshot of the rest of the team.
2. He asks a few general questions about her father, keeping his tone casual rather than urgent.
3. She answers the way she always has, repeating only that the hospital 'closed badly' without offering more.
4. Ethan doesn't mention the Polaroid, the initials, or the season's subject at all.
5. He ends the call having confirmed what he already suspected, no closer to deciding what to do with it.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mira goes back through the fourteen Polaroids a second time, slower than the first pass.
2. One face stops her — something about it feels familiar in a way that has nothing to do with the case file.
3. She can't place where she's seen it, only that it feels like someone from television or public life rather than a stranger from 1977.
4. She sets the photo apart from the others, marking it mentally as something to chase down later.
5. She says out loud that she wants to find out who this is, without connecting it yet to anything else in the room.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Mira and Ethan pull public discharge paperwork to verify the six-month window they flagged earlier.
2. The documents confirm the timing is real and unusually tight for fourteen unrelated patients.
3. Nothing on record explains why the discharges cluster the way they do.
4. Mira treats the tight window as the strongest lead of the episode so far, worth building the next episode around.
5. Ethan agrees out loud while privately watching how close the conversation is getting to the one photo he's protecting.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. As Mira reaches for the Polaroids again, Ethan quietly slides the one with his grandfather's initials toward the bottom of the stack.
2. He redirects her attention to two other patients whose discharge dates look just as promising to chase.
3. Mira follows his lead without noticing the small rearrangement, trusting his instincts as she always has.
4. Ethan tells himself he hasn't lied to her, only delayed something he still doesn't fully understand himself.
5. The audience can see what Mira can't: the photo she was drawn to is no longer anywhere near the top of the pile.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Eleanor writes her last entry of the night, describing a second, simpler set of notes she's begun keeping.
2. She explains to the page that these new notes are meant for someone outside the hospital, not anyone on staff.
3. She writes that she hasn't decided yet how or when she'll get them out, only that she intends to.
4. She hides the new pages separately from the main diary before ending the entry.
5. She allows herself, briefly, to believe she still has enough time before anyone realizes what she's collecting.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Mira tacks the unidentified Polaroid up on the War Room corkboard, separate from the other thirteen.
2. She tells Ethan she isn't going to let this one go until she knows who it is.
3. Ethan agrees with her out loud, giving no hint that he already knows more about that photo than anyone else in the room.
4. The episode ends with the fourteen names organized, one face still blank, and one secret in the room that only he's carrying.
5. The audience is left knowing the season's central deception is already in motion, even as the two leads believe they're working the same case together.
