--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Pike returns to his apartment and finds the door lock forced open.
2. He discovers one desk drawer emptied, papers scattered, nothing else in the apartment disturbed.
3. He immediately recognizes this as too selective and clean to be an ordinary burglary.
4. He connects it to the earlier warning break-in, reading this as the same method returning.
5. Panic sets in and he calls the podcast's line rather than the police, distrusting official channels.
6. He doesn't yet check whether his separately hidden personal notebook survived the search.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mira takes Pike's call and hears the fear in his voice before he finishes explaining.
2. Ethan listens in, his own private dread compounding on top of what he's already carrying.
3. Pike describes the forced lock and emptied drawer, certain the escalation is aimed at him specifically.
4. Mira decides immediately that Pike can't spend another night in that apartment.
5. Ethan agrees quickly, glad to focus on something concrete instead of his own secret.
6. They start arranging a safe location between just the two of them, telling no one else yet.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mira and Ethan drive Pike to a borrowed, off-grid location for the night.
2. Pike is visibly shaking, clutching a small bag he refused to leave behind.
3. Ethan keeps things practical, checking locks and windows instead of addressing Pike's fear directly.
4. Mira reassures Pike this is temporary, though she has no real timeline to offer him.
5. Pike thanks them without fully explaining why he trusts them enough to be seen this rattled.
6. They settle him in as night falls, agreeing someone should stay close by.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Marsh's assistant interrupts her schedule to announce an unscheduled visitor.
2. The assistant says the man wouldn't give his name but insisted Marsh would want to see him.
3. Marsh feels a flicker of dread before she's even confirmed who it is.
4. She clears her next several minutes and tells the assistant to hold all other calls.
5. She composes herself, sensing this isn't a routine constituent matter.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Victor enters Marsh's office alone, calm and unhurried in a way that unsettles her more than shouting would.
2. He invokes his firm's long history of "quiet help" to her family, framing continued cooperation as expected.
3. He states plainly what denouncing the podcast will cost her if she refuses.
4. Marsh pushes back, demanding to know what exactly he thinks he can hold over her.
5. Victor doesn't answer directly, letting the implication about her grandmother's past do the threatening for him.
6. Marsh realizes she doesn't know enough about her own family history to argue the threat away.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. After Victor leaves, Marsh sits alone with framed photographs spanning three generations of her family.
2. She lingers on a photo of her grandmother Diane, searching the image for something she can't name.
3. She recalls being told it was "impolite to ask" about Diane's life before 1974.
4. For the first time, that old family rule feels less like privacy and more like concealment.
5. She doesn't yet connect this unease to the genealogist's report she's been avoiding.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Once calmer, Pike produces a small notebook he'd kept separate from anything the intruders could find.
2. He explains it holds names of patients and staff he never entered into any official file.
3. Mira realizes this notebook is more valuable than anything that could have been in the emptied drawer.
4. Pike hesitates before handing it over, weighing risk against how far he's already committed.
5. Ethan watches the notebook change hands, aware it may contain the Director's name in Pike's own writing.
6. Pike admits there are things in it he's never spoken aloud, even to them.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Marsh's office releases a public statement questioning the podcast's credibility and motives.
2. The statement is measured but pointed, casting doubt without offering specifics.
3. It lands at the studio just as the team is already reeling from sponsor pressure.
4. The wording marks a clear shift from Marsh's earlier neutrality toward active distance.
5. Neither Mira nor Ethan yet knows what prompted Marsh's sudden change in position.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Mira and Ethan see the statement come in live, mid-broadcast, forcing an on-air reaction in real time.
2. Mira is visibly caught off guard, not expecting Marsh to turn against them directly.
3. Ethan keeps his on-air response measured, privately unsurprised given what he suspects about pressure campaigns.
4. Off air, Mira calls the statement a message rather than a coincidence, given everything else happening.
5. Ethan agrees aloud, still withholding what he actually knows about his own family's stake in it.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Back in the archive, the team pulls financial records tied to the Ashcroft estate.
2. They find a quiet annual disbursement to the Marsh family beginning in 1974 and running eleven years.
3. Mira lays the dates next to Diane Marsh's earliest public record and sees them align exactly.
4. She stops short of calling it proven, wanting one more corroborating document before airing it.
5. Ethan recognizes the trust's origin but says nothing that would connect it further to his own family.
6. They log the disbursement as the strongest lead yet on the Marsh connection.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Later, alone with Mira, Pike admits how frightened he actually is, dropping the calm front from earlier.
2. He confesses he's thought about walking away from the investigation entirely.
3. Mira gently asks why he's staying despite that fear.
4. Pike says some things matter more than his own safety, without fully explaining what.
5. Mira senses a personal history driving him that he still isn't ready to share.
6. She doesn't press, letting the confession stand as trust earned rather than information gained.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Alone in her office, Marsh rereads the genealogist's report she privately commissioned after the first Polaroid story aired.
2. She'd hoped, when she ordered it, that it would come back empty.
3. Instead she finds details she can no longer explain away as coincidence.
4. She still tells herself this might be isolated to her grandmother alone, not part of something larger.
5. She hides the report back in her desk, unwilling to share it with anyone, including her own staff.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. In a parking garage, a Victor operative meets a local contact for a handoff.
2. The contact confirms Pike's new location has already been identified and observed.
3. The operative relays that the client wants continued monitoring, nothing more yet.
4. The contact notes how quickly the target moved, unsettled Pike had somewhere prepared to go.
5. The operative shrugs it off, saying it changes nothing about the timeline.
6. They part without either naming Pike or explaining why he matters to whoever's paying.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. At the safe house, the team splits into shifts watching the windows through the night.
2. Small sounds outside put everyone on edge, though nothing materializes.
3. Mira catches herself checking the same window twice, aware she's more frightened than she's letting on.
4. Ethan volunteers for a late shift, using it as cover to be alone with his thoughts.
5. The strain of being actively hunted settles over the group, no one saying it outright.
6. Pike watches them take the danger seriously and seems steadied by it, if only slightly.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Marsh, disguised under a cap and coat, sits near the podcast team's table at a diner.
2. She watches Mira and Ethan talk, close enough to approach if she chooses to.
3. She almost stands and walks over, wanting to say something true for once.
4. Her nerve fails and she stays seated, telling herself it isn't the right moment.
5. She leaves without being recognized, the moment passing unused.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Back in the war room, Ethan and Mira weigh how to respond publicly to both Marsh's statement and the danger to Pike.
2. Mira wants to push forward with the Marsh connection despite the risk of provoking more pressure.
3. Ethan argues for caution, citing Pike's safety, though his real hesitation includes his own family's exposure.
4. Mira points out that caution hasn't protected anyone so far, only bought them time.
5. They agree to a partial approach, addressing Marsh's statement without yet naming the trust publicly.
6. Ethan feels the relief of another delay, aware he's using Pike's safety to justify what's really self-protection.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. At the safe house, Ethan steps outside alone to take a call from an unfamiliar number.
2. The caller references "the family arrangement," confirming someone from his grandfather's old network knows exactly who he is.
3. Ethan keeps his voice low and ends the call quickly rather than asking questions that might expose more.
4. When he returns, Mira notices his hands are shaking, more than the cold outside would explain.
5. He tells her it was nothing, a wrong number, before she can ask directly.
6. Mira lets it go, attributing it to the stress everyone's under, not suspecting a secret of his own.
