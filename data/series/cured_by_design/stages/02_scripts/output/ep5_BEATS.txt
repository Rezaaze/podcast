--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Mira tries to log into the podcast's shared social account and finds herself locked out.
2. Ethan checks two more accounts and finds the same lockout pattern.
3. Password reset requests bounce to recovery emails neither of them controls anymore.
4. Mira recognizes the coordination as too clean to be random hacking.
5. Ethan stays outwardly calm, privately certain this traces back to Victor.
6. They agree to move sensitive material off any cloud-linked account immediately.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mira and Ethan go live and address the lockouts directly to listeners.
2. Mira frames the attack as proof they're getting close to something someone wants buried.
3. A caller questions whether the show can be trusted if it can't secure its own accounts.
4. Ethan defends the investigation's integrity while avoiding any mention of Marsh's statement.
5. Mira feels the tone of the show shifting from revelation to survival.
6. Off air, she admits to Ethan that the account issue rattled her more than she let on.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mira works alone in the archive, cross-referencing the 1979 name-change registry against Eleanor's coded initials.
2. She hits a dead end when the county database returns a redacted entry.
3. She tries an alternate reading of the initials, still without a match she trusts.
4. Frustration mounts as she realizes she needs Pike's notebook to make sense of the code.
5. She texts Ethan for help but he doesn't respond right away.
6. She doesn't yet know the entry she's straining to unlock is his own family's.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Pike returns briefly to his apartment to collect belongings he left behind.
2. His phone rings from a blocked number moments after he steps inside.
3. The caller speaks in the same calm, rehearsed cadence as the earlier threatening call.
4. The voice references his notebook by implication, proving they know more than a simple break-in would explain.
5. Pike hangs up shaking, now certain the threat is following him, not just his home.
6. He doesn't call Mira right away, weighing whether telling her puts her at more risk.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Victor reviews a report confirming the account lockouts succeeded as planned.
2. An operative asks whether to escalate against Pike directly or continue surveillance only.
3. Victor authorizes a second, deeper breach targeting the team's cloud backups.
4. He instructs the operative to keep contact with Pike limited to psychological pressure for now.
5. He notes Ethan's usefulness privately, treating him as a resource still under control.
6. He closes the meeting confident the podcast can be contained without anything provably criminal.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Ethan slips away from the team under a thin pretext, meeting Victor in the same garage as before.
2. Victor presses him for an update on how much Mira has connected about the Ashford name.
3. Ethan downplays Mira's progress, though he knows she's closer than he's admitting.
4. Victor reminds him what exposure would cost his family, tightening the leverage.
5. Ethan agrees to keep steering the investigation away from the name, hating himself for it.
6. He rehearses an excuse for his absence before heading back inside.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Mira notices Ethan's absence stretched longer than the errand he claimed.
2. She asks him directly where he was, watching his face rather than just listening to his answer.
3. Ethan repeats the same thin excuse from before, adding an unnecessary detail to sell it.
4. Mira doesn't challenge him outright, but the inconsistency lodges itself in her memory.
5. She changes the subject, quietly adding the moment to the private notes she's been keeping.
6. Ethan reads her hesitation, wrongly, as the excuse having worked.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Pike meets Mira and Ethan at a public diner instead of anywhere traceable to the safe house.
2. He tells them about the phone call, though he doesn't repeat exactly what was implied about the notebook.
3. He says he's seriously considered disappearing and ending his cooperation altogether.
4. Mira tries to reassure him, but her own visible fear undercuts the reassurance.
5. Ethan stays quiet, aware that Pike stepping back could remove the one variable keeping his own secret contained a little longer.
6. Pike leaves without committing either way, unsettled and undecided.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Back at the archive, Mira finally gets the redacted registry entry unsealed through a records contact.
2. She finds the 1979 legal name change tied to one of Eleanor's coded initials.
3. She doesn't recognize the resulting surname or connect it to anyone she knows personally.
4. She flags it as the strongest lead yet and writes Ethan's name next to it as someone to loop in.
5. The audience recognizes, though Mira does not, that this is Ethan's own family record.
6. She sets the file aside to bring to the team in the morning.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The team debates whether to publish new material despite the ongoing account lockouts.
2. Mira argues that silence now would look like confirmation they have something to hide.
3. Ethan argues for delay, citing security, though his real fear is the registry lead surfacing publicly.
4. Mira overrules the hesitation, pointing out delay hasn't protected anyone so far.
5. They agree to air a segment referencing the registry find without naming the surname yet.
6. Ethan agrees on air, already calculating how much longer he can steer around his own name.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Victor reviews surveillance notes confirming Ethan met the deadline for containment so far.
2. An operative reports the podcast intends to air something new despite the lockouts.
3. Victor recalculates, deciding the leverage over Ethan needs tightening rather than releasing.
4. He privately weighs how much of his hold over Ethan is real evidence versus implication.
5. He decides to test Ethan's compliance with a more direct demand at the next contact.
6. He still believes Ethan remains fully controllable, misjudging how strained that control has become.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Mira and Ethan sit together during a lull, the silence heavier than usual.
2. Mira almost asks him outright about the missing hours, then holds back.
3. Ethan almost tells her the truth, then redirects the conversation to the investigation instead.
4. Neither says what they're actually thinking, the gap between them widening without a word spoken.
5. Mira notices the avoidance but attributes it to shared exhaustion rather than a specific secret.
6. The moment passes unresolved, both aware something went unsaid without naming what.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Pike returns to his apartment alone and begins packing a bag with essentials.
2. He hesitates over the notebook, considering destroying it rather than risking it falling into anyone's hands.
3. He writes a short note explaining nothing, meant only to say he's sorry.
4. He second-guesses leaving without telling Mira, then decides it's safer for her not to know his plan.
5. He sits with the bag by the door, unable to make himself leave.
6. The unresolved call from earlier hangs over every decision he makes now.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Mira discovers the cloud backup account has also been locked, deeper than the earlier breach.
2. Ethan checks the timestamp and privately recognizes it lines up with when he was in the garage with Victor.
3. Mira notes the timing coincidence aloud without realizing its true significance.
4. Ethan deflects, suggesting it's simply the same actors escalating on their own schedule.
5. The team realizes they may have lost material they hadn't backed up elsewhere.
6. Mira's urgency about publishing hardens further, now framed as a race against total silencing.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Victor calls Ethan back to the garage sooner than expected, breaking their usual spacing.
2. He tells Ethan the podcast's plan to air the registry lead is unacceptable to his clients.
3. Ethan protests that he's already done what he can without exposing himself directly.
4. Victor makes the demand explicit this time, asking Ethan to actively derail the segment.
5. Ethan refuses to answer immediately, the first real crack in his compliance Victor has seen.
6. Victor notices the hesitation and privately reconsiders how much longer Ethan can be relied on.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Mira finds Pike at the diner with his packed bag beside him, about to leave town.
2. She admits her own fear honestly, matching his instead of just offering reassurance.
3. Pike tells her about the call's implication regarding his notebook, finally saying it aloud.
4. Mira argues that vanishing now would bury the one piece of evidence that could protect him.
5. Pike weighs her argument against his exhaustion and fear, visibly torn.
6. He agrees to stay, at least for now, handing the decision back to her trust rather than his own certainty.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Back in the war room, Mira lays the account-breach timeline next to Ethan's unexplained absences.
2. She doesn't accuse him outright, but she watches his reaction closely as she states the facts plainly.
3. Ethan's answers come a beat too careful, too rehearsed, and she notices.
4. Mira decides not to confront him yet, wanting more before she risks being wrong.
5. She writes his name at the top of her private notes for the first time, no longer as a hypothetical.
6. Ethan senses the shift in her but still believes his cover is intact, unaware how close she's gotten.
