--- SCENE 1 ---
1. One day has passed since Adrian drank to steady himself before the upcoming supervised visit; it's now day fifteen of the season's timeline, and dawn finds him not in his own apartment but sprawled on the cold floor of the Cannery.
2. He wakes disoriented, with no memory of leaving his apartment last night or of anything that happened between then and now.
3. He notices dried blood crusted across his knuckles and palms — far more than any injury of his own could account for.
4. He checks himself over and finds no wound on his own body that would explain this much blood, and the absence unsettles him more than a wound would.
5. He has no memory of arriving here, of any struggle, of anything at all since leaving home the night before.
6. He forces himself upright, remembering the supervised visit with Mia is only hours away, and buries the dread to make himself move.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Back at his apartment, Adrian scrubs the dried blood from his hands and knuckles at the bathroom sink.
2. The water runs pink before finally running clear, and the sight makes him flinch harder than he expects.
3. He examines his shirt and finds it stained and torn in ways that don't match any fight he can actually recall.
4. He folds the shirt small and buries it in the locked drawer alongside the other hidden evidence from the Cannery night.
5. He tells himself again that reporting any of this would cost him Mia, and locks the drawer without a second look.
6. He says nothing to anyone, changing clothes and steadying his face to get through the day ahead.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. A colleague in the bullpen notices Adrian looks rough — pale, unsteady — and asks if he's alright.
2. Adrian brushes it off with a line about a bad night's sleep, giving nothing real away.
3. When Zhao approaches, he preemptively steers the conversation toward a casework update to head off any personal questions.
4. Zhao takes the redirect and shifts straight into task-force business, exactly as he intended.
5. He feels a flicker of relief at how easily the deflection worked, followed immediately by guilt at how practiced it's become.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone at her desk, Zhao works through the task force's current assignments and personnel one by one.
2. She calculates which cases or outside consults might eventually draw attention back to the Zenith Horizon-era files.
3. She decides to keep the unit's caseload centralized under her own sign-off, limiting who else touches sensitive material.
4. She reassures herself this reads as ordinary oversight to anyone watching from outside.
5. She has no idea her caution is converging with Kestler's own instinct to avoid scrutiny, for entirely different reasons.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. At home, Renata rehearses aloud what she plans to tell the court supervisor before today's visit.
2. She wants to sound precise about the two prior incidents without coming across as vindictive toward Adrian.
3. She feels pulled between protecting Mia and a lingering hope that today might prove her wrong about him.
4. Piper listens and offers small corrections and support, revealing nothing of her own preoccupations.
5. Renata leaves for the courthouse steadier than she feels, dreading what the visit might confirm about her brother.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. At her newsroom desk, Piper pulls up the Zenith Horizon city contract filings again, searching for anything she missed.
2. She cross-references vendor codes against department budget lines, inching closer without a clear name yet.
3. A message from Renata about today's court visit interrupts her, and guilt tightens at how normal her reply sounds.
4. She reminds herself the story and the relationship are separate, even as the two keep bleeding into each other.
5. She pushes the guilt down and goes back to the filings, aware her editor's deadline is closing in.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. In her lab, Voss reviews the latest readings from the hidden second seed emitter.
2. The signal shows another jump in strength, closer now to a threshold she privately considers critical.
3. She recalculates how much longer the receiving mind might hold before deep-sleep exposure could complete the transfer.
4. She briefly considers reporting the device and the risk to someone outside her own research, then talks herself out of it again.
5. She logs the reading privately, telling herself she'll act once she has more certainty, though the data already argues otherwise.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The supervised visit begins in a courthouse family room, a supervisor seated nearby with a clipboard.
2. Adrian tries easy, familiar small talk with Mia, working hard to seem steady and normal for her sake.
3. Mia responds warily at first, sensing something guarded in him she can't quite name.
4. The supervisor watches him closely, noting his slightly unsteady hands and the visible effort behind his calm.
5. Renata sits at the edge of the room, tense, hoping the visit passes without incident.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Mid-sentence with Mia, Adrian's voice suddenly shifts — flatter, colder, not quite his own cadence.
2. His posture stiffens unnaturally for a moment, as if someone else is briefly testing control of his body.
3. Mia flinches back, frightened, and the supervisor sits forward sharply, alarmed.
4. Adrian snaps back to his own voice and posture, disoriented and terrified at what just happened.
5. He has no memory of the seconds that just passed, only the aftermath of Mia's fear and the supervisor's alarm.
6. The audience recognizes this as Toll briefly asserting control in front of witnesses, the clearest sign yet of how strong his grip has grown.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The supervisor immediately documents the incident in writing, noting the voice change and Mia's frightened reaction.
2. Renata, shaken, pulls Adrian aside the moment the visit ends and demands to know what just happened.
3. Adrian has no memory of the moment she's describing and can only offer a stunned, inadequate denial.
4. Renata reads his blankness as further proof of denial and relapse, not confusion over something he genuinely doesn't remember.
5. The gap between what she believes and what actually happened widens, with neither of them able to see it.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At the Tidewater, Piper meets her off-record department contact again, pressing gently for more.
2. The contact, more relaxed this time, drops a specific name tied to old cold-case funding without meaning to reveal much.
3. Piper recognizes it as the first concrete thread she's had in weeks and files it away carefully.
4. She has no idea yet that the name will eventually trace back to Captain Zhao's arrangement.
5. She thanks the contact and leaves already turning the new name over, thinking about how to verify it quietly.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Word of the courthouse incident reaches the bullpen through department channels faster than Adrian expected.
2. Zhao hears it and immediately moves to contain the fallout within her own task force rather than let it reach Internal Affairs.
3. She frames it internally as protecting a valuable detective's personal privacy, not admitting it also protects her own arrangement.
4. She has a quiet word with the right people to keep the incident from being flagged upward.
5. Adrian remains unaware of how much cover Zhao is quietly providing him, for reasons that have nothing to do with loyalty to him.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Adrian visits Voss's lab under the pretext of a routine question tied to the Toll investigation.
2. He watches her closely while asking vague, general questions, testing for any reaction that might explain what's happening to him.
3. He deliberately avoids mentioning the blood, the blackouts, or the courthouse incident, unwilling to expose himself.
4. Voss answers carefully, giving nothing away about the second device or her suspicions about who the receiving mind might be.
5. Both leave the conversation having learned almost nothing concrete, each still guarding their own piece of the truth from the other.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Back at Renata's house, Piper holds her while she replays the courthouse incident, still shaken.
2. Renata voices her fear that Adrian is worse than she thought, sliding further from the man she used to know.
3. Piper offers comfort that is completely genuine, matching every bit of Renata's trust in the moment.
4. Underneath, her guilt sharpens at how fully Renata leans on her, unaware Piper is still sitting on information tied to the case.
5. Piper says nothing about the funding name from the Tidewater, choosing comfort over disclosure once again.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Later, drawn back to the Cannery without fully deciding to go, Adrian stands alone where the confrontation first happened.
2. Toll's voice surfaces inside his mind, cold and unmistakably amused this time, no longer just a flicker.
3. Toll references the courthouse moment obliquely, savoring it without explaining what actually happened.
4. Adrian demands answers, but Toll gives him nothing solid — only enough to confirm something is truly there.
5. The presence recedes deliberately, leaving Adrian shaken and finally unable to dismiss it as exhaustion.
6. The audience understands Toll is now strong enough to choose when he surfaces, not just intrude involuntarily.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone that night, Adrian retrieves the bloodied shirt from the locked drawer instead of burying it again.
2. He burns it piece by piece in the kitchen sink, watching the evidence disappear for good.
3. He tells himself this is the last loose thread, even though he still has no explanation for any of it.
4. He makes a deliberate decision never to report the blackouts, the blood, or the voice to anyone, no matter the cost.
5. He locks the now-empty drawer out of habit, alone with a truth he's chosen to bury instead of face.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Late in the newsroom, Piper cross-references the name from the Tidewater against old city contract filings.
2. She finds a redacted line item in a contract from roughly two years back, tied to a police unit she can't yet identify.
3. The redaction pattern matches exactly the kind of cover-up she's been building her story around.
4. Her stomach drops as she senses how close this line item might sit to something personal, though she can't yet confirm what.
5. She saves the document and sits back, aware she may finally be one step from an answer that could change everything.
