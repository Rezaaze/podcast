--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Two days have passed since Piper closed her laptop unable to decide her next move — it's now day nineteen of the season's timeline.
2. Zhao drops a new homicide file on Kestler's desk, pointing out wounds and staging that match Toll's signature exactly.
3. Kestler opens the file expecting a stranger's case and instead sees crime-scene photos that hit him with a wrongness he can't place.
4. He has no conscious memory of committing this killing, yet something in the photos feels uncomfortably familiar to his body, not just his eyes.
5. He masks the reaction, telling Zhao it looks like Toll's work and nothing more.
6. Zhao reads his stillness as focus on the case, unaware she's just handed him evidence of his own blackout.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Adrian wakes at dawn to find dried blood crusted along his shirt cuff, with no memory of how it got there.
2. He goes straight to the bathroom before checking his phone or the time, driven by instinct to erase the evidence first.
3. He scrubs the cuff alone at the sink, working the fabric harder than necessary to be sure nothing remains visible.
4. He avoids looking at his own face in the mirror while he does it.
5. He tells himself this is the last time, a lie he no longer half-believes even as he thinks it.
6. He dresses and leaves for the precinct without telling anyone what he found.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Renata spreads custody paperwork across the kitchen table, organizing dates, incident reports, and the lawyer's notes.
2. Piper sits beside her, sorting documents into folders and offering steady, practical help.
3. Every reassuring word Piper gives is quietly edited, stripped of the guilt sitting underneath it.
4. Renata thanks her for being so calm through all of this, unaware of what that calm is costing Piper to maintain.
5. Piper accepts the thanks without correction, choosing silence over the risk of unraveling anything today.
6. The paperwork moves the hearing one step closer to real, sealed by help built partly on concealment.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Voss checks the Cannery's remote monitor during a quiet stretch and sees a sudden, sharp spike in the hidden seed emitter's signal.
2. She reaches for her phone as if to call someone in the department, her hand actually hovering over the keypad.
3. She pictures what disclosure would require her to admit — the unauthorized device, the unauthorized trial — and stops.
4. She logs the spike in her private notes as routine interference instead of what she knows it actually is.
5. She closes the monitor without alerting anyone, choosing her research over whoever the reading now most likely threatens.
6. The audience recognizes this spike as tied to Kestler's most recent blackout, a connection Voss still hasn't made.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Piper returns to the "Unit 4" budget fragment she isolated days earlier, cross-checking it once more against precinct assignment records.
2. The numbers hold steady: the funding code traces to the same cold-case unit that operated out of Adrian's precinct two years ago.
3. She weighs typing Captain Zhao's name into her notes, since the timing and unit both point toward her, but stops short of writing it down as fact.
4. She stares at the file long enough that the cursor blinks unanswered on the screen.
5. She closes it without saving any new conclusion, unwilling to commit to a name she can't yet prove.
6. She says nothing about any of it to Renata, who is one room away.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Kestler sits with the new victim's crime-scene photos spread across his desk, working the case like any other.
2. He freezes on one image — a torn awning above the body — that snags something in him he can't immediately place.
3. A flash of sensation surfaces: fabric tearing under his own hand, though he has no context for when or where.
4. He sets the photo down and picks it back up twice, unable to shake the feeling that he was there.
5. He tells himself it's pattern recognition from years on the job, not memory.
6. The unease doesn't leave him even after he moves on to the next photo in the file.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. At the bar mid-drink, Kestler stares at his own hands resting on the counter.
2. TOLL's voice surfaces inside his mind, cold and almost pleased, telling him to look closer at his hands and the blood that was on them.
3. Kestler grips the glass, forcing himself to hold still rather than react visibly in public.
4. He demands silently to know what Toll means, but gets only amusement in return, no explanation.
5. The reference to blood lands as confirmation of something Kestler has been refusing to fully accept.
6. He leaves money on the bar and walks out early, rattled in a way he can't explain to anyone watching.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Zhao pulls Kestler aside and pushes him hard to close the new case quickly, citing clearance rates and department pressure.
2. She frames the urgency entirely as procedure, giving no hint of her real motive.
3. Underneath, she's protecting her own buried funding arrangement by keeping his unit small, contained, and unwatched.
4. Kestler agrees to move fast, relieved on a level he doesn't examine, since a quick close means less scrutiny of his own movements.
5. Neither realizes their reasons for wanting speed are pointed at entirely different secrets.
6. Zhao has no idea how literally close to home the case she's rushing already sits.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone in the dark that night, Adrian sits with the case file still open beside him.
2. TOLL surfaces mid-thought, his tone cold and amused, remarking that he was "out" again recently.
3. Toll makes clear he enjoyed it, offering the admission almost casually, without shame or hesitation.
4. Adrian goes cold, understanding for the first time that this isn't a glitch in his own mind but something taking pleasure in what it did.
5. He tries to press for more, but Toll gives him nothing further, content to let the admission sit and fester.
6. Adrian is left alone with certainty he didn't have an hour ago and no idea what to do with it.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Ahead of the hearing date, Adrian and Renata cross paths in the courthouse's marble hall on unrelated business.
2. Renata takes in his hollowed-out look and asks, careful and pointed, if he's sleeping at all.
3. Adrian deflects with a line about casework, giving nothing real away just as he has every time before.
4. Renata doesn't push further, but the exchange leaves her more convinced, not less, that something in him is deteriorating.
5. The distance between them widens without either naming what's actually driving it.
6. Adrian walks away carrying a secret heavier than the one Renata assumes he's hiding.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Back at his apartment, Adrian empties the locked drawer completely, laying its contents out on the floor.
2. He lines up blackout dates from memory against news clippings of unsolved incidents he's pulled together himself.
3. Two entries align in a way that can't be coincidence, forcing a single blurred fragment into sharper, worse focus.
4. What surfaces isn't just a memory of violence, but the unmistakable sense that his own hands did it deliberately, not accidentally.
5. He sits on the floor surrounded by the evidence of his own concealment, unable to look away from what it adds up to.
6. He says nothing to anyone, gathering it all back into the drawer with hands that won't quite stop shaking.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. At the precinct corkboard, Kestler charts his own known whereabouts and gaps against the new victim's estimated time of death.
2. He builds the timeline the way he would for any suspect, methodically, refusing to let himself flinch from it.
3. Every gap in his alibi lines up exactly with a blackout window he can't account for.
4. The pattern leaves no room left for doubt or alternate explanation.
5. He steps back from the board and understands, fully and without escape, that he is the killer he's been hunting.
6. He says nothing aloud and touches nothing on the board, standing frozen with the truth in front of him.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Alone that night, Kestler confronts the silence in his own head, demanding an answer he can no longer live without.
2. For the first time, TOLL speaks plainly rather than in fragments or taunts, confirming outright that the kill was his doing.
3. Toll names himself directly, stating there are now two wills sharing this one body, and that his is the one growing stronger.
4. He deliberately withholds any mention of Voss, the device, or the Cannery, protecting the source of his own foothold.
5. Kestler reels, torn between the horror of confirmation and the fact that he still has no idea how any of this is possible.
6. He resolves, in that moment, to tell no one — not Zhao, not Renata, not anyone — terrified of losing Mia or being taken off the case.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Piper meets her department contact at the Tidewater again, pressing with more confidence than before.
2. The contact, worn down by her persistence, confirms outright that Zenith Horizon money did reach a specific precinct unit.
3. He still won't give her a name, keeping the confirmation real but incomplete.
4. Piper leaves certain now that the unit in question circles back toward Zhao, though she still has nothing she could put in print.
5. She says nothing about any of it to Renata once she's home, sitting on the confirmation exactly as she has everything else.
6. She has no idea how close this puts her to the truth about her own girlfriend's brother.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. With Mia asleep upstairs, Renata rehearses her testimony aloud in the living room, working through what she plans to tell the judge.
2. She recounts the school incidents, the 911 call, and the courthouse episode as proof of Adrian's decline.
3. Piper listens and offers small corrections, helping her land the delivery cleanly.
4. Underneath, Piper's guilt sharpens further, aware she is helping build a case rooted in a cause Renata has entirely wrong.
5. She says nothing about the funding confirmation or the deception at the start of their relationship, choosing silence again.
6. Renata finishes the rehearsal reassured by Piper's support, unaware of everything sitting unspoken beside her.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone at night in her lab, Voss pulls up the day's spike data one more time before shutting down for the night.
2. The reading confirms what she already suspected: the signal is stronger and more frequent than any previous night.
3. She drafts, in her head, what a report to the department or to Kestler's unit might even say, then abandons the thought.
4. She weighs her research and her own exposure against a danger she still can't put a face to, and chooses herself again.
5. She locks the data away rather than warn Kestler or anyone else at the department.
6. She shuts off the monitor, leaving the threat active and unspoken for another night.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Kestler sits alone in his apartment with the full truth finally in front of him — the kill, the blackouts, and the second will inside him.
2. He turns over confessing to Zhao, but pictures immediate suspension, an investigation, and losing Mia in the same breath.
3. He turns over running, but recognizes it as a solution with no plan and no end.
4. He turns over fighting whatever Toll is, but has no idea what that would even mean without Voss's science he doesn't know exists.
5. He finds no answer that doesn't cost him everything he's trying to protect.
6. He sits with the truth unresolved as the episode ends, no closer to confession, flight, or resistance than when the night began.
