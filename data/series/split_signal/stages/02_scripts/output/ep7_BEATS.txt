--- SCENE 1 ---
1. One day has passed since Renata ordered Piper out of the house — it's now day twenty-one of the season's timeline, and Adrian sits on the floor before the locked drawer at dawn, unable to make himself open it.
2. He tries to reconstruct the blackout's blank hours and finds only fragments that don't connect into a timeline.
3. He dreads what new evidence the drawer might hold more than he dreads the blank hours themselves.
4. He has told no one — not Zhao, not Renata — about the confirmed kill or Toll's presence, and the isolation is starting to show in how long he sits frozen there.
5. He finally rises without opening the drawer, leaving the dread sealed inside along with whatever else is in there.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Toll surfaces unprompted while Adrian dresses, his tone almost casual now rather than testing.
2. He needles Adrian about the silence he's kept from everyone close to him, treating the secrecy as a private joke between them.
3. Toll remarks on how much steadier his hold on the body has become, an admission offered with open satisfaction rather than menace.
4. Adrian tries to shut him out by focusing on routine, but the presence lingers longer than it used to before receding.
5. He's left rattled by how comfortable Toll now sounds inside his own head, a familiarity that frightens him more than the earlier taunting did.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Renata drifts through her quiet house, pausing at Mia's toys left untouched since the custody stress began.
2. She stops at the fridge and rereads the hearing notice, the date now fixed and unmovable.
3. She opens her phone to a half-written message to Piper, rereading it several times.
4. She deletes the message without sending it, unwilling to reach out but unable to fully let go either.
5. She sits with the contradiction of missing Piper while still believing the confession was a calculated blow timed to the hearing.
6. She has no way to know the confession came from guilt, not strategy, and that Piper is home wrestling with the same silence.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Piper stares at a half-written Zenith Horizon story on her screen, cursor blinking over an unfinished paragraph.
2. She's already told Renata the truth about how the relationship began, but hasn't told her the specific funding lead pointing toward Zhao.
3. She weighs finishing and filing the story against what publishing it would do to Renata's family right before the hearing.
4. She recognizes that withholding the Zhao connection now protects Renata more than honesty would, a reversal of her usual guilt.
5. She closes the file without writing further, no closer to deciding whether silence is protection or just another version of the same lie.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone after hours, Zhao rereads the altered evidence logs line by line, checking each entry against what the audit is likely to examine.
2. She calculates how much further she can bend the record without creating a contradiction an auditor would catch.
3. She notices her own handwriting on one older entry looks inconsistent with the rest, a small detail that unsettles her disproportionately.
4. She considers stopping the alterations altogether and telling the truth, then rejects it as career and criminal suicide.
5. She locks the logs away again, aware the record is now more fragile than reassuring, no matter how carefully she tends it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Zhao corners Adrian about his exhaustion and the shifts he's missed, more out of her own nerves than real concern for him.
2. Adrian deflects with the same old excuse about sleep and case stress that's worked every time before.
3. Zhao, distracted by the audit and her own concealment, lets the deflection go without pressing further.
4. Neither registers that the other is hiding something with far higher stakes than tiredness.
5. Adrian feels the familiar relief of an easy lie landing, followed by the same practiced guilt as always.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Piper comes to the bullpen chasing an unrelated quote for a filler piece, keeping her visit brief and professional.
2. She passes Zhao in the hallway, exchanging only a polite nod between two strangers.
3. Neither recognizes how close their secrets already sit — Piper's funding lead and Zhao's falsified logs pointing at the same arrangement.
4. Piper gets her quote and leaves without any sense of how significant the captain she just passed actually is.
5. Zhao registers Piper only as an unfamiliar reporter and gives the encounter no further thought.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Adrian sits parked across the street from the Continuum Lab at midnight, engine off, watching the dark windows.
2. He rehearses in his head how he might ask Voss about memory loss and neural devices without exposing what he actually knows.
3. He can't find a version of the question that doesn't risk his job, his freedom, or Mia.
4. He has no idea Voss already suspects him, which would change everything about how she'd respond if he did knock.
5. He starts the car and drives away without approaching the building, the question still unasked.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone in her lab, Voss studies the hidden device's fluctuating signal data, watching the pattern strengthen night over night.
2. She cross-references the latest spike timing against what she now suspects about Adrian's visits and blackouts.
3. She opens a blank message addressed to her department oversight board and begins drafting a confession of the unauthorized device and trial.
4. She writes several sentences before deleting them, unable to commit to the professional and legal cost of sending it.
5. She closes the draft unsent, choosing to keep watching rather than act, even as her suspicion about Adrian hardens further.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. At the Tidewater, Adrian orders a soda instead of whiskey, gripping the glass like it might steady him anyway.
2. He fights the pull toward drinking himself into a blur, aware now that blurred hours are exactly what strengthens Toll.
3. He thinks of the courthouse incident and the kill Toll confessed to, using the fear of both to hold his resolve.
4. He's specifically terrified of deep sleep now, though he still doesn't know why it matters so much more than simple exhaustion.
5. He leaves the bar sober and shaking, having won a small battle he knows he'll have to fight again tomorrow.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Renata sits with her phone in hand and dials Piper's number, something in her needing to hear the line connect.
2. It rings once, and she pictures Piper's voice on the other end before she's ready to actually hear it.
3. She hangs up before the second ring, unable to decide if she's calling to fight or to forgive.
4. She sets the phone down, aware the impulse contradicts the certainty she's been telling herself she feels.
5. The moment passes unresolved, leaving her no closer to knowing what she wants than before she dialed.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. A colleague at the paper asks Piper if the Zenith Horizon story is close to finished, eager for it to run.
2. Piper lies smoothly and says it is, protecting herself from having to explain the real holdup.
3. Alone afterward, she quietly deletes the paragraphs that would name or clearly implicate Renata's family and the department unit tied to Adrian.
4. She tells herself she's preserving journalistic caution, though she knows the real reason is protecting Renata even now.
5. The story grows thinner and safer with each cut, further from what her editor actually wants.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. An Internal Affairs auditor sits down with Zhao and asks routine follow-up questions about the cold-case unit's evidence logs.
2. Zhao answers smoothly, her voice and manner giving away nothing of the alterations she made the night before.
3. The auditor notes her answers without visible suspicion and moves on to procedural closing questions.
4. After he leaves, Zhao's hands begin shaking, a delayed reaction she has to consciously stop.
5. She realizes how much effort it now takes to perform calm, a cost that's compounding with each new question.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Exhausted past reason, Adrian sits at his kitchen table fighting to stay awake, coffee gone cold beside him.
2. Toll's voice grows steadily closer and clearer as fatigue wears down Adrian's resistance.
3. Toll presses gently rather than taunting, sensing an opening in Adrian's exhaustion that outright mockery might close.
4. Adrian's eyes slip shut for a moment he doesn't register as sleep until he jerks upright, disoriented.
5. He checks the clock and can't tell if seconds or much longer passed, the uncertainty alone terrifying him.
6. He forces himself up and moving, unwilling to test how close that moment came to something worse.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Renata's lawyer calls to confirm the custody hearing date is locked and cannot be moved again.
2. She thanks him in a flat, controlled voice that doesn't match what she's actually feeling.
3. After hanging up, she sits alone in her car in the driveway, unable to make herself go inside yet.
4. She realizes the fixed date makes the fight with Adrian, and the rift with Piper, both irreversible in their own ways.
5. She stays in the car longer than she needs to, postponing the moment she has to face either.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Piper returns to Renata's house and leaves her spare key on the porch instead of knocking or ringing the bell.
2. She lingers a moment, unsure whether to hope Renata is watching or to hope she isn't.
3. Renata watches from the window, seeing Piper's hand hover near the door without following through.
4. Something in her wants to open the door and say something, but the memory of the confession holds her back.
5. Piper walks away without either of them speaking, the key on the porch becoming the only statement made.
6. Renata stays at the window after Piper is gone, no closer to resolving what she feels than before.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Adrian climbs the steps to Voss's lab in the rain, finally ready to ask the question he's rehearsed for days.
2. He raises his hand to knock, close enough now to hear movement inside.
3. At the last second, the fear of what confirmation would cost him overtakes the need to know.
4. He lowers his hand without knocking and turns back down the steps into the rain.
5. He walks away still carrying the unresolved truth alone, unaware Voss already suspects enough that the door might have opened everything.
