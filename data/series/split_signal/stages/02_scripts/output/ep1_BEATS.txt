--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It's day eleven of the season's timeline — since this is the first episode, we open already eleven days into events already in motion, with the incidents that will matter later (Mia's school reports, the neighbor's 911 call) already on record before tonight begins.
2. Zhao brings Kestler a fresh, solid lead pinpointing Toll's likely location at the Cannery.
3. Kestler pushes to move immediately and alone, arguing that backup will spook Toll or slow the approach.
4. Zhao reads his urgency as recklessness and insists on partnering him with backup before any approach.
5. Kestler appears to agree, then quietly leaves solo the moment she turns away.
6. The audience senses something more personal than procedure is driving his urgency, though neither character names it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Kestler finds Toll alone on the Cannery's upper gantry, weapon drawn, no backup with him.
2. Toll goads him, unafraid, hinting he already knows how tonight ends.
3. The confrontation turns physical, with Kestler landing a hit meant to end the standoff.
4. In the struggle, Toll triggers something concealed against his own body — a device the audience cannot yet identify.
5. Kestler feels a sudden, disorienting flash he has no way to process.
6. His memory simply cuts out mid-struggle, with no resolution shown to him or to the audience.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Kestler wakes on his own apartment floor at dawn, unsure how he got there.
2. He checks the time and realizes several hours are completely missing from memory.
3. His body is bruised and aching in ways that don't fully match what he recalls of the fight.
4. He finds his phone, keys, and weapon intact, which steadies him even as the blank hours unsettle him further.
5. He tells himself it's exhaustion and adrenaline crash, pushing down a deeper fear he isn't ready to name.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone in her lab before anyone else arrives, Voss checks the readout from the second, hidden seed emitter.
2. She finds an anomalous overnight signal spike logged automatically by the device.
3. She matches the spike's signature against her private data and confirms it belongs to Toll's neural pattern.
4. She realizes this means the bridge has already activated in a second living brain, not the outcome she expected.
5. She says nothing to her staff, filing the printout away instead of following protocol to report it.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Kestler pulls off his jacket and finds it torn in ways the gantry fight alone doesn't explain.
2. He checks his knuckles and finds bruising that looks fresher than a single confrontation should produce.
3. He weighs telling Zhao or logging the injuries, then decides against it, afraid of what scrutiny might uncover.
4. He locks the torn clothes in a drawer at his apartment, hiding the physical evidence from anyone who might see it.
5. He tells himself he's protecting the case and his job, not admitting the real fear underneath — losing Mia.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Kestler sits alone at the Tidewater, working through the fog of the missing hours over a drink.
2. He replays the confrontation with Toll in his head, trying to find the seam where memory cuts out.
3. He mutters the timeline aloud to himself, frustrated at the gap he can't fill.
4. For one cold moment, a reply comes that isn't his own thought — something answers back inside his head.
5. He goes rigid, telling himself he imagined it, unwilling to accept what he just felt.
6. The audience recognizes this as Toll's presence surfacing, though Kestler has no framework yet to understand it.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Zhao catches Kestler at the precinct looking hollowed-out and off his rhythm.
2. She presses him on why the Toll takedown failed and why he went in without backup.
3. Kestler gives a clipped account that leaves out the blackout and everything after the gantry.
4. Zhao notices the evasiveness but reads it as pride or burnout, not concealment of something larger.
5. He deflects, blaming exhaustion and case pressure, and she lets it go for now, unconvinced but unwilling to push harder yet.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Renata lays out the school's incident report and the recording of the neighbor's 911 call for Piper.
2. She walks through the pattern she's building — two incidents plus the call — as proof Adrian is drinking again.
3. Piper listens and supports her, privately carrying knowledge about her own motives that Renata doesn't have.
4. Renata decides tonight is the night she stops waiting and files for emergency custody of Mia.
5. Neither woman has any idea the real cause behind Adrian's behavior is something far outside anything Renata suspects.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Renata arrives at the courthouse with her documentation and files the emergency custody petition.
2. The clerk reviews the incident report and the 911 recording as part of intake.
3. The clerk confirms the petition qualifies for expedited review given the safety concerns cited.
4. An official hearing date is set within the month, making the custody fight formally real.
5. Renata leaves with the paperwork in hand, the abstract threat now a concrete date on a calendar.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Renata comes home and tells Piper the hearing date is officially set.
2. She feels relief at finally acting mixed with fear over what the fight will cost her and Adrian's relationship.
3. Piper reassures her, playing the supportive partner even as hidden guilt tightens underneath.
4. Piper's comfort is genuine in feeling but sits on top of a motive Renata still knows nothing about.
5. The two settle in for the night, Renata unaware of the concealment sitting right beside her.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Piper's editor confronts her, impatient with how long the Zenith Horizon story has stalled.
2. He sets a hard deadline: real, verifiable proof on the foundation's city contracts by the end of the week.
3. He warns that without it, the story gets killed outright and the resources pulled.
4. Piper feels professional pressure collide with her personal situation — a story that started as a means to an end now tangled with real love.
5. She commits to pushing harder for the lead, not yet knowing how close to home the answer actually sits.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Piper meets her department contact at the Tidewater, working the angle carefully so it doesn't look like digging.
2. She asks pointed but casual questions about which unit or officer might be tied to Zenith Horizon funding.
3. The contact stays cagey, offering only vague confirmation that some funding arrangement exists somewhere in the department.
4. Piper leaves without a name or unit, only a stronger sense that the trail is real and close.
5. She has no idea the contact's vagueness is protecting the very unit connected to Renata's brother.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Piper sits alone at her desk late at night with her Zenith Horizon research file open in front of her.
2. She looks back over her earliest notes, from when she first targeted Renata as a way into a police department source.
3. She weighs the guilt of that origin against how real her feelings have become since.
4. She decides, privately and firmly, that she will never tell Renata how the relationship actually began.
5. She closes the file, choosing to protect the relationship by burying the truth rather than risking it.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Alone after hours, Zhao pulls the old evidence logs tied to the Zenith Horizon arrangement from two years ago.
2. She checks that the entries still read as 'lab consult, no material transfer,' exactly as she falsified them.
3. She cross-checks against the original case files to make sure nothing has been flagged or questioned since.
4. The paperwork holds, but she feels a thinness to it she didn't expect, more exposed than reassured.
5. She locks the logs away again, unsettled by how close a closer look might come to unraveling it.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Later, alone again, Voss sits with the confirmed spike data from the second seed emitter.
2. She considers reporting the reading to the department or to whoever is handling Toll's case.
3. She calculates what disclosure would cost her — the unauthorized device, the unauthorized trial, possible prosecution.
4. She weighs that cost against the fact that an unnamed second person's mind is now at risk because of her technology.
5. She chooses to lock the data away instead, protecting her research and herself over the danger to whoever is now carrying Toll's presence.
6. The audience understands the stakes of this choice far more clearly than Voss does, since she doesn't yet know it's Kestler.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Renata calls Adrian to tell him the custody hearing date has been set.
2. She braces for anger or panic, but his reaction is muted, almost mechanical.
3. Adrian insists he's fine and that they'll sort it out, his voice flat in a way that doesn't match the stakes.
4. Renata presses gently, asking if he's okay, and he shuts the question down too quickly.
5. She hangs up uneasy, sensing something off in him that has nothing to do with the custody news itself.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Alone in his apartment at night, Adrian stares at the locked drawer holding the torn clothes.
2. He replays the excuse he's built for himself — exhaustion, adrenaline, a rough fight — and finds it doesn't fully cover the gap.
3. A physical unease sits with him, a sense that someone else moved through those missing hours, not just his own tired body.
4. He has none of Voss's science or Toll's confirmation, only an instinct he can't name and won't voice to anyone.
5. He leaves the drawer locked and the feeling unresolved, ending the episode still alone with a truth he can't yet face.
