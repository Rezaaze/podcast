--- SCENE 1 ---
1. One day has passed since Adrian turned back from Voss's rain-soaked doorstep without knocking — it's now day twenty-two of the season's timeline.
2. An Internal Affairs investigator arrives unannounced at Zhao's office with a subpoena demanding the cold-case unit's original, unaltered evidence logs.
3. He offers no explanation for the timing or scope, leaving her unable to gauge how much is already known.
4. She realizes the subpoena's specificity means someone has already flagged discrepancies, not just ordered a routine review.
5. She agrees to comply outwardly, giving nothing away, while privately understanding she has no way left to intercept or redirect this quietly.
6. She sits alone after he leaves, recognizing for the first time that two years of careful management may be ending today.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Adrian finally opens the locked drawer and lays every hidden fragment — torn clothing, notes, the aligned blackout dates — across his apartment floor.
2. Seeing the full pattern assembled at once pushes him past the point of believing he can keep handling this alone.
3. He calls Voss and abandons the careful, deflective questions he's used every visit before.
4. He tells her plainly that a second presence lives inside him and has already killed, and demands she explain how that's possible.
5. Voss, confronted directly rather than probed obliquely, hears confirmation of a suspicion she's been sitting on privately for days.
6. She agrees to meet him at the lab immediately rather than deny or deflect any further.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Adrian arrives at the lab and lays out everything he knows — the blackouts, the voice, the confirmed kill — refusing to leave without answers.
2. Cornered, Voss admits she built a consciousness-transfer device and that Marcus Toll obtained an unauthorized prototype from her foundation before the Cannery confrontation.
3. She admits a second, fully functional prototype exists, one she has kept hidden from the department to protect herself and her research.
4. She reveals its location — hidden at the Cannery near Toll's still-comatose body — and warns that deep sleep within its range would complete the transfer and erase him permanently.
5. She downplays, without fully admitting, how clearly she suspected Toll's real intentions when she first let him take the original device.
6. With no other option left, she agrees to go with Adrian to destroy the second device before it's too late.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. The custody hearing opens in a formal courtroom, Renata and Adrian seated on opposite sides of the aisle.
2. Neither looks at the other, the rift between them as wide as the cause Renata still doesn't understand.
3. Piper sits in the gallery, uninvited and unacknowledged, unsure herself why she came at all.
4. The judge outlines the proceedings, and the first exhibits — the school reports, the 911 recording — are entered into evidence.
5. Adrian sits rigid, aware that whatever the ruling, he still hasn't told anyone in the room the real reason behind any of it.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. The IA investigator lays Zhao's own 'lab consult, no material transfer' entry beside physical evidence proving biological samples actually left the building.
2. He asks her to reconcile the two directly, giving her no room to fall back on vague administrative language.
3. She attempts an explanation about informal consults, but the paper trail contradicts her account point by point.
4. She realizes there's no clean way to close the gap without either lying further on record or admitting the falsification outright.
5. She stalls, asking to review the documents overnight, though both of them understand exactly what the gap already proves.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Adrian and Voss reach the Cannery and find Toll's body, still comatose exactly where it was left the night of the first confrontation.
2. Nearby, they locate the hidden generator housing the second seed emitter, quietly active after months of concealment.
3. As Voss moves to examine the device's controls, Adrian's voice suddenly flattens and shifts, Toll briefly seizing control of his body.
4. Toll uses the moment to stall, positioning Adrian's body between Voss and the generator without explaining why.
5. Adrian's own voice snaps back, disoriented and now certain Toll can act with deliberate intent, not just intrude and taunt.
6. Voss, witnessing this firsthand for the first time, understands with sudden clarity exactly how much danger the delay has already caused.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. The supervised-visit supervisor's written account of the courthouse incident is read aloud into the hearing record.
2. The description details the specific texture of Adrian's confusion afterward — blank, stunned, with no memory rather than shame or denial.
3. Renata listens and registers, for the first time, that the account doesn't match the pattern of a man caught mid-relapse and lying about it.
4. A flicker of doubt about her own certainty surfaces, though she has no alternative explanation to replace it with.
5. She pushes the doubt down for now, unwilling to let it derail the case she's built.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Back at the Cannery, evidence technicians tag items recovered near the generator and Toll's body for processing into the case file.
2. Cross-referencing the recovery site against old custody records, the chain traces back through evidence originally logged under Zhao's cold-case unit.
3. Adrian recognizes the unit designation from department paperwork and realizes, without meaning to, that his own investigation is what connects back to her.
4. He has no idea a funding arrangement exists at all, only that the evidence trail he's building for himself happens to implicate his captain.
5. He files the discrepancy for the record anyway, unwilling to bury evidence the way he's buried everything about himself.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Voss begins arming the generator's internal mechanism to destroy it, following a sequence she designed but never expected to use this way.
2. Toll fights for control harder than at any point before, sensing this is his last real chance at permanence.
3. Adrian's voice cuts between his own and Toll's mid-sentence, the two wills colliding openly in front of a witness for the first time.
4. Voss keeps working through the struggle, aware that stopping to help Adrian personally could cost them their only window.
5. Adrian fights to hold his own body still long enough for her to finish, unsure with each second whether he'll still be the one in control when she does.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Adrian takes the stand and admits to the blackouts directly, no longer denying or minimizing what Renata and the court have observed.
2. He frames them as a medical crisis stemming from the night at the Cannery, one he's only now actively getting addressed.
3. He withholds the true cause entirely, letting the court believe it's a diagnosable condition rather than naming Toll or the transfer.
4. What he says is true in substance and false in specifics, protecting himself from a truth no one in the room could act on responsibly anyway.
5. Renata watches him testify, hearing an admission of harm that fits neither her relapse theory nor full honesty.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Voss completes the destruction sequence, and the generator's core burns out in a final flare before going dark.
2. Adrian's body convulses once, hard, as Toll's presence surges and then collapses inside him without the signal that sustained it.
3. Silence follows, total and unfamiliar, where a second will has pressed against his own for weeks.
4. Adrian can't tell whether Toll is truly gone or only driven dormant by the loss of the device's reinforcement.
5. Voss watches him carefully, equally uncertain, neither able to confirm anything beyond the immediate quiet.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Presented with the full evidence trail from the Cannery recovery alongside her own falsified logs, Zhao has no remaining explanation that holds.
2. Department leadership suspends her pending termination, citing evidence mishandling and falsified records rather than naming any foundation.
3. The funding arrangement itself becomes formally exposed within the department, though Zenith Horizon's deeper purpose stays officially unmentioned.
4. Zhao is removed from her office without ever learning who Marcus Toll was or what the device she unknowingly helped fund actually did.
5. She leaves the building alone, aware only that the arrangement she spent two years protecting has finally cost her everything it was meant to prevent.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. The judge delivers the ruling, denying Renata's request for full emergency custody outright.
2. Instead, the court orders a supervised joint custody arrangement, contingent on Adrian continuing the medical treatment he testified to pursuing.
3. Renata reacts with visible shock, having walked in expecting a clear win rather than a compromise she now has to live inside.
4. Adrian receives the ruling with quiet relief mixed with dread, aware the treatment condition rests on a lie he still can't correct.
5. Neither sibling voices what they're actually feeling to the other as the courtroom empties.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Piper hears through newsroom channels that a police captain has been suspended over falsified cold-case evidence logs.
2. She recognizes immediately that the captain is the same one her 'Unit 4' research had been circling for weeks.
3. She realizes her own reporting could have named Zhao days ago, had she pushed past her hesitation over Renata.
4. Weighing what publishing the deeper Zenith Horizon story would still cost Renata's family, she decides to hold it back a while longer.
5. She closes her research file without telling her editor how close she actually came, keeping the fuller story to herself for now.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Piper comes to Renata's kitchen after the ruling, drawn by news of the hearing's outcome despite their estrangement.
2. Renata confronts her, asking outright what would have happened between them if Piper had never targeted her in the first place.
3. Piper has no honest answer that separates the version of herself who used Renata from the one who now loves her.
4. Renata names the guilt she's sensed underneath every reassurance Piper ever gave her, finally putting words to what she couldn't before.
5. Neither woman resolves the question, the confrontation ending in unsettled quiet rather than any real repair.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone that night, Adrian lies down without fighting the pull of sleep for the first time in months.
2. He no longer fears deep unconsciousness itself, since the device that would have made it fatal to his mind no longer exists.
3. He still doesn't know for certain whether Toll's presence is gone entirely or only silenced for now.
4. He lets himself drift into deep sleep anyway, worn past the point of holding out any longer.
5. The episode leaves him asleep, uncertain and unresolved, without confirmation of what, if anything, made it through the night with him.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Adrian arrives for his first supervised visit with Mia since the court's ruling, the supervisor present as ordered.
2. Renata watches from the doorway, still uncertain about him but bound now by the court's compromise rather than her own conviction.
3. Mia approaches him warily at first, then relaxes slightly as the visit proceeds without incident.
4. Adrian and Renata exchange a brief, wordless look in the doorway, neither able to voice the doubt or hope sitting underneath it.
5. Something fragile and unspoken settles between the siblings, not trust exactly, but no longer open hostility either.
