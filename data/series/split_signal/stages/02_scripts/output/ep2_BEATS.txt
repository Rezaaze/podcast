--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Three days have passed since the failed Cannery takedown and the blackout that followed — it is now day fourteen of the season's timeline.
2. Zhao tells Kestler she's taking direct, personal command of the Toll task force, citing budget consolidation.
3. She offers no deeper explanation, though her real motive is controlling what reaches Internal Affairs before her funding arrangement surfaces.
4. Kestler feels relieved, since it means less scrutiny of his movements right when he can least afford it.
5. A flicker of unease crosses him at how convenient the timing is, though he doesn't examine it further.
6. Neither names the real reason driving the other's behavior.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Zhao sits alone with the falsified 'lab consult, no material transfer' logs again.
2. She counts how many people could still raise questions about the three homicide files tied to those entries.
3. She estimates how long centralizing the task force under herself can hold those questions off.
4. She reminds herself she's only ever dealt with the foundation's administrator, as if that distance still protects her.
5. She feels the arrangement narrowing around her without knowing why, since she has no idea Toll or the Continuum Bridge exist.
6. She locks the logs away again, no closer to a real plan than dread.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Adrian opens the locked drawer to check on the torn, blood-flecked shirt from the rooftop night.
2. The stains look darker and more numerous than his memory of the fight accounts for.
3. He buries the shirt deeper beneath other clothes, as if that will make it easier to stop thinking about.
4. He refuses to look closely enough to count how much blood is actually there.
5. He locks the drawer again, repeating to himself that he's protecting his job, not admitting what else it might mean.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Renata gets a call from Mia's school reporting a second troubling incident.
2. The school describes behavior consistent with a child under stress at home, without naming a cause.
3. Renata immediately reads it as further proof Adrian has relapsed into drinking.
4. With no knowledge of the Continuum Bridge or Toll, the drinking explanation is the only one that makes sense to her.
5. She adds the incident to the documentation she's building for the custody hearing.
6. Her certainty about the wrong cause hardens exactly as she believes she's closing in on the truth.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Voss checks the second seed emitter's monitor during a quiet moment and sees a brief spike in the dormant signal.
2. She recognizes it as the same neural signature she confirmed belongs to Toll.
3. The spike reads stronger than the one she logged the morning after the Cannery confrontation.
4. She weighs reporting it against admitting the unauthorized device and trial exist at all.
5. She decides to wait and keep watching, wrongly assuming she still has time before it matters.
6. The audience understands this delay endangers Kestler more than Voss lets herself realize.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Adrian sits with a drink, sketching the missing Cannery hours on a napkin.
2. He tries to line up what he remembers with what his bruises and torn shirt suggest happened.
3. Midway through muttering a guess at the timeline, a reply answers him that isn't his own thought.
4. The reply is cold, amused, and specific in a way pure imagination wouldn't be.
5. Adrian freezes, then forces himself to write it off as stress talking to itself.
6. The audience recognizes this as Toll asserting himself more clearly than before.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Zhao quietly redirects an outside forensic consult request that would have touched evidence linked to the Zenith Horizon-era case files.
2. Internally she frames the redirection as protecting the task force's timeline, not as self-protection.
3. Kestler notices the consult is delayed longer than procedure normally allows.
4. He asks about it in passing, and Zhao gives a vague administrative reason.
5. He files the delay away as odd without connecting it to anything larger.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Piper cross-references city budget filings against police department funding lines late into the night.
2. She reconfirms a private foundation's money threading through department funding somewhere.
3. She still can't match the funding to a specific unit, officer, or case.
4. She has no idea the trail she's circling leads directly to Renata's own brother.
5. She sets the file aside, frustrated at how close and how blocked she feels.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Renata calls Adrian to tell him about the second school incident directly.
2. Drawing on old habits from his drinking days, Adrian sounds calm and reasonable as he denies anything is wrong.
3. He believes the lie is protecting Mia, not registering how much ground it's costing him.
4. Renata hears exactly the evasiveness she's come to expect, which only confirms her existing belief.
5. Neither names what's actually happening, for entirely different reasons.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Renata tells Piper she's now formally logging every incident for the custody hearing file.
2. She lists the school calls and the flat, evasive phone conversations as her evidence.
3. Piper listens and offers comfort that is emotionally genuine.
4. Underneath, Piper is aware she's confiding in someone who doesn't know how their relationship actually started.
5. Renata has no idea Piper is carrying any secret at all, and confides freely.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Alone again, Adrian tries once more to reconstruct the blackout timeline on paper, pressing harder than before.
2. Toll's voice surfaces again, more clearly this time, needling him with a fragment of true detail about the missing hours.
3. Toll is careful never to reveal the mechanism behind what's happening, only enough to unsettle him.
4. Unwilling to accept a second presence, Adrian tells himself it's exhaustion producing intrusive thoughts.
5. The gap between what Adrian believes and what the audience now knows widens further.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Zhao visits Voss's lab under the pretext of a routine check-in on the research funding arrangement.
2. Voss reads the visit as Zhao checking in on their old arrangement and responds with careful, coded small talk.
3. Believing the foundation's work is unrelated to any active case, Zhao treats the visit as simple due diligence.
4. Voss reveals nothing about Toll, the transfer, or the hidden second device, protecting her own secret.
5. Each woman leaves with a completely different and incomplete read on what the other is protecting.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Kestler mildly asks Zhao why she's centralizing the task force under herself right now.
2. Zhao shuts the question down by invoking her rank rather than offering a real answer.
3. Kestler backs off, reading her tone as final rather than evasive.
4. He remains quietly suspicious with no way to pursue it further.
5. The audience recognizes her rank-pulling as another layer of the same concealment from scene one.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Voss makes an unauthorized visit to check on Toll's comatose body, keeping it off any official log.
2. She checks the hidden device's readings directly at the source and confirms the signal has grown stronger again.
3. Her dread deepens as she registers how much closer the second transfer has come to completion.
4. She still doesn't know the receiving mind is Kestler's, so her fear stays abstract rather than personal.
5. She leaves without reporting the visit or the readings to anyone.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Renata watches Piper play easily with Mia and feels a rush of ordinary domestic warmth.
2. She registers how much she's built around this relationship and how afraid she is of the custody fight threatening it.
3. Piper's tenderness with Mia is genuine, unconnected to her original motive for getting close to Renata.
4. Watching that same tenderness sharpens Piper's guilt over never telling Renata the truth about how they met.
5. Piper stays silent, choosing the moment's warmth over the risk of honesty.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Zhao formally presents the task-force restructuring to the department board.
2. She frames the entire change in budget and efficiency language, with no mention of Zenith Horizon or Internal Affairs.
3. The board accepts her framing without objection or further questions.
4. The restructuring becomes official, giving Zhao direct oversight of exactly what she wanted controlled.
5. No one in the room has any idea what motive actually lies behind the vote they just approved.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Adrian prepares for an upcoming supervised visit tied to the custody case and drinks to steady his nerves.
2. He tells himself the drink is only to get through the visit, not a slide back into anything worse.
3. As the alcohol takes hold, he feels Toll's presence settle closer and steadier than it has before.
4. The sensation is no longer a flicker but something that lingers, unwilling to recede once he notices it.
5. He has no name for what's happening and no one he's willing to tell, ending the episode still alone with it.
