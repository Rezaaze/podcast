--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Moorhouse pours two glasses of brandy, sets both down untouched, and lets the silence stretch before he speaks, making clear this is performance, not hospitality.
2. He tells Ada, without preamble, that Wycliffe's debts were cleared the exact month the discharge signatures started matching Wycliffe's countersignature to Moorhouse's forgeries.
3. Wycliffe appears in the doorway mid-sentence, and Moorhouse doesn't stop or soften his words for the interruption — he lets Wycliffe hear himself being named.
4. The audience, already knowing Moorhouse orchestrated Wycliffe's silence through debt, watches him now weaponize that same fact as a public unmasking rather than a private threat.
5. Wycliffe goes rigid, unable to step forward or retreat, his stillness confirming the truth of the claim more than any words could.
6. Ada watches Wycliffe's reaction rather than Moorhouse's face, and it's Wycliffe's failure to deny it that convinces her before Moorhouse finishes speaking.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Ada's understanding shifts in real time — the man she'd hoped might help her expose Moorhouse is recast as another of his instruments.
2. She recalls, in this new light, every moment Wycliffe hesitated or flinched, reinterpreting his fear as guilt rather than reluctant conscience.
3. Moorhouse watches her face rather than speaking, patient and unhurried, reading her isolation arrive the way a physician reads a symptom he induced on purpose.
4. He allows a small satisfaction to show, not gloating but quietly confirming to himself that removing her last ally is proceeding exactly as planned.
5. Ada doesn't look at Wycliffe again, denying him even the acknowledgment of betrayal, which lands on him harder than an accusation would.
6. The audience, aware Moorhouse suspects Wycliffe has already gone outside the clinic, watches him savor a victory that is already compromised without his knowledge.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Moorhouse steps closer to Ada, lowering his voice into something almost gentle, and offers her a better post, better pay, distance from the ward's rear wall.
2. He frames it as a private arrangement between reasonable people, the same offer of comfortable silence that once worked on Wycliffe.
3. Ada says nothing yet, caught between the plainness of the bribe and the horror of how easily it might have worked on someone with less to lose.
4. Wycliffe breaks before she can answer, blurting out that it's too late for offers because he already told someone outside the clinic that the ward count doesn't match the ledger.
5. Moorhouse's calm fractures for the first time this episode, the news landing as the exact danger he'd suspected but hoped to prevent by isolating Ada first.
6. The audience learns, ahead of both doctors fully reckoning with it, that Wycliffe's earlier silence in the doorway was never submission — it was a man who had already acted and was only now confessing it.
