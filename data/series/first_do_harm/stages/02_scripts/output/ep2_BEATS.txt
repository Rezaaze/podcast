--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Ada finds Wycliffe alone in the apothecary and drops a returned letter, stamped "Address Unknown," onto the counter between them.
2. She states plainly that Mrs. Hale's sister never received her and demands to know where she actually went.
3. Wycliffe's hand stops mid-reach on a brown bottle, the same stillness that betrayed him in the theatre, now impossible to pass off as reflex.
4. Ada notes the stillness but reads it as a man caught in a bureaucratic error, not as the one who invented the missing address himself.
5. He forces his hand to move again, setting the bottle down too carefully, using the motion to buy a second to choose which lie to tell.
6. The audience, knowing he selects these false addresses precisely because they can't be verified, watches him calculate rather than recall.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Wycliffe offers that patients often go to lodge with distant relatives during convalescence, and names a cousin in Leeds for Mrs. Hale.
2. Ada asks why no such cousin appears anywhere in Mrs. Hale's admission file, and he has no ready answer for the gap.
3. He pivots to warning her that questioning Dr. Moorhouse's protocol is not a nurse's place, and could cost her the position she needs.
4. His voice cracks on the word "protocol," the first fracture in the authority he's trying to project.
5. Ada hears the lie underneath the warning, though she still takes it as Wycliffe protecting his own weakness, not as him protecting Moorhouse out of debt.
6. She leaves the warning unanswered but visibly unpersuaded, telling Wycliffe his threat has failed to close the matter the way he needs it to.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Ada waits until after curfew and lets herself into the locked ward, chart in hand, moving cell to cell to check names against numbers.
2. She reaches the cell marked vacated for Mrs. Hale and pauses, expecting silence to confirm what the record already claims.
3. Instead she hears slow, ragged breathing through the iron slot, and something metal — a chain — drags across the floor inside.
4. She tells herself it could be another patient misassigned to the wrong cell, refusing to let the alternative fully form as a thought.
5. The audience, holding what Moorhouse conceals — that this cell was never truly vacant — recognizes the sound as the exact secret he believes no night nurse could uncover.
6. Ada reaches for the bolt to look inside, the scene ending on her hand at the lock rather than on what she'll find.
