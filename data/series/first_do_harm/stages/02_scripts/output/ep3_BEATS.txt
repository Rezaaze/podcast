--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Ada picks the lock on the apothecary cabinet after hours, chart discipline abandoned for something closer to theft.
2. She finds the tin marked "Effects — Discharged" filed among ordinary stock, exactly where Wycliffe would have placed it without drawing notice.
3. Inside, under a layer of gauze, she finds the silver locket and recognizes the engraved initials before she even turns it over.
4. She holds it against the memory of Mrs. Hale wearing it the night she vanished, closing the gap between record and reality.
5. The audience, already knowing Wycliffe filed this tin himself on Moorhouse's order, watches Ada arrive at proof without yet knowing whose hand placed it there.
6. She pockets the locket rather than reporting the find, choosing to confront a person with it before she trusts any institution with it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Ada finds Wycliffe alone again and opens her fist to show him the locket without a word of explanation needed.
2. He doesn't reach for a bottle or a story this time — his stillness is total, the calculating pause from before replaced by simple failure to perform.
3. He asks her, voice breaking, whether she's told anyone else, revealing by the question alone that the locket means exactly what she feared.
4. Ada hears in his asking not concern for Mrs. Hale but fear for himself, though she still doesn't grasp the debt that binds him to Moorhouse.
5. He doesn't confirm or deny anything aloud, but his failure to offer a single explanation is itself the confession Ada has been working toward.
6. Ada leaves him standing there, taking his silence as confirmation that this goes beyond him — straight to Moorhouse — and turns to go find her answer at the source.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Ada walks the corridor to Moorhouse's study with the locket gripped so tightly the chain bites her palm, done asking anyone but him.
2. She reaches for the door only to find it already filling with his silhouette, as if he'd been standing there waiting rather than working.
3. He says her name first, calm and unsurprised, robbing her of the confrontation she'd rehearsed on the way over.
4. The audience, knowing Moorhouse has tracked her suspicion since her very first questions, recognizes this isn't a man caught off guard but one closing a trap he's had time to set.
5. Ada registers his calm as wrong before she can name why, some instinct warning her that arriving with proof was itself expected.
6. The scene ends on the two of them facing each other in the doorway, her evidence in hand but the advantage already somehow his.
