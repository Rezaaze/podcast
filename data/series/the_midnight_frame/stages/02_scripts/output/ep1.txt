--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Every morning at dawn, Harbor City homicide detective Noah Kestrel receives a photograph of his own murdered body — timestamped for midnight that same night, the killer's face never the same twice. He has one day to survive whoever it is this time, knowing that every move he makes to stay alive reshuffles the danger for tomorrow. Around him, a captain with a secret program, an estranged wife with a secret grief, a rival with a secret debt, and a reporter chasing a story that's getting too close to her own heart all carry pieces of a truth none of them can see whole. This is a season about what people hide from the ones who love them — and what happens when a man is forced to live, over and over, exactly one day ahead of his own death.

[NARRATOR]
Six Weeks of Mornings. Noah Kestrel's apartment, before sunrise, the forty-second day of a ritual that started as terror and has since become procedure.
