--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Roughly ten hours have passed since Zhao's quiet relief at the end of the previous night; it's now dawn on day forty-four of the unbroken photograph ritual.
2. The morning's photograph has already arrived and gone up on the wall, extending the grid by one more day.
3. This time Noah goes further than studying the face — he opens the image file itself and digs into its raw metadata.
4. Buried in the data he finds a recurring server-ping timestamp, structured in a way that feels deliberate rather than random.
5. He checks it against his own phone and camera logs and confirms it doesn't originate from either — it reads as external.
6. Unnerved, he decides to bring it to Zhao before anyone else, assuming her institutional access can explain it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. At the coffee stand before shift, Kai clocks Noah's jittery, distracted energy and needles him about not sleeping.
2. Noah deflects with the same worn excuse about case workload he's used before.
3. As Kai turns to order, Noah quickly folds the metadata printout and tucks it into his jacket unseen.
4. Kai catches the quick movement but not what's on the page, and doesn't push.
5. The residual coolness since discovering the photo wall keeps Kai from digging further than he might have once.
6. Noah walks off believing the discovery is still entirely his secret.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Noah brings the metadata printout to Zhao's office and shows her the recurring timestamp.
2. Zhao studies it with practiced calm, instantly recognizing it as her own hidden Panopticon server's signature.
3. She tells him it's likely an artifact of old case-archive backups running on a legacy schedule.
4. Before he can push for more detail, she redirects the conversation to the active homicide case.
5. Noah accepts the explanation on the surface but privately files away a flicker of doubt.
6. The audience sees what he doesn't: her calm is a deliberate lie, and it has only partly worked.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Noah visits Feng and recounts the timestamp discovery along with Zhao's archive-backup explanation.
2. Feng recognizes at once that the timestamp likely belongs to a system he designed himself.
3. He deflects gently, offering a vague technical-sounding agreement with Zhao's story rather than a real answer.
4. Noah notices a beat of hesitation before Feng speaks but can't name what feels off about it.
5. Feng hides that he built the very system now stalking his friend, still unwilling to admit it.
6. Noah leaves more unsettled than before, his doubt about Zhao's explanation deepening rather than easing.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone in the restaurant's back office, Danny takes a call from Sonny.
2. Sonny arranges a new handoff method — a folder left at the diner counter instead of the usual garage meeting.
3. Danny agrees smoothly, keeping his tone light and controlled for the length of the call.
4. The moment the line goes dead, his easy charm cracks and he sits in visible tension.
5. He tells himself this is still just protecting his family, even as the new method unsettles him.
6. He hides the call and the new arrangement from everyone, including his mother and Hae.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Danny and Hae share a quiet dinner at the restaurant.
2. He mentions he'll need to step out later that night, offering a thin, vague reason.
3. Hae senses the evasion in how quickly he moves the conversation elsewhere afterward.
4. She chooses not to press, partly from her own guardedness and partly from being used to things half-said.
5. Neither mentions Noah aloud, though his unresolved presence sits quietly under the meal.
6. Danny hides that the real reason is the diner drop-off arranged with Sonny.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Danny arrives at the diner and leaves a photocopied case folder at the counter as arranged.
2. A stranger nods once, picks it up, and pockets it without a word exchanged.
3. Kai walks in moments later and catches the tail end of the handoff.
4. Kai doesn't understand what he's seeing, but the exchange registers as wrong.
5. Danny spots Kai and quickly covers the moment with his usual easy demeanor.
6. Kai's unease sharpens, though he says nothing to Danny in the moment.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Kai corners Danny privately afterward and asks directly about the folder and the stranger.
2. Danny claims it was for an old confidential informant from a closed case, manufacturing the story on the spot.
3. Kai has no evidence to contradict him and doesn't want to believe his partner's rival is lying to him.
4. He reluctantly accepts the explanation, though it doesn't fully sit right.
5. Danny reads Kai's hesitation and smooths it over with a joke.
6. Kai lets it go outwardly, but the unease stays with him underneath.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Back at his desk, Kai brings up a vague unease about Danny without naming what he actually saw.
2. Noah, still preoccupied with the timestamp and the reactions from Zhao and Feng, only half-listens.
3. Kai himself downplays it, unsure if it's even worth mentioning, and lets the comment trail off.
4. Noah offers a distracted, noncommittal response instead of following up.
5. Neither realizes how much the other is currently sitting on secrets of his own.
6. The moment passes without either connecting Danny's behavior to anything larger.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Aya receives a new encrypted tip from her anonymous source referencing the precinct's basement records room.
2. She notes this detail is more specific than anything leaked before, edging closer to the operation's actual location.
3. She cross-checks it quietly through a department contact, and it holds up.
4. She grows further convinced her source is a disgruntled junior officer with real inside access.
5. She has no idea the tip came from Zhao herself, deliberately steering her toward a controlled reveal.
6. The audience recognizes Zhao is tightening a leash Aya believes she's following on her own.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Aya meets Noah for another interview, ostensibly to talk about the ongoing case.
2. She gently probes his guarded, distracted mood, sensing something is off.
3. Noah deflects on specifics but lets more warmth show through than he intends.
4. Neither names what's really bothering them — the timestamp for him, the deadline and her source for her.
5. Something between them softens further, complicating both their professional instincts.
6. Aya notices herself pulling back from pressing him the way the story would normally demand.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Alone at night, Noah takes out the metadata printout again and rereads the timestamp.
2. He remains unconvinced by Zhao's archive-backup explanation, the numbers still nagging at him.
3. Rather than raising it again or investigating further, he hides the printout behind one of the wall's photographs.
4. He tells himself he's tabling the question, not abandoning it, though he takes no next step tonight.
5. He still has no suspicion that Zhao or Feng is connected to the timestamp's true source.
6. The printout stays concealed, a loose thread he isn't ready to pull yet.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Danny calls Sonny to confirm the diner drop-off went unnoticed.
2. Sonny confirms it did and mentions another incremental reduction to the family debt.
3. Danny's relief at the confirmation quickly curdles into guilt once the call ends.
4. He briefly recalls Kai's earlier suspicion and pushes the thought away.
5. He steps back out to the dinner rush and forces his usual easy smile over the tension.
6. He hides the full weight of what just happened from everyone around him, including his mother working the floor.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Alone at home, Hae opens a text draft to Noah about their still-unfinalized divorce paperwork.
2. She types and deletes several versions, unable to settle on the right tone.
3. She ultimately deletes the message entirely rather than send it.
4. She turns instead to old drawings taped to her fridge, a quieter, safer place to put her attention.
5. She still has no idea about the investigator, the wire transfer, or what her father may have done.
6. The unfinished text underscores how unresolved her feelings for Noah remain beneath her life with Danny.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Aya's editor calls her in and warns that the story's window is closing faster than she thinks.
2. He reminds her a rival reporter is circling and could break the story first.
3. He pushes her to chase her source harder and land a namable suspect within days, not weeks.
4. Aya feels the pressure sharpen against her still-unfinished draft naming Noah.
5. She leaves more determined to follow the basement-records lead than to protect her growing feelings for him.
6. The audience senses this pressure is about to accelerate her collision course with Zhao's operation.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. After Noah leaves her office, Zhao waits until she's alone before checking the hidden Panopticon feed on her phone.
2. She reviews the system's logs, confirming her archive-backup explanation appears to have held for now.
3. She notes the timestamp discovery came closer than she'd like and weighs whether to adjust the server's exposure.
4. She also registers, distantly, the tightening pressure of Aya's editor deadline as another variable in her timeline.
5. She exhales once, allowing herself a brief moment of relief before returning to her by-the-book composure.
6. The scene reaffirms her complete awareness and control, even as the walls around her operation begin narrowing.
