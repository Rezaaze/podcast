--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Roughly a few hours have passed since Noah stood before his wall deciding to wait on Feng at the end of the previous episode; it's now well past midnight, sliding into the dawn of day forty-eight of the photograph ritual.
2. Aya rereads the final draft of her exposé, the one naming Noah as the detective at its center.
3. She weighs her editor's deadline against her unresolved feelings for him one last time.
4. She decides the story is too far along, and too important, to hold back any longer.
5. She hits send to her editor without warning Noah first.
6. She still believes her anonymous source is a disgruntled junior officer, unaware she's about to detonate Zhao's carefully steered timetable ahead of schedule.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. It's dawn on day forty-eight, only hours after Aya sent her story in.
2. Noah's phone buzzes with the new composite, arriving exactly on schedule as it has every morning since the ritual began.
3. The face is unfamiliar again, but the timestamp matches the same unbroken pattern.
4. He pins it beside the other forty-plus photographs without hesitation or visible reaction.
5. His numbness to the ritual now reads as habit, masking how close he privately believes he already is to naming Feng.
6. He has no idea Aya's story is already set to run within hours.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. At shift change, a folded copy of the Herald lands on Noah's desk.
2. The front page names an illegal department surveillance program in explicit detail.
3. The bullpen goes quiet as officers realize the story describes their own precinct.
4. Noah recognizes the byline as Aya's and feels a jolt of betrayal cut through the shock.
5. He scans specifics that could only have come from someone inside the department.
6. His suspicion of a leak sharpens instantly, though he has no idea yet who's actually behind it.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone behind her office glass, Zhao reads the story in full.
2. She recognizes nearly every detail as something she personally fed to Aya over the past months.
3. She registers that the story broke earlier and harder than she planned, well ahead of her own timetable.
4. She picks up the burner phone reserved for Aya, thumb hovering over the contact.
5. She sets it down without dialing, unwilling to risk any contact that could expose the arrangement now.
6. Her calm expression hides real fear that Panopticon isn't ready to survive this level of scrutiny.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In the restaurant's back office, Danny's phone rings with Sonny's number.
2. Sonny tells him the arrangement is changing: the full eighty-thousand-dollar balance in seventy-two hours, or one last, much bigger piece of information instead.
3. Danny asks what kind of information would settle it; Sonny declines to specify yet.
4. Danny's easy composure cracks the moment the call ends.
5. He has no idea the sudden ultimatum is tied to the network's own panic over the morning's exposé.
6. He hides the call and the new deadline from both his mother and Hae.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Over cold coffee, Kai reads the story aloud in disbelief, marveling at how deep the leak must run.
2. Noah privately recognizes several details that match his own case files almost word for word.
3. He says nothing to Kai about the timestamp, Zhao, or Feng.
4. Kai assumes the leak is some disgruntled officer venting to the press, with no idea Zhao is involved.
5. Noah's suspicion quietly shifts toward someone close to the investigation rather than a random leaker.
6. Neither of them yet connects the story to Panopticon or the basement server.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Over breakfast, Hae reads the same front-page story on her phone.
2. The mention of a hidden department program tracking someone in secret pulls her thoughts straight back to Noah's torn investigator receipt.
3. She still believes his explanation that the investigator was only about an old break-in.
4. The parallel unsettles her in a way she can't quite name or dismiss.
5. She wonders, briefly and uneasily, whether Noah's secret and this story's secret program are shaped the same way.
6. She has no idea the real investigation concerns her own father.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Still rattled from Sonny's call, Danny snaps sharply at a line cook over a minor mistake.
2. The kitchen staff exchange looks, unused to his temper showing this openly.
3. Hae stops by unannounced and immediately clocks the tension in the room.
4. She asks him directly if something's wrong.
5. Danny forces his usual charm back into place and waves off her concern.
6. He hides the seventy-two-hour deadline and everything behind it from her completely.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. In the newsroom, Aya's editor raises a toast to the front page landing exactly as demanded.
2. Colleagues congratulate her on breaking the department surveillance story ahead of the rival reporter.
3. Aya accepts the praise outwardly while privately bracing for what comes next.
4. She can only think about how Noah will look at her once he sees what she published.
5. She still has no idea her anonymous source is Zhao, or that the story just forced Zhao's hand early.
6. Her pride in the scoop and her dread of his reaction sit uneasily side by side.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Noah brings the morning's Herald to Feng's shop and sets it on the counter.
2. Feng reads the description of the illegal surveillance program and his hands go completely still.
3. Noah watches the stillness closely, already primed by his suspicion from the pocketed camera component.
4. Feng recovers quickly, forcing his hands back into motion and changing the subject to something mundane.
5. Noah notices the deflection lands even thinner than the last two excuses.
6. Feng still can't bring himself to admit he built the very system the story describes.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Zhao gathers her detectives, Noah and Danny among them, for an emergency briefing.
2. She orders total silence to the press about internal matters, framing it as standard procedure during a leak investigation.
3. Her tone stays brisk and controlled, giving away nothing of her personal stake in the story.
4. Noah watches her carefully, his doubt about her past explanations sharpening beneath the surface.
5. Danny listens distracted, his mind still on Sonny's seventy-two-hour deadline rather than the briefing.
6. No one in the room suspects how close the story actually cuts to their captain.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Examining a body tied to Noah's active case, Kai notices a detail from the file that shouldn't have been public knowledge.
2. He points out that this detail closely matches something mentioned in the Herald story.
3. Noah realizes the leak runs deeper into their actual casework than the broad program description suggested.
4. Neither of them has any idea the detail traces back to Danny's arrangement with Sonny.
5. Kai speculates aloud about who inside the department could have that level of access.
6. The moment plants a new thread of suspicion neither of them will let go of easily.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Alone after closing, Danny pulls up Noah's open case file on his phone.
2. He scrolls through details, weighing which single piece Sonny might consider big enough to end the arrangement.
3. He recognizes that whatever he chooses will cross further than anything he's leaked before.
4. He has no idea two people who already appeared as "killers" in Noah's photographs were selected partly from his past leaks.
5. He hesitates, torn between the deadline and the risk of exposing Noah directly.
6. He closes the phone without deciding, the weight of the choice pressing harder than before.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Danny nearly tells Hae about the debt and the seventy-two-hour deadline, going further than he ever has before.
2. He gets as far as naming the amount before his nerve fails him.
3. He deflects at the last second, turning the near-confession into a joke about restaurant finances.
4. Hae laughs along but senses the same retreat pattern she's seen from him before.
5. She chooses not to push, still telling herself she's giving him space rather than repeating old mistakes with Noah.
6. Danny's secret and the deadline stay fully hidden from her.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Off the books, Kai meets Aya and presses her directly to name her source.
2. He states plainly that he believes the leak is someone inside the precinct.
3. Aya refuses to give up her source, citing journalistic protection.
4. She reflexively defends the source's credibility, unknowingly praising Zhao's own careful positioning.
5. Kai reads her refusal as professional principle, not realizing how literally close to home the truth sits.
6. Neither of them suspects Zhao at any point in the conversation.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone with his corkboard that night, Noah lays the Herald story beside the metadata printout he hid weeks ago.
2. He rereads the story's description of a hidden department program alongside the recurring server timestamp.
3. He recalls Zhao's "archive backup" explanation and finds it thinner now than it did at the time.
4. He begins to suspect the story and the timestamp describe the very same system.
5. He still has no idea Feng designed that system or that Zhao is the one secretly running it.
6. His doubt about Zhao shifts from vague unease into something closer to conviction.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Aya meets Noah expecting anger over the story naming him.
2. He surprises her by staying quiet and controlled instead of confronting her with fury.
3. He asks her plainly how much more she knows that she hasn't printed yet.
4. Aya is caught off guard, unsure whether this is investigative pressure or something more personal.
5. She hesitates over whether to mention the basement server photo, still unconnected in her mind to Zhao.
6. Neither says aloud what's actually shifted between them, though both feel the ground move.
