--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Roughly twelve hours have passed since Noah stood before his wall with Feng's component in hand at the end of the previous episode; it's now the next morning, day forty-seven.
2. Aya rereads her editor's message, the one-week ultimatum now down to its final days.
3. The newsroom's fluorescent hum presses on her, making every unexplored lead feel urgent.
4. She decides she can't wait passively on her anonymous source any longer and needs to dig on her own.
5. She still believes that source is a disgruntled junior officer, unaware it's Zhao steering her.
6. She resolves to chase the vague "old equipment" tip herself instead of waiting for the next message.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Digging through public-records filings, Aya finds a years-old request for cold-case files that was never formally closed or denied.
2. She notices the request traces back to a unit connected to the precinct's old records basement.
3. She has no idea this dormant paperwork is a loose thread from Panopticon's original shutdown.
4. She recognizes it as the first lead in weeks that didn't come from her source.
5. She decides this gives her a plausible, legitimate reason to get physically inside the building's archives.
6. She tells no one at the paper what she's about to attempt.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Aya arrives at the precinct citing the dormant records request as her reason for access.
2. She talks her way past a bored clerk who barely checks her credentials against the old filing.
3. The clerk has no idea the request connects to anything sensitive still running in the basement.
4. Aya is waved through toward the old storage basement, phone already in hand.
5. She descends alone, aware she's pushing further than a normal interview visit would allow.
6. She hides from the clerk how much she's hoping to find down there.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Behind stacked file boxes, Aya spots a server rack humming quietly, out of place among the dusty archives.
2. She can't identify the equipment or explain why it's still powered in a supposedly dead records room.
3. She raises her phone and snaps a photo before anyone can notice her presence.
4. She has no idea this is the very Panopticon server Zhao has been secretly running for four months.
5. She slips back out before anyone catches her, adrenaline outpacing her understanding of what she's found.
6. She still doesn't connect the discovery to her anonymous source at all.

--- SCENE 5 ---

2. He turns the morning's photograph over in his hands without really seeing it.
3. He is now privately convinced Feng built the very rig generating these images, though he's told no one.
4. He hasn't decided whether to confront Feng outright or wait for more proof.
5. He also can't shake his growing doubt that Zhao's "archive backup" explanation was a deliberate misdirection.
6. He keeps both suspicions locked away, sharing them with no one at the precinct.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Kai notices Noah staring into space again and presses him about his mood.
2. Noah deflects with a vague line about the case, same as always.
3. He can't bring himself to say aloud that the man he trusts most may have built the machine stalking him.
4. Kai has no idea Feng is involved in any way, or that such a suspicion even exists.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Noah corners Zhao again with pointed questions about the photograph's real origin.
2. Zhao brushes past them with brisk, practiced authority, refusing to engage further.
3. She orders him to file his report and treat the day like any other.
4. She has no idea he now suspects Feng specifically rather than her archive-backup story.
5. She reads his persistence as ordinary case stress rather than a sign he's circling the truth.
6. Noah's doubt about her explanation deepens further, though he says nothing more and drops it for now.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Danny smiles his way through the dinner rush, plates clattering and lanterns glowing around him.
2. Underneath the charm, he's silently rehearsing excuses for Sonny's next expected call.
3. He calculates what small detail he might still be able to offer without risking too much.
4. His mother and the staff read nothing but his usual easy confidence.
5. He hides completely how tightly the arrangement is now gripping him.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. A cheerful text from his mother arrives, thanking him for finally clearing the family debt.
2. Danny stares at it, unable to correct a belief he let stand a year ago.
3. He realizes fixing this lie now would mean unraveling everything else he's hidden alongside it.
4. He forces a reply that matches her warmth, burying the truth deeper instead of ending it.
5. He hides his reaction from everyone around him in the kitchen.
6. The moment leaves him more trapped than reassured, despite the debt's incremental relief.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. At home, Hae goes to finally return Noah's forgotten coat and starts tidying it before folding it away.
2. Her fingers catch on stiff paper caught in the lining.
3. She pulls out a torn, half-legible receipt tucked where it clearly wasn't meant to be found.
4. She has no idea yet what it says, only that Noah hid it deliberately.
5. Her curiosity sharpens into something closer to alarm.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Hae unfolds the receipt fully and reads a private investigator's letterhead.
2. She realizes Noah has been paying someone in secret, without ever mentioning it to her.
3. She has no idea yet that the investigation concerns her late father specifically.
4. She feels the ground shift, certain now that something has been kept from her.
5. She decides she needs to hear it from Noah directly, not guess at it alone.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Hae calls Noah over on a thin pretext, giving no hint of what she's actually found.
2. She lays the torn receipt flat on the kitchen table, waiting for him to notice it himself.
3. She rehearses how she'll react, believing whatever he admits will be the whole truth.
4. Noah has no idea what's waiting for him when he walks through her door.
5. The quiet in the kitchen sits deliberately staged, though she keeps her expression neutral.

3. He claims the investigation was only about an old break-in, deliberately hiding that it concerns her father's death.
4. Hae accepts the explanation at face value, relieved to have something concrete at last.
5. Noah privately knows this new lie only buries the deeper one further, with no plan for how to sustain it.

--- SCENE 14 ---

--- SCENE 15 ---

2. She weighs filing it now to meet her editor's tightening deadline against holding it back for more context.
3. She still hasn't connected the image to her anonymous source or to Zhao at all.
4. She senses the photo could be the break the story needs, but fears publishing too soon.
5. She sets it aside undecided, the pressure of the deadline gnawing at her regardless.

--- SCENE 16 ---

2. Her growing feelings for him keep her from mentioning the basement photo at all.
3. Noah, still turning over his suspicions about Feng and Zhao, is more distracted than usual.
4. Neither notices how close the other is quietly sitting to a piece of the larger truth.
5. Both keep their private discoveries entirely to themselves, protecting the ease between them instead.
6. The audience recognizes how little either name would need to change everything, if only they said it aloud.

2. He weighs confronting Feng outright against staying quiet a while longer to gather more proof.
3. He has no knowledge of Zhao's basement server, Aya's photograph, or how the receipt lie is already settling with Hae.

6. The scene ends with his suspicion intact but unspoken, the season's threads drawing tighter without any of them touching yet.
