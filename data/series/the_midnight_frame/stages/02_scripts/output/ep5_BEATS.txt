--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Roughly six hours have passed since Noah got home past midnight at the end of the previous episode; it's now dawn on day forty-six of the unbroken photograph ritual.
2. His phone buzzes with the new composite, arriving on schedule as it has every morning since.
3. This time he notices something different about the print itself — a fine, distinct grain texture unlike any of the previous forty-five photographs.
4. He runs his fingers over the surface, trying to place why it looks technically off in a way he can't articulate.
5. The anomaly nags at him differently than the metadata timestamp did — this feels physical, traceable.
6. He decides today he needs someone who understands photography and print technology, not another round with Zhao.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. At the coffee stand, Kai ribs Noah about his phone buzzing, assuming it's another message from Aya.
2. Noah lets the assumption stand, allowing Kai to believe romance is behind his distraction.
3. He doesn't correct it, even though the real cause is the odd grain in this morning's photo.
4. He mentions, almost offhand, that he's taking today's print to Feng for a second look.
5. Kai doesn't press further, assuming it's just Noah's usual routine with his old mentor.
6. Kai has no idea how much weight Noah is quietly placing on Feng's reaction this time.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Danny and Hae share a quiet Sunday breakfast, the ease from the night before still lingering.
2. Danny's phone lights up with a text from Sonny.
3. He turns the screen away and pockets it before Hae can see the name.
4. He covers the movement with a light, offhand comment about work.
5. Hae notices the quick reflex but says nothing, chalking it up to the restaurant.
6. Danny hides that the morning's ease is already fraying under the resumed contact.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Aya's editor calls her in, reminding her the one-week ultimatum is now down to days.
2. He pushes her to name a suspect soon or lose the story to the circling rival reporter.
3. Aya insists her source's information has checked out every single time, defending its reliability.
4. She still frames the source privately as a disgruntled junior officer with real inside access.
5. The audience knows what she doesn't: the trusted source is Captain Zhao, steering her deliberately.
6. She leaves more determined to chase the basement-equipment lead than to protect her own feelings for Noah.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In the back office, Danny returns Sonny's call privately.
2. Sonny asks for a specific new detail from an active case file.
3. Danny hesitates only briefly before handing it over.
4. He tells himself this one detail changes nothing, the same rationalization as always.
5. He hates how easily the lie comes now, though he pushes the feeling down before it can settle.
6. He hides the call completely from his mother and Hae, both elsewhere in the restaurant.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Noah meets Kai for coffee before heading to Feng's shop.
2. He mentions, almost in passing, that he needs to swing by Hae's to pick up a coat he left behind.
3. Kai perks up, needling him gently about whether this is a step toward reconciliation.
4. Noah deflects, insisting it's only about the coat and nothing more.
5. He says nothing about the investigator, the wire transfer, or anything that actually keeps him tied to Hae's orbit.
6. Kai lets the teasing go but clocks Noah's evasiveness as more than simple annoyance.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Hae opens the door and hands Noah his coat, the exchange stiff and careful.
2. Small talk passes between them, neither saying what actually sits underneath it.
3. Noah wants to say something honest about the divorce papers, or the investigator, and says nothing.
4. Hae wants to ask why he's been so distant, and doesn't.
5. Neither mentions Danny by name, though his presence is felt by both.
6. The door closes on a moment that could have shifted something and didn't.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Noah arrives at Feng's shop and sets today's photograph on the counter.
2. He points out the odd grain texture, asking for a second opinion the way he has a dozen times before.
3. He frames the request casually, unaware how differently Feng is about to react to this one.
4. Feng reaches for his loupe out of long habit, expecting another routine look.
5. Noah has no idea this photo's texture is about to expose something Feng has hidden for fifteen years.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Feng bends over the loupe and studies the print's grain pattern closely.
2. He recognizes it instantly — the exact signature of a camera rig he designed and built himself years ago.
3. His hands begin to shake almost imperceptibly as the recognition lands.
4. He understands immediately what it means: a live composite is still being generated by a system he built and believed long buried.
5. He says nothing yet, buying himself a beat to compose an answer that hides the truth.
6. The audience now has explicit confirmation, through Feng's own reaction, of what the case file has stated all along.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Noah notices the shake in Feng's hands and asks directly what's wrong.
2. Feng invents a story about an old colleague who once built rigs with a similar signature, years back.
3. The explanation is plausible but thinner and less precise than Feng's usual certainty.
4. Noah senses the gap between Feng's words and his composure, though he can't yet name what's off.
5. Feng's deflection buys time but doesn't fully close the crack Noah has now seen in him.
6. Feng hides that the "old colleague" is himself, protecting Noah at the cost of the truth.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Kai stops by the restaurant for takeout and catches Danny mid-call, tense and clipped.
2. Danny ends the call abruptly the moment he spots Kai.
3. Kai asks lightly who it was; Danny brushes it off with a vague answer about a supplier.
4. Kai's suspicion from the earlier diner incident resurfaces, sharper than before.
5. Danny talks his way past it with practiced ease, and Kai lets it go outwardly.
6. Kai still can't name what he suspects, but the unease compounds rather than fades.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Noah returns to the precinct still visibly rattled from the shop visit.
2. Kai notices immediately and asks what happened.
3. Noah tells him only that Feng seemed "off" today, nothing more specific.
4. He withholds the grain match, the camera rig, and the shake in Feng's hands entirely.
5. Kai senses there's more but doesn't push, given how little ground they've regained since the ledger lie.
6. Noah carries the real weight of his suspicion alone, telling no one what he now believes about Feng.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Aya receives a new message from her anonymous source referencing old equipment still running somewhere in the precinct.
2. The tip is vaguer than previous ones, giving her nothing concrete enough to print.
3. She can't yet tell if it's a real lead or a dead end, and it unsettles her more than it clarifies.
4. She still attributes the tip to a disgruntled junior officer.
5. The audience recognizes this as Zhao steering her again, tightening the leash toward the basement server.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Aya meets Noah again, framed as an interview but carrying the same warmth from their recent dinners.
2. Their rapport deepens further, the line between reporter and something more continuing to blur.
3. Noah's distraction over Feng slips through despite his effort to seem present.
4. Aya notices the shift and gently asks if something's wrong.
5. Noah brushes it off, unwilling to explain even a fraction of what he now suspects.
6. Neither raises the story, the deadline, or the tip about old equipment sitting between them unspoken.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Later that day, Danny and Hae sit together, the earlier tension from breakfast still faintly present.
2. Danny opens his mouth to tell her about the family debt and Sonny, further than he's ever gone before.
3. He loses his nerve partway through and deflects into something unrelated.
4. Hae senses the retreat, recognizing the same pattern from her marriage.
5. She chooses not to push, telling herself she's giving him space rather than repeating old mistakes.
6. Danny's secret stays intact, though the cost of carrying it shows more plainly than before.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Noah returns to Feng's shop alone after closing, drawn back by an instinct he doesn't fully explain to himself.
2. He lets himself into the workshop area, telling himself he's only checking on Feng.
3. Among Feng's tools he finds a spare camera component unlike anything sold commercially.
4. He examines it and recognizes design elements that match nothing he's seen in any retail rig.
5. The discovery lines up with the grain signature from Feng's earlier reaction, and the pieces begin to connect in his mind.
6. He pockets the component, aware he's crossing into territory that will change everything if he's right.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Back home, Noah stands before the photograph wall with the camera component still in hand.
2. He ties a new length of red string, connecting the grid of photographs to a card bearing Feng's shop name.
3. He stares at the name, unable to fully accept what the evidence is telling him.
4. He still doesn't understand why Feng would be involved or what the system even is, only that his mentor built the thing haunting him.
5. He recognizes he has no idea whether to feel afraid, betrayed, or something he doesn't yet have a word for.
6. The scene ends with Noah's trust in the one person he never doubted now visibly, irreversibly cracked.
