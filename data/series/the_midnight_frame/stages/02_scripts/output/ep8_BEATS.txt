--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Only about an hour has passed since Aya cornered Noah at the end of the previous episode; it's still morning on day forty-eight.
2. The bullpen has now fully absorbed the story, officers clustering around printed copies and hushed phone screens.
3. Noah rereads paragraphs he's already memorized, watching the room's reaction more than the words themselves.
4. Kai stares at one paragraph, stunned by how specifically it describes internal department behavior.
5. Kai says aloud that whoever leaked this must have been inside command, not just a beat cop.
6. Noah says nothing about his private suspicion of Zhao, letting Kai's theory drift unchecked.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Zhao returns from being called upstairs over the story's fallout, already sensing her command hangs in the balance though nothing is decided yet.
2. Noah intercepts her in the hallway, pressing her again about the timestamp and the story's uncanny parallels to his own case.
3. She deflects with clipped, brisk authority, insisting she has no time for this now.
4. She turns away toward the stairwell leading to the records basement instead of back to her office.
5. Noah notices the direction is wrong for her usual route and grows suspicious of where she's actually headed.
6. He decides to follow her rather than let the moment pass.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Noah reaches the basement door to find it locked against him and forces it open anyway.
2. Inside, he finds Zhao standing before the humming server rack, phone in hand, mid-check on a live feed.
3. He glimpses his own name and data scrolling on the screen before she can close it.
4. He confronts her outright, demanding to know what the system is and why it's tracking him.
5. Zhao doesn't bother denying what he's plainly seeing, her composure cracking for the first time all season.
6. The case file's central lie collapses in real time, exactly as Noah's suspicion has been building toward.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Zhao admits the server is Panopticon, active and generating Noah's photographs every night for four months.
2. She confesses she chose him deliberately because he has no ties to the corrupt network she's hunting.
3. She reveals her real target: the network she believes killed Feng's son, a rookie officer, ten years ago.
4. She admits she never told Noah he was bait, calculating his unwitting exposure would flush the network out faster.
5. She downplays how much of her own freedom she's gambling now that Aya's story has made the program impossible to keep fully buried.
6. Noah realizes some of his morning "killers" were shaped by leaks from inside his own precinct, though he doesn't yet know Danny's name or the network's full membership.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Noah goes straight from the basement to Feng's shop, still reeling from Zhao's confession.
2. He tells Feng plainly that he knows the system is real and that Zhao has been running it for months.
3. Feng doesn't try to deny building it, his shaking hands finally going still.
4. He admits he built Panopticon fifteen years ago and never fully let go of it after it was shut down.
5. He confesses the deeper reason: his son's murder ten years ago and his belief that Zhao's operation was the only path to justice the department wouldn't pursue openly.
6. He doesn't excuse Zhao's choice to use Noah without consent, but he also doesn't fully disown his own part in building a weapon that could be aimed at anyone.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. After closing, Danny goes over the books once more, the eighty-thousand-dollar debt still sitting there unchanged.
2. He recalls Sonny's seventy-two-hour ultimatum and realizes stalling any longer only deepens the trap.
3. He weighs the cost of confession against the cost of the one final, larger leak Sonny is demanding.
4. He decides he can't hand over more case details without crossing a line he won't come back from.
5. He resolves to end the arrangement himself rather than wait for Sonny to force his hand.
6. He tells no one yet — not his mother, not Hae — that he's about to walk into the precinct and confess.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Danny arrives at the precinct and asks to speak to Noah and Zhao together.
2. He tells them he's been passing case details to a contact named Sonny for two years in exchange for debt forgiveness.
3. He admits the family restaurant's eighty-thousand-dollar debt was never actually cleared, despite what his mother believes.
4. He says nothing about Hae or his relationship with her — this isn't the confession he came to make.
5. Zhao's captain composure returns as she starts weighing Danny as a lead into Sonny's side of the network rather than simply a discipline problem.
6. Noah listens with rising recognition, gaps in his own case files finally explaining themselves.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Noah tells Danny directly that some of his morning "killer" photographs were shaped by information Danny leaked.
2. Danny is stunned, realizing for the first time that his leaks reached further than he ever let himself imagine.
3. Noah feels both vindicated and freshly wounded, the gaps in his files finally making sense.
4. Zhao makes clear that Danny's bid for detective first grade is over, though prosecution is a separate question she's still weighing against what more he could reveal about Sonny.
5. Danny accepts the loss of the promotion almost as an afterthought against the scale of what he's just admitted.
6. Neither Noah nor Zhao yet knows Danny has been seeing Hae for the past three months.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Noah asks Hae to meet him — the first face-to-face sit-down since they separated eight months ago.
2. They sit with cooling coffee between them, the silence heavier than either expected.
3. Hae senses this isn't about the coat or the divorce paperwork, but something larger he's finally ready to say.
4. Noah hesitates, aware that once he says this aloud he can't take it back.
5. Hae's unspoken relationship with Danny sits quietly under the moment for her alone; Noah has no idea it exists.
6. The scene holds on the threshold before the confession, both bracing without naming what for.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Noah admits the investigator was never about an old break-in, correcting the lie he told her weeks earlier.
2. He tells her the investigation has actually been into her late father's death from the start.
3. He confesses he spent fourteen thousand dollars of a discretionary case fund on it without authorization or her knowledge.
4. He reveals the investigator's early findings suggest her father may not have only been a victim of the network, but complicit in it.
5. He admits he hid this because he couldn't bring himself to say it once he read the notes.
6. Hae hears the second lie stacked on the first, the accusation against her father landing before she can process either.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Hae's first instinct is to stand up and walk out of the booth entirely.
2. She stays anyway, her body rigid but rooted to the seat.
3. She doesn't tell Noah the real reason she's staying — that she still hasn't told him about Danny, and leaving now would feel like running from two truths at once.
4. She presses him for exactly what the investigator found, needing specifics rather than vague suspicion.
5. Noah gives her what he has, aware even as he speaks that he still knows nothing about Danny's place in her life.
6. The scene ends unresolved, her fury intact but her chair still pulled up to the table.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Aya's editor pushes her again to name a suspect now that the program story has run, warning the rival reporter is still circling.
2. Aya starts mentally listing every tip her source has ever sent her, checking them against who could plausibly access that level of detail.
3. She realizes the precision and timing only make sense coming from someone at or near command level, not a junior officer.
4. The thought unsettles her, though she doesn't yet let herself land fully on Zhao's name.
5. She decides she needs to test the theory directly rather than keep guessing from her desk.
6. She leaves the newsroom already planning how to corner the person she now suspects.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Aya finds Zhao alone and asks to speak off the record.
2. She lays out the pattern she's noticed — tips too precise, too well-timed, too close to command knowledge.
3. She states her guess plainly: that Zhao has been her anonymous source all along.
4. Zhao doesn't deny it outright, choosing silence over the lie she's used with everyone else.
5. Knowing her command is already being quietly stripped over the story's fallout, Zhao gambles that Aya's hesitation about Noah will buy her more time than a denial would.
6. She doesn't reveal how deliberately she used Aya from the start as a pressure valve, letting Aya believe she stumbled onto this alone.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Word spreads through the precinct that Zhao is being stripped of command pending review over the surveillance program.
2. Kai hears the news secondhand in the bullpen, along with enough detail to finally understand what Panopticon actually is.
3. He learns his own captain built and ran a hidden predictive-surveillance program from the basement for months.
4. He learns in the same breath that his partner has been its unwitting test subject the entire time.
5. Kai's shock curdles quickly into a specific, personal hurt rather than general precinct gossip.
6. He goes looking for Noah immediately, unwilling to let this sit unaddressed.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Kai confronts Noah directly, demanding to know if he knew about Panopticon this whole time.
2. He initially assumes Noah knew and hid it from him too, same as the ledger lie and the confidential-informant excuse before it.
3. Noah's genuine shock at the accusation registers immediately as real, not performed.
4. He tells Kai he only learned the full truth himself earlier that same day, in the basement with Zhao.
5. Kai's anger cools as he realizes his partner was blindsided exactly like he was.
6. The hurt doesn't fully resolve, but the two are left standing on the same side of it rather than opposite.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Noah returns home and stands before the wall of photographs for the first time since learning the full truth.
2. He starts peeling them off one by one, the ritual of guessing at a hidden watcher finally ending.
3. He reflects that the case is still technically open, even though the reason he built this wall no longer holds.
4. He sets the peeled photographs into a stack rather than throwing them away, unable to discard six weeks of obsession outright.
5. He thinks of Feng, Zhao, Danny, and Hae in quick succession, aware how many people were keeping something from him at once.
6. The bare wall behind the last photograph looks stranger to him than the crowded one ever did.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. At dawn, Noah's phone buzzes one more time, the same ritual he thought he'd just ended.
2. The image that loads is different from every one before it: no timestamp, no composited face, just an empty street.
3. He doesn't recognize the location at first glance, unable to place it among anywhere familiar.
4. He checks the file for metadata out of old habit and finds none, unlike Panopticon's usual server-ping signature.
5. He can't tell yet whether this came from the same system, from someone else entirely, or from no system at all.
6. The audience is left with the same uncertainty Noah is, the season's central threat reopening exactly where it seemed to have closed.
