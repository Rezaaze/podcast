--- SCENE 1 ---
1. This is the forty-second consecutive morning since the photographs began, roughly six weeks ago — the season's timeline starts here, at the tail end of an established ritual, not its beginning.
2. At dawn, Noah's phone buzzes with today's image: an unfamiliar face composited over his own body, arriving exactly as it has every morning since.
3. He carries it to the wall and pins it beside the other forty-plus photographs, extending the grid by one more day.
4. He studies the new face for a moment, searching for recognition, and finds none yet.
5. He logs the date and time on the back before leaving for work, treating the ritual as data collection rather than panic.
6. The sheer scale of the wall — weeks of mornings, all unbroken — shows an obsession that has quietly hardened into routine.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. At the precinct, Kai clocks Noah's exhaustion — the dark circles, the distracted stare — and calls him out on it.
2. Noah deflects, blaming the current homicide case's workload for his rough night.
3. He hides the real cause: hours spent cross-referencing photographs instead of sleeping.
4. Kai lets the excuse stand but privately files away that something feels off.
5. Kai remains completely unaware the photographs exist at all.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. In the precinct's underground garage, away from cameras, Danny meets his contact Sonny.
2. Danny hands over details on Noah's active homicide case.
3. In exchange, Sonny confirms a fresh reduction to the Wu family's debt.
4. Danny tells himself this is harmless — a private arrangement that hurts no one.
5. He has no idea Sonny reports to the same corrupt network implicated in Feng's son's murder.
6. He hides this meeting completely from Noah, Kai, and his mother.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone at night, Noah lays six weeks of photographs out chronologically beside his case notebook.
2. He cross-references each face against the people he crossed paths with the day before each photo arrived.
3. A pattern emerges: the face in each photo always matches someone he saw most recently.
4. He concludes that whoever is doing this is reacting to his own movements in real time.
5. This is correct as far as it goes, but he wrongly assumes a person is watching him rather than an algorithm recalculating from his actions.
6. He has no suspicion of Zhao, Feng, or anything called Panopticon.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Zhao runs the morning briefing on the week's homicide case, brisk and clipped as always.
2. She assigns tasks to Noah and Kai with no deviation from routine procedure.
3. She gives no indication that a hidden system she controls has already calculated tomorrow's photograph.
4. She quietly reads Noah's fatigue during the briefing, aware of its cause though she says nothing.
5. Neither Noah nor Kai has any reason to suspect her involvement.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Noah and Kai examine the week's homicide victim together, trading dark jokes to manage the grimness of the work.
2. Noah tries to stay focused, but his mind repeatedly drifts back to his own morning photograph.
3. Kai notices him zoning out and assumes it's ordinary case fatigue.
4. Noah catches himself and forces his attention back to the evidence, hiding where his thoughts actually went.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Noah visits Feng and shows him today's photograph, seeking the comfort their talks usually bring.
2. Feng studies the image a beat too long, instantly recognizing it as his own system's output.
3. He says nothing about that recognition, offering only gentle, noncommittal reassurance.
4. He hides that he built the very machine generating the image now stalking his friend.
5. Noah reads only comfort in Feng's response, unaware of the weight behind Feng's hesitation.
6. Feng's silence is driven by guilt and a belief that staying close is the safest way to protect Noah without reopening his own son's death.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Over diner coffee, Kai gently asks whether Noah's heard anything from Hae.
2. Noah deflects, steering the conversation elsewhere.
3. He hides how often he still checks his phone hoping to see her name.
4. Kai senses the sore spot and doesn't press further.
5. The moment underscores that the divorce is still unfinalized and the wound still raw.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone at home, Hae straightens a framed photo of her late father, lost in thought.
2. She quietly thinks about Noah's unanswered calls, unwilling to admit to herself she's hoping for one.
3. Her phone lights up with Danny's name, interrupting the thought.
4. The moment reveals unresolved feelings for Noah she isn't voicing even privately.
5. She answers, shifting toward the steadier, simpler dynamic she has with Danny.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. In the restaurant's back office, Danny and Hae share a quiet dinner together.
2. Danny repeatedly checks his phone, visibly unsettled from the earlier meeting with Sonny.
3. Hae notices his nervousness but chooses not to push him about it.
4. Neither mentions Noah aloud, though both are privately aware of the space he still occupies.
5. Danny hides the true source of his stress — the debt, the leaks, all of it — from Hae completely.

--- SCENE 11 ---

2. The eighty-thousand-dollar inherited debt is still there on the page, barely reduced despite Sonny's forgiveness.
3. He reflects that his mother believes this debt was cleared a year ago — a belief he has deliberately let stand.
4. He weighs the incremental relief against the risk he's taking, still not knowing who Sonny actually answers to.
5. He closes the books having decided, again, to keep the truth from his mother.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. At the precinct, Danny and Noah trade competitive, joking barbs about the open detective-first-grade slot.
2. Danny's charm covers how urgently he now needs that promotion, tied directly to his family's debt.
3. Noah treats it as ordinary professional rivalry, unaware of Danny's financial desperation.
4. Noah also has no idea Danny is dating his estranged wife.
5. Danny gives nothing away about Hae, and Noah gives nothing away about his own exhaustion or suspicions.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Aya meets Noah for their eleventh in-person interview since the story began.
2. She gently probes him about the photographs, testing how much he'll disclose.
3. Noah shares more than he probably should, trusting her more than her role as a reporter warrants.
4. Aya notices herself softening toward him personally, which complicates her professional instincts.
5. She holds back from pushing as hard as she normally would, without examining why.

--- SCENE 14 ---

2. She remains convinced the source is a disgruntled junior officer, not Captain Zhao.

--- SCENE 15 ---

2. She checks the hidden Panopticon server, confirming it's still running undetected four months in.
3. She watches Noah's data update in silence, seeing tomorrow's threat calculation take shape in real time.
4. She weighs keeping Noah unwitting a while longer against the risk of the network noticing before her trap is ready.
5. The scene confirms in full what has only been implied: Zhao's complete awareness and control of the program.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Kai shows up at Noah's place unannounced, prompted by concern after the day's odd behavior.
2. He discovers the wall covered in forty-plus pinned photographs for the first time.
3. He's stunned, realizing how far his partner's private obsession has gone without his knowledge.
4. Noah is caught off guard and forced to explain at least part of what Kai is seeing.
5. Kai remains entirely ignorant of Panopticon, Zhao's role, or Feng's involvement — he only sees a partner unraveling.
6. The discovery shifts their dynamic: Kai now knows something is deeply wrong, even without knowing why.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. At midnight, Noah's phone buzzes with the next day's photograph.
2. The face shown belongs to someone he spoke with only hours earlier that same day.
3. This confirms his theory that the pattern always tracks his most recent interactions.
4. His dread deepens at how immediate and personal the connection feels this time.
5. The audience recognizes the true cause — Panopticon recalculating from his own daily actions — while Noah still believes a person is watching and reacting to him.
