--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Uniformed officers and a dockworker discover a body at the Tannery Docks in the early morning.
2. A crime scene tech spots a postcard tucked deliberately near the body, placed rather than dropped.
3. The postcard is addressed, in neat handwriting, to Detective Elias Vance by name.
4. No one at the scene yet grasps the significance beyond an unusually personal touch to a crime scene.
5. The postcard is bagged as evidence before any officer reads its full contents.
6. The audience sees the killer's deliberate staging before any character understands what they're really looking at.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Captain Zhou calls Elias and Lin into her office to assign them the Tannery Docks case.
2. Zhou describes the case in procedural terms: unidentified body, unusual evidence recovered at the scene.
3. Zhou does not yet know the postcard was addressed to Elias personally, working only from a generic evidence-log summary.
4. Elias reacts to the assignment with controlled interest, giving no outward sign he already suspects something is different about this one.
5. Lin watches his reaction closely, sensing he's more focused than the briefing alone would explain.
6. Zhou names Elias lead investigator, unknowingly handing the killer's intended target the case directly.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Elias and Lin examine the postcard together at the evidence table back at the precinct.
2. Elias reads his own name on the card and goes quiet for a beat too long.
3. Forensics confirms no usable prints or fibers were recovered from the postcard.
4. Lin notes there's no return address and asks what the verse on the card actually says.
5. Elias paraphrases only part of the verse aloud, quietly withholding the rest even from her.
6. Elias privately decides not to flag the personal-address detail prominently when he reports up to Zhou.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Mira finds Elias already awake and half-dressed before their usual morning routine has started.
2. Elias mentions the docks case only in the vaguest terms, offering no real details.
3. Mira notices him check his phone twice in under a minute, turning slightly away from her each time.
4. Elias leaves earlier than usual, kissing her goodbye distractedly rather than the way he normally does.
5. Mira stands alone in the kitchen a moment after he's gone, unable to name exactly what felt off.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Elias and Lin grab coffee and trade early theories about the body and the killer's method.
2. Lin floats the idea that the postcard is a taunt aimed at the department in general.
3. Elias lets her believe that, without correcting her assumption that it wasn't aimed at him specifically.
4. Lin asks again, lightly, what the verse says in full, and Elias deflects with a joke about still working it out.
5. Lin lets it go rather than push, telling herself she doesn't want to overstep this early in their partnership.
6. The audience sees Elias's deflection land exactly as intended, pulling him further into a private contest.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Elias waits until the bullpen empties before pulling the postcard back out at his desk.
2. He reads the verse over several times, isolating a phrase he doesn't recognize.
3. He starts a private notebook page for the case that isn't part of the official file.
4. He says almost nothing aloud, just a few muttered words as he circles the unfamiliar phrase.
5. The audience recognizes the verse as bait engineered to be traceable only through one specific channel.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Elias brings the isolated phrase to Boru's shop, framing it as a professional consultation rather than part of an active case.
2. He doesn't mention the postcard is evidence in a murder, presenting his question as idle curiosity.
3. Boru recognizes the phrase instantly but takes a deliberate beat before answering, as though searching his memory.
4. Boru offers a partial answer, enough to feel genuinely useful but not enough to close the loop.
5. Elias leaves feeling he's made real progress, unaware he's been handed exactly what was meant for him.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Boru chats warmly with Elias, asking easy, disarming questions about the case that read as harmless interest.
2. Elias, without quite deciding to, shares more than he intended to about the postcard.
3. A small detail lands wrong for Elias — a phrase or reaction of Boru's that doesn't quite fit — but he can't place why.
4. Boru notices the flicker of unease and smoothly redirects the conversation before it can take root.
5. The audience sees Boru's practiced control beneath the warmth, invisible to Elias.
6. Elias leaves trusting Boru more than he did an hour earlier, exactly as engineered.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Regular customers move through Han's Grocery on an unremarkable afternoon.
2. A radio or overheard conversation carries a brief mention of the body found at the docks, treated as neighborhood gossip.
3. The ordinariness of the scene sits in deliberate contrast to the quiet machinery running elsewhere in town.
4. Nothing here connects directly to the main case yet; it exists only to ground the world the murder has entered.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Elias and Mira sit down to dinner, and he answers her questions a half-beat too late throughout.
2. Mira asks about his day and he gives a vague, generic answer that avoids the case entirely.
3. His phone buzzes on the table; he glances at it and silences it without explaining who it was.
4. Mira watches this happen and says nothing, filing it away rather than asking directly.
5. Elias, unaware of how the silence reads to her, believes he's protecting her by not burdening her with the case.
6. The audience sees the gap widening between what Elias intends and what Mira is actually experiencing.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Lin reviews the case file and notices gaps where Elias's notes should be more detailed.
2. She considers asking him directly whether he's working an angle he hasn't shared.
3. She decides against it, not wanting to seem like she's questioning his lead on the case so early.
4. She tells herself he's just being thorough — a plausible explanation that happens to be wrong.
5. The audience sees Lin choose trust over suspicion, unaware Elias is already working the case partly alone.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Elias returns to the docks alone, well after his shift has ended, without telling anyone where he's going.
2. He walks the scene slowly, re-examining details already logged and photographed.
3. He connects the postcard's placement to the layout of the scene, refining his read on the killer's precision.
4. He doesn't call Lin or Mira to mention where he is, treating the visit as private rather than procedural.
5. The audience senses how much of Elias's night hours are now being quietly consumed by this one case.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mira calls her sister that evening, needing to talk to someone about the strange distance she's feeling.
2. She struggles to describe specifics — no fights, no evidence of anything, just a persistent wrongness.
3. Her sister asks the obvious question: whether Mira thinks Elias might be seeing someone else.
4. Mira doesn't confirm it outright but doesn't dismiss it either, and the suspicion settles a little deeper in her.
5. Mira decides not to say any of this to Elias directly, choosing to keep watching instead.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Zhou notices Elias's badge-swipe log shows late-night entries and exits well past his shift.
2. She catches him in the hallway and asks, casually but pointedly, how late he's been working the docks case.
3. Elias downplays it, framing the extra hours as normal diligence rather than a private obsession.
4. Zhou lets the explanation stand for now but makes a mental note to keep an eye on him.
5. The audience sees the first outside crack forming in Elias's ability to keep the case compartmentalized.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. After locking the shop door, Boru moves to a back room he keeps closed to customers.
2. He handles a small keepsake connected to his late son Daniel with practiced, careful reverence.
3. He reviews materials tied to the next clue meant for Elias, arranging them with total precision.
4. His manner is calm rather than triumphant, more grief than malice in how he goes about the task.
5. The audience alone understands this ritual as the private engine behind everything happening to Elias.
6. Nothing in the scene is stated aloud that would confirm his guilt to any other character.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Elias comes home very late, and Mira is still awake, waiting by the door rather than in bed.
2. She asks where he's been, and he gives an answer that is technically true but leaves out everything that matters.
3. Mira's questions grow sharper, though she still stops short of naming her real fear directly.
4. Elias, reading her tension as suspicion about the hours rather than about faithfulness, reassures her in a way that doesn't address what she's actually afraid of.
5. The exchange ends without resolution, both of them retreating rather than closing the gap between them.
6. The audience recognizes the exact miscommunication at the heart of the marriage thread crystallizing in this moment.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Alone in the precinct well past midnight, Elias finally connects the verse to its source text with certainty.
2. He feels a surge of satisfaction at solving what no one else has even started working on.
3. A small detail in his solution stirs a flicker of doubt — how neatly it all lines up, how easy the trail was to follow.
4. He glances around the empty bullpen, suddenly certain, without evidence, that he's being watched or tested.
5. He dismisses the feeling as exhaustion rather than instinct, and returns to his notes.
6. The audience recognizes his unease as the first crack of true insight into the trap closing around him.
