--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Zhou takes a call from someone above him relaying public complaints and pressure to close the Han case fast.
2. He hears the demand for a resolution phrased as concern, but recognizes it as impatience wearing a polite mask.
3. He does not share the substance of this call with Elias or Lin yet, keeping the pressure his own to carry.
4. He privately weighs how thin the evidence against Han still is, against how expensive it would be to admit that now.
5. The audience sees the clock on Han's arrest start moving before anyone in the room knows it has.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Elias sits down with the case file he intends to use to justify moving on Han and reads back through it.
2. He arranges the file's strongest pieces up front and lets the weaker ones sit further back, unremarked.
3. He tells himself this is just organizing a persuasive document, not shaping the truth to fit a conclusion.
4. Reading his own file back, he finds it more convincing than the raw evidence actually supports.
5. The audience sees a file built to persuade, mistaken by its own author for a file built to prove.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Boru finalizes the wording of a new clue, checking it line by line against what he knows of Elias's mind.
2. He recognizes this phrasing sits closer to the plain truth than anything he's planted before.
3. He weighs the risk of being read correctly against the cost of Elias's attention going slack, and chooses the risk.
4. He confirms, satisfied, that no one else on the case would parse the reference the way Elias will.
5. He arranges for the clue to be found rather than delivered directly, keeping his own hand invisible.
6. The audience learns this "discovery" is a calculated gamble, not a break in the case.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Elias and Lin are called to a scene near the water where the newest clue has been left with a body.
2. Lin photographs and logs the physical clue, noting its material details without comment.
3. Elias's first instinct on seeing it is to slot it immediately into the Han theory he's already committed to.
4. Lin feels a small, immediate wrongness in the phrasing but says nothing yet in front of the responding officers.
5. The audience sees the clue enter the case exactly as Boru intended, undetected as a plant.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Back at the precinct, Elias and Lin sit down together to read the new clue's language closely.
2. Elias argues aloud that the phrasing can still be stretched to fit Han, if you read it loosely enough.
3. Lin reads it plainly and states that, read straight, it doesn't point to Han at all.
4. Elias pushes back hard and fast, treating her reading as a distraction rather than a real possibility.
5. Lin backs off from pressing it further with him, though she doesn't actually change her own reading.
6. The audience sees the plain truth of the clue rejected by the one person building the case around it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone afterward, Lin works the clue's specific references without telling Elias what she's doing.
2. She traces the materials and phrasing to a narrow supplier: a specialty antiquarian bookshop.
3. She doesn't bring this to Elias or Zhou yet, uncertain if it's a real lead or her own bias against Elias's certainty.
4. She decides to visit the shop herself before saying a word to anyone.
5. The audience watches her alternate lead take a concrete, physical shape for the first time.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. The next morning, Mira is drained from sitting outside the darkened shopfront the night before.
2. She replays what she saw: Elias entering and leaving through a side door like it was routine, nothing more.
3. She tries to fit a shop visit into her affair theory and finds it doesn't sit cleanly, no woman, no obvious tryst.
4. Rather than question the theory itself, she assumes she's just missing the full picture, not that the picture is wrong.
5. The audience holds the gap between how close she came to the truth and how far her interpretation still is from it.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Elias comes home that evening with no idea Mira followed him the night before.
2. Mira has a clear opening to ask him directly about the shop and lets it pass.
3. She asks something adjacent instead, vague enough not to reveal what she knows, and watches his face for a reaction.
4. Elias, unaware anything is being tested, answers normally, missing that the question was a trap he can't see.
5. The audience sees her choose continued surveillance over confrontation, again.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Han and his wife sit down after closing to decide whether to keep the shop open through the harassment.
2. Their daughter says plainly she wants to leave, and neither parent has a real answer for her.
3. Han argues for staying, still believing his years in the community and his innocence will keep them safe.
4. His wife argues that staying open is inviting more of what happened to the window, not protection from it.
5. They settle nothing, agreeing only to decide again tomorrow.
6. The audience sees a family holding on by a decision that resets every single day.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Zhou and Elias meet again, tension from their last conversation still unresolved between them.
2. Zhou finally voices the institutional pressure from above, telling Elias the department wants this closed soon.
3. Elias treats the pressure as confirmation he should push harder, not as a reason for caution.
4. Zhou raises the weak financial evidence again; Elias dismisses it as Han simply being careful.
5. Neither backs down, and the meeting ends with more distance between them than before.
6. The audience sees Zhou's private doubt and Elias's certainty collide openly for the first time.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Alone afterward, Zhou weighs the institutional pressure against his own private doubts about the case.
2. He decides the pressure from above outweighs what he privately believes about the evidence.
3. He tells himself signing off now still leaves room to slow things down later if needed.
4. He does not consult Lin before making this decision, unaware she has anything new to offer.
5. The audience sees Zhou choose institutional momentum over the doubt he's kept to himself for weeks.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Boru moves through his shop with deliberate calm, straightening stock that doesn't need straightening.
2. He mentally rehearses the cover story he'd give if anyone asked pointed questions about his sources.
3. He allows himself a private thought that the larger design is nearing its final, necessary movements.
4. He checks the back door and the shop's sightlines, an old habit sharpened rather than nervous.
5. The audience senses a man readying himself for scrutiny he expects may be coming.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Lin enters the shop under a plain research pretext, asking about materials tied to the new clue.
2. Boru listens closely to how much she already seems to know before answering anything.
3. He offers precise, helpful-sounding expertise, giving her just enough to seem cooperative.
4. Internally, he registers her visit as the closest anyone investigating has come to him directly.
5. He keeps his true identity and his connection to the killings completely hidden from her.
6. The audience watches him control the exchange while Lin has no idea who she's actually speaking to.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Boru answers Lin's specific question in a way that satisfies it without opening any further thread.
2. Lin leaves believing she's spoken to a helpful, slightly odd bystander with useful knowledge, nothing more.
3. She doesn't connect anything she's learned to danger, filing the visit as background research completed.
4. Once she's gone, Boru privately judges the risk fully contained, more confident than the encounter actually earned him.
5. The audience sees how narrowly the case just missed him, and how little either of them registered it.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Elias brings his prepared case file to Zhou, arguing forcefully that it's time to move on Han.
2. He leaves out the new clue's plain contradiction, folding it into the file's weaker, buried section instead.
3. He leans on the public pressure Zhou mentioned earlier, using it as leverage for urgency rather than caution.
4. Zhou listens, his private doubts still unspoken, and doesn't challenge the file's gaps hard enough to stop it.
5. The audience sees Elias actively steering the case toward Han rather than following where the evidence points.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Alone that night, Mira takes out the folded note again and turns Elias's deflection over in her mind.
2. She considers finally telling him what she saw outside the shop and decides against it, not ready for his answer.
3. She weighs going back to the shop herself in daylight, to see it as more than a dark, unreadable building.
4. She refolds the note and puts it away again, choosing to keep watching rather than to speak.
5. The audience sees her settle deeper into silence, running in parallel to Elias's own.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Zhou formally signs the order authorizing Han's arrest, despite the reservations he never voiced aloud.
2. Elias receives the order and feels it as vindication, proof his instincts were right all along.
3. Word reaches Lin that the arrest is moving forward now, faster than she expected.
4. She realizes, too late, that she believed she still had time to bring her bookshop lead forward and didn't.
5. The audience sees the irreversible step taken, closing the window every character believed was still open.
