--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Mira finds a folded note in unfamiliar handwriting in Elias's coat pocket while doing the laundry.
2. She reads it: odd phrasing, a reference or address she doesn't recognize, no name attached.
3. She checks the coat's other pockets for anything that might explain it and finds nothing.
4. She hears Elias in another room and refolds the note quickly instead of confronting him with it in hand.
5. She slips the note into her own pocket rather than putting it back where she found it.
6. The audience knows this note is a fragment of Elias's secret exchange with the killer, information Mira doesn't have.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Alone later, Mira rereads the note and works through every innocent explanation she can think of, discarding each one.
2. She settles on the explanation that fits what she's already been noticing: another woman.
3. She lines up the cancellations, the vagueness, the late nights, and slots them all into this new theory.
4. She decides not to confront him yet, choosing to watch him first and gather more before forcing it.
5. She hides the note somewhere he won't find it, keeping her own secret now too.
6. The audience holds the gap between what she believes and what's actually true.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Lin opens her private notebook and starts a new page tracking irregularities specifically tied to the Han warrant.
2. She lists the dates Elias pushed for broader authority against her own stated objections.
3. She notes how each escalation lined up with a new killing, a pattern she's uneasy naming out loud.
4. She catches herself already rehearsing how she'd justify keeping this list private if anyone asked.
5. The audience sees her private record splitting into a second, parallel thread aimed at the same person.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Elias reviews the financial records pulled under the new warrant, searching for a clear link between Han and the killings.
2. The records show nothing but ordinary grocery transactions and unremarkable finances.
3. He rereads the same figures repeatedly, looking for an angle instead of accepting there isn't one.
4. He tells himself the connection must exist somewhere he hasn't looked yet, rather than questioning the premise.
5. Lin watches him do this and says nothing, though she recognizes exactly what she's seeing.
6. The audience sees the case refusing to bend into the shape Elias already decided it has.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Lin has the words ready to tell Elias the records clear Han rather than implicate him.
2. She watches him keep hunting for an angle anyway and decides this isn't the moment to press it.
3. She thinks of her notebook, a drawer away, and how close she is to needing it.
4. She lets the silence stand, telling herself she'll raise it once she has something more concrete.
5. The audience sees another piece of truth she's chosen not to speak aloud.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Boru locks the shop's front door early and turns the sign to closed for a client who enters through the back.
2. He speaks to this visitor in a register different from the one he uses with Elias — precise, unhurried, controlled.
3. The content of the exchange stays vague to the audience, but Boru's calm command of it is unmistakable.
4. He mentions timing, patience, and something coming to its proper conclusion soon.
5. The audience is left unsure who this client is, but senses this meeting matters more than any of Elias's visits.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Elias returns to Boru's shop under the same research label he's used before, though the official case thread has gone quiet.
2. He brings up the newest postcard's phrasing without naming it directly as such.
3. Boru offers careful, guiding answers that steer Elias's thinking without ever seeming to lead it.
4. Elias leaves feeling he's gained insight, unaware how much of it Boru placed there on purpose.
5. The audience watches the label "research" stretch further past anything it can honestly cover.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Han locks up for the night and finds the financial-records notice confirming the broader warrant is now active.
2. He sits with his wife and explains what the new paperwork means for the business and their finances.
3. Their daughter asks again why this is happening, and neither parent has an answer that satisfies her.
4. His wife raises her voice for the first time, frustrated at his insistence on carrying it all alone.
5. He admits quietly that he doesn't know how much more he can absorb while still keeping up the calm front.
6. The audience sees the family fraying under a warrant built on nothing real.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Zhou finds Lin alone in the break room and starts to ask if something's been bothering her lately.
2. Lin hesitates, her notebook a thought away, and comes closer than ever to telling him everything.
3. An interruption breaks the moment before either of them commits to the real question.
4. Both let the interruption end the conversation without pushing back against it.
5. The audience sees the closest near-miss yet between the two separate threads of doubt.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Later, Zhou reviews the outside inquiry about the Han case again, alone in his office.
2. Down the hall, Lin adds another entry to her notebook, alone at her desk.
3. Neither knows the other is doing almost exactly the same thing at almost exactly the same time.
4. Each privately decides again that it isn't yet time to bring the concern to the other.
5. The audience sees the two threads of doubt run parallel once more, close enough to touch and still not touching.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Alone, Elias rereads the newest postcard's verse and works out a response he believes only the killer will understand.
2. He writes it carefully, telling himself the act is investigative bait rather than participation.
3. He keeps the draft out of his case file, exactly as before, and prepares to send it through the same private channel.
4. He allows himself a private satisfaction at solving the reference, missing what accepting the exchange again actually means.
5. The audience sees him step deeper into the game while still believing he's the one setting its terms.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Mira, having watched Elias for days without anything that eases her theory, decides she can't wait any longer.
2. She takes out the note and asks him directly where it came from and who wrote it.
3. Elias's face changes for a moment before he catches himself, a reaction Mira clearly registers.
4. She watches him closely, waiting to see whether he'll actually tell her the truth.
5. The audience feels the first real crack in the space Elias has kept sealed off from her.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Elias claims the note is nothing more than case paperwork, unrelated to anything personal.
2. He offers no detail about which case, keeping the explanation deliberately thin enough to end the conversation.
3. Mira presses once, asking why paperwork would be handwritten and folded like something private.
4. He deflects again, citing exhaustion and case pressure to close the subject.
5. She lets it drop for now, though nothing in her has actually settled.
6. The audience knows exactly how far short this answer falls of the truth.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Zhou and Elias meet at Ruby's diner, ostensibly to review case progress away from the precinct.
2. Zhou raises the fact that the financial records turned up nothing and asks what Elias intends to do next.
3. Elias pushes back, arguing the absence of evidence just means Han has been careful, not that he's innocent.
4. Zhou's patience audibly thins, reminding him the department can't keep spending resources chasing a theory that isn't proving out.
5. The conversation ends unresolved, both leaving with more tension between them than either states outright.
6. The audience sees the first open friction between Zhou's caution and Elias's certainty.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Han arrives at the shop to find hateful words scrawled across the front window in paint, larger and more direct than before.
2. A small crowd of neighbors and onlookers has already gathered, watching rather than helping.
3. He starts scrubbing alone, refusing help offered again by the same neighbor as before.
4. His wife arrives partway through and stands beside him this time without being asked.
5. The audience sees the frame's toll move from private fear into public spectacle.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Mira replays Elias's answer about the note and finds she doesn't believe a word of it.
2. She weighs confronting him again against finding out the truth herself first.
3. She decides to follow him the next time he leaves without explanation, rather than ask again and hear another deflection.
4. She tells herself she isn't spying, only finally getting an answer he won't give her directly.
5. The audience sees her shift from passive watching to active pursuit of the truth.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Elias leaves the house that evening without saying where he's going, and Mira follows in her own car.
2. She keeps a careful distance, staying three car lengths back through the turns toward the old part of town.
3. He parks outside a small shop she doesn't recognize and lets himself in through a side door like someone who's done it many times before.
4. She sits outside with the engine off, staring at the darkened shopfront, no closer to an answer than before, only a new question.
5. The audience recognizes the shop as Boru's, watching two separate threads of the story converge without either character realizing it yet.
