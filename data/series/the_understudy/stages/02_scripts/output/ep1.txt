--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
In a city where the fog off the docks never quite lifts, a brilliant detective receives a postcard with no return address — a single line of verse, and a promise that more will follow. What begins as an irresistible intellectual challenge becomes something else entirely: a season-long descent in which the hunter forgets, one compromise at a time, that he was never the one setting the terms of the game.

[NARRATOR]
The First Postcard. Tannery Docks, before dawn, fog thick over the water. A dockworker's shift ends in a discovery no one expects.

[SFX: a boat horn, distant, low]

[NARRATOR]
Uniformed officers cordon the pier while a technician works the ground beside the body.

[SFX: a camera shutter clicking rapid-fire]

[NARRATOR]
Near the victim's hand, tucked beneath a coil of rope, a postcard lies, dry despite the damp planks — placed, not dropped. Neat handwriting. A name. Detective Elias Vance.

[SFX: a glove snapping as it's pulled on]

[NARRATOR]
No one here understands what they're holding. The card is bagged, sealed, unread beyond that single line, carried into the gathering light — the first move in a game only one man was chosen to play.
