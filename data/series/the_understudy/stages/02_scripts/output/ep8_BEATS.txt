--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Dawn reveals fresh graffiti sprayed across Han's grocery shutters, an accusation visible to the whole street.
2. A neighbor films it and posts it online before any official statement is issued.
3. Patrol logs the vandalism as a second overnight incident, filed alongside last night's still-unread report.
4. Passersby read the graffiti as confirmation of guilt rather than the product of a leak and rumor.
5. No one connects the escalating public punishment to evidence that was never Han's to begin with.
6. The audience sees the frame's damage compounding on a family with no way to answer it.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Han's wife scrubs at the paint without fully removing it, leaving a ghost of the word underneath.
2. Their daughter offers to help and is told to go back inside, shielded from the worst of it.
3. A regular customer crosses the street rather than approach, and Han's wife registers exactly who.
4. She calculates aloud, to no one, how many days the shutters can stay down before they lose the lease.
5. She still doesn't know the supplier arrangement is what actually put Han near the docks, only what he's told her.
6. The audience watches a family absorb consequences built entirely on evidence planted by a stranger none of them has met.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Han's court-appointed lawyer requests the full evidence log, standard procedure escalated by public attention.
2. Reviewing timestamps, the lawyer finds a chain-of-custody gap on the watch that doesn't add up.
3. The lawyer visits Han to lay out the discrepancy and ask what he was actually doing near the docks that night.
4. Han, weighing the supplier secret against a murder charge, deflects again without naming the real reason.
5. The lawyer, working with an incomplete answer, decides the timestamp gap alone is enough to file formally.
6. The audience knows the gap is real and points toward Han's innocence, even though his own evasion keeps muddying it.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Zhou brings the defense's formal filing on the timestamp gap directly to Elias.
2. Elias frames the gap as routine paperwork sloppiness, not a hole in the case itself.
3. He downplays the doubt he privately felt during the raid, presenting only certainty to Zhou.
4. Zhou accepts the framing on the surface while something in his own gut doesn't fully settle.
5. Elias doesn't mention that Han asked him directly whether the watch was truly found in the store.
6. The audience sees Elias protecting the case by minimizing exactly the doubt he can't afford to examine.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Lin asks Zhou back to the same diner booth, this time with her written log in a closed folder on the table.
2. She doesn't open it yet, watching to see whether he'll ask what's inside or wait her out.
3. Zhou mentions the defense's filing, testing whether her count of six shortcuts already includes it.
4. Lin confirms it does, and adds that Elias never disclosed Han's direct question about the watch.
5. Neither says aloud that they're each independently losing confidence in the same case.
6. The audience sees the diner becoming the place where institutional doubt keeps almost surfacing and doesn't.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone that night, Lin rereads her log start to finish for the first time since she began keeping it.
2. She weighs handing it to Zhou whole against carrying it over his head to Internal Affairs.
3. She drafts and deletes a message asking Elias to explain the sixth entry himself first.
4. She admits to herself, briefly, that she's protected him by raising each item one at a time instead of all at once.
5. She closes the folder having decided the delay ends tomorrow, whatever it costs their partnership.
6. The audience sees the private guilt she hides from both Zhou and Elias driving the decision more than procedure does.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Elias returns to Simon's bookshop, telling himself and Simon it's for background reading on the case.
2. Simon welcomes him easily, giving no sign this visit confirms anything to him.
3. Elias talks the case's shape out loud, relieved to have a listener who isn't measuring him for mistakes.
4. Simon steers the conversation with a single well-placed historical reference, watching Elias take it as his own idea.
5. Elias leaves believing he came for research, not admitting even to himself that he came to be heard.
6. The audience alone sees Simon's nudge landing exactly as designed while Elias reads warmth instead of steering.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Elias comes home to find the guest room bed already turned down, an arrangement neither of them announces aloud.
2. Mira offers a practical reason for the change that both of them know isn't the real one.
3. Elias accepts it without pressing, relieved not to have the harder conversation tonight.
4. Alone in the guest room, he registers the new distance but tells himself it's temporary, tied to the case ending.
5. Down the hall, Mira lies awake certain the distance is entirely her doing, not his silence.
6. The audience watches a marriage rearrange its geography around a secret only one side is actually keeping.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Elias stands in front of the case board after hours, rearranging postcard photos into a new sequence.
2. He layers in the reference Simon planted earlier, certain it's his own analytical leap.
3. He reads the emerging pattern as the killer circling toward a final, personal confrontation.
4. He feels something like being respected by a worthy opponent, rather than led.
5. He decides, without telling Lin, to follow this new thread alone before bringing it to the team.
6. The audience sees the trap closing exactly as Simon intended, mistaken by Elias for his own breakthrough.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Lin walks into Zhou's office without an appointment and sets the full log on his desk, closed.
2. She tells him this is everything, all six instances, in her own handwriting, and asks him to actually read it now.
3. Zhou opens it in front of her instead of setting it aside for later.
4. Reading it, he recognizes some entries as things he'd already half-noticed and let go.
5. Lin doesn't say aloud that she fears this ends her partnership with Elias, though it sits behind every word.
6. The audience sees the moment Lin's private record finally becomes something Zhou can no longer un-know.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Zhou calls Elias in and, for the first time, asks pointed questions about specific incidents rather than general reassurance.
2. He references at least one item precisely enough that Elias realizes someone has been documenting him.
3. Elias answers defensively, casting the scrutiny as institutional caution rather than a legitimate concern about his conduct.
4. Zhou doesn't tell him Lin brought the log or what he's decided to do about it yet.
5. Elias leaves rattled but tells himself Zhou is covering for the department, not questioning him specifically.
6. The audience sees Elias misreading the first real institutional consequence as one more obstacle in his way.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Zhou takes a call confirming the DA's office is now openly nervous given the timestamp gap and public scrutiny.
2. He starts quietly assembling an alternate plan in case the case against Han has to be dropped or renegotiated.
3. He does this from his office with the door closed, one wall away from where Elias sits confident in the win.
4. He tells no one, not Elias and not yet Lin, how far the contingency planning has already gone.
5. He still frames it to himself as prudence, not yet as an admission that Han may be innocent.
6. The audience sees the case's collapse being managed in secret while its architect celebrates one room over.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mira meets a colleague from her practice for coffee and, for the first time, says the word affair out loud about her own marriage.
2. She lays out the postcard fragment and matchbook as evidence, watching her friend's face for confirmation.
3. The colleague, without contradicting her, asks what Mira actually wants to do about it.
4. Mira admits she's already looked up a family lawyer, a step she hasn't told Elias about.
5. She hears herself describing a version of Elias that feels certain to her, though it isn't the truth.
6. The audience knows the affair Mira is now treating as confirmed has never happened, and her legal step is a wound about to become irreversible.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Simon closes the shop and sits alone in the back room under a single lamp, surrounded by his own private archive of the case.
2. He reviews Elias's unannounced visit against his private schedule, satisfied it landed exactly as engineered.
3. He allows himself a rare, private acknowledgment of the mentor and institution that broke him decades ago, the true origin of the design.
4. He decides the next material clue can move up, given how quickly Elias is advancing on his own.
5. He prepares the envelope for delivery without any outward change in his ordinary routine.
6. The audience alone sees the full weight of what drives him, still invisible to everyone investigating him.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. An envelope reaches Elias through an untraceable method, consistent with the pattern of prior clues.
2. Lin, present when it arrives, notices its phrasing echoes a piece of local history with no match in any case file.
3. She keeps to herself that she's already quietly begun looking into who in the city would know that history.
4. Elias reads the clue as further evidence of a killer nearing the endgame he's proud to be facing.
5. Neither of them names the possibility that whoever wrote it knows the city, and Elias, personally.
6. The audience recognizes the true origin of the reference in Simon's private history, still unseen by either detective.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Zhou drafts formal paperwork placing Elias on restricted duty pending review of the procedural log.
2. He weighs how to deliver it, choosing to do it in writing before saying anything to Elias's face.
3. He still withholds from the document any mention of the DA's contingency planning or the collapsing Frame case.
4. Lin is told the paperwork is moving, without being told exactly how Zhou plans to frame it to Elias.
5. Zhou tells himself this is accountability, though it's also containment, keeping the fallout inside the building a little longer.
6. The audience sees the first formal consequence taking shape, still hidden from the one person it's about.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Elias sits with the new envelope, mistaking Simon's design for a worthy opponent's respect.
2. Mira lies awake having already taken the legal step Elias doesn't know about, certain of a betrayal that never happened.
3. Lin quietly follows the thread of local history toward an answer she still can't imagine points at Simon.
4. Zhou signs the restricted-duty paperwork he hasn't yet shown Elias, still not telling anyone how deep the DA's case fears go.
5. Han sits behind a closed grocery shutter, still naming the wrong enemy in his own mind.
6. Simon, in the quiet of his shop, allows himself certainty that every wall in the city is now working exactly as he built it.
