[SFX: gloved hands rustling through debris]

[NARRATOR]

[SFX: a small object dropped into a plastic evidence bag]

[NARRATOR]
A matchbook. Then, folded beneath it, a receipt, soft with damp but legible. Both carry the printed header of a neighborhood grocery three blocks inland — Han's Grocery — and the receipt's handwritten total is in a hand a second technician will later match, item for item, to Han Mei's own.

[SFX: a camera shutter clicking rapid-fire]

[NARRATOR]
Photographs are taken before either item is touched again. Neither the matchbook nor the receipt sits anywhere near where the body was found — both rest at the tableau's outer edge, in a spot swept clean enough, and dry enough beneath the litter, that nothing about their position reads as accidental. To the two technicians logging the find, that detail doesn't register as strange. It registers as luck.

[NARRATOR]
The lead technician radios it in as the case's first physical evidence tying a name to the scene — a name, not yet a suspect. Neither technician has any reason to ask why something meant to be found waited three days to be seen, or why it was still readable at all after three days of dock weather. It looks, to every eye that will examine it going forward, exactly like a genuine break. Only one man, working quietly some blocks away with a bookshop's bell above his door, knows otherwise.

[SFX: a car door closing, an engine turning over in the distance]

[NARRATOR]
By the time the evidence bag reaches the precinct that afternoon, it will already be spoken of as the first real lead in the Tannery Docks case.
