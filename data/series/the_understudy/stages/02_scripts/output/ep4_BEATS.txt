--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Zhou grants a limited warrant, authorizing a search of Han's Grocery but withholding the financial-records request for now.
2. She tells Elias this is provisional, contingent on the search turning up something concrete.
3. The case board is updated with Han's photo alongside the docks tableau, making his name an official part of the investigation for the first time.
4. Lin watches the board update with visible unease she doesn't voice in front of Zhou.
5. Elias reads the partial approval as full validation, already treating Han as good as confirmed.
6. The audience sees Zhou's caution flattened by Elias's certainty before the search has even started.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Elias returns to the second clue's trail, the physical evidence pointing toward a specific kind of antiquarian restoration work.
2. He cross-references restoration suppliers and narrows the list to a handful of specialty shops, one of them Boru's.
3. He tells himself this is a separate thread from Han, unrelated busywork he can chase alone.
4. He doesn't log this line of inquiry in the shared case file, keeping it in his private notebook instead.
5. The audience recognizes the trail was engineered to lead exactly here, though Elias believes he found it himself.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Zhou pulls the records request Elias filed and compares it against the official case file, finding it still doesn't fully match.
2. She considers raising it formally, then decides again to ask Elias about it herself, quietly, first.
3. She catches him before he leaves and asks directly what the pull was for.
4. Elias gives a partial, technically true answer that omits the antiquarian angle entirely.
5. Zhou accepts it for now, though something in his answer doesn't fully settle her.
6. The audience sees Zhou's private concern harden by one more degree, still unshared with Lin.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Han opens the shop again, scrubbing a fresh trace of last night's paint from the window before customers arrive.
2. He greets his regulars with the same practiced warmth, giving no sign of what's happened.
3. A regular customer asks, carefully, if everything is alright, and Han deflects with a joke.
4. His wife notices him checking the street through the window more than usual.
5. The audience watches an ordinary morning start to fray at the edges under pressure only Han can feel.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Elias arrives at Boru's shop for his second visit, framing it as routine case research into rare book restoration.
2. Boru greets him warmly, as if pleased to see a familiar face rather than an investigator.
3. Elias asks technical questions about archival paper and ink, taking notes for a case file he isn't fully keeping current.
4. Boru answers precisely, guiding Elias's understanding just enough to point toward his own back-room work without naming it.
5. Boru privately notes how naturally Elias has begun returning to him for answers.
6. The audience sees Elias walking further into the trap while believing he is simply doing his job.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Mira sets the table for the anniversary dinner Elias promised he wouldn't miss again.
2. He calls to cancel at the last minute, citing case pressure without specifics.
3. She sits alone at the set table for a moment before clearing it away.
4. She doesn't call him back to argue, choosing silence over another circular conversation.
5. The audience feels the weight of an absence Elias doesn't register as abandonment.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Elias comes home late, and Mira asks only where he's been, keeping her tone even.
2. He answers vaguely about the case, offering nothing about the bookshop or the postcard.
3. He notices her guardedness but reads it as tiredness rather than suspicion.
4. He tells himself he's protecting her by keeping the details away from her.
5. Neither says the word actually on both their minds, for entirely different reasons.
6. The audience holds both misunderstandings at once, knowing neither party has the full picture.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Boru sends a small gift to the precinct for Elias, a rare pamphlet tied to a case detail Elias mentioned in passing.
2. The gesture reads as thoughtful generosity to everyone who sees it arrive.
3. Elias is touched by the courtesy and feels a private obligation to Boru forming.
4. Lin remarks that it's unusual for a witness or informal contact to send gifts, and Elias brushes the comment aside.
5. The audience recognizes the gift as another thread tightening, dressed as kindness.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Zhou asks Elias directly, in front of Lin this time, what the bookshop visits are actually for.
2. Elias frames them as research into the clue's origin, technically true but incomplete.
3. Lin asks a simple follow-up about how many visits there have been, and Elias undercounts without quite lying outright.
4. He feels the word "research" settle over something that no longer feels professional to him alone.
5. The audience sees him choosing a label that lets him keep going without calling it what it is.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Lin sits with her own notes after hours, listing dates where Elias skipped consultation or filed late.
2. She adds today's undercounted visits to the same list without telling anyone.
3. She notices the pattern is no longer occasional, now closer to constant.
4. She still doesn't take the notes to Zhou, telling herself she needs more before raising it formally.
5. The audience sees her private record growing heavier than her spoken concern lets on.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Zhou finds a moment alone with Lin and asks, carefully, how she thinks Elias is holding up.
2. Lin gives a measured, mostly honest answer, but stops short of mentioning her notes.
3. Zhou doesn't mention his own records concern either, each protecting a version of loyalty to Elias.
4. Both leave the conversation quietly relieved not to have gone further.
5. The audience sees two separate, aligned suspicions fail to connect for a second time.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. A new body is found staged with the same deliberate care as the first tableau, another literary clue placed among it.
2. The clue is more advanced than the last, assuming knowledge only someone already deep in the pattern would have.
3. Elias recognizes the escalation immediately and feels the private thrill of being addressed directly again.
4. Lin notes the killer is accelerating, and asks whether that's about opportunity or about Elias specifically.
5. The audience watches Boru's timetable move forward exactly as he intended.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Elias uses the new murder to argue for the full financial-records warrant on Han that Zhou withheld before.
2. He argues the frequency of killings means they can't afford another round of caution.
3. Lin repeats her standing objection that nothing ties Han to this new scene at all.
4. Elias counters that a broader picture of Han's finances could still close the gap Lin keeps raising.
5. The audience hears him reaching for evidence to fit a conclusion he's already settled on.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Zhou weighs the new killing against her earlier caution and feels the pressure to be seen doing something.
2. She raises the thin evidentiary grounds one more time, out loud, for the record.
3. Elias's confidence and the mounting body count tip her decision toward approval.
4. She signs the full warrant without voicing her remaining reservations to anyone in the room.
5. The audience sees the system bend exactly the way it once failed to protect Boru's son.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. A neighbor offers to help Han scrub the vandalism and stand with him publicly against the rumors.
2. Han refuses the offer, not wanting to draw more attention to himself or his family.
3. His wife quietly wishes he'd accepted, seeing the isolation growing around them.
4. Han tells himself keeping his head down is the safest way through this.
5. The audience sees an innocent man decline the one gesture that might have eased what's coming.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Overnight, the vandalism returns, larger this time and impossible to fully scrub away before opening.
2. A customer cancels a standing order without explanation, the first concrete business loss.
3. Han's daughter asks why people are saying things about her father, and he has no good answer for her.
4. He finally tells his wife the full truth about the old immigration paperwork, afraid of what's coming next.
5. The audience sees the frame's cost move from reputation into his family's daily life.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. A new postcard arrives for Elias, its verse referencing details of the second body that were never released publicly.
2. Elias realizes the killer knows something only someone close to the investigation could know, and feels real fear for the first time.
3. He debates, alone, whether this postcard is safe to bring to Zhou in full or must be managed like the others.
4. He conceals from Lin exactly how much the verse unsettles him, keeping his reaction unreadable in front of her.
5. The audience is left with a sharper question hanging as the episode ends: how close to the investigation the killer's knowledge truly runs.
