--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Forensics technicians combing the tableau site from before find a matchbook and a handwritten receipt tucked among the debris.
2. Both items bear Han's Grocery markings, one with handwriting matching Han Mei's.
3. Neither item is found anywhere near where a body would have been, only near the tableau's edge, as if placed to be found rather than dropped.
4. The technicians log the find as the first physical evidence tying the location to a name.
5. The audience recognizes this as Boru's construction, indistinguishable to investigators from a genuine break.
6. No one on scene has any reason yet to doubt the items are what they appear to be.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Zhou presents the matchbook and receipt to Elias and Lin as the first solid lead the case has produced.
2. She assigns them to interview Han Mei directly, treating the physical evidence as good reason to look at him.
3. Elias feels an immediate pull toward the lead, more eager than the thin evidence alone would justify.
4. Lin flags quietly that the items don't place Han at the docks itself, only nearby.
5. Zhou acknowledges the gap but wants Han ruled in or out quickly given how little else they have.
6. The audience notes Elias's eagerness is already outrunning what the evidence supports.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Han Mei opens his shop as usual, arranging produce and greeting a regular customer by name.
2. Family photos are visible behind the register — a wife, kids, an ordinary domestic life.
3. Nothing in his morning routine suggests a man with anything to hide about a murder.
4. A small, private moment shows him glancing at an old envelope in a drawer before closing it again, unexplained to the audience.
5. The audience is shown Han as an ordinary, sympathetic figure just before the investigation reaches him.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Before heading out, Elias reviews the file on Han and feels the relief of finally having something concrete to move on.
2. He tells himself the case has been too abstract for too long and this is a chance to close a real gap.
3. He doesn't examine why he wants this lead to hold up as much as he does.
4. Lin notices his focus sharpening in a way that reads more like hope than analysis.
5. The audience sees Elias's judgment already bending toward the outcome he wants, not the one the evidence proves.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Elias stops by the shop before the interview, mentioning the new lead in passing.
2. Boru asks a careful question about the matchbook, testing how far Elias's certainty has already gone.
3. Elias, flattered by Boru's interest, explains the theory fully, unaware he's confirming the plan is working.
4. Boru offers one small, seemingly offhand comment that subtly reinforces Elias's confidence in the Han lead.
5. Boru privately notes how easily Elias absorbs suggestion now compared to earlier in the case.
6. The audience sees Boru actively deepening the frame rather than merely observing it take hold.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone in the house, Mira takes the note back out and examines it again in better light.
2. She checks Elias's coat pockets a second time, looking for anything else that might explain it.
3. She finds nothing further, only the same unfamiliar handwriting and no name.
4. She feels a flicker of guilt for searching, followed immediately by a stronger urge to keep doing it.
5. She puts everything back exactly as she found it before he returns.
6. The audience sees her private suspicion hardening into quiet investigation of her own.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Elias and Lin question Han about the matchbook and receipt, and Han answers everything asked without hesitation.
2. Han grows visibly nervous under the questioning, shifting and avoiding eye contact at points.
3. Elias reads the nervousness as a sign of guilt; Lin reads it as simple fear of police.
4. Han doesn't mention his old immigration paperwork trouble, worried it will make him look worse.
5. Neither detective asks him directly for an alibi for the night in question.
6. The audience knows Han's fear has nothing to do with the murders, a fact neither detective can see.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Over food, Lin lays out plainly to Elias that a matchbook and a receipt don't place Han at the docks.
2. She points out that his alibi for the night in question hasn't actually been checked yet.
3. Elias pushes back, arguing that Han's nervousness in the interview speaks louder than paperwork.
4. Lin holds her position more firmly than she has before, no longer softening it into a passing comment.
5. Elias's certainty doesn't move, even as her doubt becomes harder for her to hide.
6. The audience sees the first open disagreement between them since the case began.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Back at the precinct, Elias tells Zhou he wants to move forward treating Han as the primary suspect.
2. Lin, present for the discussion, raises the alibi gap again, more directly this time.
3. Elias argues his instincts have been right before and asks to be trusted on this one.
4. Zhou, weighing his track record, leans toward giving him room to keep pursuing it.
5. Lin backs down in front of Zhou rather than pushing further, though her unease doesn't leave her.
6. The audience watches Elias's certainty win out over evidence for the second time this episode.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. After closing, Han sits with his wife and admits the police questioning frightened him more than he let on.
2. He doesn't tell her about the old immigration issue either, keeping that fear compartmentalized even at home.
3. His wife asks if he's done anything wrong, and he answers honestly that he hasn't, though his fear doesn't match his innocence.
4. He wonders aloud why the matchbook and receipt would even be at the docks, genuinely bewildered.
5. The audience watches an innocent man's fear look, from the outside, exactly like guilt.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Mira takes out the note one final time and reads it slowly, weighing every word against what she knows of Elias.
2. She lands on the interpretation that feels most obvious to her: another person, a secret, an affair.
3. She decides, without confronting him, that she now believes this is what's happening.
4. She hides the note somewhere new, no longer content to leave it where he might notice it's been moved.
5. The audience knows her conclusion is wrong, built on evidence that means something entirely different.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Zhou calls Elias in and asks for the complete file on the postcard, including the exact verse, no partial version this time.
2. She frames it as standard procedure now that the case is escalating with a named suspect involved.
3. Elias hesitates for a beat before agreeing, the same pause Lin has already started noticing in him elsewhere.
4. He tells Zhou he'll have it to her shortly, another promise like the one he made about sourcing the lead.
5. Zhou's request is reasonable and routine, which makes Elias's reluctance to comply fully feel heavier to the audience.
6. The audience senses the walls around Elias's private notebook are being tested from a new direction.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Alone, Elias drafts the file for Zhou, copying most of the verse in but stopping short of the final line again.
2. He tells himself he'll add it once he's fully certain of its meaning, a rationalization worn thin by repetition.
3. He submits the file knowing it's incomplete, technically compliant but not honest.
4. The audience sees the gap between what Zhou asked for and what she'll actually receive.
5. Nothing about his manner at his desk signals to anyone nearby that the file is anything but finished.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Elias returns to the tableau site to look over the matchbook and receipt's original placement one more time.
2. He notices, briefly, that the items look almost too clean for something exposed to the docks' weather.
3. He considers the thought seriously for a moment before setting it aside as overthinking.
4. He photographs the placement again for his own notes rather than raising the observation with anyone else.
5. The audience recognizes this as a real, correct doubt that Elias talks himself out of.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. After the shop closes, Boru takes a locked box from a back room and adds a small object to it, handling it with care.
2. He arranges the box's contents in a specific order, as if following a plan only he can see.
3. He pauses over one item longer than the others, something clearly tied to his son rather than to Elias's case.
4. He puts the box away exactly as it was, leaving no visible trace of having opened it.
5. The audience is shown, without explanation yet, that Boru's design has a personal cost driving it beyond the case itself.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Han arrives to open the shop and finds a hostile word scrawled across the front window overnight.
2. He scrubs at it quickly before any customers arrive, saying nothing to his family about it.
3. A neighbor mentions having heard, secondhand, that police questioned him about the docks case.
4. Han realizes word of the investigation has already spread beyond the interview room.
5. The audience sees the frame beginning to cost Han something before any formal charge has even been made.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Elias brings Zhou his case for a warrant to search Han's Grocery and financial records.
2. He presents the matchbook, the receipt, and Han's nervousness in questioning as sufficient grounds.
3. Zhou points out the same gaps Lin already raised — no alibi check, no direct placement at the scene.
4. Elias argues that moving now, while the trail is fresh, matters more than closing every gap first.
5. Zhou considers his request without answering yet, the decision hanging as the episode ends.
6. The audience is left uncertain whether the system is about to fail Han the way it once failed Boru's son.
