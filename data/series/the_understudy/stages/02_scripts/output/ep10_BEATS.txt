--- SCENE 1 ---
1. In the confusion of Lin's team sweeping the shop's front room, Simon slips out a rear door onto the dock structure itself, the fog swallowing him within seconds.
2. Elias follows without calling for backup, telling himself he's finishing what he started rather than choosing to go in unwitnessed.
3. Lin's shout for him to wait goes unanswered, lost in the fog and the distance already opening between them.
4. Elias carries the last note's message with him, still reading it as a killer's unraveling rather than the final, deliberate step of a plan.
5. The audience recognizes the isolation Simon needed is completing itself in real time, seconds ahead of anyone who might stop it.
6. Narrator frames the walk as the season's true ending beginning, alone, exactly as the design required.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Elias finds Simon standing at the water's edge, weapon drawn between them for the second time that night.
2. Simon doesn't retreat or plead, meeting the gun with the same stillness he showed inside the shop.
3. Elias orders him to explain himself fully now, done accepting partial answers.
4. Simon asks first whether Elias came alone, already knowing the answer from how the fog sounds.
5. Neither man acknowledges how far they've moved from anyone who could witness what happens next.
6. The audience sees the true stage of Simon's plan revealed to be this spot, not the shop, not the crowd closing in behind them.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Simon speaks his brother's name aloud for the first time, Aldous, without hedging or metaphor.
2. He describes the conviction forty years ago, built entirely on another detective's certainty rather than proof.
3. He states plainly that Aldous died in prison still insisting his innocence, and that no one outside Simon ever believed him.
4. Elias, still holding his weapon level, begins to understand this isn't confession but grievance, decades old and undischarged.
5. Simon's voice changes here, the performance of the courteous bookseller finally gone entirely.
6. The audience hears the story's true origin stated in full for the first time this season, from the one voice that always knew it.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Simon tells Elias directly that he was never the hunter in this case, only ever the instrument.
2. He explains that every clue, every fabricated tie to Han Mei, existed to walk Elias to this exact moment.
3. He says the case was never about who killed those people, but about what kind of man Elias would become answering it.
4. Elias hears this and doesn't yet grasp that Simon is asking to be killed, not explaining a crime.
5. Simon steps closer to the water and to Elias both, closing the last physical distance between them.
6. The audience watches Simon spend his final honesty trying to finish the one thing he built all this to cause.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Simon moves suddenly, forcing Elias's hand in the fog and the dark with no one else present to see it.
2. The confrontation ends in violence, Simon dying at Elias's hand exactly as engineered, off the record and unrecorded.
3. No arrest is made, no warning given beforehand that could be logged, no other officer close enough to witness it.
4. Elias stands over what's left of the moment understanding only that it's over, not what it cost him to end it this way.
5. The fog closes back in around the dock, leaving no trace of sequence or justification behind.
6. The audience alone holds the full truth of what just happened, unwitnessed by anyone who could ever corroborate it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Dawn comes up slow over the docks, the water and the fog indifferent to what occurred there hours earlier.
2. Elias remains a long while before moving, the silence around him doing nothing to explain itself.
3. No crime scene tape, no photographs, no procedure marks this as anything but empty ground by the time light returns.
4. Lin's search team finds the area only after Elias has already begun walking back toward them.
5. The audience understands the docks have taken in the one thing Simon's whole design needed kept private.
6. Narrator closes the sequence on fog and silence, the case's true climax already unrecorded and unrepeatable.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Zhou receives Elias's written report the next morning, describing a confrontation that omits how far outside protocol the night went.
2. He notices the report is careful in a way that doesn't match Elias's usual bluntness, more composed than the circumstances suggest.
3. He weighs whether to accept it as written or push for details Elias clearly chose to leave out.
4. He decides, for now, to file it as given, telling himself containment still matters more than confrontation.
5. His own doubt about the report's completeness stays with him, unspoken to anyone above him in the chain.
6. The audience sees Zhou choosing protection over inquiry again, the same instinct that cost him a week earlier.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Lin, reviewing the case file to close it out, lines Elias's report against the evidence log timestamps from earlier in the case.
2. She finds a gap that doesn't reconcile, a sequence of hours Elias's account doesn't fully account for.
3. She checks it twice, ruling out her own error before considering what it might mean about her partner.
4. She says nothing to Zhou yet, unwilling to escalate before she's certain of what she's actually found.
5. The discrepancy sits alongside everything else she's chosen not to say all season, one more thing weighing against her silence.
6. The audience recognizes the exact thread that could unravel the sanitized version Elias gave everyone but himself.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Lin corners Elias away from the precinct, asking him directly what actually happened at the docks.
2. He gives her the same account he gave Zhou, flatter now from repetition rather than more convincing.
3. She tells him the timeline doesn't hold up against the evidence log, and watches for how he responds to being caught in it.
4. He doesn't deny the gap, but doesn't fill it either, leaving her with silence instead of an answer.
5. She tells him she hasn't decided yet whether to file what she knows, and that his silence now is doing the deciding for her.
6. The audience watches loyalty and conscience pull against each other in Lin, still unresolved by the scene's end.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Officers clearing out Boru's Rare Books for evidence find a locked back room no one had reason to search before.
2. Inside, they find a handwritten letter addressed only to Elias by name, not to the department or any court.
3. The letter lays out Simon's crimes and his reasons in his own words, written before that final night.
4. It was never filed as evidence, existing outside any official record of how the case actually resolved.
5. Whoever finds it must decide whether to bring it forward or let it stay between Simon and Elias alone.
6. The audience recognizes this letter as the one piece of proof that could confirm everything Elias already knows privately.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Han receives formal word that all charges against him are dropped, delivered through his lawyer rather than the precinct directly.
2. No accompanying statement explains why, or that the evidence tying him to the murders was fabricated from the start.
3. He reads the notice twice, searching for language that isn't there, some acknowledgment beyond the fact of dismissal.
4. His wife asks if this means it's finally over, and he doesn't have an answer that feels true yet.
5. He understands, without being told, that the department intends to let this end quietly rather than publicly.
6. The audience sees the gap between being cleared on paper and being cleared in the world Han still has to live in.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Elias comes to Han's Grocery on his own time, not on any precinct business, still on restricted duty.
2. He tells Han he was wrong about him, as plainly as he's able to say it outside a formal statement.
3. He doesn't explain the full truth of how the real killer was found, giving Han the same trimmed account he gave his captain.
4. He offers no accounting for the shuttered street outside, the graffiti, the customers who still won't come in.
5. He leaves the apology deliberately personal, aware it carries no institutional weight behind it.
6. The audience recognizes the apology as sincere and still fundamentally insufficient to what Han actually lost.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Han listens to the apology without interrupting, his composure giving away nothing of what it costs him to hold it.
2. He tells Elias that a dropped charge isn't the same as a cleared name, and that no one outside this room has said otherwise.
3. He doesn't raise his voice or list every specific injury, keeping the full weight of his anger to himself.
4. Elias has no answer that addresses what Han just said, since the precinct never gave him one to offer.
5. Han goes back to restocking his shelves before Elias has fully finished speaking, ending the conversation on his own terms.
6. The audience sees exactly how much Han is choosing not to say, even now, protecting himself as much as confronting Elias.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Elias returns to the Vance house for the first time since the docks, moving through the rooms differently than before.
2. Mira, home briefly to collect more of her things, notices the change in him before either of them says a word.
3. She asks what happened, and he hesitates in a way that tells her something did, without telling her what.
4. He doesn't reach for her, and she doesn't move toward him either, both circling the same unspoken distance.
5. The silence between them carries more information than either of them means to give away.
6. The audience watches Mira register the shift correctly as significant while still misreading entirely what it means.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Elias gives Mira an account of Simon's death that matches what he told Zhou, softened and incomplete in the same places.
2. He describes a confrontation, a struggle, an ending, without naming that no one else was present to see any of it.
3. Mira listens for anything that resembles the affair she's spent months believing in, and finds nothing that confirms or denies it outright.
4. He doesn't correct her old theory even now, letting her keep carrying a truth that was never actually true.
5. She doesn't tell him how close she'd already come to leaving before he walked back through the door.
6. The audience watches two people occupy the same room while withholding the two different truths that could actually reach each other.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Zhou and Elias sit across from each other in a back booth at Ruby's Diner, off the clock and off the record.
2. Zhou doesn't ask again about the gap in the timeline, though it's clear from his silence that he hasn't stopped noticing it.
3. Elias doesn't offer anything beyond what's already in the file, and Zhou doesn't push him to.
4. Neither man raises the week of delay before the restricted-duty paperwork, though it sits between them unspoken.
5. The conversation ends without resolution, coffee going cold, nothing between them actually settled.
6. The audience recognizes an understanding passing between the two men that neither is willing to name aloud.
