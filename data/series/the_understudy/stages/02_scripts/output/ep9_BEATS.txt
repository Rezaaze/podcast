--- SCENE 1 ---
1. A full week has passed since Zhou signed the restricted-duty paperwork, though he still hasn't shown it to Elias face to face.
2. Elias has been quietly sidelined from active leads, technically still on the case in name but cut out of new assignments.
3. Lin has taken over daily briefings, feeling the absence of her partner in every meeting.
4. Elias interprets the sidelining as bureaucratic slowness rather than the deliberate consequence Zhou intended.
5. Zhou keeps finding new reasons to delay the actual conversation, telling himself the timing isn't right yet.
6. The audience sees a full week burned on hesitation while Elias remains unaware anything formal has already been decided.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Zhou finally calls Elias into his office and closes the door before either of them sits down.
2. He hands over the restricted-duty paperwork Elias didn't know existed until this moment.
3. Elias reads the effective date and realizes it was signed a full week earlier, not decided today.
4. He asks why Zhou waited, and Zhou answers only that he was hoping it wouldn't be necessary.
5. Elias hears the decision as betrayal rather than the accountability Zhou intends it to be.
6. The audience understands Zhou's week of delay has cost him any chance of Elias accepting this calmly.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. The DA's office formally files notice that all charges against Han Mei are dropped for insufficient evidence.
2. Zhou receives the filing first and reads the language as an implicit rebuke of the precinct's handling.
3. No mention is made in the filing of who actually planted the watch or fibers, since no one investigating knows yet.
4. Han's lawyer receives the same notice and calls it a vindication, though it resolves nothing about who framed him.
5. Elias, restricted from active work, is not consulted before the decision is finalized.
6. The audience knows the charges collapsing changes nothing about who the real architect is, since that answer sits with Simon alone.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Han returns to open the grocery for the first time since the charges dropped, half-scrubbed graffiti still visible on the shutter.
2. A few neighbors greet him carefully, unsure whether dropped charges mean innocent or just unproven.
3. The regular customer who once crossed the street to avoid him now nods but still doesn't come inside.
4. Han's wife watches him test the new normal and sees it isn't the return to before that either of them hoped for.
5. Han still says nothing to his family about the supplier arrangement, protecting one secret even as the bigger one lifts.
6. The audience recognizes the frame's damage outlasting the frame itself, and that Han's own silence keeps part of the wound open.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Elias comes home on a day off to find Mira's side of the closet stripped down to hangers.
2. A note on the counter, brief and practical, explains she's moved into a friend's spare room, not further than that.
3. He calls her, and the conversation stays logistical, about mail and a few shared bills, nothing about why.
4. He almost tells her about the postcards in the pause after she asks if he's alright, and doesn't.
5. She hangs up still carrying the affair theory as settled fact, and he hangs up still believing it's too late to correct it.
6. The audience watches the one conversation that could unravel the misunderstanding pass by unused, by his own silence.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone in the sidelined quiet of restricted duty, Elias tallies what the week has actually cost him: the case, Zhou's trust, Mira.
2. He reasons that if the institution has already decided he's finished, its rules no longer bind his next move.
3. He doesn't call Lin to talk it through, deciding in advance that she'd try to stop him.
4. He starts privately reviewing the postcard sequence again, no longer as an investigator but as a man with nothing left to protect.
5. The decision to go around the department, not just past it, starts to feel less like defiance to him and more like the only clean option left.
6. The audience sees the exact mixture of grief and freedom Simon's design was counting on taking hold.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. A final envelope reaches Elias, this one departing from the pattern by being addressed to him by name in handwriting instead of typed.
2. Its message reads less like a clue to be solved and more like a specific time and place, an unmistakable invitation.
3. Elias notices the shift in tone but reads it as the killer's confidence cracking, not as an intentional change in the game's rules.
4. He doesn't report the envelope to Zhou or Lin, aware that surfacing it now would only speed up his own removal from the case.
5. He recognizes phrasing that echoes something he heard recently in casual conversation, without placing exactly where.
6. The audience recognizes Simon's own voice in the note, the first time the killer has spoken to Elias almost directly.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Elias lays the handwritten note beside the earlier clues, tracing the historical reference back to the docks specifically.
2. He connects the invitation's language to the story Simon told him during his last visit to the shop, still crediting it as his own memory rather than a planted detail.
3. He works out the location precisely: the docks, and Boru's shop itself, sitting almost adjacent to where the pattern points.
4. He tells himself the killer chose the docks for symbolic reasons tied to the case, not recognizing it as a location chosen because Simon owns it.
5. He decides on a time to go, tonight, without naming the destination to anyone else in the department.
6. The audience watches Elias solve exactly the puzzle Simon built for him to solve, arriving at Simon's own doorstep by design.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Lin finds Elias packing his own gear quietly instead of turning in his badge and weapon as restricted duty requires.
2. She tells him directly that she knows about the log, the six entries, and that she's the one who brought them to Zhou.
3. He absorbs this without much visible reaction, already past the point of caring how the institutional consequences landed.
4. She asks him outright where he's going tonight, and he answers only that it's nothing she needs to worry about.
5. She warns him that going anywhere alone tonight, on today of all days, will be the thing that ends whatever's left of his career.
6. The audience sees Lin's honesty arriving exactly when it could matter most, and Elias choosing not to hear it anyway.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Simon closes the shop early, an unremarkable deviation from routine to anyone who might notice.
2. He moves through preparations at the docks with the same care he's shown at every earlier stage of the design.
3. He allows himself one more private moment addressed to the mentor who broke him decades ago, treating tonight as the argument finally being won.
4. He reviews his own schedule against Elias's habits and satisfies himself that the timing is exact, arriving alone as required.
5. He does not account for how far Lin has already carried her own suspicions about the bookshop's history.
6. The audience alone understands that Simon believes he has closed every door except the one he's deliberately left open for Elias.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Elias drives to the docks after dark, leaving his phone behind rather than risk it being tracked or ringing at the wrong moment.
2. He moves toward Boru's shop with his weapon drawn but his reasoning still framed entirely as pursuing a killer, not walking into a trap.
3. He passes the spot marked in the very first postcard without registering it as the same location, now closing a loop he doesn't see.
4. He knocks rather than forces entry, some instinct in him still treating Simon as the ally he's always been.
5. He steps inside alone, exactly as required, with no one in the department aware of where he's gone.
6. The audience watches the season's central trap complete itself the moment Elias crosses that threshold.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Mira, unable to sleep, picks up the phone to call Elias, the first time she's reached out first in weeks.
2. She rehearses what she might finally ask him directly about the postcard fragment and the matchbook, ready to hear the truth for once.
3. The call rings through to nothing, since Elias left his phone behind at the house.
4. She reads the silence as confirmation rather than absence, another data point for a theory that was never true.
5. She doesn't try a second time, deciding instead that the lack of an answer is itself the answer.
6. The audience watches the one call that might have changed everything land on an empty house.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Lin, still uneasy after their exchange, tries reaching Elias herself and gets no response either.
2. She goes to Zhou directly, telling him Elias is off the grid tonight and that she has a bad feeling tied to more than just the restricted-duty order.
3. Zhou learns for the first time that Elias never turned in his weapon, despite the paperwork requiring it.
4. Lin lays out her own research into the local history referenced in the clues, pointing toward the docks and the bookshop without yet naming Simon as a suspect.
5. Zhou authorizes an immediate search, escalating past his earlier instinct to contain the situation quietly.
6. The audience sees institutional doubt finally converting into action, one crucial step behind where Elias already stands.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Simon greets Elias inside the shop with the same warmth as every prior visit, giving no immediate sign anything has changed.
2. Elias states plainly that the clues, the history, the timing all point here, to Simon, and waits to see how he'll respond.
3. Simon doesn't deny it outright, instead asking Elias to walk through exactly how he worked it out, watching the reasoning unfold.
4. Elias, still convinced he arrived here through his own deduction, doesn't notice he's answering a question designed to confirm the design succeeded.
5. Something in Simon's stillness, entirely unlike his usual manner, finally registers to Elias as wrong.
6. The audience watches the mask begin to lift exactly as Simon intends, the confrontation unfolding on his terms, not Elias's.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Simon confirms it himself, plainly, that every clue Elias followed this season was his, placed and timed by his own hand.
2. He explains, without apology, that none of the deaths were committed directly by him, each one staged and let fall on someone else.
3. He names his terminal diagnosis for the first time out loud, framing tonight as the last chapter of something he's building rather than escaping.
4. Elias, weapon still raised, hears the shape of a confession forming and doesn't yet understand what Simon actually wants from him.
5. Simon watches Elias's hand on the weapon with something closer to anticipation than fear.
6. The audience finally hears the truth stated outright, watching Elias absorb only part of what it means.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Instead of the shot Simon has spent months engineering him to take, Elias lowers his weapon slightly and asks why, specifically why him.
2. Simon, caught by the question outside his own script, hesitates for the first time all season.
3. Elias presses further, asking about the mentor, the institution, the decades-old betrayal Simon let slip once before without meaning to.
4. Simon's careful composure cracks, the old wound surfacing in a way he never intended to show Elias directly.
5. The pause stretches long enough that the shot the entire design was built around does not happen in this moment.
6. The audience recognizes Elias, without knowing it, has just refused the one act Simon needed from him.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Footsteps and light sweep in from outside as Lin and backup arrive at the docks, exactly as far behind as Simon failed to account for.
2. Simon's expression shifts, the first real break in his composure, as he registers that the ending is no longer entirely his to write.
3. Elias, weapon still lowered, doesn't fire even as Simon moves, the justified shooting the whole design demanded never arriving.
4. Lin enters to find her partner alive, unarmed in the sense that matters, and a case that just became something none of them expected.
5. Zhou, arriving moments later, sees enough to understand the restricted-duty order and the log that built it will now matter far less than what just didn't happen in this room.
6. The audience is left holding every thread at once: Han still silent about his real secret, Mira still certain of a lie, and Simon's masterwork denied its final, necessary hand.
