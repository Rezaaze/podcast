--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the first morning of the Meridian Star salvage dispute — the wreck's cargo rights hearing has just been scheduled by the harbor commission, and this is the season's opening move.
2. Vince Lo slides a sealed folder across the corner booth table and lets Kai read the 1974 harbor commission minutes for the first time.
3. Kai realizes the minutes contradict the official accident report on file, though Vince does not explain to her what the discrepancy actually proves about Hok Chen.
4. Vince instructs Kai to lock in the Meridian Star hearing date immediately, framing it only as a scheduling advantage, not as leverage tied to the minutes.
5. Kai agrees to set the date, filing the documents away as evidence damaging to Chen Towing without yet connecting them to Danny's family by name.
6. Vince pockets the folder again, making clear this conversation and the minutes themselves stay strictly between the two of them.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mei steps away from the yard office to take a call from her lender, who confirms the balloon payment on her hidden loan is due in six weeks.
2. The confirmed deadline lands as a hard number for the first time — Mei now knows the exact date she has to make work, not just a vague worry.
3. She hangs up and finds Frank on the dock, pushing him to move faster on the Meridian Star bid without giving him a real reason.
4. Frank reads her urgency as her usual over-caution about cash flow and brushes it off, unaware any loan or fixed deadline exists.
5. Mei nearly mentions the storm-damage repairs and the insurance lapse, then stops herself, unwilling to reopen the blame Frank once placed on her.
6. She settles for pressing him harder on timeline instead, leaving the real stakes of the six weeks entirely hidden from him.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Kai meets Danny somewhere they've used before and tells him her firm's filing deadline on the Meridian Star case is closing in fast.
2. She gives him only the bare fact of the timeline, withholding any mention of the 1974 minutes or what they contain.
3. Danny presses for more detail, but Kai holds her line, treating the vague warning as the furthest she can go without crossing into leaking her firm's case.
4. Rattled by how close the deadline now feels, Danny worries aloud that their usual meeting spot is getting too risky given who might notice them together.
5. They agree, for the first time, to find a new place to meet, treating it as a necessary precaution rather than a sign anything is wrong between them.
6. Neither says aloud that the coming hearing could force their relationship into the open, though the thought passes between them unspoken.
