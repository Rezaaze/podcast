--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Three days have passed since the harbor commission set the hearing date in the season's opening scene; today was the actual hearing session, and Frank, Mei, and Hok have just returned from it to the yard.
2. Frank tells the crew that Vince's lawyer produced sealed 1974 harbor commission minutes, trying to argue the Chens' salvage priority was never legitimately earned.
3. Rather than ruling on the challenge, the commission postponed its decision pending further review, leaving the Meridian Star bid open but now shadowed by the threat.
4. At the mention of "1974," Hok's hands visibly shake and he has to sit down, a reaction sharper than anything he's shown before.
5. Frank puts it down to Hok's age and the stress of the room, staying convinced Vince is just running legal harassment with no real substance behind it.
6. Mei watches Hok's reaction closely and quietly decides she's going to find out what actually happened in 1974, a resolve she keeps entirely to herself.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Later that afternoon, Frank goes through title paperwork for the Meridian Star financing and finds Mei's signature as co-signer on a loan against the dock he's never seen before.
2. He confronts her on the spot, holding the papers, demanding to know when she took out a loan against the family's title without telling him.
3. Mei admits to the loan but calls it a small line of credit for "routine yard expenses," saying nothing about the storm damage, the lapsed insurance, or the six-week balloon payment.
4. Genuinely blindsided, Frank presses her on why she'd hide it at all, and wrongly assumes she's covering for some financial trouble of her own making.
5. Mei neither confirms nor denies his suspicion, letting him believe the worse but vaguer version rather than the truth that would reopen his old blame over the insurance lapse.
6. The confrontation ends unresolved, with Frank telling her he wants the full loan documents in his hands by the end of the week — a new, concrete deadline now bearing down on Mei alongside the loan itself.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Danny and Kai leave The Compass together late at night, using it for the first time since agreeing it would be their new, safer meeting spot.
2. Footsteps sound just outside, and Danny freezes, recognizing the walk of one of his father's deckhands rounding the corner toward them.
3. They split at the last second, Kai ducking into a side alley, and the deckhand passes by without any sign of having clocked them together.
4. Shaken, Danny already settles on a vague excuse for where he'll say he was tonight if anyone asks — a lie by omission he's now committed to telling.
5. Once they regroup, Kai pushes him hard for more caution between them, pointing out that the hearing's postponement has put their two companies into open public conflict.
6. Privately, Kai starts worrying for the first time that if she keeps passing Danny warnings about her firm's timeline, it could eventually be traced straight back to her — a fear she doesn't say aloud to him.
