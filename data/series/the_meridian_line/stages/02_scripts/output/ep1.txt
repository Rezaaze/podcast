--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR | style: neutral, measured]
Past the fish markets and the container cranes, where the fog never quite lifts before noon, three tugboats named after three generations of women wait at anchor. This is a family business built on foghorns, favors, and debts nobody writes down. When a sunken cargo ship becomes the harbor's most contested prize, the trouble on the surface — money, rivalry — is only the trouble anyone's willing to say out loud.

[NARRATOR | style: neutral, measured]
A diner two blocks from the harbor commission office, just after nine in the morning. Vince Lo, owner of Lo Marine Salvage, sits in the corner booth with Kai Park, his project coordinator.

[VINCE_LO | style: low, controlled]
Coffee's getting cold. Sit.

[KAI_PARK | style: brisk, professional]
You said this couldn't wait till the office.

[VINCE_LO | style: quiet, deliberate]
It couldn't.

[SFX: a folder sliding across a laminate tabletop]

[VINCE_LO | style: flat, watching her]
Read it. Take your time. Nobody's rushing you.

[KAI_PARK | style: careful, scanning]
Harbor commission minutes. Nineteen seventy-four.

[VINCE_LO | style: even]
Keep going.

[KAI_PARK | style: slowing, uncertain]
This doesn't match the accident report. The one in our own file, the one we pulled for the Meridian Star brief — this says the towline failure was called avoidable. Ours says it wasn't.

[VINCE_LO | style: unreadable, calm]
Interesting, isn't it. Old paperwork disagreeing with itself.

[KAI_PARK | style: pressing, sharp]
Vince, where did this come from.

[VINCE_LO | style: closed, final]
Doesn't matter where. Matters what we do with it.

[KAI_PARK | style: wary]
And what do we do with it.

[VINCE_LO | style: brisk, businesslike]
Nothing. Not yet. You lock in the hearing date for the Meridian Star cargo rights. Today. Before anyone else gets a look at the commission calendar.

[KAI_PARK | style: measured]
That's just scheduling.

[VINCE_LO | style: level]
That's an advantage. Take it while it's sitting there.

[KAI_PARK | style: quiet, considering]
Fine. I can have it locked by this afternoon.

[VINCE_LO | style: satisfied, low]
Good.

[SFX: a folder being pulled back across the table]

[KAI_PARK | style: careful, testing]
And this stays where.

[VINCE_LO | style: cold, precise]
Between us. Not the office. Not your notes. Not anyone at Chen Towing, obviously, but not our own people either. You didn't see this, and neither did I.

[KAI_PARK | style: steady, guarded]
Understood.

[VINCE_LO | style: quiet, final]
Get the date locked, Kai. Let the paperwork do its job.

[NARRATOR | style: neutral, measured]
Vince slides the folder back into his coat. Kai gathers her things, the hearing date already forming in her mind, the full weight of what she's just seen not yet landing anywhere near home.

--- PART 2 ---

[NARRATOR]
Behind the Chen Towing office, a stretch of cracked asphalt looks out over the boat slips. Mei Chen, the company's bookkeeper and Frank's older sister, steps away from the yard to take a call alone.

[MEI_CHEN | style: measured, guarded]
This is Mei.

[MEI_CHEN | style: tight, controlled]
Yes. I'm here.

[MEI_CHEN | style: quiet, bracing]
Six weeks. Say the date again.

[MEI_CHEN | style: flat, absorbing a blow]
The fourteenth. Understood.

[MEI_CHEN | style: careful, professional]
No, that's — that's enough for now. I'll call before then.

[SFX: a phone call ending with a soft click]

[MEI_CHEN | style: barely audible, alone]
Six weeks.

[NARRATOR]
She finds her younger brother Frank, the company's owner-captain, out on the dock, coiling line by the tug.

[SFX: heavy rope coiling dropped onto wood planking]

[MEI_CHEN | style: brisk, urgent]
Frank. The Meridian Star paperwork. Where's it sitting.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: easy, unhurried]
Sitting where it always sits. With the commission. It'll move when it moves.

[MEI_CHEN | style: pressing, clipped]
That's not good enough anymore. We need that bid filed this week, not next month.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: mildly amused, dismissive]
Mei. It's a salvage claim, not a bank run. Nobody's rushed a harbor hearing since Dad was working the boats.

[MEI_CHEN | style: sharper, controlled]
I'm asking you to push it. Call the clerk yourself if you have to.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: patient, a little tired]
You always do this before a big job. Get in front of the numbers before there's even numbers to worry about.

[MEI_CHEN | style: quiet, almost breaking through]
This isn't— 

[NARRATOR]
She stops herself, the sentence unfinished.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: gentle, teasing]
This isn't what.

[MEI_CHEN | style: recovering, even]
Nothing. Just — the cash flow's tighter than you think, that's all. I'd feel better with the date moved up.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: reassuring, warm]
It'll come through. It always does.

[MEI_CHEN | style: low, insistent]
Just talk to the clerk, Frank. This week.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: relenting, still light]
Fine. I'll call. Since it means that much to you.

[SFX: a gull crying overhead, close]

[MEI_CHEN | style: still, watching him walk off]
Thank you.

--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]
A little past nine, in a diner three streets back from the harbor, vinyl booths and a coffee machine that never fully cools. Danny Chen — Frank's son, training to take the wheel of the family boats — is already in the back booth. This is where he and Kai always meet.

[KAI_PARK | style: low, controlled]
Don't stand up. I don't want anyone by the window seeing you move for me.

[SFX: a vinyl booth creaking as someone slides in]

[DANNY_CHEN | style: quiet, wary]
You said it was urgent. You never say urgent.

[KAI_PARK | style: measured, guarded]
The Meridian Star filing. It's moving faster than anyone expected. There's a hearing date now.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: alert]
A date. Since when.

[KAI_PARK | style: careful]
Since this morning. That's as much as I can give you, Danny.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: pressing, frustrated]
Give me more than a date, then. What's in the filing. Why is your firm moving on my family's case like it's already decided.

[KAI_PARK | style: firm, pained]
Because I work there. Because I see the calendar before anyone else does. That's the line, and I'm not crossing it. Not even for you.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: quiet]
Kai.

[KAI_PARK | style: soft, tired]
I'm already further out on this branch than I should be.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: subdued]
Okay. I hear you.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: uneasy, glancing]
Vince's people eat at that counter on Thursdays. I've clocked two of them in here tonight.

[KAI_PARK | style: alert, quiet]
Since when do you notice who eats where.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: low]
Since a hearing date got set on a case with my family's name on it, even if nobody's said that part out loud.

[KAI_PARK | style: quiet]
Then we don't come back here.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: careful]
Somewhere new. Somewhere neither of us has a reason to be.

[KAI_PARK | style: quiet, resolved]
I'll find one. I'll send you the day. Not the place, not over text.

[SFX: a mug set down hard on the table]

[DANNY_CHEN | style: wry, tired]
Careful. That mug's the only thing making it look like I've touched my coffee tonight.

[KAI_PARK | style: soft, almost a laugh]
Drink it. You need it more than I do.

[NARRATOR]
The hearing date is set. Somewhere in a yard office, a six-week clock has just started running, though only one person in this story knows the number. And in the back booth of a diner three streets from the water, two people who were never supposed to find each other are still deciding, for tonight, which secret to protect a little longer.
