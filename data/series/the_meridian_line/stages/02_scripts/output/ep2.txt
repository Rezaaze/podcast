--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Previously on The Meridian Line: the harbor commission set a hearing date for the Meridian Star's salvage rights, with Chen Towing and Lo Marine Salvage on opposing sides. Mei Chen quietly carries a debt against the family dock with six weeks left on its clock. Danny Chen and Kai Park, a coordinator at Lo Marine, have kept their relationship hidden from both families.

[NARRATOR]
Three days later, the hearing has come and gone. Frank Chen, captain of the family's towing company, walks up from the dock with his sister Mei, the company's bookkeeper, and their father Hok, who founded the business decades ago.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: clipped, still hot from the room]
Sealed minutes. Nineteen seventy-four. Vince's lawyer pulls them out like he's been sitting on them for Christmas morning.

[MEI_CHEN | style: even, watchful]
And the commission just let him enter them.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: bitter]
Didn't rule on a damn thing. Postponed. Pending review, they said. Meridian Star's still open, but now it's got this hanging over it.

[SFX: a metal gate creaking open]

[FRANK_CHEN | style: low, to himself as much as her]
Nineteen seventy-four. Before I was even walking the dock.

[HOK_CHEN | style: suddenly unsteady]
What year did you say.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: caught off guard]
Seventy-four, Pop. Why.

[NARRATOR]
Hok's hands begin to shake. He reaches for the rail of an old bench and lowers himself onto it.

[MEI_CHEN | style: alarmed, moving toward him]
Ba. Sit. Breathe.

[HOK_CHEN | style: faint, waving her off]
I'm sitting. I'm fine.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: gentler now]
It's a long morning in a hot room, that's all this is. Man's eighty-one years old.

[HOK_CHEN | style: quiet, distant]
Long morning.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: reassuring himself]
Vince has got nothing. Old paper, some lawyer's trick to spook the commission before the ruling. That's all it is.

[SFX: a foghorn in the distance]

[MEI_CHEN | style: carefully neutral]
Sure. Nothing.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: already moving on, tired]
I want the crew briefed before end of day. Nobody talks about this outside the yard.

[NARRATOR]
Frank heads toward the office. Mei stays a moment longer, watching her father's hands, still not quite steady, resting in his lap.

[HOK_CHEN | style: barely audible]
Seventy-four.

[MEI_CHEN | style: to herself, resolved]
I'm going to find out what that year means to you.

--- PART 2 ---

[NARRATOR]
Later that afternoon, in the cramped office above the Chen Towing dock, Frank goes through the financing paperwork for the Meridian Star bid. Mei, the company's bookkeeper and Frank's older sister, is at her desk across the room.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: controlled, building]
Mei. What is this.

[SFX: papers slapped down on a desk]

[MEI_CHEN | style: guarded, careful]
That's the county filing packet. It should be in the blue folder.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: sharp, incredulous]
Your name's on it. Co-signer. On a loan against the dock's title. When did you sign this?

[MEI_CHEN | style: even, deflecting]
A while back. It's nothing, Frank. A line of credit for yard expenses.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: rising, hurt]
Yard expenses. This is our father's title, Mei. Our name. And you put it up against a bank without telling me a single word.

[MEI_CHEN | style: flat, holding steady]
It was routine. Rope, fuel, a compressor that died on us. I didn't think it needed a family meeting.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: cold, disbelieving]
Routine doesn't put your signature on the deed. Routine doesn't get hidden in a folder for years. What is going on with you.

[MEI_CHEN | style: quiet, tight]
Nothing is going on with me.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: low, wounded]
Then why does it feel like you've been keeping the books for this family and half the truth for yourself.

[MEI_CHEN | style: measured, closed off]
Because sometimes the books are cleaner without you standing over my shoulder asking why every number moves.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: hard, pressing]
That's not an answer. If you're in trouble — money trouble, whatever it is — I need to know. This is the dock, Mei. It's not just yours to gamble.

[MEI_CHEN | style: still, giving nothing]
I'm not gambling anything. I handled a problem so you wouldn't have to.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: exhausted, unsatisfied]
That's not the same as telling me the truth.

[MEI_CHEN | style: quiet, unmoving]
It's the truth I'm giving you today.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: firm, final]
I want the full loan papers. Every page. On my desk by end of week.

[MEI_CHEN | style: barely holding it together]
Fine.

[FRANK_CHEN | style: cold]
End of the week, Mei.

[SFX: a door pulled shut, latch catching hard]

--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]
Late night at The Compass, down by the marina — Danny Chen and Kai Park, the coordinator from the rival salvage firm he's been seeing in secret, are leaving together for the first time since picking this place as somewhere safer.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: hushed, alert]
Wait.

[SFX: footsteps approaching on gravel]

[DANNY_CHEN | style: barely audible]
That's Ruiz. I know that walk.

[KAI_PARK | style: quiet, urgent]
Which way.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: low, fast]
Alley. Go.

[SFX: quick footsteps scattering on gravel]

[NARRATOR]
Ruiz rounds the corner and keeps walking, never breaking stride.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: shaken, quiet]
He didn't clock us.

[KAI_PARK | style: shaken, controlled]
Not tonight, he didn't.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: low, rehearsed]
If anyone asks, I was at the shop. Going over the tow rigging.

[KAI_PARK | style: firm, low]
Danny. Today, in that room, it was my company against your father's. On the record. This isn't a month ago anymore.

[DANNY_CHEN | style: defensive, soft]
I know what today was.

[KAI_PARK | style: measured, careful]
Then you know we don't use this place twice. We don't use any place twice.

[KAI_PARK | style: quiet, catching herself]
And if I keep telling you things I shouldn't be telling you—

[DANNY_CHEN | style: gentle]
Kai.

[KAI_PARK | style: soft, unfinished]
Never mind. Just — be careful walking back.

[NARRATOR]
The harbor commission never ruled on the Meridian Star tonight — only postponed, the salvage rights frozen while the board reviews further. Mei's balloon payment is still coming due in six weeks. The 1974 minutes are already on record with the commission now, entered as evidence for anyone on the board to review. And what almost happened outside The Compass hasn't reached anyone at Chen Towing yet. Down at the harbor, all the same, the water is starting to move.
