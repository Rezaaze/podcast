--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the café's opening week of the new season, the first morning after the co-op finished repainting the front room — no prior episodes to reconcile against.
2. Daniel Wu walks into The Counter carrying a worn folder of his late aunt Grace's paperwork, introducing himself to Mei, Sora, and David as her nephew.
3. Daniel states plainly that Grace's 15% buy-back was never legally finalized and that he intends to claim her ownership stake.
4. Mei's first reaction is disbelief, assuming Daniel is mistaken since she has always understood the buy-back was completed cleanly eight years ago.
5. Sora and David are equally blindsided, but Sora's alarm is quieter and more inward, worried about anything that might draw scrutiny toward the partners right now.
6. Daniel leaves copies of the paperwork on the counter, saying he'll return for a real conversation once someone senior is available, unaware his claim is factually correct.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. In the Roastery, Sora and David steal a few private minutes together, comfortable and unguarded in a way they never are on the floor.
2. Their ease briefly cracks when workplace-relationship talk brushes close to the surface, and David tenses in a way Sora notices but doesn't understand.
3. Sora lets it go, having learned not to push when David gets like this, unaware it traces back to a specific past incident he's never told her.
4. Mei's footsteps sound in the hallway, heading toward the Roastery.
5. David reacts first, pulling back and repositioning them into a plausible work-related pose half a second before Sora catches on.
6. Mei walks in already distracted by Daniel's claim and doesn't register anything off about the two of them.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Still shaken from Scene 1, Mei steps into the Roastery office nook looking for old ownership records to make sense of Daniel's claim.
2. She finds a loan document left open on the desk, referencing the roasting equipment, and realizes it's not something she's ever seen on the books.
3. Mei confronts Jordan directly, holding up the paperwork and asking what it is.
4. Jordan admits a loan exists against the equipment, but downplays it, vaguely implying it's recent and minor.
5. He says nothing about the loan being three years old, nothing about Meridian Roasters, and nothing about the audit risk driving his urgency.
6. Mei is unsettled less by the loan itself than by the realization that Jordan kept it from her at all, though she doesn't yet connect it to Daniel's claim.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. After Daniel's paperwork copies are left behind on the counter, Mei brings them to Linda, hoping the bookkeeper can quickly clear up the confusion.
2. Mei asks Linda directly whether Grace's buy-back was ever fully signed and completed.
3. Linda, who has kept the unsigned originals locked in a drawer for eight years at Jordan's request, calmly assures Mei the paperwork was handled at the time.
4. Linda steers the conversation toward reassurance and busywork, offering to "double-check the old files" rather than answering the specific question about a signature.
5. Mei, already stretched thin by the loan discovery, accepts the deflection and lets the matter drop for now.
6. The audience, per the case file, knows Linda is lying by omission and that the drawer holds the exact proof Daniel needs — a fact no character in the room can yet confirm.
