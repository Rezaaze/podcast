--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
A worker-owned café on a corner that's survived two recessions and one very bad breakup between its founders. Everyone behind the counter owns a piece of the place — which means everyone has something to lose. This season: a stranger with old paperwork, a partner with a secret debt, and two co-owners falling for each other at the worst possible time. The coffee's still good. Everything else is up for a vote.

[NARRATOR]
Opening week of the new season at The Counter, the morning after the front room got its first coat of fresh paint in years. Mei Lin, the café's managing partner, is at the register with Sora Kim, the head roaster, and David Lin, who runs the floor — when the door chimes.

[SFX: door chime, then the door swinging shut]

[MEI_LIN | style: warm, welcoming]
Morning. Give us one more day before you judge the paint smell, we're still airing it out.

[DANIEL_WU | style: polite but tense, rehearsed]
It's fine. Actually — I'm looking for whoever runs this place. My name's Daniel Wu.

[MEI_LIN | style: friendly, curious]
That's me, more or less. Mei. What can I get you?

[DANIEL_WU | style: careful, holding steady]
Nothing to drink. My aunt was Grace Wu.

[SORA_KIM | style: surprised, softening]
Grace — one of the founders. Before my time, but I've heard the name a hundred times.

[DANIEL_WU | style: quiet, sincere]
She passed almost two years ago. I've been going through her things. Slowly. I kept putting off the box with all this in it.

[SFX: a worn folder set down on the counter, papers sliding]

[DAVID_LIN | style: guarded, watching him carefully]
What exactly is "this"?

[DANIEL_WU | style: steady, unflinching]
Her ownership papers. The buy-back the co-op supposedly did when she retired — it was never finalized. Not legally. Two people have looked at this folder now, and they both told me the same thing. Her fifteen percent never actually transferred.

[MEI_LIN | style: stunned, disbelieving]
That's — no. That can't be right. That buy-back closed eight years ago. I was here.

[DANIEL_WU | style: calm, not backing down]
I understand why you'd think that. I'm telling you what the paperwork says, not what anyone told you at the time.

[SORA_KIM | style: quiet, inward, careful]
Mr. Wu, I don't doubt you believe that. But this is a lot, out of nowhere.

[DANIEL_WU | style: reasonable, patient]
I know how it sounds. Walking in on a Tuesday with a dead woman's folder. I'm not asking anyone to hand me anything today.

[MEI_LIN | style: trying to steady herself]
Then what are you asking?

[DANIEL_WU | style: even, deliberate]
A real conversation. With whoever actually handles the ownership side of things. I'll leave copies. I'm not going anywhere — I'm back in town for good.

[SFX: folder papers sliding across the counter]

[DAVID_LIN | style: tight, controlled]
We'll look at it.

[DANIEL_WU | style: polite, final]
That's all I'm asking. I'll come back when someone senior's around.

[NARRATOR]
The door chimes again as Daniel Wu steps back out onto the corner, leaving the folder behind on the counter between them.

--- PART 2 ---

[NARRATOR]
A little later, in the Roastery at the back of The Counter, away from the front room where Daniel's folder still sits.
[NARRATOR]
Sora Kim and David Lin steal a few unwatched minutes before the doors open.

[SORA_KIM | style: soft, teasing]
Nobody comes back here before eight. It's the one honest minute we get all day.

[DAVID_LIN | style: warm, relaxed]
Don't tell Jordan. He'll want to bill us for the privacy.

[SORA_KIM | style: light, amused]
He bills everyone for everything. It's practically a personality.

[SORA_KIM | style: careful, testing]
You ever wonder what actually happens, bylaws-wise, if two partners are — involved. Vote-wise, I mean.

[DAVID_LIN | style: sudden, clipped]
Don't.

[SORA_KIM | style: quiet, pulling back]
Okay. Okay.

[DAVID_LIN | style: lower, tight]
I just — not today. Not with a stranger's folder sitting out there.

[SORA_KIM | style: gentle, letting it go]
You always do that. Fine. I'm not pushing.

[SFX: footsteps approaching down the hallway]

[DAVID_LIN | style: fast, low]
Mei.

[SFX: a bag of beans dropped hard onto the scale]

[DAVID_LIN | style: brisk, businesslike]
— twenty-six seconds on the second pull, that's your sweet spot, not thirty.

[SORA_KIM | style: matching, brisk]
Twenty-six. Marking the dial now.

[MEI_LIN | style: distracted]
Have either of you seen the old ownership binder? The original filing, not the copies.

[SORA_KIM | style: casual]
Storage closet. Bottom shelf, I think.

[MEI_LIN | style: already moving]
Right. Thanks.

[NARRATOR]
She's gone as quickly as she came.

--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]
Later that same morning, Mei leaves the front room behind and steps into the office nook tucked off the Roastery, hunting for the old ownership file that might make sense of Daniel's claim.
[NARRATOR]
The desk lamp is still burning from the night before, and a drawer sits half-open beside a stack of invoices.

[MEI_LIN | style: distracted, searching]
Ownership transfer, ownership transfer... it has to be filed under Grace's name somewhere in here.

[SFX: papers shuffling and a folder sliding across a desk]

[MEI_LIN | style: freezing mid-motion]
What is this.

[MEI_LIN | style: quiet, reading]
"Secured against roasting equipment." I've never seen this. This isn't in any report I've ever signed off on.

[NARRATOR]
Jordan appears in the doorway — the co-op's finance partner — drawn by the sound of drawers opening.

[JORDAN_REYES | style: casual, testing the room]
Hey. You're up here early. You holding up okay, after this morning?

[MEI_LIN | style: sharp, holding up the page]
Jordan. What is this.

[JORDAN_REYES | style: a beat too smooth]
That's just some paperwork on the roaster line. It's fine, Mei.

[MEI_LIN | style: incredulous]
"Fine" doesn't come with a lien against equipment we all own together. Is this a loan?

[JORDAN_REYES | style: measured, minimizing]
It's — yeah. A small one. Against the equipment, short-term. I was going to loop everybody in once the numbers settled down.

[MEI_LIN | style: cutting]
Settled when? This has an origination date on it, Jordan. It's not new.

[JORDAN_REYES | style: deflecting, quick]
It's recent enough. Nothing that touches the co-op's standing. Honestly, it's barely worth its own line item.

[MEI_LIN | style: quiet, unconvinced]
Then why did I find it under a pile of invoices instead of hearing it from you, in a meeting, like a partner?

[JORDAN_REYES | style: careful, trying to close the subject]
Because I didn't want people panicking over something that's already handled. It is handled. Especially with everything else landing on us today.

[MEI_LIN | style: low, steady]
Don't do that. Don't fold this into Daniel and act like it's the same fire.

[NARRATOR]
Mei doesn't move right away. She's still holding the page, and Jordan is still standing a little too close to the drawer he never closed.

[MEI_LIN | style: quiet, more troubled than angry]
It's not even the loan that's bothering me most. It's that you sat across from me for years and never once said a word.

[NARRATOR]
She folds the document into her folder beside Daniel's paperwork and heads for the front office — and for Linda, who has always had an answer ready for everything.

--- PART 4 ---

[NARRATOR]
Later that same morning, in the cramped back office of The Counter, Mei finds Linda Chu — the co-op's longtime bookkeeper — bent over a ledger, Daniel's folder still warm from the counter in Mei's hand.

[MEI_LIN | style: rattled, trying to sound composed]
Linda. Can I ask you something, before I say anything to Jordan or Sora or anyone.

[LINDA_CHU | style: warm, unhurried]
Of course. Sit down, you look like you haven't eaten.

[MEI_LIN | style: pressing gently but directly]
A man came in this morning. Says he's Grace Wu's nephew. Says her buy-back was never actually signed — that she still legally owns fifteen percent of this place.

[LINDA_CHU | style: calm, reassuring, a beat too smooth]
Oh, Mei. That was handled years ago. I remember the whole thing — it was a long process, but it closed out properly.

[MEI_LIN | style: needing certainty]
So there's a signed copy. Somewhere. With her signature on it.

[LINDA_CHU | style: gently redirecting]
Everything from that period's in the old archive boxes, not the active files. Let me pull them tonight and double-check the dates for you, so you're not carrying this around all day.

[SFX: a file drawer rolling shut]

[MEI_LIN | style: exhausted, relenting]
Okay. Thank you. I just — today's been a lot.

[LINDA_CHU | style: soothing]
I know. Go breathe. I'll find you the second I have something.

[NARRATOR]
Daniel's papers are still on the table when the lights go out over The Counter — and somewhere between Jordan's forced confession and Linda's silence, nobody has yet said the word "audit" out loud, though everyone in that back room is already thinking it.
