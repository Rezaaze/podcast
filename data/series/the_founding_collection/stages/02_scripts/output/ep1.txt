--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Esmond Harbor's Vantry Auction House has spent eighty-seven years selling other people's histories under gleaming chandeliers. Its own history, the family always assumed, was settled — signed, sealed, and appraised long ago. Then a routine insurance re-appraisal turns up a ledger that doesn't add up, a stranger arrives asking careful questions she has her own reasons for asking, and a rival across town starts calling in debts nobody knew the family owed. Nobody in this house — or in the family that was quietly wronged to build it — knows the whole truth yet. That gap is where this story lives.

[NARRATOR | style: measured, scene-setting]
Appraisal Day. Early autumn, the first week of the season. Inside the Vantry gallery, Marguerite Vantry — chair of the auction house, matriarch of the family — walks the floor as the Founding Collection is uncrated for cataloguing.

[SFX: a wooden crate lid being pried open]

[MARGUERITE_VANTRY | style: proud, expansive]
Forty-seven lots, Nora. Look at them. Every one of them found, bought, and defended by a man who trusted his own eye over every dealer in this hemisphere.

[NORA_VANTRY | style: warm but focused]
Augustin had nerve. Nobody's disputing that.

[MARGUERITE_VANTRY | style: fond, certain]
Nerve, and taste nobody could teach him. He built this collection alone, piece by piece, before anyone else in Esmond Harbor thought Chinese export porcelain was worth a second glance. That's the whole story. It always has been.

[NARRATOR | style: neutral]
Nora Vantry, Julian's wife and the house's chief appraiser, has come to begin the archival cross-check the insurer requires before any loan can go forward. She says nothing to contradict Marguerite. She has no reason to yet.

[NORA_VANTRY | style: professional, gentle]
I'll start with the archive copies against the physical pieces. Insurer wants every signature matched before the museum people even see a manifest.

[MARGUERITE_VANTRY | style: pleased]
As it should be. Let them see what they're borrowing.

[SFX: a door opening briskly]

[NARRATOR | style: brief transition]
Julian Vantry, CEO and Marguerite's son, appears at the gallery door only long enough to be noticed.

[JULIAN_VANTRY | style: rushed, distracted]
Mother — Nora — I can't stay, I've got calls stacked up. This is just paperwork before the loan clears, correct? Nothing that needs me standing here.

[NORA_VANTRY | style: curious, a little concerned]

[JULIAN_VANTRY | style: deflecting, too quick]

[SFX: the door closing]

[NARRATOR | style: quiet, observational]

[MARGUERITE_VANTRY | style: soft, reverent]
