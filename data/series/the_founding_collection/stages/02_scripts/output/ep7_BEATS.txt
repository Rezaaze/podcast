--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is three days after Julian and Nora's reconciliation at the close of episode six, the morning of the gala, roughly seventy-two hours since Julian's confession to Marguerite.
2. Marguerite walks the gallery with the gala staff, rehearsing table placements and toast wording as though nothing underneath the house has changed.
3. She privately rehearses careful, technically true answers in case a lender raises the "archival review" she has already mentioned in passing to the board.
4. She has still not told Julian how far she got with Anna, or that Anna refused to decide anything on Simon's behalf.
5. She reminds staff to seat Marcus Tang at a visible but not central table, believing she can still manage his presence rather than dread it.
6. The audience sees Marguerite treating tonight as a negotiation she still believes she can control, unaware how much closer Marcus and Simon both already are to forcing her hand.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. At home, Julian and Nora dress for the gala in a bedroom quieter than it used to be, the silence careful rather than cold.
2. Julian offers, unprompted, to skip the gala entirely if Nora isn't ready to perform a settled couple in public yet.
3. Nora tells him she wants to go, that hiding from the house tonight would feel like another kind of secret between them.
4. She admits, briefly, that she still checks his phone out of habit, and he doesn't flinch or defend himself, just listens.
5. Neither mentions Marcus Tang or the remaining debt, both aware the gala will have to hold that danger too without being asked to name it tonight.
6. The audience watches two people choosing to show up for each other in public before they've fully finished healing in private.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. In the workshop, Simon irons his one good, rented tuxedo while Anna finishes dressing nearby, the two of them alone before the guests arrive.
2. Anna asks him directly how much he intends to reveal tonight, if anything, given Marguerite's private offer still sitting unanswered.
3. Simon tells her he has already given the family a private deadline to come forward on their own, and that deadline is close.
4. He doesn't yet tell her exactly what he'll do if they don't meet it, keeping even her partly outside his final decision.
5. Anna tells him she won't speak to guests as anything but the appraiser tonight, unwilling to out either of them before he's ready.
6. The audience learns Simon has already decided more than either Anna or the Vantrys know, and that tonight is not open-ended for him the way Marguerite still believes it is.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. At Meridian Auctions, Marcus meets his senior associate for a final review before the gala, confirming every shell company's paperwork is untraceable to him.
2. He decides tonight is the moment to force the provenance question into the open in front of the lenders, rather than wait any longer on Marguerite.
3. He instructs his associate to seed the question through a trusted lender contact rather than raise it himself, keeping his own hands visibly clean.
4. He reminds himself privately of his grandfather Wing-Ho Tang's lost 1952 bid, treating tonight as the moment two years of patience finally pays out.
5. He still believes he alone controls how and when the forgery becomes public, unaware Simon Lin has already set his own deadline in motion.
6. The audience sees Marcus preparing to spring a trap on a house that is, unknown to him, already further along toward collapse than his own information accounts for.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. The gala opens under full chandelier light, the Founding Collection displayed as the evening's centerpiece exactly as it has been every year before.
2. Marguerite delivers her toast, speaking again of Augustin's vision and the house's proud history, her voice steady though the words cost her more than they show.
3. Julian and Nora move through the room together, practiced and warm, drawing no notice from guests who assume nothing has changed.
4. Anna stands near the collection in her professional capacity, aware of Marcus circulating the room and Simon posted quietly near the back.
5. Simon watches the gathering from the edges, unrecognized by nearly everyone in the room as anything but house staff.
6. The audience watches the gilded surface of the evening sit directly over four unresolved threads, each one now only one wrong question away from breaking through.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. A senior lender, prompted earlier by one of Marcus's contacts, asks Marguerite directly and audibly whether there's truth to rumors about a provenance problem with the Founding Collection.
2. The question lands in front of a cluster of guests, more pointed than the small talk around it, and conversation nearby quiets to listen.
3. Marguerite answers smoothly that it's an ongoing archival review, nothing more, holding to the same minimized language she's used with the board.
4. She feels the practiced answer sound thinner out loud than it did in her head, aware Julian is close enough to hear every word.
5. Nora watches Marguerite's easy tone and, knowing what she now knows, recognizes the answer as a careful evasion rather than a lie of ignorance.
6. The audience sees the first public crack open in the story the house has told for decades, with the room still unaware of how deep it runs.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Marcus steps into the small circle around the lender, feigning casual interest rather than design.
2. He raises, mildly, that "archival review" seems an unusual description for a collection whose valuation history he's heard questioned before.
3. He doesn't name the ledgers, Lin Wei-Chen, or his own family directly, letting the implication do the same work it did with Marguerite in private.
4. Marguerite feels the private hint from their second meeting turned public and aimed at her in front of witnesses for the first time.
5. Julian recognizes exactly what Marcus is doing and understands the family no longer has the luxury of careful ambiguity.
6. The audience watches Marcus force into a public room the same pressure he has, until now, only applied behind closed doors.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Attention turns to Anna as the insurer's appraiser, guests and the lender pressing her for a professional opinion on the record.
2. She answers carefully, confirming only that her re-appraisal is ongoing and that she isn't yet in a position to share findings publicly.
3. She avoids any personal framing, giving no hint that the grandfather standing twenty feet away is anything more to her than a colleague.
4. Simon watches her hold the line from across the room, aware of exactly how much restraint the answer costs her.
5. Anna feels Marguerite's earlier offer and Simon's private deadline pressing against the professional neutrality she's forcing herself to perform.
6. The audience sees Anna protecting both her professional integrity and her family's secret in the same breath, unable to fully serve either.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. The lender turns the question back to Julian directly, asking as a Vantry rather than as management what he can say about the rumors.
2. Julian, instead of retreating to Marguerite's minimizing language, chooses careful but real honesty, acknowledging that questions have been raised about the 1951 ledgers that the family takes seriously.
3. He stops short of naming Augustin's forgery, Simon, or Anna outright, but he doesn't deny that something substantive is under review.
4. Marguerite's expression tightens at hearing her son depart from the story she just told moments earlier, though she says nothing in front of the guests.
5. Marcus watches Julian's answer with interest, recalculating whether the family is closer to voluntary disclosure than he assumed.
6. The audience sees Julian make the first genuinely honest public statement anyone in the family has offered all season, even as he still withholds the full truth.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Nora watches Julian answer the lender's question without flinching, without reaching for a half-truth designed only to protect himself.
2. She notices he doesn't look to Marguerite for permission or to Anna with any private, guilty glance, only holds the room's attention himself.
3. Something in her that has spent weeks quietly cataloguing his every interaction with Anna eases, unprompted, watching him choose honesty in the hardest possible setting.
4. She doesn't tell him what she's just felt shift, filing it away privately rather than interrupting the moment.
5. She still doesn't know how long trust actually takes to rebuild, but recognizes tonight as real evidence rather than another data point to doubt.
6. The audience sees Nora's private healing move forward specifically because of a moment that had nothing to do with reassuring her.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. After the last guests leave and staff begin clearing glasses, Simon approaches Marguerite and Julian directly instead of returning to the workshop.
2. He tells them plainly that he's watched the family manage tonight's question in public exactly as carefully as they've managed it in private, and that his patience has a limit.
3. He states that he intends to go to the insurer or the press himself if the family doesn't come forward voluntarily, and names a short window in which he expects an answer.
4. Marguerite realizes the private, negotiated resolution she planned three days ago never accounted for Simon setting his own terms independently of her or Anna.
5. Julian understands that whatever quiet path he imagined after his confession to Nora no longer exists, and that the family's timeline is now Simon's, not their own.
6. The audience is left holding the certainty that the gala meant to buy the house time has instead ended it, with Simon's deadline now the fact every character must answer to next.
