--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the next morning, roughly twelve hours after episode four's close, and Anna goes straight to Simon's workshop before the house properly opens.
2. She asks him directly, without pretext this time, whether he has ever known more about Lin Wei-Chen than he's let on.
3. Simon sets down his tools and looks at her for a long moment, the deflection he's used for fifty years failing to come.
4. He tells her, plainly, that yes — he has always known, and that it's time she did too.
5. Anna feels the ground shift under a question she didn't fully know she was asking.
6. The audience watches fifty years of careful silence end in a single sentence.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Simon crosses to a cabinet at the back of the workshop that Anna has never seen him open.
2. He produces a key he keeps on his own person, not on the house's ring, and unlocks a drawer built into the workbench itself.
3. He lifts out a bundle of correspondence and several original ledger pages, worn but intact, and sets them on the table between them.
4. He tells her these are the real 1938 records, kept separate from anything the house has ever catalogued or restricted.
5. Anna recognizes the handwriting from a photograph in her own coat pocket before Simon says another word.
6. The audience watches the physical proof of the forgery surface for the first time outside Simon's private keeping.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Simon walks Anna through the pages one by one, showing her the original 1938 partnership agreement giving Lin Wei-Chen a forty percent stake.
2. He shows her the unaltered appraisal sums, far lower than the figures Anna already flagged in the 1951 ledger, confirming the inflation she calculated.
3. He points out the original signatures on each entry, both his father's and Augustin's, where the current ledger shows only one.
4. He explains how Augustin used the inflated, single-signed ledger to secure a loan and then bought out Lin Wei-Chen's stake at its original, un-inflated value.
5. Anna sees her own professional findings and Simon's private evidence lock together into a single, undeniable account.
6. The audience watches the season's central forgery become fully proven for the first time, to a character equipped to understand exactly what it means.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Simon sets the last page down and tells Anna plainly that Lin Wei-Chen was his father.
2. He tells her his own son left Esmond Harbor in 1989 after a bitter argument over Simon's refusal to ever challenge the Vantrys — and that the son was her father.
3. He tells her, without softening it, that he is her grandfather.
4. Anna sits with the photograph from her pocket now explained, the family lore of "a bad business deal" suddenly given a face and a name.
5. Neither reaches for the other yet, the fact still too large to hold in the same breath as the forgery.
6. The audience watches the season's two central secrets — the fraud and the bloodline — resolve into one revelation at the same moment.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Anna asks him, once the shock has room to breathe, why he never told anyone — not the family, not the authorities, not even her father until it was too late.
2. Simon tells her about the buyout year: an immigrant with no real legal standing, a fabricated smuggling threat hanging over him if he pushed back.
3. He tells her he stayed on at the house in a demoted, uncredited role rather than lose everything trying to fight it.
4. He tells her he kept the proof anyway, all these years, not knowing what he was keeping it for until now.
5. Anna understands, watching him, that his silence was never surrender — only survival with the evidence intact.
6. The audience sees Simon's fifty-year secret finally answered for, without excusing what it cost him or his son.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Julian arrives at the workshop with a routine question about frames needed for the upcoming gala, expecting the usual quiet errand.
2. He stops in the doorway, catching the tail end of Simon's voice naming Augustin, the ledgers, and a stake bought out for nothing.
3. He sees the ledger pages and correspondence spread across the table and Anna's face, still raw with what she's just learned.
4. Simon and Anna both go quiet, but not quickly enough to undo what Julian has already heard.
5. Julian doesn't yet have the full account, only enough fragments to know this is bigger and older than the discrepancies he and Nora found.
6. The audience watches the private revelation become a shared one before either Simon or Anna is ready.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Julian follows Anna to the empty salesroom minutes later, unwilling to let the fragments sit unexplained.
2. Shaken, Anna decides there's no version of this she can responsibly withhold from him now and tells him the rest.
3. She lays out the forgery in full — the stripped signature, the inflated sums, the buyout at the original valuation — confirming everything Julian and Nora had only suspected.
4. She tells him, too, that Simon Lin is her grandfather, and that Lin Wei-Chen was her great-grandfather.
5. Julian realizes the woman he's been quietly consulting for weeks is the very family his own grandfather defrauded.
6. The audience watches Julian's private ledger investigation and Anna's private family history collapse into a single, undeniable truth.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Julian sits with what he's just learned, weighing it against Marcus Tang's pointed hint to Marguerite and the still-looming museum-loan deadline.
2. He recognizes that a partial, weaponized version of this truth may already be moving through Marcus's hands, whether Marcus fully understands it yet or not.
3. He decides that containment — the quiet, careful path he's kept to for weeks — is no longer possible, and no longer right.
4. He resolves that his mother has to hear the truth from him, tonight, before anyone else forces it into the open on worse terms.
5. Anna, still absorbing that Simon is her grandfather, tells Julian she isn't ready to face Marguerite herself yet, and he agrees to go alone.
6. The audience watches Julian choose disclosure over the caution that has defined his last several weeks.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Julian finds Marguerite alone that evening and asks her to sit before he says anything else.
2. He tells her, plainly and without softening it, that Augustin forged the 1951 ledgers, stripped Lin Wei-Chen's signature, and cheated him out of his true stake.
3. He tells her Simon Lin has held the original correspondence and ledger pages for decades, and that the outside appraiser she welcomed into the house is Simon's own granddaughter.
4. Marguerite doesn't speak for a long moment, fifty years of the story she's told and believed collapsing in real time.
5. She asks Julian to leave her alone with it rather than react in front of him, needing time before she can decide what any of this means for the family or the house.
6. The audience watches the season's founding myth break for the one character who believed it most completely.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Drawn by the closed door and the low, tense voices inside, Nora comes looking for Julian and finds him and Marguerite shutting the conversation down as she arrives.
2. She asks what's happened, and neither Julian nor Marguerite tells her anything, both too raw and unready to explain.
3. Nora reads the closed door, the shaken faces, and the silence as further proof of exactly what she's suspected for weeks.
4. She concludes this is more evidence of Julian and Anna, not a family secret that simply hasn't reached her yet.
5. She leaves without pressing further, her suspicion hardening into something she now feels sure of.
6. The audience, holding the true cause of the room's tension, watches Nora walk away more certain of the wrong thing than ever.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At Meridian Auctions, Marcus's contacts report unusual activity inside the Vantry house tonight — hushed movement, a closed-door meeting, a visibly shaken household.
2. Marcus presses for specifics and gets none, only that something has shifted suddenly and significantly.
3. He projects confidence to his staff, framing whatever has happened as likely working in his favor, though privately he isn't certain if it strengthens or destroys his position.
4. He decides to hold his planned pressure on Marguerite until he understands what's actually happened inside the house.
5. The audience, knowing the truth has just broken open on the Vantrys' own terms, watches Marcus circle a fire he still doesn't know he's standing next to.
