--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is the next morning, roughly twelve hours after Simon's ultimatum in the empty salesroom at the close of episode seven.
2. Marguerite gathers Julian and Nora before the house opens, still absorbing both Simon's short deadline and her own private offer to Anna three days earlier.
3. She admits to them together, for the first time, that Anna refused to decide anything on Simon's behalf and that her plan for a quiet settlement never had real footing.
4. Julian tells her his honest answer to the lender at the gala has already closed off whatever room for further careful ambiguity she still imagined.
5. Weighing Simon's deadline, the museum-loan clock, and Marcus's public probing against each other, Marguerite decides the family will come forward on its own before anyone forces the timing for them.
6. Nora, hearing the decision made in front of her rather than managed around her, feels steadied by being included after months of being kept at the edge of the family's real business.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Marguerite asks Simon and Anna to bring the true 1938 documents to the archive so the whole family can see them together, not secondhand through Julian's account.
2. Simon lays out the original correspondence and unaltered ledger pages before Marguerite, Julian, and Nora, the same bundle he first showed Anna days earlier.
3. Marguerite reads Augustin's own hand beside the forged 1951 entries herself, seeing exactly where her great-grandfather's signature replaced Lin Wei-Chen's.
4. Nora recognizes the mismatched signature she flagged weeks ago now fully explained, her earliest suspicion finally given the whole picture behind it.
5. Anna sets her own appraisal calculations beside Simon's documents, her professional findings and his private evidence confirming the exact same account in front of the family for the first time.
6. Marguerite tells Simon plainly, with everyone present, that the house owes his family more than an apology, though she doesn't yet say what that will look like.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Later that day, alone again in the workshop, Simon and Anna talk through the morning's meeting without an audience for the first time.
2. Anna tells him she's decided to stay in Esmond Harbor past the end of her assignment, unwilling to close the file and leave now that she has a grandfather in it.
3. Simon tells her he doesn't want them to go back to being a careful restorer and appraiser who happen to share blood once the scandal settles.
4. They agree plainly to be family in the open from now on, not something managed quietly around the house's convenience.
5. Anna asks what he wants said publicly about himself, and Simon tells her he wants his name and his father's name on the record, not folded into hers.
6. The audience watches fifty years of managed distance close into a relationship neither of them has to conceal anymore.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. That evening at the Vantry house, Julian sits with Nora and lays out the loan paperwork in full for the first time, not just the fact of it but the exact terms and the exact company behind the call-in.
2. He tells her the lawyer has now traced the shell company through two layers back to Meridian Auctions, confirming what he could only suspect before.
3. Nora asks why he's telling her everything now instead of managing it quietly the way he's managed things all season.
4. Julian tells her he's done deciding alone what she can handle, and that the debt is theirs to face together, not his to carry silently on her behalf.
5. Nora sets the old hotel receipt on the table between them and tells him she's ready to let it stop meaning what she once believed it meant.
6. Both recognize, without needing to say it, that the trust between them has been rebuilt through weeks of chosen honesty rather than any single conversation.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. That night, Marcus hears through his lender contact that the Vantry family met privately with the outside appraiser and the old restorer, and that a public statement may be close.
2. He recognizes his carefully timed pressure at the gala has backfired, pushing the family toward disclosure instead of toward him.
3. He moves to arrange one last approach to Marguerite before any statement goes out, hoping to still secure a discounted sale framed as rescue rather than opportunism.
4. He instructs his staff to keep the full extent of his family's shell company holdings and the mortgage debt they control out of any conversation with the Vantrys, whatever becomes public about the ledgers.
5. He tells himself the ledger scandal, if it breaks first, doesn't have to end his two-year campaign, only change its shape.
6. The audience watches Marcus race a clock he no longer controls, still withholding the one piece of leverage that hasn't yet been taken from him.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. The next morning, the family gathers in the salesroom with Simon and Anna present, the insurer and a small circle of press contacted ahead of time.
2. Marguerite reads a prepared statement acknowledging the 1951 ledgers were altered, naming both Augustin Vantry and Lin Wei-Chen on the record for the first time in the collection's history.
3. Julian confirms publicly that the family's own review and the insurer's independent re-appraisal reached the same conclusion, closing off any question of the house minimizing the finding.
4. Nora stands beside Julian through the statement, present as his wife rather than watching from a distance the way she has for most of the season.
5. Simon stands in the room this time rather than at its edges, introduced to the gathered press as Lin Wei-Chen's son and the collection's longtime restorer.
6. Marcus, arriving too late to make his planned approach, watches his remaining leverage evaporate in the same room where he first raised the rumor publicly.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. After the statement, Marguerite and Simon sit together privately to settle what the family will actually give back, no longer negotiating through Anna as an intermediary.
2. Marguerite offers Simon formal recognition in the collection's own records, a corrected appraisal history, and a financial settlement reflecting the true value of his father's original stake.
3. Simon tells her recognition matters more to him than money ever will, though he doesn't refuse the settlement either.
4. Marguerite admits her first private offer was built on a misunderstanding of Anna's authority, and that this is the conversation she should have had from the start.
5. They agree on terms neither treats as fully sufficient for fifty years, but both accept as honest rather than another quiet containment.
6. The audience sees restitution arrive not as closure exactly, but as the first honest accounting the house has ever offered the Lin family.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Marguerite and Julian go to Meridian Auctions together to answer Marcus directly rather than wait for his next approach.
2. They tell him plainly that the family has already gone public, and that whatever leverage his partial knowledge of the ledgers gave him no longer exists.
3. Marcus, hearing the statement has already broken, drops the pretense of rescue and reveals instead that he still holds the family's mortgage debt and three minority shares.
4. Julian tells him the debt and the shares are now an ordinary business matter, to be resolved through normal channels rather than pressure or threat.
5. Marcus concedes the ledger campaign is over but makes clear, without saying how, that he has no intention of walking away from the debt or shares he already controls.
6. The audience sees one thread of the season close while another — the money and shares still in Marcus's hands — stays open on his terms, not the Vantrys'.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. That evening, Simon and Anna walk the waterfront together, the same pier where she first arrived at the season's start.
2. A passerby who read the morning's statement recognizes them by name, the first small proof their relationship is no longer private.
3. Anna takes out the old photograph of Lin Wei-Chen she's carried all season and tells Simon she wants a new one taken, of the two of them, to sit beside it.
4. Simon tells her he never expected to get this, only ever expected to keep the proof and hope someone worthy of it eventually found it.
5. They stand together as grandfather and granddaughter in full view of the harbor, nothing left between them to manage or conceal.
6. The audience watches the Hidden Heir thread resolve into the relationship the season set out to complete.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. At home, Julian and Nora sit together the way they haven't all season, without paperwork, ledgers, or unfinished conversation between them.
2. Nora tells him the last of her suspicion is gone, not because of any single fact but because he kept choosing to tell her the truth even when it cost him.
3. Julian tells her he understands now that protecting her by staying silent was never really protection, only a habit he's glad to be done with.
4. They talk plainly about the debt and shares Marcus still holds, agreeing to face whatever comes next as partners rather than one shielding the other.
5. Neither pretends the last months didn't happen, but both recognize the marriage left standing is stronger for having been tested rather than spared.
6. The audience sees the Affair That Wasn't thread close on earned trust rather than a single tidy apology.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Narration turns to the harbor at the close of the week that began with a gala and ended with the house's history rewritten in public.
2. The Founding Collection remains on display, its catalogue now listing both Augustin Vantry and Lin Wei-Chen as its founders, a sixty-year erasure undone.
3. Marguerite has told the family's story differently for the first time in her life, and finds the truth harder to hold but easier to live inside than the myth was.
4. Simon and Anna's names now sit publicly beside a history he spent fifty years guarding alone, though he never expected to see it read the way it does now.
5. Marcus Tang's debt and shares remain unresolved, a deliberately open thread the family will face on ordinary terms rather than under threat.
6. The audience is left at the water's edge with the season's central forgery finally accounted for, and one quiet, unfinished danger still waiting its turn.
