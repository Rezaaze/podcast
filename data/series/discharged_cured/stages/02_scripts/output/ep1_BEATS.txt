--- SCENE 1 ---
1. A demolition crew works the last standing wing of Hollow Pines Asylum, 2026, ahead of a scheduled teardown.
2. A wrecking claw punches through an interior wall that was clearly bricked up long after the original construction.
3. The foreman calls a halt when the debris reveals a cavity instead of solid wall.
4. Inside the cavity sits a sealed metal box, undisturbed for decades.
5. Nobody on site yet knows what the box contains or why it was hidden.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The foreman pries the rusted seal off the box in front of a skeleton local news crew filming a routine demolition-day segment.
2. Inside are stacks of Polaroids and several handwritten diaries, water-stained but legible.
3. The foreman flips through a few photos aloud, reading handwritten names and dates off the backs without grasping their significance.
4. The news crew films the discovery mostly for a human-interest angle, not realizing it's anything more than an oddity.
5. The audience sees enough of the photos and captions to register 'discharged cured' as a recurring phrase — a detail no one on screen comments on.
6. The box is bagged as evidence/curiosity and logged, its contents not yet examined by anyone who understands what they're looking at.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Elias shows Maya the local news clip of the demolition find, buzzing about it as a story lead.
2. He frames it as a quirky human-interest hook — sealed time capsule, creepy old asylum, easy audience appeal.
3. Maya is lukewarm at first, more interested in their current caseload than an old building's basement.
4. Elias pushes, noting his archive contacts could get them access to the actual box contents fast.
5. Maya agrees to look into it, still treating it as a minor, likely-uneventful curiosity story.
6. Neither of them yet suspects the discrepancy between the diaries and the public record.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Maya sits alone reviewing digital scans of the Polaroids Elias's contact sent over.
2. She flips through face after face, jotting notes on the 'discharged cured' pattern as sloppy old paperwork.
3. One photograph stops her — a young woman's face she recognizes, though she can't place why at first.
4. A beat later it lands: the resemblance, maybe the name scrawled on the back, ties uncomfortably close to her own family.
5. She sets the photo down, visibly shaken, and doesn't call Elias over to share it.
6. The audience sees her hide the photo separately from the rest of the stack.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Elias notices Maya has gone quiet and asks if something in the photos got to her.
2. Maya deflects, saying it's nothing, just an old building and sad old photos.
3. Elias half-believes her but clocks that her reaction seemed personal, not just professional.
4. Maya changes the subject back to logistics — archive hours, who they need to talk to next.
5. The audience knows she's lying by omission about the photo that unsettled her; Elias doesn't push further, yet.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. A junior aide in Senator Diana Chen's office flags a local news segment about the Hollow Pines demolition find.
2. The aide frames it as a nothing-story — old building, old photos, mild local interest, filing it as a courtesy heads-up.
3. Diana's attention sharpens the instant she hears 'Hollow Pines,' though she keeps her reaction controlled in front of the aide.
4. She asks a couple of pointed follow-up questions — who's covering it, is anyone digging — that reveal more interest than the aide expects.
5. The aide leaves thinking he's handled a minor task; the audience senses Diana's calm is a mask.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Diana calls Tao Wu privately, away from her staff.
2. She tells him about the demolition find and instructs him to keep an eye on the podcast's interest without making a scene.
3. She frames it to Tao as routine due diligence, not urgent — 'probably nothing, but watch it.'
4. Tao agrees smoothly, giving no sign of how much he already understands about what could be in that capsule.
5. The audience senses Diana knows more than she's letting on to Tao, and Tao knows more than he's letting on to her — neither fully levels with the other.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Alone at his desk, Tao pulls up everything public he can find on the Hollow Pines demolition and the podcast duo now sniffing around it.
2. He cross-references the little available information about the diaries with old family stories he's never repeated to anyone, including Diana.
3. A name surfaces in his research — Mei Tam — that hits him with immediate, private recognition.
4. He sits with that recognition for a long moment, saying nothing, betraying nothing on a call or in a note to Diana.
5. The audience learns Tao has a personal, hidden connection to Mei Tam that Diana does not know about.
6. He closes his laptop having decided, silently, to handle this thread himself.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Maya and Elias sit at the county archive combing through Hollow Pines discharge records from the 1970s.
2. They cross-reference patient names from the Polaroids against the official discharge ledger.
3. Every name they check comes back the same way: 'discharged cured,' followed by no further public trace — no obituary, no subsequent address, nothing.
4. Elias, still trusting the process, assumes they just need to check a different archive or ask a records clerk for deeper files.
5. Maya says less than she's thinking, still not mentioning the photo that unsettled her earlier.
6. The pattern of dead ends starts to feel less like bad bookkeeping and more like a wall.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Elias tallies the discharge outcomes against death certificate records for the same names and time period.
2. The numbers don't add up — patients who should have died of natural causes over fifty years have no death certificates anywhere in the county or state system.
3. Too many names show the exact same blank: discharged, then nothing, ever again.
4. Elias voices the uncomfortable thought aloud — this isn't sloppy paperwork, this is a pattern.
5. Maya, unsettled by her own private discovery, agrees more readily than her usual skepticism would allow.
6. Neither of them can yet explain why, but both now believe something was deliberately hidden.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Elderly Mei Tam sits alone in her apartment watching the demolition segment on the evening news.
2. The footage shows the wall coming down and a brief glimpse of the box being opened.
3. Her face registers recognition and dread that she doesn't share with anyone in the room, because there is no one else in the room.
4. She mutes the television and sits in silence, the weight of fifty years of secrecy visibly pressing on her.
5. The audience understands, without her saying it, that she knows exactly what's in that capsule and has been dreading this moment for decades.
6. She makes no move to contact anyone — not the news, not the podcast, not authorities — reinforcing her belief that staying quiet has always worked before.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Maya and Elias walk the overgrown grounds behind the Hollow Pines ruins, following an old cemetery plot map from the archive.
2. They find St. Agnes Cemetery's back rows: markers bearing only numbers, no names, no dates.
3. The number of graves roughly matches the number of 'discharged cured' patients they can't otherwise trace.
4. Elias voices the growing, unspoken theory: these might be the same people the discharge records claim went home.
5. Maya stays quiet longer than usual, the personal stakes she's hiding pressing harder now that the theory is forming a shape.
6. Both leave with the same unspoken certainty: this story is much bigger, and much darker, than either expected.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. At Sunday family dinner, Diana steers casual conversation toward 'an old story surfacing' about Hollow Pines, watching her son Daniel closely as she does.
2. She keeps her tone light, testing whether the name means anything to him yet.
3. Daniel reacts with mild, genuine indifference — to him it's just an old building he's never thought about.
4. Diana relaxes slightly at his lack of reaction, though she doesn't explain to him or anyone else at the table why she was watching so carefully.
5. The audience recognizes this as Diana quietly assessing how contained the family's exposure still is.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Later, Daniel walks through the Harborview Medical Foundation building for an unrelated reason — a meeting, an event, routine family business.
2. He passes the donor wall bearing the Chen family name in gold lettering, dated 1979.
3. He glances at it the way he always has, with pride and zero suspicion, thinking nothing of the timing.
4. The audience, primed by what we already know, registers the one-year gap between the asylum's 1978 closure and the foundation's 1979 founding that Daniel doesn't even see.
5. Daniel moves on, oblivious, reinforcing how effectively the family history has been buried from even its own next generation.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Tao meets Diana in private to report on what he's learned so far.
2. He tells her the capsule doesn't just hold photographs — it holds handwritten diaries, which raises the stakes considerably.
3. Diana's composure cracks slightly at 'diaries,' understanding immediately that a diary can narrate in a way photos can't.
4. She presses Tao on whether the diaries could be traced back to specifics — to her grandfather, to the settlement fund — and he gives a carefully incomplete answer.
5. Tao still says nothing about recognizing Mei Tam's name, letting Diana believe he's starting from the same blank slate she is.
6. Diana ends the conversation more rattled than she shows, her earlier assumption that everything is safely sealed now visibly shaken.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Maya calls her mother with a seemingly offhand question, asking about her grandmother's time and connection to the area near Hollow Pines.
2. Her mother's tone shifts immediately — clipped, redirecting, uncharacteristically evasive for a normally chatty conversation.
3. Maya presses gently, and her mother deflects harder, changing the subject entirely rather than answering.
4. Maya hangs up more unsettled than before, now certain her family connection to the photo isn't a coincidence.
5. The audience is left with the same open question as Maya: what does her mother know, and why won't she say it.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Diana meets with her campaign team, having decided overnight how to handle the growing risk.
2. She overrules any suggestion to wait and see, insisting they need to get ahead of the story before the podcast or press frame it first.
3. She proposes shaping a sympathetic public narrative around the Chen family's philanthropic legacy, preemptively, before anyone asks a hard question.
4. Tao watches her make this move without revealing what he now privately knows about Mei Tam or the diaries' contents.
5. The audience understands Diana is choosing offense over concealment, unaware that the true depth of the story — including a living witness — is already further along than she thinks.
6. The episode ends on Diana's resolve, setting up a collision course with Maya and Elias's investigation in the next episode.
