--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Maya is at the Harborview Medical Foundation building, following a hunch from the ledger photographs.
2. She notices a donor recognition wall listing early benefactors and medical directors of affiliated institutions, including Hollow Pines.
3. The plaque names Dr. Edmund Chen as Hollow Pines' medical director, 1968 to 1975 - the first time the investigation has seen the name in writing anywhere public.
4. Maya photographs the plaque, her pulse picking up at the surname's possible tie to the trial-era funding.
5. She doesn't yet connect it to Diana Chen specifically, only registering "Chen" as a lead worth chasing.
6. She calls Elias immediately, unable to sit on the discovery.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Maya and Elias sit down with the plaque photo and Diana Chen's public biographical material.
2. They trace Diana's published family tree back to a grandfather named Edmund Chen, matching the plaque exactly.
3. Elias lays out the timeline: Edmund Chen directed Hollow Pines from 1968 to 1975, covering the trial years Mei Tam described.
4. Maya feels the pieces lock together - funding, falsified discharges, and Mei Tam's near-slip all pointing to one family.
5. Elias cautions that a shared surname and matching dates aren't proof enough to name Chen publicly yet.
6. They agree the next move is confirming it directly with Mei Tam before anything airs.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Maya returns to Riverside and shows Mei Tam the plaque photo without saying the name aloud first.
2. Mei Tam goes still the instant she sees "Edmund Chen," a stillness Maya reads as recognition rather than surprise.
3. Maya asks plainly whether this is the man who ordered the drug trial.
4. Mei Tam says nothing for a long moment, visibly weighing how much this confirms against what it risks exposing.
5. She neither confirms nor denies, telling Maya only that she needs time before she says anything more.
6. Maya leaves certain she's watched a decades-old secret nearly surface, unaware Mei Tam is protecting far more than the doctor's name.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone in her room, Mei Tam takes out a small locked box hidden from staff and visitors alike.
2. Inside is a page torn from an old diary, written in her own hand decades ago, naming Dr. Chen and describing doses given without consent.
3. She reads her younger handwriting, remembering the fear that made her keep this record instead of destroying it.
4. She weighs giving Maya this one page against everything else the diary could reveal if read in full.
5. She decides the page naming Chen is a secret she can afford to lose; the rest of the diary, including any mention of the patient she saved, stays hidden.
6. She sets the single page aside for Maya, keeping the rest of the diary locked away.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Daniel's editor calls him in and assigns him the story on Harborview Foundation's historic donors.
2. The editor notes, half compliment and half strategy, that Daniel's family connection to Harborview could open doors other reporters don't have.
3. Daniel senses more calculation behind the assignment than the editor admits, though he doesn't push it.
4. He accepts without mentioning how close it already sits to the story Maya is chasing.
5. The audience recognizes what Daniel doesn't yet grasp: his own outlet is quietly using his family access as a resource.
6. Daniel leaves the meeting already thinking about how to keep this assignment separate from his mother.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. A staffer briefs Diana that the "Chen" name has surfaced in reporter inquiries around Hollow Pines.
2. Diana keeps her reaction controlled, giving nothing away about how much she already knows.
3. She instructs the team to prepare a general statement on the family's philanthropic history, avoiding Hollow Pines directly.
4. Internally, she calculates how long the "shocked descendant" posture can hold now that the name itself is moving.
5. She decides not to loop in Daniel yet, wanting to control when and how he learns anything from her directly.
6. The audience sees her already managing a crisis she has, in fact, known about for years.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Tao learns from a contact that Maya was seen photographing the donor wall at Harborview.
2. He recognizes immediately that the plaque names Edmund Chen and that Maya now has it in writing.
3. He begins making calls to see whether the plaque's wording or provenance can be quietly reviewed before more reporters see it.
4. He doesn't yet know Daniel has also been assigned a story that intersects with the same building.
5. He decides against telling Diana about the plaque specifically, judging it easier to handle without alarming her further.
6. The audience senses his countermeasures are already a step behind what's already been photographed and shared.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Maya meets Daniel at their usual diner and, testing him, shows him the plaque photo without warning.
2. Daniel's reaction is a half-second too controlled, confirming to Maya that the name isn't new to him.
3. He admits only that Edmund Chen was his great-grandfather, downplaying how much weight that carries for him.
4. Maya watches him carefully, her earlier suspicion sharpening into something closer to certainty.
5. Daniel doesn't mention his new assignment from his editor, keeping that overlap to himself.
6. Maya leaves believing Daniel knows more than he's saying, though she still can't prove it.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Daniel goes to Diana directly and asks outright what she knows about Edmund Chen and Hollow Pines.
2. Diana gives a careful, partial answer, admitting the family connection but claiming she's only just learning the details herself.
3. Daniel presses on his father's old warning about an "old scandal," asking if this is what his father meant.
4. Diana's composure holds, but something in her timing makes Daniel doubt she's telling him everything.
5. She reassures him this is being handled and asks him to be careful with his own reporting, which he notices without naming aloud.
6. Daniel leaves with his suspicion about his mother's long-standing knowledge quietly confirmed, though still unspoken.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Maya returns to Riverside and Mei Tam is ready this time, the single diary page already set aside.
2. Mei Tam hands over the page, naming Dr. Edmund Chen and describing doses given without consent, in her own decades-old handwriting.
3. She tells Maya this is everything she's willing to give, closing off further questions about herself or anyone else involved.
4. Maya asks about the patients who never came back, and Mei Tam repeats that this page is all there is.
5. Internally, Mei Tam believes naming only Chen will satisfy the investigation and keep the deeper secret safely buried.
6. Maya leaves with documentary proof at last, unaware of how much more Mei Tam is still protecting.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Elias cross-checks the diary page and plaque against county incorporation and licensing filings for Hollow Pines.
2. The filings confirm Edmund Chen as medical director from 1968 to 1975, matching every other source they have.
3. He also finds the filings link the Chen family's charitable program to research funding in the same years, tightening the connection to Harborview.
4. Elias feels the story is now airtight, no longer dependent on memory or a nurse's word alone.
5. He doesn't yet see the pressure building around Diana or Tao's earlier countermeasures, focused only on the documents in front of him.
6. He tells Maya the story is ready.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Maya and Elias agree the evidence - diary page, plaque, filings - is enough to name Edmund Chen publicly.
2. Elias feels a flicker of unease about how fast this is moving and what it could cost the show, but frames it as ordinary editorial caution instead of voicing the real worry.
3. Maya, eager to move, doesn't notice the hesitation runs deeper than he lets on.
4. They set a plan to air the segment on the next broadcast of The Static Hour.
5. Neither raises Daniel by name, though Maya's private uncertainty about him goes unspoken into the decision.
6. They shake on the timeline, both believing the story is now safe to run.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Tao learns through his contacts that Maya and Elias are preparing to air the Edmund Chen story imminently.
2. He goes to Diana and tells her directly the broadcast is coming, without admitting how close his earlier countermeasures came to failing.
3. Diana asks what can still be done, and Tao is forced to admit there's no way to stop it in time.
4. He recommends she prepare a public statement now rather than be caught reacting live.
5. Diana's composure flickers at the confirmation that the "shocked descendant" strategy is about to be tested in real time.
6. Tao leaves the meeting aware that containment has failed, though he still doesn't tell her the full extent of how it slipped.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. The Static Hour airs the segment: Edmund Chen named publicly as Hollow Pines' medical director during the unconsented drug trial years.
2. Maya and Elias present the diary page, the plaque, and the county filings as corroborating evidence.
3. The segment stops short of naming Diana or the current Chen family, focusing only on the historical facts.
4. Listeners immediately begin connecting Edmund Chen to the prominent Chen family and Diana's political profile.
5. This marks the first time any of the objective facts have been spoken aloud publicly rather than pieced together privately.
6. The audience senses the story has now left the reporters' control and become public property.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Diana's phone starts ringing within minutes of the broadcast - press, allies, donors, all wanting a response.
2. She attempts her prepared "shocked descendant" statement, but the volume and speed of the calls outpaces her composure.
3. A pointed question about the foundation's funding history catches her off guard, and her controlled tone slips for a moment.
4. She recovers quickly, but not before the crack is visible to whoever is on the other end of that call.
5. Internally she registers, for the first time, that this crisis may not be as manageable as every prior one she's handled.
6. The audience witnesses the first real fracture in the composure she's maintained since Tao began managing this.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Daniel watches the broadcast land and immediately thinks of his own assignment on Harborview's donor history.
2. He weighs writing the story straight, which would corroborate Maya's reporting, against softening it to protect his mother.
3. He hasn't told his editor how personally close this story now sits, and feels that omission catching up with him.
4. He decides, at least for tonight, not to file anything that would add fuel to what's already airing.
5. He tells himself this is professional caution, though it's really loyalty to Diana pulling the decision.
6. The audience sees him choosing family over the story for the first time, a choice he hasn't admitted even to himself.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. Maya and Daniel meet again after the broadcast, the earlier ease between them now charged with everything unsaid.
2. Maya watches him for any reaction to his family's name being aired, and Daniel doesn't pretend it isn't personal.
3. He hints, carefully, that there's more to the story than what just went out - more than even he's told her.
4. Maya presses gently, but Daniel pulls back before saying anything specific.
5. Neither says aloud that his family loyalty and her growing suspicion are now pulling them in opposite directions.
6. The scene ends with Daniel's hint hanging between them, confirming Maya's suspicion without resolving it.
