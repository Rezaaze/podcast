--- SCENE 1 ---
1. The Narrator sets the scene at Static Hour Studio on publication morning, the "Buried Patients: The Reckoning" episode queued to go live at nine.
2. The Narrator recaps that every thread — the ledger, the diary, Grace Sullivan's name — has quietly converged over the last week without anyone holding the full picture until now.
3. The Narrator notes that today several people will hear truths spoken aloud for the first time, some by choice and some by accident.
4. The scene closes on the studio's quiet before the upload, the calm before every phone in three storylines starts ringing at once.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Maya and Elias sit before the mic recording their closing lines for the finale episode, the draft script open between them.
2. Elias admits, for the first time plainly, how frightened he's been since the legal threat, no longer hiding it behind logistics talk.
3. Maya acknowledges the fear but says the story is finished being buried, referencing Mei Tam's diary and the county records without yet naming Grace Sullivan on air.
4. They agree the episode will name the falsified discharge records and the numbered graves, but leave Tao Wu's family and Maya's personal connection out of the public version for now.
5. Elias hits publish, and the two sit in the silence after, aware they can't unpublish what happens next.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. At Riverside, Mei Tam listens to the finale episode air on a staff member's borrowed phone, headphones pressed close.
2. She hears her own falsified paperwork described in broad strokes, relieved Maya kept her identity and Grace's name out of the aired version.
3. Despite the relief, she feels the old fear resurface — that speaking any of this aloud, even secondhand, could still bring consequences down on her.
4. A staff member notices her reaction and asks if she's alright; Mei Tam deflects, saying only that it's "an old story finally told."

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. In Diana's Capitol office, she and Tao watch the episode's release numbers climb in real time.
2. Diana asks Tao whether the cease-and-desist can still contain the story now that it's public; Tao admits the framing was contained but the underlying facts are not.
3. Tao doesn't mention that he showed Diana only one page of the ledger, still withholding how much more it names.
4. Diana's phone begins buzzing with press inquiries before she can finish the thought, the fallout arriving faster than either expected.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. At his newsroom, Daniel finalizes and files his own independent account, breaking explicitly from the framing his mother's team has been managing.
2. He includes the ledger's existence and Wu Jian's falsified records, though he leaves out the precise personal debt Tao described to him on the dock.
3. He knows this will read as a direct rebuke of his mother's silence, and he publishes it anyway rather than folding his reporting into her narrative.
4. An editor asks if he's sure about running a story that implicates his own family; Daniel says he's more sure of this than anything he's written all year.
5. The audience recognizes Daniel is now publicly and irreversibly on record against his mother's version of events.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. At St. Agnes Cemetery, county workers begin cross-referencing the numbered potter's-field plots against the discharge dates named in Maya's episode.
2. A supervisor confirms aloud that several burial dates fall within days of official 'discharge' dates, matching the objective facts the podcast surfaced.
3. Local reporters start gathering at the cemetery gate, drawn by Daniel's independent story naming specific plots.
4. A worker flags one grave as unmatched to any name yet, setting it aside for later — the same plot Tao will later visit alone.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Back in Diana's office, the phones do not stop, aides relaying calls from press, donors, and party leadership in overlapping waves.
2. An aide tells Diana the crisis PR firm recommends silence; Diana overrules them, saying silence now would look identical to guilt.
3. Diana decides she will make a public statement herself rather than let the story be shaped entirely by Daniel's account and the podcast.
4. Tao warns her that any statement risks confirming details she can't take back; Diana says she'd rather control what's confirmed than let it leak piecemeal.
5. She tells her staff to schedule the statement for that afternoon, giving herself only hours to decide what to admit and what to withhold.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Alone with Diana before the statement, Tao asks directly whether his father's name will be spoken aloud in what she's about to say.
2. Diana says the statement will reference "a Hollow Pines employee" without naming Wu Jian specifically, an offer of protection.
3. Tao doesn't fully trust the offer, aware Diana's own instinct for self-preservation could change her mind under press pressure.
4. He tells her plainly that if his father's name becomes public, it will be on Tao's own terms, not leaked through her statement or anyone else's account.
5. Diana agrees, though something unresolved remains between them — the ledger's fuller contents still unspoken, her own suppression still only partly acknowledged even to him.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Diana delivers her press statement, confirming her grandfather's foundation was seeded from Hollow Pines-era money and that she is stepping back from the governor's race.
2. She frames her decision as taking responsibility, while carefully omitting the decades of sealing requests her own legal team filed, including the ones Tao's father's connections began.
3. She characterizes what suppression she does admit as her family's distant past rather than her own recent, active choices.
4. Watching from the office, Tao notes she never mentions the ledger or his father by name, honoring what he asked — but he also notices how much of her own hand in this she's left out.
5. Diana privately believes that stepping back from politics closes the chapter for good, unaware of how much still sits unresolved beneath the statement she just gave.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. That evening at the Chen estate, Daniel and Diana finally have the conversation they've avoided for years, no aides or cameras present.
2. Diana admits to Daniel the full scope of her grandfather's trials and the suppression that followed, no longer softening it the way she did in her public statement.
3. Daniel tells her he already knows most of it — from Tao, from his own reporting — and that what hurt more was being managed instead of told.
4. Diana confirms Tao's father's role to Daniel directly, completing what Daniel had pieced together on the dock, though she still doesn't detail her own legal team's full suppression efforts.
5. Something between them shifts toward honesty for the first time, even as Diana quietly holds onto the belief that this conversation, like her statement, is enough to close things.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At Riverside, Maya returns to thank Mei Tam in person for finally telling her the truth about the diary and the falsified records.
2. Mei Tam admits the relief of finally saying it aloud, even secondhand through the aired episode, is bigger than she expected.
3. Underneath the relief, Mei Tam confesses she still fears consequences — that a duress defense might not protect a woman her age from whatever comes next.
4. Maya reassures her, but privately isn't certain either, aware the legal fallout is still unfolding around Diana and Tao.
5. The scene sets up the moment to come, Maya telling Mei Tam there's one more thing she needs to ask her — about Grace.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Still at Riverside, Maya calls her mother again, this time with Mei Tam seated beside her rather than alone in the studio.
2. Maya's mother confirms what Maya has suspected since finding the old paper — that Grace Sullivan was her grandmother's original name before she remarried in Korea.
3. Mei Tam, hearing this in real time, finally learns that Grace survived, remarried, and that Maya is her direct granddaughter.
4. The confirmation lands as both relief and grief for Mei Tam — the patient she risked everything for did survive, decades after Mei Tam assumed she'd died anonymously.
5. Maya and Mei Tam sit with the moment together, the distance between "a case I read about" and "my grandmother" closing in front of both of them.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. At the Hollow Pines ruins, a small ceremony gathers to place a marker bearing the names of patients identified so far from the matched graves.
2. A county official reads the names aloud, including several confirmed only that week through the cemetery cross-referencing.
3. Grace Sullivan's name is not among those read, since her file was never a death record to begin with — a quiet, unspoken exception only Maya and Mei Tam would understand.
4. The gathering is small but marks the first official acknowledgment that the 'discharged cured' files concealed something else entirely.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. At St. Agnes, Tao visits the still-unmarked grave the county workers set aside earlier, alone and unannounced.
2. He confirms, from his father's ledger, that this is one of the patients Wu Jian personally helped "process out."
3. He leaves something behind at the grave — not named publicly, a private gesture rather than a legal or press act.
4. He still doesn't say his father's name aloud to anyone here, holding to what he told Diana: that if it's spoken, it will be on his terms.
5. The moment reveals Tao's private reckoning is separate from and larger than the public statement Diana gave, a debt he's decided to carry his own way.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. At the Night Owl Diner, Daniel and Maya meet again, both changed by the day's disclosures.
2. Daniel tells her about the conversation with his mother, no longer holding back the parts he protected her from at their last meeting.
3. Maya tells him about the call with her mother and Mei Tam — that Grace Sullivan was her grandmother, confirmed beyond doubt now.
4. They talk through what comes next for the story — the follow-up episode, the unresolved question of what happens to Mei Tam and to Tao's father's memory.
5. Underneath the story talk, both acknowledge, more directly than before, that whatever this has become between them isn't only about the case anymore.
6. They leave the diner without a finished plan, but for the first time without anything major left unspoken between them.

--- SCENE 16 ---
1. Late that night at Static Hour Studio, Maya and Elias record a closing note for the series, acknowledging what's still unresolved.
2. They state on air that Diana Chen's statement left the full extent of her legal team's suppression efforts unaddressed, without accusing her outright.
3. They note that the identity of the Hollow Pines employee behind the falsified records remains unnamed publicly, respecting that it isn't their story to force into the open.
4. Maya adds a personal line, carefully worded, about a patient once written off as lost who turned out not to be — without stating outright that the patient is her own grandmother.
5. Elias, no longer hiding his nerves, admits on the record that finishing this story scared him the whole way through, and that he's glad they didn't stop.

--- SCENE 17 ---
1. The Narrator returns at the Hollow Pines ruins at dusk, describing the final demolition equipment leaving the site.
2. The Narrator notes that the ground, disturbed for months by workers, cameras, and grief, is quiet for the first time in fifty years.
3. The Narrator reflects that not every question got answered — Tao's father's name still unspoken, Diana's full suppression still only partly confessed — and that some debts outlast the people who owe them.
4. The Narrator closes on the image of the marked and unmarked graves alike, side by side, the story ending not with resolution but with the ground finally being allowed to rest.
