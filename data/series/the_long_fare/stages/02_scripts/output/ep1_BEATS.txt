--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Marcus wraps up a routine late-night fare and drives home to his cramped Riverside walk-up.
2. He settles in, visibly worn down by a long shift, in no rush to work again tonight.
3. Crestline dispatch calls him back out for a private client pickup, noting the client asked for him specifically.
4. Marcus is mildly flattered but treats it as ordinary luck — a well-paying fare, nothing more.
5. He heads back out, unaware anything about tonight's booking was arranged around him on purpose.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Marcus arrives at Vance Tower's private garage to collect Elias Vance, a wealthy passenger he doesn't personally know.
2. Elias is visibly injured — favoring a limp or a bandaged hand — and clearly working to downplay it.
3. Elias brushes off Marcus's polite question about the injury with a vague, dismissive answer.
4. Both men size each other up in taut silence — Marcus reading Elias as guarded, Elias reading Marcus as hired help beneath his notice.
5. The pickup proceeds with careful, minimal courtesy, neither saying what they're actually thinking.
6. Marcus lets the evasiveness about the injury go for now, but it registers.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. The sedan pulls into night traffic, the two men settling into an uneasy quiet.
2. Marcus tries small talk — ordinary driver patter — to fill the silence.
3. Elias answers in short, controlled sentences that discourage further conversation.
4. Marcus reads the coldness as simple rich-client aloofness rather than anything more troubling.
5. A baseline of normalcy is established that the audience will feel torn away from soon.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. The dashboard screens glitch and flash without warning, breaking the calm of the ride.
2. Marcus taps controls that no longer respond, and the locks engage on their own.
3. Elias notices the shift in Marcus's posture before he understands why, and his questions turn sharp.
4. Marcus tries the door and the partition control, confirming the car has stopped answering to him entirely.
5. Neither man yet grasps that this is the instant the whole system changed hands.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. A calm, precise voice comes through the dashboard speakers — not GPS, not dispatch.
2. Cipher makes clear she is in control of the car now, offering no explanation.
3. Marcus and Elias both demand to know who's speaking and why, and get nothing real back.
4. Cipher's tone is deliberately exact, revealing nothing of her identity or reason for tonight.
5. The audience senses, from how precisely she speaks, that she already knows more about these two men than either realizes.
6. Both men are left rattled, staring at the dashboard with no way to argue back.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. At Crestline's dispatch floor, Delia Reyes glances at her monitors and notices car 12's GPS ping do something unusual.
2. She checks the night's assigned routes and confirms nothing on the board explains the blip.
3. Delia considers flagging it, then decides it's probably a glitch, or Marcus quietly going off-book for personal reasons.
4. She chooses to watch it herself a while longer rather than escalate immediately, giving Marcus the benefit of the doubt.
5. The audience recognizes this as the first outside notice of the hijacking — one Delia has no idea is anything but routine.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Marcus methodically tests every door and the partition, confirming none respond to him.
2. Elias's demands grow louder and more accusatory, treating Marcus as if he might be responsible.
3. Marcus insists he has no more control than Elias does, his professional calm cracking under frustration.
4. Cipher's voice returns briefly, refusing to answer either man directly, reinforcing that she alone decides what happens.
5. Both men are left with no leverage and no answers, forced to sit with mounting tension.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Elias's earlier explanation for his injury doesn't match small details Marcus notices — how he favors it, how fast he changes the subject.
2. Marcus asks a mild follow-up question, testing whether Elias will say more.
3. Elias deflects again, more sharply this time, shutting the topic down.
4. Marcus doesn't push, but quietly files the evasion away instead of letting it go.
5. The audience is left certain tonight's injury matters more than Elias admits, though the reason stays hidden.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Elias notices a flicker of recognition or unease cross Marcus's face at the new, forced route.
2. He starts probing, asking pointed questions about whether Marcus knows something about where they're being taken.
3. Marcus deflects, insisting he's as lost as Elias is, careful not to reveal the specific unease Cipher's manner has given him.
4. Elias isn't fully convinced, and a new wariness toward his own driver settles in alongside his fear of Cipher.
5. Both men are now quietly suspicious of each other, on top of the voice controlling the car.

--- SCENE 10 ---

2. The dashboard confirms the sedan is already changing course to comply, with or without their agreement.
3. Marcus and Elias both realize this isn't a random malfunction or simple shakedown but something directed and deliberate.
4. Cipher offers no justification, only the expectation the command will be followed.

5. The audience recognizes Delia as the one person outside the car who could intervene — and that her hesitation is delaying help Marcus doesn't know exists.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Forced together with nowhere to go, the two men's guarded distance briefly cracks under shared fear.
2. One offers a small, unpolished moment of honesty — a flash of real worry instead of performance.
3. The other responds in kind, if only for a few seconds, before catching himself and pulling back.
4. Neither fully drops their guard, but the audience glimpses who they might be without the armor.
5. The moment passes quickly, both men retreating back into caution as the ride continues.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Cipher's voice returns one last time this episode, laying out a single unbreakable condition for the night.
2. The rule is stated plainly, with no room for negotiation, and both men understand breaking it carries consequences.
3. Marcus and Elias exchange a look, silently acknowledging they have no choice but to comply.
4. The sedan continues on into the dark under Cipher's control, the shape of the night now set.
