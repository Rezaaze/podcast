--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Grey dawn light spreads across the wreck as the frozen standoff finally breaks, the digits on the dashboard clock ticking forward instead of holding.
2. Wei Su, still shaking from what he heard Marcus say, is the first to move, stepping past the fence line onto the frost-hardened ground he hasn't crossed since Danny died.
3. He walks toward the sedan rather than the group at the wreck, drawn by something in the way the voice earlier had cracked, unwilling yet to name what he suspects.
4. Each step across the open ground costs him visibly, ten years of avoiding this exact stretch of dirt compressed into the walk.
5. He stops just short of the open driver's door, close enough now to see the dashboard screen glowing inside, and to be heard if he speaks.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Cipher moves to the sedan's trunk to retrieve the data drive, and the interior light catches her face as she finally pulls the mask free.
2. Marcus and Elias see her clearly for the first time — younger than either expected, and, at first glance, a stranger to both.
3. Elias's attention snags instead on what the trunk light also reveals: a rigged charge whose casing and wiring he recognizes instantly as salvage from a decommissioned Vance Development site.
4. He says nothing yet about recognizing it, filing the fact away rather than naming it aloud.
5. Marcus studies Cipher's bare face and finds no name to put to it, only an unplaced, uncomfortable familiarity he keeps to himself.
6. The dashboard screen mirrors the trunk-cam feed for a moment, giving both men — and Wei Su, watching through the windshield — the same unobstructed look at the device.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Wei Su, now within arm's reach of the open door, hears Cipher speak again — not through the exterior speaker this time, but directly, a few feet away.
2. The voice lands on him like a decade collapsing at once; he doesn't need to see her face to know it.
3. He grips the doorframe, silent, recognizing his daughter's voice alive after ten years of believing he'd never hear it again.
4. Marcus and Elias notice the old man's reaction before they understand its cause, confused by how completely it has undone him.
5. Wei Su says nothing yet to either man, unable to trust his own voice enough to speak her name aloud.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Cipher turns to Marcus directly and states the exact number and date of the safety certification he signed, details no outsider should have.
2. For one flickering moment, old habit rises in him — the impulse to deny it, to reduce it to paperwork he barely remembers — before he catches himself.
3. He chooses not to lie, but it's visible how close he came to doing it anyway, the same self-protection that shaped his choice ten years ago almost repeating itself tonight.
4. The specificity of what she knows — not just that he signed, but exactly what the report said — finally tips Marcus's earlier unplaced familiarity into recognition, though he still doesn't say her name.
5. He looks at her differently now, no longer as a threat but as someone connected to what that report cost.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. In the bureau's operations room, Chen's team isolates the intrusion's final relay point and pins it to a location minutes outside the city, near Harrow Warehouse.
2. Chen assumes, based on the sophistication of the access, that they're closing in on an organized extortion operation, not a lone actor.
3. He orders the team to move immediately, treating the sedan's continued live control as an active hostage situation.
4. A tech confirms the sedan's systems are still fully compromised as of the last ping, meaning whoever is driving is still in control as the convoy rolls out.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Delia, monitoring Marcus's GPS ping from dispatch, watches it settle on a location she's never once heard him mention in ten years of friendship.
2. Her supervisor orders her to stay at her post and let Chen's team handle it, citing exactly the protocol she'd expect to hear.
3. She weighs the order for a single beat before grabbing her jacket, telling herself she'll accept whatever consequence comes after Marcus is safe.
4. Riding out, she tells herself she still believes Marcus is only a victim in whatever this is — though the location gnaws at that certainty more than she admits.
5. She leaves her post without answering dispatch's second call, already past the point of turning back.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Back at the wreck, Elias — trying to shift the moment's weight off himself — turns on Marcus and demands he tell Wei Su, plainly, what the settlement money actually was.
2. Marcus, cornered by the demand and by Wei Su's presence, admits aloud for the first time that he signed the false certification for money he needed to cover his sister's medical bills.
3. Wei Su absorbs this with a stillness more frightening than anger, hearing at last the fact he never knew was the one he'd wanted most.
4. Cornered in turn by his own tactic, Elias's composure cracks, and he admits — in front of Wei Su, unprompted this time — that the inquiry's "worker error" finding was a conclusion his own lawyers shaped to close the file.
5. He says the word "cover-up" about his own decision for the first time in ten years, and immediately looks like he wishes he could take it back.
6. Wei Su realizes, hearing both admissions inside the same hour, that he has not been the only one carrying this weight for a decade — he had simply never been told.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Wei Su turns from the two men toward the sedan and, for the first time all night, says his daughter's name aloud — Mei — through the open window.
2. Cipher goes still at the sound of it, the last of her distance already gone now that her father has spoken.
3. Wei Su tells her he believed for years that she had simply vanished into her grief, never once imagining she'd spent that decade learning exactly what it would take to do this.
4. Mei doesn't confirm or deny anything about the charge yet, but for the first time all night she doesn't correct him when he calls what she's doing dangerous rather than righteous.
5. He asks her, voice breaking, whether there's another way to finish this that doesn't end with the wing coming down — the first time all night anyone has asked her to consider it.
6. Something in the question visibly reaches her in a way none of Elias's fear or Marcus's confession did.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. The dashboard clock crosses into its final marked minute, the change audible now as a faster, insistent tone.
2. Elias, reflexively, starts talking about his security detail and lawyers again, already trying to plan how this gets managed once it's over.
3. Mei doesn't answer him, her attention still entirely on her father's question rather than on Elias's damage control.
4. Marcus watches her hand hover near, but not on, whatever controls the charge, reading in it the same restraint he noticed hours ago.
5. Wei Su doesn't move or speak again, refusing to crowd the decision he can see his daughter working through in real time.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Chen's convoy and Delia's car reach the fence line together, headlights cutting across the group gathered at the wreck.
2. Chen registers instantly that there's no criminal ring here — just an old man, two exhausted hostages, and a masked figure at a car door — and starts recalculating everything he expected to find.
3. Delia spots Marcus standing upright and breathing, and for one relieved second that's the only fact that matters to her.
4. Then she takes in the rest of the scene — his face, Wei Su beside him, the tension still hanging in the air — and the certainty that he's simply a victim tonight starts to waver for the first time.
5. Neither Chen nor Delia moves past the fence yet, both reading the standoff as still live and unfinished.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Mei looks once more at her father, then at the trunk, and makes the choice he asked her to consider.
2. She walks to the trunk and disables the detonator by hand, the faster tone on the dashboard cutting out mid-tick.
3. In the same motion, she connects the data drive to a transmitter, sending the unaltered original Harrow inspection reports and settlement records directly to Chen's bureau and a list of press contacts at once.
4. She tells the group plainly, for the first time, that the charge was never built to kill either man — only to make sure the truth came out one way or another.
5. Wei Su is the only one who doesn't look surprised by that admission.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. With the detonator disabled and the drive's contents already transmitting, the sedan's systems release their hold, the doors unlocking on their own for the first time since the hijacking began.
2. Marcus and Elias step fully out from beside the car for the first time all night.
3. Chen's team moves in to secure the scene and confirms, checking in with the bureau, that the intrusion has already stopped transmitting — the threat was disarmed before they ever crossed the fence, by the person who created it.
4. Delia reaches Marcus first, catching enough of what's still being said at the wreck to realize he was the foreman who signed off on Harrow, a fact he never once told her in ten years of friendship.
5. She has to reconcile the friend she's always known with the choice he made, even as her relief at seeing him alive doesn't waver.
6. Elias, already reaching for his phone, is the only one still thinking about how to manage what happens next.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Days later, morning light fills Marcus's apartment, the first quiet he's had since the sedan pulled through Harrow's gate.
2. Delia is there, having stayed the kind of night that doesn't get explained with a single reason, and neither of them tries to name what it means yet.
3. Marcus admits, for the first time, that he never expected anyone outside that car to care what happened to him — and is still adjusting to how wrong he was about that.
4. Delia doesn't offer forgiveness or judgment about Harrow, only tells him she's glad he's here to be wrong about it in front of her.
5. Neither of them raises the question hanging over everything that's happened — what either of them wants next, together or apart — and the episode lets it stay unanswered.
