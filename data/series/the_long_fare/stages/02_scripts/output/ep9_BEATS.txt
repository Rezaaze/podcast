--- SCENE 1 ---
1. The sedan rolls through the open gate and comes to rest inside Harrow's fenced perimeter, exactly where Cipher intended.
2. The dashboard countdown shifts into a new, faster-ticking phase the instant the car stops moving.
3. Marcus notices the change in the numbers before Elias does, and doesn't say why it frightens him more than the first countdown did.
4. Elias scans the darkness beyond the headlights, still expecting to see a stranger he can place.
5. Wei Su, frozen at the fence only yards away, realizes the car isn't passing by — it has stopped for good.
6. Neither man in the car yet notices Wei Su watching them from just beyond the fence line.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Wei Su steps back from the fence as the headlights cut off, unsure whether to approach or retreat toward the house.
2. He tells himself again that this has nothing to do with him, even as every instinct says otherwise.
3. He thinks of the crew badge in his drawer and, for the first time, lets himself wonder if tonight is the answer he's waited a decade for.
4. A shape moves in the dark past the sedan — not Marcus, not Elias — and Wei Su goes still, watching a second person he doesn't recognize.
5. He decides to stay at the fence rather than retreat, some instinct telling him leaving now would mean losing his only chance to understand.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Cipher's voice comes through the exterior speaker one last time, telling both men to step out of the car.
2. As they do, a masked figure emerges from the dark near the wreck, and the voice that's spoken over the speaker all night now speaks directly, unfiltered.
3. Elias stares at her shape and bearing, searching for a build he recognizes from his professional past, and finds nothing that fits.
4. Marcus feels a jolt at the way she stands, some fragment of familiarity surfacing that he can't yet place and doesn't voice.
5. Cipher keeps her distance and her mask, watching both men absorb the fact that the voice has always had a body.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Cipher moves a few careful steps closer, still outside the reach of the headlights, testing how close she can come without giving herself away.
2. Marcus's unplaced familiarity sharpens with her nearness, something in how she carries her weight that scratches at memory, and he keeps it to himself.
3. Elias, reading only threat in the closing gap, asks who she is and what she wants, still certain the answer will be a name from a boardroom or a job site.
4. Cipher tells them plainly that the countdown won't stop until she hears the truth about Harrow, from both of them, in full.
5. Marcus and Elias exchange a look, the fragile truce from the sedan already forgotten now that the demand is finally spoken aloud.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Cipher turns her attention to Elias first, telling him she already knows about the safety report he overruled ten years ago.
2. Elias tries to reduce it to a single decision, a single report, hoping that's the full account she wants.
3. Cipher presses further, naming the second engineer's follow-up warning that Elias also had buried that same week, a detail he's never spoken to anyone.
4. Elias's composure finally breaks, and he admits it out loud for the first time — two warnings ignored, not one.
5. Marcus hears the second warning named and realizes, for the first time, that his own report was never the only thing standing between Danny Su and survival.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Cipher shifts her attention to Marcus, telling him she knows about the anonymous letter and money he mailed after the settlement.
2. Marcus, stunned that this detail even exists outside his own memory, asks how she could possibly know something he never told a living soul.
3. Wei Su, close enough now at the fence to hear fragments of the exchange, catches the words "letter" and "money" and goes rigid.
4. Cipher tells Marcus plainly that the letter was burned unopened, the money with it, and that its sender was never known.
5. Wei Su, hearing this from feet away, realizes with sick clarity that the driver standing in the headlights is the anonymous sender he assumed for years was Vance Development.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Wei Su steps out from the dark at the fence, drawing all three of their attention at once.
2. Elias, seeing an old man instead of a rival executive or fired employee, feels his assumption about Cipher's identity crumble along with his composure.
3. Marcus locks eyes with Wei Su for the first time in ten years, the apology he never delivered now impossible to avoid.
4. Wei Su tells Marcus, voice shaking, that he burned the letter believing it was hush money, never once suspecting it came from him.
5. Cipher watches the collision she engineered finally happen without her needing to say another word, and for a moment says nothing at all.
6. The audience registers that Elias's wrongly-held theory about Cipher's identity has just quietly died, though he hasn't yet grasped who she really is.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The countdown on the dashboard, visible through the open car door, ticks past another marked interval, pulling all three men's attention back to the present danger.
2. Elias, still shaken, tries to fall back on his earlier plan, wondering aloud if his security detail is close enough to matter now.
3. Cipher states flatly that the timer doesn't care about anyone's rescue plan and hasn't paused once tonight despite every opportunity to.
4. Marcus, watching her closely, notices she still hasn't detonated anything even now that both confessions are half out — a restraint that doesn't match Elias's fear of her.
5. Wei Su, torn between the bomb and the reckoning in front of him, refuses to step back from the group despite the danger.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Cipher directs all three men toward the collapsed east wing itself, insisting the rest of tonight's truth gets said standing where Danny died.
2. Marcus recognizes the shape of the wreckage even after ten years and hesitates before making himself walk toward it.
3. Elias, forced onto the same ground he's avoided since the settlement, realizes there's no more distance left to manage this from.
4. Wei Su walks the last steps to the wreck ahead of both of them, wanting to be the one who leads them there instead of being led.
5. Cipher follows at her same careful distance, still unwilling to let herself be seen clearly even standing this close to the place all of this began.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Chen and Delia's headlights sweep the same gate Wei Su stood at earlier, and they roll to a stop just inside the perimeter.
2. Chen spots the sedan abandoned near the wreck and registers, with a cold certainty, that they're not early.
3. Delia notices the masked figure near the group at the wreck and radios nothing, unwilling to trigger a response before she understands what she's seeing.
4. Chen decides against approaching directly, choosing instead to move along the fence line toward the wreck under cover of the dark.
5. Neither of them yet knows about the live device wired into the sedan's trunk only yards from where they've parked.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. At the wreck, Cipher tells all three men that she was never after money, silence, or even a confession alone — she wanted the truth said aloud, together, where it happened.
2. Elias asks, finally, why any of this matters to her personally, and Cipher deflects without confirming or denying a connection to Harrow's dead.
3. Marcus studies her again at this closer range, the earlier flicker of recognition sharpening into something closer to certainty he still won't say aloud.
4. Wei Su asks her directly if she knew Danny, and Cipher's silence in response is longer than anything she's let stand all night.
5. The audience senses, through that unanswered silence, that Cipher's tie to the dead is close enough to be personal, though no one at the wreck has named it yet.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Cipher tells Marcus the countdown will stop the moment he says to Wei Su, directly and without hiding behind the letter, what he's never said in ten years.
2. Marcus hesitates, aware that every version of this apology he's rehearsed in his head suddenly feels inadequate standing in front of the man himself.
3. Elias, for once, says nothing and steps back, recognizing this isn't his moment to manage or negotiate.
4. Wei Su waits, giving no help and no forgiveness offered in advance, forcing Marcus to find the words entirely on his own.
5. Marcus begins to speak, the first honest words to Wei Su's face in a decade, with no letter and no distance left to hide behind.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. As Marcus's apology finally reaches Wei Su directly, the dashboard countdown — still visible through the open car door — crosses into its last marked segment.
2. Cipher watches the exchange complete itself and, for the first time all night, makes no move toward the device at all.
3. Chen and Delia close the last of the distance along the fence line, now near enough to see the group but not yet near enough to intervene.
4. Wei Su, hearing the truth at last, doesn't answer Marcus in words, leaving the moment suspended rather than resolved.
5. The countdown ticks down to its final visible digits as Cipher stands motionless, the choice of what happens next held entirely in her silence.
6. The episode ends on the timer's last seconds, Chen and Delia frozen at the edge of the group, and Cipher still unmasked in name only, her final intention unspoken.
