--- SCENE 1 ---

2. Elias assumes this is another test of his wealth and starts to offer money before he's even heard the full request.
3. Cipher cuts him off, making clear the demand isn't for him at all — it's aimed squarely at Marcus.
4. Elias's practiced calm slips for the first time all night, thrown by being sidelined rather than targeted.
5. Marcus goes rigid, sensing before Cipher finishes that whatever comes next will cost him personally.
6. Neither man yet understands why a request for information feels more dangerous than any of tonight's physical stops.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Introduce Wei Su arriving alone at the chain-link fence around the ruined Harrow Warehouse, a stop he's clearly made many times before.
2. He checks the padlock and a familiar gap in the fence out of old habit rather than real concern.
3. He settles into a spot he's used before, watching the dark shape of the collapsed east wing.
4. Establish that this vigil has nothing to do with tonight's hijacking — Wei Su has no idea a car is heading anywhere near this district.
5. His patience and ritual signal a man still waiting for something he can't name, ten years on.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Wei Su takes out a small, worn memorial he keeps hidden near the fence and tends to it quietly.
2. The careful upkeep shows this is a yearly ritual, not a single act of grief long since finished.
3. He speaks, or thinks, toward the memory of his son, revealing guilt over never having proven what really happened.
4. He reaffirms privately that the inquest's ruling never satisfied him and never will.
5. He has no way of knowing the man who buried the original warning is, at this moment, riding toward this same stretch of river.

--- SCENE 4 ---

2. Marcus notices the shift and finds it unsettles him more than any of Elias's earlier bluster did.
3. Marcus can't explain, even to himself, why watching Elias afraid for real reasons feels worse than watching him panic over money.
4. Elias catches Marcus studying him and shuts the moment down, retreating into careful composure.
5. Neither man voices what just passed between them, but both register a shift in how they're sizing each other up.

--- SCENE 5 ---

--- SCENE 11 ---

3. He senses, without being able to explain why, that tonight doesn't feel like every other year he's stood at this fence.
4. He makes a quiet decision to stay and watch rather than retreat, choosing to see what's coming instead of avoiding it.

2. Each man privately holds a question for the other — Marcus about what the district means to Elias, Elias about what the name meant to Marcus.

4. Their earlier suspicion settles into something quieter — not open hostility, but a mutual, wary refusal to ask first.
5. The audience feels the missed opportunity acutely: two men circling the same unspoken history, one question away from recognizing each other.
