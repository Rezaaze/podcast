--- SCENE 1 ---
1. The sedan pulls up to a nondescript loading dock, the pickup address Cipher assigned earlier now directly in front of them.
2. Marcus scans the building for anyone waiting, uneasy that no one seems to be there to hand off anything.
3. Cipher's voice redirects them: the "package" was never meant to be collected by hand — she tells them to check the trunk instead.
4. Elias tenses at the redirection, sensing the pickup task was never a real errand.
5. Marcus pops the trunk release from inside, unwilling to leave his seat while Cipher is still listening.
6. Neither man moves to get out first, each unwilling to be the one who walks back and looks.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Marcus finally steps out and lifts the trunk, Elias following a step behind him.
2. They find a sealed case wired directly into the car's battery, nothing like the contraband either expected.
3. Cipher's voice follows them outside through the exterior speaker, confirming what the wiring already suggests: it's a live explosive.
4. She states it's tied to a timer, though she doesn't yet say when or where the timer ends.
5. Elias's composure cracks first, his instinct to negotiate scrambling for a response that isn't there.
6. Marcus closes the trunk slowly, absorbing the confirmation without letting Cipher hear it rattle him as much as it does.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Back inside the car, Elias asks Marcus point-blank if he knew about the bomb, and Marcus answers honestly that this part he didn't see coming.
2. For the first time all night, both men are afraid of the exact same thing at the exact same moment.
3. Elias proposes, carefully, that whatever's unresolved between them should wait until they're not sitting on a live charge.
4. Marcus agrees, though the truce feels thinner than either says aloud.
5. Unseen by Marcus, Elias's hand drifts to the hidden panel button near his seat, testing it without pressing hard enough to trigger it.
6. The audience sees Elias already breaking the truce he just proposed, keeping his security-detail plan to himself.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Chen parks two blocks from the storage facility, the address matching the final proxy hop he traced hours ago.
2. He runs a plate check on a car near the unit and gets a hit: leased under the name M. Sutton, paid entirely in cash.
3. He requests the warrant status from dispatch and learns it's still pending, slower than he wants tonight.
4. He weighs walking up to the unit now against waiting for paperwork, and starts moving before he's finished deciding.
5. He tells himself he's only looking, not entering, a distinction he already suspects won't hold.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Delia tells the night supervisor she's taking a break, with no intention of coming back to her post tonight.
2. She drives toward the coordinates Chen gave her, aware she's logged the trip nowhere official.
3. She rehearses what she'll say if anyone later asks why dispatch's GPS specialist went off-site without sign-off.
4. She thinks briefly of Wei Su's folder and the sealed file, and decides again to keep both out of tonight's conversation with Chen.
5. She spots Chen's car already parked near the facility, headlights off, and pulls in behind it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Wei Su stands at his back window, watching a pair of headlights sweep slowly along the road bordering the warehouse fence.
2. He tells himself it's nothing new, kids cutting through the property like before.
3. The headlights move with a deliberateness that unsettles him more than he admits.
4. He thinks of the earlier call warning him someone else has been asking about Danny, and wonders, briefly, if the two things connect.
5. He pushes the thought away as fast as it comes, unwilling to let himself hope for anything from strangers in cars at night.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Cipher's voice returns to the sedan with a single instruction: proceed directly to Harrow, no further stops.
2. A countdown appears on the dashboard display for the first time, numbers falling steadily instead of sitting static.
3. Marcus reads the number and calculates, silently, how many minutes of driving remain before it hits zero.
4. Elias asks what happens when it reaches the end; Cipher doesn't answer, and her silence lands harder than a threat would.
5. Marcus grips the wheel and eases the sedan back onto the road, falling back on the only thing he knows how to do: drive steady and hope it's enough.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Elias, out of Marcus's line of sight, presses the hidden panel button fully this time, sending a silent alert to his security detail.
2. He follows it with a text from a backup phone Marcus doesn't know he carries, calling in every contact who might mobilize before the timer runs out.
3. He tells himself this is only insurance, not a betrayal of the fragile truce with Marcus, though he suspects Marcus would see it differently.
4. Cipher, monitoring the car's systems, registers the outgoing signal but gives no reaction the men can hear.
5. The audience recognizes Elias is quietly staking everything on the belief that money and manpower can still outrun a countdown Cipher controls completely.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Chen and Delia meet face to face outside the storage facility, dropping the careful distance they'd kept on the phone.
2. Delia lays out the GPS loop tightening around the river; Chen lays out the proxy trace ending at the same unit.
3. Neither mentions their own withheld piece — Chen's missing warrant, Delia's unlogged detour — as they focus on the shared read of the night.
4. They agree the sedan is heading somewhere specific, not wandering, and that place is close enough to reach.
5. Chen decides to move toward Harrow directly, warrant or not, rather than lose more time at a unit that's already served its purpose.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Wei Su, unable to sit still any longer, walks out to the section of fence closest to where the headlights have been circling.
2. He grips the cold chain-link and watches the lights slow, then begin to curve toward the gate itself.
3. His old assumption — kids, scavengers, nothing — finally gives way to a dread he can't yet name.
4. He thinks of the crew badge still hidden in his drawer, Marcus's name printed beside Danny's, and wonders if tonight is the night that name comes back.
5. He doesn't move from the fence, some instinct telling him whatever's coming is already meant for this exact spot.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. In the sedan, Marcus falls back on the calm, measured voice he used for years placating difficult clients, trying it now on Cipher instead.
2. He tells her, evenly, that hurting them changes nothing about what already happened at Harrow, hoping reason still works on whoever's listening.
3. Cipher's response is a flat correction: she was never trying to change the past, only to hear it said aloud — and he's said only part of it.
4. Marcus realizes, with a cold drop, that his instinct to stay calm and not provoke was never going to be enough, because calm isn't what she came for.
5. Elias watches the exchange, filing away the phrase "only part of it" without yet grasping how much more Marcus is still hiding.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. The sedan turns onto the access road running alongside Harrow's perimeter fence, the warehouse's dark shape rising ahead.
2. The dashboard countdown ticks lower, closing in on the number tied to the fence line itself.
3. Marcus spots a figure standing at the fence in the distance and feels a jolt of recognition he can't yet place.
4. Elias notices Marcus's reaction and asks who's out there; Marcus says he doesn't know, which is only half a lie.
5. Neither man says aloud that they're now close enough to see the place both of their confessions have been circling all night.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. The sedan crosses the tree line and the fence comes fully into view, headlights sweeping across Wei Su where he stands.
2. Wei Su recognizes this isn't scavengers or kids, and takes a step toward the gate before he's even decided to.
3. Behind them on the access road, Chen and Delia's car turns in, still minutes out.
4. Cipher, watching every thread converge through channels none of the others know she has, allows herself something close to satisfaction.
5. The countdown on the dashboard crosses its final marked interval, the number now small enough that Marcus stops trying to calculate it.
6. The episode ends on the sedan rolling toward the gate, Wei Su frozen at the fence, and the timer still falling.
