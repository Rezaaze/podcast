--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day twenty-seven of the season, twenty-nine days since Tommy's release and three days before the auction, the day of Nora's secret ultrasound appointment.
2. Nora crosses the lobby dressed for an outing, clearly headed somewhere specific rather than running a casual errand.
3. Desmond offers to walk her out or call her a car, wanting to help now that she's further along.
4. Nora declines lightly, calling it nothing, just an errand, and gives no destination.
5. Desmond lets it go, privately assuming she's simply handling ordinary pregnancy business she'd rather manage alone.
6. The audience knows she's leaving for the ultrasound appointment she has told absolutely no one about, protecting both Julian's secret and the assumption about Tommy.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Nine weeks into a diagnosis he has told no one, with his next oncology follow-up looming, Augustine returns to the building again in the afternoon.
2. Desmond meets him at the door and asks directly where he's been, his patience with vague answers visibly thinning.
3. Augustine says it was just a doctor's appointment for his back, the same kind of cover he's leaned on before.
4. Desmond's expression shows he's stopped fully believing the excuses, though he doesn't say so outright.
5. Augustine reads the shift in Desmond's face and senses the cover is wearing thin.
6. Desmond privately assumes it's something minor, an old injury acting up, never imagining anything urgent, and lets the subject drop.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Tommy helps Augustine water the greenhouse, matching the older man's slower pace without remarking on it.
2. He asks lightly if Augustine knows where Nora's gotten off to today, more curious than suspicious.
3. Augustine answers only that she had somewhere to be, giving nothing further.
4. Sensing the question might linger, he shifts the talk to the old east-wing fire, asking Tommy some general remembrance of the building before it burned.
5. Tommy, drawn into the new topic, lets his question about Nora drop entirely, unaware how close he came to pressing on something that mattered.
6. The audience sees Augustine use one buried subject to smother curiosity about another, giving away nothing about Nora or the ledger sitting a few feet from them.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Three days before the auction, Iris brings the copied bank records to Lila at the diner, asking for a second pair of eyes.
2. She frames it as probably nothing, just wanting to be thorough before the sale closes.
3. Lila studies the unfamiliar account and recognizes the same kind of pattern she noticed in Henry's old discrepancy, transactions repeating at oddly regular intervals.
4. She can't yet place whose account it is or connect it to anything she already suspects.
5. Iris, unaware Lila is already chasing a separate thread through Henry's books, has no idea she's just handed her a second piece of the same puzzle.
6. The audience watches two threads of financial suspicion edge closer together without either woman realizing it.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Later that day, Nora returns to the greenhouse alone, the ultrasound appointment now behind her.
2. She sits quietly among Augustine's plants, replaying the image and heartbeat she watched entirely by herself.
3. She thinks of Julian, who still doesn't know she's pregnant, and of having gone to the appointment without telling anyone at the Amaranth.
4. She considers briefly telling Tommy or Lila where she really was, then decides against it, unwilling to unravel the story she's let stand.
5. She sits with the moment alone, no one else in the building aware the appointment even happened.
6. The audience feels the full weight of a private milestone Nora has chosen to carry by herself, protecting both Julian's engagement and Tommy's belief.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. That same day, Lila finds Augustine and sets Henry's ledger page on the counter between them again.
2. She asks gently, not accusingly, whether he's had any further thoughts about the discrepancy since she first showed him.
3. Augustine's hands go still over the page for a moment before he repeats that he doesn't know anything about it.
4. Lila watches the stillness closely, sensing he's holding something back, though she can't guess how large it actually is.
5. She lets herself almost believe his denial, unwilling to lose the version of Henry she's built her grief around.
6. The audience recognizes Augustine has held the same lie to her face a second time, the ledger still hidden feet away.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. That evening, Nora slides into her usual booth at the diner, still carrying the day's appointment inside her.
2. Lila notices she's quieter than her usual joking self while setting down her order.
3. Lila asks if she's alright, and Nora answers too fast, talking past the question toward some unrelated complaint about her feet or the weather.
4. Lila reads the quiet as ordinary pregnancy nerves, not suspecting anything is being hidden.
5. She still assumes, as she always has, that Tommy is the father, and sees no reason to doubt it tonight.
6. The audience feels how close Nora came to being truly seen, and how easily the moment passed instead.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. With the auction three days away, Desmond finds Iris and tells her plainly that his own basement inspection still contradicts the engineer's report.
2. He lays out the photographs he's kept since his second inspection, more direct with her than he's been before.
3. He admits he still has no name for who might have commissioned false findings, only that something about the report doesn't hold up.
4. For the first time, Iris doesn't immediately dismiss him, her certainty visibly wavering as she looks at the photos.
5. She doesn't agree to delay anything yet, but she no longer waves the concern off as simple loyalty.
6. The audience senses the auction's momentum, unquestioned until now, has finally met its first real crack in Iris's resolve.
