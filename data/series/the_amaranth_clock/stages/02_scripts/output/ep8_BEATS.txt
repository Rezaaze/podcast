--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day sixteen of the season, two days after Tommy asked Lila what was bothering her and she deflected toward the auction in episode seven.
2. Desmond finds Lila between the lunch rush and tells her he's missed Augustine at their usual Tuesday coffee two weeks running now.
3. He admits, more than he usually would, that the pattern is starting to genuinely worry him.
4. Lila listens with real sympathy but says nothing about her own suspicion regarding Henry's ledger or her tense exchange with Augustine in the archive.
5. Neither realizes their separate worries about Augustine, hers from his flat denial, his from the missed coffees, are pointing at the same hidden truth.
6. The audience recognizes two threads of concern about Augustine are quietly converging without either of them noticing.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Iris waits alone in Julian's study while he steps outside to take a call, his briefcase left open on the desk.
2. A restlessness she can't quite name draws her eyes to the bank statements peeking out from inside it.
3. The records show two years of payments tied to the LLC's debt purchases, all routed through one shared account she doesn't recognize.
4. She reflexively assumes the account must belong to some associate handling Julian's affairs, not to Julian himself.
5. Unsettled enough to want proof of what she's seen, she slips the pages to the office copier down the hall and copies them before he returns.
6. She replaces the originals exactly as they were, and Julian comes back from his call with no idea she's seen anything at all.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Nora joins Augustine in the greenhouse for their usual quiet watering routine.
2. Lifting a heavier can, Augustine winces sharply, then catches himself and blames a bad knee, the same excuse he's used before.
3. Preoccupied with her own concealed appointment coming up, Nora takes the explanation at face value without a second thought.
4. She offers to handle the heavier watering herself, reading his trouble as simply his age catching up with him.
5. Augustine accepts gratefully, relieved the moment passes without her looking any closer.
6. The audience sees both quietly guarding separate secrets in the same room, neither aware of the other's.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Later that afternoon, Desmond catches Augustine coming back into the lobby and asks, lightly, where he'd gotten to.
2. Augustine answers easily that he was at the hardware store for parts, the same kind of vague cover he's leaned on before.
3. As Augustine shifts his coat, Desmond glimpses a clinic parking stub in the pocket, plainly contradicting the story.
4. Desmond says nothing about it directly, but the mismatch lands hard on top of the missed Tuesday coffees already worrying him.
5. He silently assumes Augustine must be embarrassed about something ordinary, money trouble or a minor doctor's visit, never imagining it could be serious.
6. Augustine, sensing something registered, steers the talk to a lobby repair, and Desmond lets the moment close without pressing further.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Iris finds Desmond and shows him the copied bank records, admitting the unfamiliar shared account has been nagging at her.
2. She frames it as probably nothing, some associate of Julian's managing the LLC's paperwork rather than Julian himself.
3. Desmond studies the records and recognizes the same pattern of debt purchases he's suspected since the engineer's evasive call.
4. For the first time, his suspicion about the fabricated report and Iris's discovery feel like two pieces of one puzzle rather than separate worries.
5. He still has no name or proof tying the account directly to Julian, so he keeps that leap to himself.
6. Iris, relieved to have shared it with someone, has no idea she's just handed Desmond the missing piece he's been circling for weeks.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Still rattled by how closely Desmond noticed the clinic stub, Augustine makes his way down to the basement archive alone.
2. He settles on an old crate near the wall with the loose brick, not reaching for it, just sitting with the weight of the day.
3. He silently tallies how many near-misses have piled up since Tommy's release, and how each one costs him more to recover from now.
4. He considers, briefly, simply telling someone about the diagnosis, then sets the thought aside, unable to make himself take that step.
5. The ledger stays exactly where it's always been, untouched, as Augustine sits a few feet from both secrets he's carrying.
6. The audience senses Augustine is closer than he's been in weeks to breaking under the combined weight of illness and concealment, even without touching the brick.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Tommy notices Lila seems distracted while refilling his coffee, her mind plainly somewhere else.
2. He asks directly what's wrong, his concern plain and uncomplicated.
3. Unwilling to voice her suspicion about Henry and the fire before she's ready, Lila deflects toward the coming auction instead.
4. She talks instead about the tenants' uncertain futures, letting that stand in for what's actually unsettling her.
5. Tommy, having no idea her distraction has anything to do with his own conviction, reads it as ordinary stress about the building's sale.
6. He lets her change the subject without pushing, having learned in this building that people deflect for their own reasons.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. That evening, Iris sits alone beneath the lobby's stopped clock with the photocopied bank records spread in front of her.
2. She turns over the possibility of confronting Julian outright, rehearsing what she might even say to him.
3. She talks herself back from it, deciding the account is probably just some assistant's paperwork, not worth the friction of an accusation.
4. She folds the pages away and pockets them instead, choosing to keep watching quietly rather than raise it yet.
5. The audience recognizes Iris has just talked herself out of the one conversation that could unravel Julian's plan.
6. The stopped clock above her marks another day passing in a countdown she still believes is entirely her own to control.
