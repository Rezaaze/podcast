--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day eight of the season, two days after Tommy's unsettled visit to the basement archive in episode three.
2. Tommy asks Desmond how he might get a look at the original paperwork from his case, wanting to understand who said what against him.
3. Desmond, wanting to help and having no reason to suspect danger in the request, tells him the old tenant records room in the basement archive keeps files going back decades.
4. Desmond has no idea the archive also holds the ledger that would clear Tommy, since he's never been told it exists.
5. Tommy thanks him plainly, giving no sign of how much this small piece of help means to him after years of being denied answers.
6. The audience recognizes Desmond has just unknowingly sent Tommy back toward the exact hiding place of the proof of his innocence.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Tommy returns to the archive and begins pulling water-damaged folders from a shelf one section over from the loose brick, closer than he's been before.
2. Augustine appears in the doorway, his easy tone masking real alarm at how near Tommy has gotten.
3. Augustine steers him firmly but warmly toward a different, older box of tenant complaints, calling it a more likely place for anything related to the fire.
4. Tommy, trusting Augustine's long familiarity with the building, follows the redirection without suspicion.
5. Augustine watches Tommy start through the decoy file, relieved but visibly strained by how close the moment came.
6. The audience sees Augustine actively manufacture a dead end to protect a secret Tommy doesn't know is inches away.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Iris finds Desmond near the front desk and mentions, casually, that Julian is coming by that afternoon to go over auction documents.
2. She frames it as routine business, nothing she expects Desmond to have any opinion on.
3. Desmond nods along, still holding his own contradicting basement findings without connecting them to Julian in any way.
4. Iris has no idea Julian's numbers are the same ones Desmond already knows to be false, since she's never told anyone what those numbers depend on.
5. The audience recognizes Desmond is sitting on evidence that could unravel Julian's plan without realizing whose plan it actually threatens.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Julian slides into Nora's booth at the diner under the pretext of discussing auction logistics with her landlord's-adjacent knowledge of the building.
2. His eyes linger on her a beat too long, and Nora feels the old pull of what happened between them resurface in his expression.
3. She steers the conversation hard and fast toward his engagement to Iris, asking pointed questions about wedding plans to shut down the moment.
4. Julian follows her lead, letting the subject shift, with no idea she is pregnant or that she's protecting anything by doing it.
5. Nora hides how rattled the near-miss left her, giving no outward sign once the talk turns safely to Iris.
6. The audience recognizes Nora has just steered Julian away from a discovery that would upend both their secrets at once.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone in the greenhouse after steering Tommy away, Augustine works the irrigation line with hands that aren't quite steady.
2. He silently tallies how many near-misses like today's he's had since Tommy's release, and how the number keeps climbing.
3. He weighs that tally against his own private count of weeks left, aware the two clocks are now racing each other.
4. No one is present to see the toll it takes, and he straightens his posture out of long habit the moment he hears footsteps approaching.
5. The audience is shown that today's close call has sharpened, not eased, the pressure Augustine is carrying alone.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Still shaken from the archive, Tommy sits at the diner counter and tells Lila the records turned up nothing useful on his case.
2. He vents his frustration about hitting another dead end, careless with his tone in a way he wouldn't be with anyone from the neighborhood.
3. Lila listens and pours his coffee without pressing him for details, her sympathy plain but incurious.
4. Neither of them knows the dead end was arranged rather than accidental, or that the real file sits a few feet from where Tommy was just searching.
5. The audience feels the gap between Tommy's belief that today was bad luck and the deliberate hand that shaped it.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Crossing the lobby straight from the diner, Nora is still pale and unsettled from the near-miss with Julian.
2. Desmond notices and asks if she's alright, his concern genuine and immediate.
3. Nora waves him off, blaming exhaustion and the pregnancy rather than what actually just happened.
4. Desmond accepts the explanation easily, still reading her paleness as ordinary fatigue, same as before.
5. The audience recognizes Desmond remains fully unaware of how close Julian came to noticing anything, or why Nora looks the way she does.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. That evening, Lila brings Augustine his dinner in the greenhouse, continuing their long-standing ritual.
2. Their conversation stays easy and familiar, filled with small warmth neither questions.
3. Augustine lets her fill the silence with memories of Henry, giving no hint of the ledger or the diagnosis both sitting untouched beneath his calm.
4. Lila notices nothing amiss, comforted by the routine of their friendship rather than alerted by it.
5. The audience closes the episode aware that Augustine is carrying two secrets deeper into the season, each conversation with Lila another day he chooses not to tell her either one.
