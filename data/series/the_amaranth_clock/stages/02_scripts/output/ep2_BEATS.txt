--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day four of the season, three days after the events of episode one and the start of the thirty-day countdown Iris announced.
2. Iris lays the completed structural engineer's report in front of Desmond, pointing to its findings as proof the building is failing.
3. Desmond tells her he checked the basement himself in the past few days and found it structurally sound, contradicting the report.
4. Iris dismisses his concern, saying she trusts a licensed engineer's findings over an untrained walkthrough.
5. Desmond doesn't push further, though the audience now knows he holds real evidence against the report that Iris refuses to hear.
6. Iris formally locks in day thirty as the auction date, certain the decision is hers alone and unaware it serves Julian's plan.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Iris stops by the diner to tell Lila the auction date is now officially locked in.
2. Lila responds with warm congratulations, masking a flicker of private unease.
3. When Iris mentions the building's supposed deferred maintenance debt, it triggers Lila's memory of how solid Henry's books always looked.
4. Lila privately notes the numbers don't match what she remembers, but says nothing, unwilling to reopen old ledgers.
5. Iris, unaware Lila has any specific financial knowledge, reads her quiet as simple sadness about the building's fate.
6. The audience sees Lila's private suspicion deepening even as she keeps protecting the version of Henry she's built her grief around.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Desmond is sorting the morning mail at the brass boxes when Augustine arrives.
2. Augustine moves quickly to intercept an unopened envelope from his own slot before Desmond can hand it to him.
3. Desmond asks lightly what it is; Augustine gives a vague, offhand answer that shuts the question down.
4. Desmond senses nothing urgent in it and lets the moment pass without pressing.
5. The audience recognizes this deflection as routine reflex for Augustine now, not panic.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Alone in the greenhouse, Augustine opens the envelope he took from Desmond.
2. It confirms another oncology follow-up appointment reminder.
3. He tucks it behind the seed packets, joining others already hidden there.
4. The audience glimpses the small stash of reminders, silent evidence of how long he's been concealing his diagnosis.
5. He returns to his watering routine, giving no outward sign of what he just hid.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Nora slips into the greenhouse to escape tenants gossiping about her and Tommy in the lobby.
2. Augustine notices her tension and offers quiet company without asking what's wrong.
3. Nora is relieved he doesn't press, though her real reason for distress, Julian, stays hidden beneath the gossip she's actually avoiding.
4. Augustine reads her unease as ordinary pregnancy nerves, unaware of the deeper truth underneath it.
5. The audience recognizes both are quietly guarding separate secrets in the same room, neither aware of the other's.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Lila serves Nora pie at the diner and gently asks about the baby's father, plainly assuming Tommy.
2. Nora deflects with a joke rather than confirming or denying anything.
3. Nora steers the conversation to the day's auction news to escape the topic entirely.
4. Lila, having no reason to doubt the Tommy assumption, reads Nora's deflection as simple shyness.
5. The audience recognizes Nora is once again letting a false assumption stand to protect Julian's secret.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Desmond mentions to Augustine that he seemed oddly quick to grab his own mail earlier.
2. Augustine brushes the comment off and pivots the talk to the day's auction news.
3. Desmond, still uneasy over his own basement findings, welcomes the change of subject.
4. Both men let their own evasions pass unremarked, each protecting a separate secret from the other.
5. The audience sees two parallel deflections happening in the same brief exchange, unnoticed by either man.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. That evening, Iris sits alone beneath the lobby's stopped clock with the auction paperwork.
2. She reviews the report's numbers one last time, fully convinced of her own reasoning.
3. She signs the paperwork, formally fixing the auction for day thirty.
4. The audience is reminded this act, which Iris believes is entirely her own choice, is the exact outcome Julian has spent two years engineering.
5. The stopped clock above her underscores the irony of fixing so precise a countdown beneath a timepiece that no longer moves.
