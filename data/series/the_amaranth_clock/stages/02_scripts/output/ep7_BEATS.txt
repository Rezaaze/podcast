--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day fourteen of the season, two days after Desmond found Tommy repairing the lobby fixture in episode six.
2. After closing the diner, Lila pulls out Henry's old handwritten ledgers to settle her nerves, telling herself she's just tidying paperwork.
3. She lines the 1998 renovation fund entries against her own diner books from the same year out of old habit.
4. She finds a set of numbers in Henry's hand that don't correspond to any expense or income she can account for.
5. A cold unease sets in as she rereads the entry, unable to explain it away as a simple bookkeeping slip.
6. The audience watches Lila cross from vague memory into concrete, provable evidence that something is wrong, without yet grasping how far it reaches.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Unable to let it sit until morning, Lila carries the ledger page down to the basement archive where she knows Augustine still works late.
2. She lays the page in front of him and asks directly what he knows about Walter's 1998 renovation fund.
3. Augustine's face goes carefully still as he recognizes exactly what she's found, though he lets none of that show.
4. He tells her flatly he doesn't know anything about it, that Henry's books were always a mystery to him.
5. Lila searches his face for a moment, unconvinced but unable to push past his calm, and lets the subject drop.
6. The audience recognizes Augustine is protecting Lila's grief with a direct lie, standing feet from the very ledger that would prove her right.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Still shaken from Lila's visit, Augustine retreats to the greenhouse to water the beds and steady himself.
2. A sudden wave of pain doubles him over the potting bench, sharper than the ones before.
3. He breathes through it deliberately, counting seconds the way he's learned to, until the wave passes.
4. He hears Nora's footsteps on the stairs and forces his posture straight, wiping his face clear of strain just before she rounds the corner.
5. Nora asks if he's alright, and he answers with practiced ease, giving no hint of the pain or of Lila's visit.
6. The audience sees Augustine absorbing two separate burdens, physical and emotional, in near-total isolation.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Tommy crosses the lobby and nearly collides with Nora, the unanswered question from days earlier still sitting between them.
2. He searches her face for anything resembling an answer, but she offers only a tired smile and a comment about the weather.
3. Nora keeps the exchange brief, aware that lingering risks him asking again in a way she can't deflect a second time.
4. Tommy lets her go, telling himself her silence is simply exhaustion rather than evasion.
5. Neither mentions Julian, the ledger Lila is chasing, or the auction, though all three continue to shape the space around them.
6. The audience watches Nora's deflection hold for one more day, the gap between what Tommy believes and what's true still widening.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. At the diner, Desmond brings up the engineer's evasive phone call again, hoping a second attempt will land differently.
2. He lists the specific inconsistencies once more, careful to keep his tone measured rather than accusatory.
3. Iris repeats that she trusts the licensed findings over Desmond's secondhand suspicion of one uncomfortable phone call.
4. She adds that Julian has reviewed the same paperwork and found nothing irregular, treating his review as confirmation.
5. Desmond doesn't mention Julian by name in his own suspicions, still with no reason to connect him to the report.
6. The audience recognizes Iris's trust in Julian is now actively reinforcing the very deception Desmond is circling.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Leaving the diner still unsettled, Lila catches Desmond alone in the lobby before he can head upstairs.
2. She tells him only that something about Henry's old books feels wrong, without naming the ledger or the number she found.
3. Desmond, concerned, offers to look at whatever she found with her, sensing this might connect to something real.
4. Lila pulls back, insisting it's nothing and that she doesn't want to drag old paperwork into daylight.
5. Desmond lets her refusal stand, unaware her unease and his own basement suspicions are edging toward the same buried truth.
6. The audience sees two separate threads of doubt nearly converge in this exchange, kept apart only by what neither character says.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. The next day, Tommy returns to the archive to keep working through the tenant complaint box Augustine steered him toward weeks earlier.
2. He has no idea Lila stood in this same room the night before, holding proof tied to the very fire that convicted him.
3. He works methodically, checking dates and names against what he remembers of his trial, finding nothing new.
4. The loose brick sits a few feet away, undisturbed since Augustine last resealed it, though Tommy still doesn't register it as anything more than old masonry.
5. His frustration builds quietly, the search once again yielding nothing that helps his case.
6. The audience feels the irony sharpen further, watching how close two separate searches for the truth keep passing each other without connecting.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Later that evening, Augustine lingers alone by the loose brick, the weight of lying to Lila's face still sitting heavy in him.
2. He presses a hand flat against the brick without pulling it free, caught between exhaustion and the same choice he's made every week.
3. Desmond's voice carries down the stairwell, checking that he's alright, a note of real concern in it.
4. Augustine doesn't answer right away, his body slow to respond, the pain and fatigue from earlier still dragging at him.
5. The delay lasts just long enough to register as strange before Augustine finally calls back that he's fine.
6. The audience closes the episode watching Augustine's physical decline and emotional toll compound in the same silent moment, with Desmond none the wiser about either cause.
