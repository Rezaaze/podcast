--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day twelve of the season, two days after Tommy's late-night confession to Lila that the baby doesn't add up to being his.
2. Desmond lays out for Iris exactly what happened on the call — the engineer's evasions, the refusal to name who commissioned the report — and asks her to delay the auction until it's resolved.
3. Iris refuses, saying a licensed engineer's findings outweigh a phone call full of vague answers.
4. Desmond holds back mentioning his own basement inspection and photos, sensing she isn't ready to hear it from him directly.
5. Iris reaffirms the auction date stands, treating Desmond's concern as loyalty rather than evidence.
6. The audience sees Iris's refusal push the timeline further out of anyone's reach to stop, unknowingly protecting Julian.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Augustine works beside Nora in the greenhouse, his movements visibly slower and more deliberate than they were even a week earlier.
2. A jolt of pain crosses his face mid-task; he covers it with the same practiced shift of his grip he's used before.
3. Nora notices but says nothing, following the pattern she's kept since his stumble by the shelf.
4. Augustine gently asks how she's really doing, sensing she's carrying more than pregnancy nerves, but doesn't press for specifics.
5. Nora answers with a vague deflection, relieved he lets it go unremarked.
6. The audience watches both extend real care to each other while each keeps their own countdown hidden.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. That night, Augustine descends to the archive alone, his steps noticeably slower than the week before.
2. He pulls the loose brick free and opens the fireproof box, the ledger sitting exactly where he left it.
3. He holds it longer this time, his declining strength sharpening the weight of the choice he's made every week since Tommy's release.
4. He weighs Tommy's twelve stolen years against Lila's memory of Henry once more, and once more cannot choose Tommy's vindication.
5. He reseals the box and fits the brick back into place, leaving the archive exactly as he found it.
6. The audience is reminded the proof stays hidden by choice, now made harder by Augustine's failing body.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Tommy sits with Lila over pie and admits how badly he wants the baby to be his.
2. He tells her having someone to be worth something for is the only thing making the last twelve years feel survivable.
3. Lila, still certain he's the father, hears this as love rather than doubt.
4. She urges him again to just ask Nora outright instead of carrying the hope silently.
5. Tommy admits he's scared of what she might say, though he doesn't explain why.
6. The audience senses Tommy is being pushed toward a question that could expose more than paternity.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Tommy finds Nora alone at the mailboxes and asks her plainly if the baby is his.
2. Nora hesitates, caught between the easy lie and a truth she can't afford to speak.
3. She neither confirms nor denies it, deflecting with a line about not being ready to talk about it yet.
4. Tommy reads her non-answer as confirmation, choosing to hear what he needs to hear.
5. Nora lets his interpretation stand, a conscious escalation from simply not correcting an assumption to actively withholding the truth.
6. The audience recognizes this as the moment Nora's deception shifts from passive to deliberate.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Still shaken, Nora goes to the diner and catches Lila between the lunch rush.
2. She tells Lila only that Tommy asked her something hard, without saying what.
3. Lila, unaware of the specifics, assumes Tommy finally opened up about his feelings for her.
4. She reads Nora's shaken mood as evidence of real emotion between them and encourages her to give Tommy a real answer soon.
5. Nora lets Lila's misreading stand, too raw to correct it or explain further.
6. The audience sees Lila unknowingly cementing the very deception her earlier advice helped set in motion.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. That evening, Iris carries the inspection report up to the greenhouse, seeking quiet to think it through one more time.
2. She rereads the cited defects line by line, testing her own resolve against the numbers.
3. She tells herself plainly that grief hasn't clouded her judgment, that the sale is the responsible choice regardless of how it feels.
4. She has no idea the report she's trusting was built specifically to lead her to this exact conclusion.
5. The audience watches Iris talk herself deeper into Julian's plan while believing she's proving her independence from it.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Desmond finds Tommy repairing a broken lobby fixture that no one asked him to fix.
2. He watches for a moment before Tommy notices him, taking in the quiet, unasked-for effort.
3. Desmond tells him plainly that the building remembers who helped it, not just who hurt it.
4. Tommy, caught off guard by the acknowledgment, has no easy response and keeps working.
5. Neither mentions Nora, the ledger, or the auction, though all three sit under the moment.
6. The audience closes the episode watching Tommy take one more quiet step toward belonging, still unaware how much of the building's real history remains hidden from him.
