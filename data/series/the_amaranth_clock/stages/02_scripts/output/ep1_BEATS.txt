--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day one of the season, three days since Tommy's release on parole after serving twelve of his fifteen-year sentence.
2. Tommy steps into the Amaranth's lobby for the first time since the fire, visibly steeling himself before crossing the threshold.
3. Desmond crosses the lobby to greet him with open warmth, shaking his hand and welcoming him back by name.
4. Several tenants nearby go quiet and watch the exchange without approaching, their silence making clear the neighborhood hasn't forgiven or forgotten.
5. Tommy keeps his expression carefully neutral, hiding from Desmond how much the tenants' silence stings him.
6. The audience registers that Desmond's warmth is not shared by the building at large, setting up Tommy's isolation going forward.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Iris enters the lobby with paperwork in hand, finding Desmond still near the front desk after Tommy has gone.
2. Iris announces that the will's contest window closed today, which she frames as simply a routine legal formality.
3. She states plainly that this starts a thirty-day countdown to the building's auction, treating the timeline as fixed and unavoidable.
4. Desmond reacts with unease at how fast the thirty days feels, though he doesn't yet say why it troubles him.
5. Iris reads his hesitation as concern for the tenants' displacement, not suspicion of the process itself, since she genuinely believes the decision and its timing are hers alone.
6. The audience senses Desmond is holding back a specific worry he isn't ready to name to her yet.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Nora enters the lobby visibly pregnant, drawing Desmond's attention as he's still processing the morning's news from Iris.
2. Desmond congratulates Nora warmly, then extends the congratulations to Tommy as well, plainly assuming Tommy is the father.
3. Nora hesitates for only a beat before thanking him, letting the assumption stand rather than correcting it.
4. The audience is reminded Tommy was released only three days ago, making the paternity assumption's timing already quietly implausible without anyone in the room noticing.
5. Nora's ease masks a private calculation: correcting Desmond would raise questions about Julian she isn't ready to answer.
6. Desmond, satisfied and pleased for them, has no idea he's just reinforced a lie that isn't his to know about.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Lila spots Tommy in the lobby later that day and insists on seating him in Henry's old booth for a meal on the house.
2. She talks warmly about Henry keeping the building's books for years, calling him a good, careful man, unaware of what he actually did.
3. Tommy listens and says little, his silence covering old anger he doesn't let surface in front of her.
4. Lila's grief for Henry visibly rises to the surface as she talks, and Tommy recognizes he can't let his bitterness show without wounding her further.
5. Neither says anything about the fire directly, though both are clearly thinking about it.
6. The audience feels the dramatic irony that Lila is comforting Tommy in the booth of the man partly responsible for his conviction.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Alone after hours, Augustine moves through the greenhouse watering plants in his usual unhurried routine.
2. A sudden stab of physical pain stops him mid-motion; he sets down the watering can and grips the potting bench until it passes.
3. He straightens up before anyone could see, smoothing his expression back to normal out of habit, though no one is there.
4. In the quiet, he silently counts off in his head roughly how many weeks he might have left, weighing it against how long he's been keeping the secret.
5. The audience learns, through this private moment, that Augustine is carrying a hidden clock of his own that no other character in the episode knows about.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Iris meets Lila for coffee, treating it as a casual catch-up before mentioning the sale.
2. She brings up the coming structural engineer's report and repeats the thirty-day auction timeline as simple bad luck about the building's condition.
3. Lila listens with a flicker of private doubt, recalling the years she helped Henry with the real books and how solid the finances actually looked.
4. Iris, unaware Lila has any specific financial knowledge of the building, treats her as just a sympathetic old friend rather than someone who could challenge the report.
5. Lila decides not to voice her suspicion, unwilling to reopen old ledgers or the version of Henry she's built her grief around.
6. The audience recognizes Lila now holds a piece of the auction puzzle that could undercut Julian's scheme, though she doesn't yet know its significance.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. That same night, Augustine descends to the basement archive alone, as he has every week since Tommy's release.
2. He pulls the loose brick free and opens the fireproof box hidden behind it, revealing the embezzlement ledger inside.
3. He holds the ledger for a long moment, clearly aware of exactly what it would prove and for whom.
4. He weighs Tommy's twelve stolen years against Lila's memory of Henry, and once again cannot bring himself to choose Tommy's vindication over her grief.
5. He replaces the ledger, reseals the box, and fits the brick back into place, leaving everything exactly as it was.
6. The audience is shown directly what only Augustine knows: proof of Tommy's innocence exists a few feet from where Tommy just ate dinner, and stays hidden by choice, not chance.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Later that night, Nora climbs to the rooftop to be alone and finds Tommy already there, restless in the dark.
2. Their conversation stays cautious at first, circling the one night they spent together without either naming what came of it.
3. Tommy's guardedness softens slightly with Nora in a way it hasn't with anyone else since his release, hinting at real unfinished feeling between them.
4. Nora watches him carefully, aware she is letting him half-believe something about the pregnancy that isn't true, and says nothing to dispel it.
5. Neither mentions Julian, the baby, or the auction, though the weight of all three sits under the silence between them.
6. The audience closes the episode aware that Tommy and Nora's unresolved history is now entangled with a truth neither of them is naming aloud.
