--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]

[NORA_VANCE | style: tired, deflated]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: dry, gentle]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: warm, unhurried]

[NORA_VANCE | style: soft, grateful]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: light, deflecting]

[SFX: water pouring slowly into soil]

[NORA_VANCE | style: quiet, observant]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: brushing it off, calm]

[NORA_VANCE | style: easy, unworried]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: amused, fond]

[NARRATOR]

[NORA_VANCE | style: content, settling]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: quiet, kind]

[SFX: pruning shears snipping a single stem]

[NARRATOR]
For a while neither of them speaks. Augustine works the shears along a row of basil, unhurried, while Nora sits with the watering can resting against her knees, her free hand drifting once to rest on her stomach before she seems to catch herself and let it fall away.

[NORA_VANCE | style: half to herself, drowsy]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: gentle, sincere]
No. Up here, everybody just wants to grow.

[NARRATOR]
He says nothing more, careful to keep his breathing even, his hands steady on the shears. She doesn't ask why he's slower than he used to be. He doesn't ask what she's carrying besides the watering can. The greenhouse holds them both in the same unbroken quiet, each one grateful, for entirely different reasons, that the other doesn't push.

--- PART 4 ---

[NARRATOR]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: patient, a little tired of the subject]

[DESMOND_HALE | style: gentle, backing off]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: warm again]

[DESMOND_HALE | style: quiet]

[NARRATOR]

[SFX: an elevator door chiming faintly down the hall]

[NARRATOR]
Iris disappears upward, certain of a decision she believes is entirely her own. Desmond stays a moment longer by the stopped clock, the contradiction he raised and let drop already fading under the weight of the morning's other small tasks.
