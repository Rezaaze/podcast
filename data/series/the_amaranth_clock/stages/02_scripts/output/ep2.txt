--- PART 1 ---

[NARRATOR]
Three days ago, Tommy Lau came home to the Amaranth, and the building had already folded back into old habits by the next morning.

[NARRATOR]
Nora let an assumption about her baby's father stand unspoken in the lobby. Augustine climbed down to the basement archive one more time, and left whatever he keeps there exactly where he found it.

[NARRATOR]
Now, as the clock on the sale ticks toward its thirtieth day, the residents of the Amaranth move through their mornings unaware how many of those routines are quietly rehearsed lies.

[NARRATOR]
Four days into that countdown, Desmond Hale — the Amaranth's building manager for eighteen years — is at the desk off the lobby when Iris Tanaka, the late owner's daughter, walks in with a folder under her arm.

[SFX: a folder dropped flat onto a wooden desk]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: brisk, certain]
Read it. Page four especially. The support beams in the east wall — the engineer flagged three separate points of failure.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: careful, measured]
I read it last night, Iris. I want to show you something first.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: impatient]
Show me what?

[DESMOND_HALE | style: level, quiet urgency]
I was down there myself Tuesday. And again Thursday. Those beams your report calls compromised — I put my hand on every one of them. They're sound. Not a hairline crack.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: flat, dismissive]
Desmond. You've kept this building running for eighteen years, and I respect that. But you're not an engineer. He's licensed. He's certified. He doesn't have a reason to lie about what he found down there.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: restrained, holding something back]
I'm not saying he's lying. I'm saying what I saw doesn't match what's on that page.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: firm, closing the subject]
Then maybe you missed something a trained eye wouldn't.

[NARRATOR]
Desmond looks at her a moment longer, then says nothing more.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: resolved, quietly emotional underneath]
This building has been falling apart since my father died, Desmond. I don't enjoy this. But I'm not going to pretend a report like this is nothing because it's inconvenient.

[SFX: a pen uncapped and set against paper]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: steady, final]
Day thirty. That's the date. I'm signing off on it this week — the auction goes forward exactly on schedule.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: subdued]
Understood.

[NARRATOR]
Iris gathers the folder back into her arms, already thinking of her next stop. Desmond stays where he is, the shape of the wall he touched still more real to him than anything printed on that page.

--- PART 2 ---

[NARRATOR]
The Corner Diner, mid-morning, the same day. Across from the Amaranth, in the cracked red booth by the window, Lila Chen — who has run this diner alone since her husband's death — refills a coffee she hasn't been asked to refill.

[LILA_CHEN | style: warm, welcoming]
Iris Tanaka. In my diner twice in one week. I should frame the receipt.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: composed, a little wound-tight]
I wanted you to hear it from me before it's just a notice taped to a door. It's locked. Day thirty. The auction's official.

[SFX: a coffee cup set down on a table]

[LILA_CHEN | style: bright, careful]
Well. Congratulations, I guess. That's — that's a big thing to finally have a date on.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: relieved, exhaling something]
It is. I keep waiting to feel like I did something wrong. I don't. The engineer's report made it easy, honestly. Once you see the numbers, there's nothing left to argue with.

[LILA_CHEN | style: light, prompting]
Numbers like what?

[IRIS_TANAKA]
Deferred maintenance, mostly. Apparently it's been piling up for years — the building's been quietly falling apart underneath all of us and nobody wanted to say it out loud.

[NARRATOR]
Lila's hand stills on the coffee pot.

[LILA_CHEN | style: steady on the surface, thoughts elsewhere]
Years, huh.

[IRIS_TANAKA]
That's what it says. I know it doesn't match the building you remember. It didn't match mine either.

[LILA_CHEN | style: gentle, deflecting]
Henry kept books on that place, you know. Way back. Man was fussy about it — every pipe, every repair, written down twice so he wouldn't forget which version was right.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: touched, a little wistful]
I didn't know that.

[LILA_CHEN]
Long time ago now.

[NARRATOR]
She doesn't finish the thought. The numbers Iris just described don't sit right against the ledgers she remembers looking over Henry's shoulder — but she isn't reaching for that shoebox of old pages, not today, maybe not ever.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: soft]
You've gone quiet on me.

[LILA_CHEN | style: covering, warm again]
Just thinking about your father. Thirty days. He'd have hated watching a calendar do that to his building.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: quiet]
Yeah. Some days I think that's the only reason any of this hurts.

[LILA_CHEN]
Pie's on the house today. Don't argue with me.

[NARRATOR]
Iris doesn't argue. Lila turns back toward the counter, her smile already back in place, the old ledgers staying exactly where she's kept them — closed, and out of sight.

--- PART 3 ---

[NARRATOR]
Day four of the countdown, mid-morning, the lobby beneath the stopped clock. Desmond sorts the day's mail at the brass boxes.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: relaxed, half to himself]
Water bill, water bill, something for the Petersons that isn't theirs...

[SFX: a stack of envelopes being shuffled and squared against a metal box]

[NARRATOR]
Augustine Kwok, the building's longest-tenured resident, comes down the stairs and crosses straight to him.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: brisk, a shade too casual]
Morning, Desmond. Anything for me?

[DESMOND_HALE | style: unhurried]
Was just getting to K. Hang on—

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: quick, reaching past him]
I've got it, I've got it.

[SFX: an envelope sliding free from a stack of mail]

[NARRATOR]
Augustine's hand closes around a single envelope from his own slot before Desmond can lift it out.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: mildly curious]
Eager. What is it, sweepstakes?

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: light, already turning away]
Seed catalog. They send three of the same one every spring, I don't know why I bother opening them anymore.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: shrugging it off]
Free country. Or free catalog, anyway.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: already moving]
Something like that.

[NARRATOR]
Augustine tucks the envelope into his jacket without looking at it again and heads for the stairwell.

[DESMOND_HALE | style: calling after him, easy]
Tell the roof I said hello.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: off, warm]
It never listens.

--- PART 4 ---

[NARRATOR]
Day four of the countdown, six weeks since a diagnosis only one man in this building carries. Up on the roof, in the greenhouse he built by hand years ago, Augustine Kwok is alone with the tomato vines and the drip of the irrigation line.

[SFX: envelope tearing open]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: flat, reading aloud to no one]
"Please confirm your upcoming oncology follow-up." Same letterhead. Same cheerful little exclamation mark they don't need.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: dry, quiet]
Tuesday. Ten o'clock. Same waiting room chair too, if they let a man have preferences.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: low, glancing toward the door]
Nobody on the stairs. Good.

[SFX: a small wooden panel sliding, seed packets shifting]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: private, almost tender]
Behind the tomato packets, then. Where your brothers and sisters are already keeping quiet.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: wry, counting under his breath]
One, two, three, four — and five, now. A man could open a proper filing office back here. Tidier than the one downstairs, at least.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: softer, weighing something]
Tomorrow, maybe. I'll tell someone tomorrow.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: businesslike, shutting the thought]
Or not. Enough of that. The peppers don't water themselves.

[SFX: watering can filling, water pouring onto soil]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: gentle, ordinary]
There you are. Thirstier than you let on, same as everyone in this building.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: calm, unremarkable]
Second tray's gone heavy again. Root rot, if I'm not careful. That's the real emergency around here — not letters, not clinics. Roots.

[SFX: irrigation valve turning, a thin hiss of water]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: quiet, settling into rhythm]
Steady as you were, all of you. Nothing's changed. Nothing to see.

[NARRATOR]
The greenhouse door hasn't latched behind him — and down the stairwell, footsteps are already climbing toward it, looking for a quieter place than the lobby.

--- PART 5 ---

[NARRATOR | style: neutral, orienting]
Still the rooftop greenhouse, day four of the countdown. A few minutes after Augustine hides the envelope, footsteps climb the stairwell behind him.

[SFX: a greenhouse door creaking open]

[NORA_VANCE | style: breathless, trying to sound casual]
Sorry — is it okay if I just sit up here for a minute?

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: unhurried, warm]
It's always okay. Watch the hose, the tomatoes are greedy today.

[NORA_VANCE | style: relieved but still wound tight]
They were all standing by the mailboxes downstairs. Talking. Not even quietly.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: mild, not fishing]
People downstairs like a subject they can chew on for free.

[NORA_VANCE | style: half a laugh, brittle underneath]
Yeah. I'm apparently this month's.

[SFX: water trickling into a clay pot]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: gentle, unbothered]
You don't have to explain any of it to me.

[NORA_VANCE | style: quiet, genuinely grateful]
That's — thank you. Everyone else has a question shaped like a favor.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: dry, kind]
I've got dirt under my nails and tomatoes to feed. I'm a bad audience for gossip.

[NORA_VANCE | style: softer, letting her guard down a fraction]
I just needed somewhere that wasn't the lobby.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: steady, fatherly]
Then sit. Nobody's timing you.

[NORA_VANCE | style: a small, real laugh]
Everyone else in that building is timing something.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: wry]
Thirty days, so I hear. Iris made sure of that this morning.

[NORA_VANCE | style: glad for the redirect, quick]
See, that's a much better thing to talk about than me.

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: amused, letting it go]

[SFX: a metal watering can being lifted off a shelf]

[NORA_VANCE | style: settling, the tension easing out of her voice]

[AUGUSTINE_KWOK | style: easy, matter-of-fact]
That's more than most people manage before noon.

[NARRATOR | style: neutral, closing the scene]
Nora stays among the tomato vines a while longer, saying nothing more about the lobby, or about what actually put her there.

--- PART 6 ---

[NARRATOR]
Later that afternoon, the Corner Diner across from the Amaranth. Lila Chen, who has run the place two decades since her husband's death, sets a plate down at the cracked red booth.

[LILA_CHEN | style: warm, fussing]
Blueberry. Don't argue with me, you're eating for two.

[NORA_VANCE | style: soft laugh]
I wasn't going to argue.

[SFX: a fork set down against a ceramic plate]

[LILA_CHEN | style: gentle, careful]
You look tired, honey. Tommy keeping you up nights already, or is that just the two of you settling in?

[NORA_VANCE | style: light, deflecting]
Lila.

[LILA_CHEN | style: teasing, not unkind]
I'm just asking. A woman's allowed to ask.

[NORA_VANCE | style: forced brightness]
If I told you the real story you wouldn't believe me. I'm saving it. Whole building's gonna want popcorn.

[LILA_CHEN | style: chuckling]
That bad, huh.

[NORA_VANCE | style: quick, moving past it]
Worse. Anyway — did you hear? Iris made it official today. Day thirty. She told Desmond right in front of me, practically framed it.

[LILA_CHEN | style: her warmth cooling slightly, careful]

[NORA_VANCE | style: curious]

[LILA_CHEN | style: covering, brisk]

[LILA_CHEN | style: distracted, half to herself]

[SFX: a coffee pot set down on a counter]

[NORA_VANCE | style: mock innocence]

--- PART 7 ---

--- PART 8 ---

[NARRATOR]
That evening, the lobby is empty. Above the brass mailboxes, the clock still reads four forty-seven, its hands frozen as they've been for years. Iris sits alone beneath it, the engineer's report open on her lap.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: quiet, resolved]
Forty-one thousand in the roofline. Sixty-two in the foundation. Water damage nobody bothered to log.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: steady, to herself]
Dad would've fought this. But dad isn't the one holding the numbers.

[SFX: a pen uncapping]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: firm]
Day thirty. That's the date.

[SFX: pen scratching against paper, a signature]

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: exhaling, certain]
Done. Whatever this building was, it isn't sustaining itself. At least now there's a clock on it.

[NARRATOR]
She looks up at the stopped clock above her, the one nobody's fixed since her father died.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: wry, tired]
Four forty-seven. Forever.

[IRIS_TANAKA | style: soft, almost to the building itself]
Thirty days, though. That one's going to move whether you do or not.

[SFX: papers being gathered and squared against a table]

[NARRATOR]
She slides the signed pages into a folder, seals it, and sits a moment longer in the frozen light of a clock that will never again tell her the time — while somewhere outside these walls, the thirty days she believes are hers alone have already been spent, twice over, by someone else.
