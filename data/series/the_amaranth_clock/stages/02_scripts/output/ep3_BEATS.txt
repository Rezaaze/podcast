--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day six of the season, two days after Iris signed the auction paperwork beneath the stopped lobby clock.
2. Desmond leads Tommy on a rounds of small building fixes — a sticking door, a loose stair rail — treating him as ordinary help rather than someone recently released.
3. Tommy works with quiet competence, visibly grateful for tasks that ask nothing of his past.
4. Desmond deliberately avoids any mention of the twelve years, keeping the exchanges focused only on the work in front of them.
5. Neither man raises the auction, though its thirty-day countdown sits unspoken under the routine.
6. The audience senses Desmond is consciously building Tommy back into the building's daily rhythm, one small task at a time.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Lila brings Nora her usual order at the diner, hovering over whether she's eating and resting enough.
2. She gently angles toward the baby's father again, careful not to push too hard.
3. Nora answers with vague warmth that gives away nothing concrete, redirecting to how tired she's been instead.
4. Lila accepts the deflection without suspicion, still certain the answer is Tommy and simply respecting Nora's privacy.
5. The audience notes Nora is growing more practiced at these deflections, each one costing her a little less visible effort.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Nora comes into the greenhouse seeking quiet after another round of lobby gossip about her and Tommy.
2. Augustine works beside her in comfortable silence, handing her a watering can without being asked.
3. Nora notices he moves more slowly than usual and pauses longer between tasks, but reads it as simple fatigue and doesn't remark on it.
4. Augustine, focused on keeping his own exhaustion invisible, is relieved she doesn't ask.
5. Neither names what's actually weighing on them, Nora's pregnancy secret and Augustine's diagnosis both staying folded away in the same quiet room.
6. The audience recognizes two hidden countdowns running side by side, unknown to each other and to the two people carrying them.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Iris finds Desmond in the lobby and thanks him sincerely for keeping the building running through the transition.
2. She reaffirms that selling is the only responsible choice left, given the report and the debt she believes is real.
3. Desmond, moved by her gratitude, mentions again, only in passing, that the basement seemed sound when he checked it himself.
4. Iris nods it off warmly as him worrying on her behalf, thanking him for caring rather than engaging with what he actually found.
5. Desmond lets it drop a second time, unwilling to turn her gratitude into an argument.
6. The audience sees the contradiction surface again and get absorbed without consequence, deepening Iris's blind trust in the report.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Augustine and Lila share their weekly dinner in her usual booth, a ritual they've kept since Henry died.
2. Lila reminisces fondly about how meticulous Henry was with the building's books, calling it the one thing he was never careless about.
3. Augustine listens and nods along, giving no outward sign that he removed those very books from Henry's reach twelve years ago.
4. Her warmth needles at what he's chosen never to correct, though his face stays composed out of long habit.
5. Neither raises Tommy, the fire, or the ledger, though the unspoken subject sits heavier in Augustine than in Lila.
6. The audience feels the widening gap between the Henry Lila grieves and the one Augustine actually knew.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Desmond spends the afternoon fixing the greenhouse's dripping irrigation line while Augustine potters nearby.
2. He notices Augustine wincing slightly as he lifts a watering can and asks if he's alright.
3. Augustine waves it off, blaming an old man's aching back after years of bending over these beds.
4. Desmond accepts the explanation without a second thought, having no reason to suspect anything more serious.
5. The audience, already aware of Augustine's diagnosis, watches the lie land easily and go completely unquestioned.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Alone in the basement archive, Tommy runs his hand along shelves he half-remembers from childhood visits with his father.
2. He pauses near the wall where the loose brick sits, sensing something faintly familiar without being able to place it.
3. A wave of old grief and disorientation overtakes him, and he steps back rather than investigate further.
4. He leaves the archive with the same unanswered unease he came in with, the ledger a few feet away and still undiscovered.
5. The audience recognizes how close Tommy has come, without knowing it, to the exact proof that could clear his name.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Tommy walks Nora up to her floor after her shift ends, their conversation easy in a way neither quite examines.
2. He carries her bag without being asked, and she lets him, the small gesture more intimate than either acknowledges aloud.
3. Both circle near the subject of their one night together, then let it pass unspoken, same as before.
4. Nora feels the pull of his growing attachment to a version of the pregnancy that isn't true, and still says nothing to correct it.
5. Tommy, content with the ease between them, chooses again not to ask the question sitting under all of it.
6. The audience closes the episode watching the unresolved tangle between them deepen further with every day neither one speaks.
