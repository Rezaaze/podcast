--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day ten of the season, two days after Tommy's diner conversation with Lila about hitting a dead end in episode four.
2. Desmond goes back alone to the same basement wall he inspected weeks earlier, wanting one more look before letting the engineer's report stand unchallenged in his mind.
3. He runs his hand along the concrete where the report claims cracking is present and finds the surface just as sound as it was the first time.
4. He photographs the wall from several angles, a private record he isn't sure yet what he'll do with.
5. The audience sees Desmond quietly building his own case against the report, still without any idea Julian is the one it protects.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Back in his office, Desmond dials the structural engineer's number and reads off the report's cited defects one at a time.
2. The engineer grows evasive, giving vague technical language instead of direct answers to Desmond's specific questions.
3. Desmond asks plainly who commissioned the report and who saw the basement before he did; the engineer deflects, refusing to give a name.
4. Desmond presses once more, and the engineer ends the call abruptly, citing another appointment.
5. The deflection leaves Desmond more convinced something is wrong with the report, though he still has no way to connect it to Julian.
6. The audience recognizes Desmond now holds real suspicion pointed at the report itself, with the person actually responsible for it still completely invisible to him.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Tommy eats alone in his old booth, the same one Lila seated him in his first day back.
2. Lila sets down a cup of coffee without being asked and without charging him, saying little as she does.
3. Their exchange stays polite and brief, both keeping to safe, small subjects.
4. Tommy senses the years of history neither of them has ever put into words, and lets the silence stand rather than open it.
5. Lila lingers a moment longer than the coffee requires before returning to the counter, her own unspoken feelings about Henry and the fire still folded away.
6. The audience feels how much both are carrying that neither will say to the other, even now.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Nora climbs to the greenhouse again, drawn by the same need for quiet that's brought her there before.
2. She finds Augustine watering the beds more slowly than usual, pausing between each plant longer than he used to.
3. Their small talk stays light, circling the auction and the weather, neither naming what's actually weighing on them.
4. Nora notices his pace again but, distracted by her own hidden countdown, doesn't ask him about it directly.
5. Augustine, focused on keeping his exhaustion invisible, is relieved she lets it go unremarked.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Augustine reaches up to a high shelf for a bag of soil, and a sudden stab of pain drops him hard against the shelf's edge.
2. Nora crosses the room fast and catches his arm before he can fall further, alarm plain on her face.
3. The pain is the same cancer-driven pain that has struck him before, though Nora has no way of knowing that.
4. The instant he sees her notice, Augustine waves her off, blaming his back and brushing away her concern with practiced ease.
5. Nora doesn't fully believe the explanation but has no reason to suspect it's anything more than his age catching up with him.
6. The audience watches Augustine protect a six-week-old secret in real time, inches from being caught by someone who isn't looking for it.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Still shaken, Nora heads down to the lobby and runs into Tommy at the mailboxes.
2. He notices she's pale and asks if she's alright, his concern immediate.
3. Nora deflects by telling him about Augustine's stumble in the greenhouse rather than admitting how much it rattled her.
4. Tommy takes the deflection at face value, his worry shifting naturally onto Augustine instead of her.
5. Neither realizes how much Nora is once again steering a conversation away from herself, this time without even meaning to hide anything about the baby.
6. The audience sees Nora's habit of deflection extending now even to moments that have nothing to do with Julian.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Desmond finds Augustine sorting through the tenant files Tommy searched days earlier and vents his frustration about the engineer's stonewalling.
2. He describes the call in detail, the deflections, the refusal to name who commissioned the report.
3. Desmond stops short of saying he suspects someone paid for the false findings, unwilling to voice a theory he can't yet prove.
4. Augustine listens and offers only mild, noncommittal sympathy, giving no sign of his own unrelated secrets sitting nearby in the very room they're standing in.
5. Neither man realizes how close their two separate concealments, one about the building's debt, one about a ledger just steps away, sit to unraveling in the same conversation.
6. The audience recognizes Desmond's suspicion is growing steadily closer to the truth even as the person he's talking to holds a different truth entirely.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Late that night, Tommy finds Lila closing up the diner and admits he can't stop doing the math on the baby, and that it doesn't add up to him being the father.
2. He confides this only to her, not yet ready to say it to Nora directly.
3. Lila listens gently, still certain herself that Tommy is the father and reading his doubt as ordinary new-parent anxiety.
4. She encourages him to ask Nora outright instead of carrying the question alone, meaning it as simple, caring advice.
5. Neither of them knows Lila's encouragement is nudging Tommy toward a conversation that could expose Julian's involvement along with the truth about paternity.
6. The audience closes the episode aware that Lila has just unknowingly set in motion the very confrontation that could unravel Nora's carefully held secret.
