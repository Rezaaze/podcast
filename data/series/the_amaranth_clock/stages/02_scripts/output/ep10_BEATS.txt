--- SCENE 1 ---
1. It is day thirty of the season, the day of the auction, three days after Nora's secret ultrasound appointment in episode nine.
2. Desmond moves through the lobby setting out rows of folding chairs for the tenants and bidders gathering for the auction.
3. Iris arrives dressed for the proceedings, the crack of doubt Desmond's basement photographs opened in her pushed back down beneath her resolve to see the sale through.
4. She still believes, as she has all season, that the decision to sell was entirely her own, with no idea Julian intends to be the auction's winning bidder.
5. Desmond watches her cross the lobby, still holding his own confirmed suspicion about the report with no way yet to connect it to her fiancé.
6. The audience senses the countdown that began with Walter's death is about to close in exactly the shape Julian engineered it to.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Before the auction begins downstairs, Tommy is back in the archive going through the same dead-end files one more time, unable to stop searching even with the sale looming.
2. Augustine finds him there, and watching him dig through the decoy box yet again, feels his tally of near-misses and his private countdown finally converge into a decision.
3. Without offering Tommy any explanation, Augustine crosses to the wall, pries the loose brick free himself, and lifts out the fireproof box he's hidden behind it for twelve years.
4. Tommy stares first at the box, then at Augustine, too stunned by the sudden reversal to ask anything yet.
5. Augustine tells him only that this needs Lila in the room too before he opens it, and asks Tommy to bring her down.
6. The audience recognizes this as the moment Augustine finally chooses Tommy's vindication over the protection he's maintained since the fire.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Lila arrives at Tommy's summons, confused and uneasy, with no idea what's waiting for her in the archive.
2. Augustine opens the fireproof box in front of both of them and lifts out the embezzlement ledger he removed from Walter's office two nights before the fire.
3. He reads aloud how Walter and Henry, both facing bankruptcy after Walter's embezzlement from the renovation fund, arranged the fire together for the insurance payout.
4. He continues, reading the entry recording the money paid to security guard Ray Odell to testify against Tommy.
5. Tommy hears, for the first time, real names and real proof behind the frame he's carried unexplained for twelve years, though nothing here tells him who paid for the false structural report or that Nora is carrying Julian's child.
6. Lila hears, in the same reading, that the husband she has spent years mourning as careful and honest helped destroy an innocent teenager's life, with no way now to soften what she's just learned.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Tommy, unable to sit still with what he's just heard, leaves the archive to be alone with it, leaving Augustine and Lila standing together over the open box.
2. In the quiet that follows, Augustine tells Lila plainly that he was diagnosed six weeks ago with terminal pancreatic cancer and has told no one, not even her.
3. He explains, without asking forgiveness, that having just broken twelve years of silence about Henry, he can't make himself carry a second silence one more day.
4. Lila realizes in the same breath that the tiredness and slowness she'd noticed in him for weeks was never his age or the greenhouse, but this.
5. She has no time yet to process what Henry did before this second blow lands directly on top of it, one grief overwriting the space meant for the other.
6. The audience sees Augustine finally set down both burdens he's carried alone all season, in the same conversation, to the same person.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Downstairs, the auction proceeds on schedule, the lobby full of tenants and bidders, none aware of what has just happened in the basement.
2. Julian arrives ostensibly to support Iris, taking a seat near the front rather than among the general bidders.
3. As bidding opens, a representative for the numbered LLC steps forward with paperwork, and Iris notices Julian conferring quietly with him just before he speaks.
4. Recognizing the same account details from the bank records she copied weeks earlier in the paperwork the representative displays, Iris connects the pattern she's been tracing straight to Julian.
5. Before the auctioneer can call for bids, Iris interrupts and states aloud, in front of the gathered tenants, that Julian is the controlling signatory behind the LLC.
6. The audience watches the reveal land as much a shock to Iris herself as to everyone else, her voice outpacing her own ability to absorb the betrayal.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. In the stunned silence that follows, Desmond steps forward and confirms publicly that his own basement inspection found the report's cited defects never existed.
2. Tenants who watched Tommy dismissed for years now watch Julian's scheme unravel in the same lobby, the irony plain to everyone present.
3. Julian tries to downplay Iris's accusation, but his inability to give a straight answer about the shared account only confirms it.
4. Iris, still standing amid the wreckage of her own announcement, begins to register two years of the relationship as calculated rather than real.
5. The auctioneer, uncertain how to proceed, calls a halt to the proceedings rather than accept a bid under open dispute.
6. The audience sees Iris's public unmasking of the scheme curdle immediately into a private grief she has no space yet to feel.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Reeling from what he's just learned about Walter, Henry, and Ray Odell, Tommy climbs to the greenhouse to be alone and finds Nora already there.
2. Nora tells him, for the first time, that she went to a doctor's appointment for the pregnancy, the ultrasound, entirely alone, without saying why.
3. Tommy, still raw from the ledger, tells her he can't carry one more thing he doesn't actually know today, and asks her again, straight out, whether the baby is his.
4. Nora hesitates, the neighborhood's old assumption about Tommy still the only thing standing between Julian and the fact of the pregnancy, even with his engagement now lying in ruins downstairs.
5. She still doesn't answer, deflecting instead toward how overwhelming the day already is for both of them.
6. Tommy, given nothing this time to read as confirmation, leaves the greenhouse without a response, the question still open between them.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Past midnight, Lila sits alone in the closed diner with Henry's ledger page in front of her, the suspicion now confirmed fact.
2. She turns over Augustine's confession in the same breath, still fresh, still unprocessed.
3. She can't decide whether she's grieving the husband she thought she knew, the dying man who kept two truths from her, or the boy who lost twelve years to both their silences.
4. No one comes to sit with her, and the diner she's run alone for years feels, for the first time, entirely too big.
5. The audience closes the episode watching Lila carry alone the two griefs that everyone else at the Amaranth is only now beginning to learn were connected all along.
