6. The audience registers the private scale of this realization long before anyone else in the room does.

5. He believes he can still hand everything over once he "wins" this exchange, not recognizing there's no such finish line.

3. She notes Daniel's record shows nothing beyond a minor nonviolent charge, undercutting any pattern of violence.

--- SCENE 9 ---

3. He finds what he expected, confirming the killer is still active despite Daniel's arrest.
4. His need to handle this personally, without oversight, outweighs any procedural caution.

--- SCENE 11 ---

6. The audience sees Reid's isolation and the killer's decade-long scope converging in the same private moment.
