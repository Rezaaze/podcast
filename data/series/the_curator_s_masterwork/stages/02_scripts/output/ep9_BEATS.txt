--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Reid clears his desk and pins every cipher, tableau photo, and note from the season across the table in chronological order.
2. He starts connecting them not by the murders they marked but by the private choices he made around each one.
3. He realizes the clues track his compromises more precisely than they track any crime scene.
4. He can't yet accept the implication and tells himself he's imagining a pattern that isn't there.
5. He keeps this line of thinking to himself, unwilling to say it aloud even in an empty room.
6. The audience sees Reid standing at the edge of the truth the Curator has built the whole season toward.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. The Curator studies his private collection under lamplight, several objects representing turning points from Reid's season.
2. He makes one final, careful adjustment to the newest piece, aligning it with the rest.
3. He steps back and views the whole arrangement as complete rather than ongoing.
4. His calm signals the design phase is finished and something else is about to begin.
5. Nothing here reveals what happens next, only that the waiting is over.
6. The audience understands the collection was never about the murders — it was always about Reid.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Park sits alone and writes out, in her own hand, every instance of Reid's conduct she's documented this season.
2. She lists the lie about the missing report, the informant meeting, and the evasions, dated and specific.
3. Writing it forces her to admit this is no longer a pattern she can excuse as pressure or grief.
4. She hesitates over what to do with the file once it's finished, still not committing to reporting him.
5. She hides from herself how much this costs her personally, given how much she still trusts him.
6. The audience sees the moment Park's private suspicion becomes something formal and undeniable.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Mia tells Reid directly and without softening that she has already consulted a divorce attorney.
2. She states it as fact, not threat, making clear the decision is further along than he assumed.
3. Reid tries to respond but has nothing prepared that meets the weight of what she's said.
4. Mia doesn't accuse him of anything new, letting the plain statement do the damage alone.
5. Neither says the marriage is over, but both hear it in the space after she speaks.
6. The audience recognizes this as Mia closing the gap between quietly leaving and being unmistakably clear.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Joy Chen receives word from a legal contact that exculpatory evidence existed before Daniel's plea was signed.
2. She initially struggles to believe timing this cruel could be true.
3. She demands confirmation and gets enough to know the claim is solid, not rumor.
4. She immediately understands this changes everything about how she sees the department's handling of the case.
5. She doesn't yet know Reid specifically sat on it, only that someone did.
6. The audience feels the countdown start toward Reid's private failure becoming public.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Park sets her written file down in front of Reid and asks him to walk her through it, item by item.
2. She names the lie about the report and the unexplained gap in his account of that missing evening.
3. Reid tries the same calm denial that worked before, but Park doesn't accept it this time.
4. She tells him plainly she's seen enough to know he's lying to her face, not just omitting.
5. Reid doesn't confess outright but stops actively denying, which is its own admission between them.
6. The audience watches the first time Reid's concealment fails to hold against someone who knows him.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. A new drop from the Curator arrives, describing the exact drawer where Reid locked the report away.
2. No one but Reid should know that detail, since he never told Park, Hollis, or anyone else.
3. He reads it alone and feels the ground shift under the belief that this was ever his investigation to run.
4. He can't explain the knowledge through any conventional leak or surveillance he can identify.
5. He says nothing about it to Park, even fresh from her confronting him, deepening his isolation.
6. The audience gets explicit confirmation the Curator has had inside knowledge of Reid's private choices all along.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Hollis reviews what Park has brought him and finds it too specific and too documented to dismiss.
2. He realizes protecting Reid quietly is no longer an option available to him.
3. He opens a formal internal inquiry into Reid's conduct, making it official rather than a private conversation.
4. He tells himself this is the responsible move, though guilt over his past leniency toward Reid lingers underneath.
5. He doesn't yet know about the Curator's summons or the season's larger design closing in.
6. The audience sees the machinery of accountability start turning just as something far larger is arriving.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Alone among evidence boards and case machinery, Reid lets himself sit with everything he's seen today.
2. He considers, for the first time openly, that the season's clues were built around him rather than for him to solve.
3. He starts to grasp he might have been the one being shaped, not the one shaping the hunt.
4. He still can't fully name what he was being shaped into or why.
5. He doesn't share this dawning realization with anyone, keeping it as private as everything else he's hidden.
6. The audience recognizes this as Reid approaching the truth without yet reaching its full shape.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Reid and Mia have the fight that's been building all season, sharper and less controlled than any before.
2. Mia tells him she doesn't recognize who he's become over these months.
3. Reid tries to defend himself by pointing to the case, and Mia rejects that justification outright.
4. Neither backs down this time, and the argument ends without resolution or repair.
5. Mia follows through by packing boxes and setting them by the door, making the separation physical.
6. The audience sees the marriage collapse pass from private erosion into visible, undeniable rupture.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Park and Mia meet again, this time with more at stake than shared worry.
2. Mia tells Park about the boxes by the door, and Park tells Mia enough about the inquiry to explain the timing.
3. Neither reveals every detail she's holding, but each gives the other more than before.
4. They recognize they're both bracing for something neither can yet define.
5. They commit to leaning on each other rather than carrying it alone, a shift from earlier caution.
6. The audience feels two separate threads of Reid's unraveling converge through the women closest to him.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Joy Chen goes to the press with what she's learned about the evidence that predated Daniel's plea.
2. She names the department directly and, by clear implication, Reid as the one who handled the case.
3. The story breaks publicly, turning a private failure into a departmental scandal.
4. She still believes this was about ambition or carelessness, not grasping the deeper reason Reid delayed.
5. She feels vindicated for pushing but takes no comfort in what it cost to get here.
6. The audience sees the frame-up thread's consequences become impossible for anyone to contain quietly.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Reid receives formal written notice that he's being pulled from the case pending the internal review.
2. The notice is procedural and impersonal, giving him no room to argue his case in the moment.
3. He reads it against the backdrop of Park's confrontation, the scandal, and the Curator's note, all landing together.
4. He recognizes, for the first time, how much of his authority over the investigation is already gone.
5. He doesn't yet tell anyone how thoroughly the different pressures have converged on him at once.
6. The audience sees the formal consequence catching up to conduct the audience watched accumulate scene by scene.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. A summons arrives from the Curator, precise in its details, naming a specific place and time.
2. It addresses Reid directly by his first name, breaking the impersonal tone of everything sent before.
3. The wording carries an almost gentle certainty, as if inviting rather than threatening.
4. Reid understands this is different from every previous clue — a direct address meant only for him.
5. He doesn't report the summons to Park or Hollis, choosing to hold it as his alone to answer.
6. The audience recognizes the season's design reaching its intended culmination, with Reid stepping toward it unknowingly.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Reid sits alone in the now-empty apartment with the summons in front of him.
2. He tells himself he is choosing to go, that he is still the one directing how this ends.
3. He doesn't yet recognize that arriving on the Curator's exact terms is itself the surrender of that control.
4. He sets aside the suspension, the scandal, and Mia's absence as things he'll address only after this is finished.
5. He treats the summons as the one thing left he can still control, rather than the trap it is.
6. The audience alone understands his belief in control is the last illusion the Curator was counting on him to keep.
