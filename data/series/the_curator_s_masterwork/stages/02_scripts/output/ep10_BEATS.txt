--- SCENE 1 ---
1. A dead drop arrives for Reid that breaks every pattern of the season: an address, a time, and his first name instead of a riddle.
2. The tone reads as an invitation, gentle and certain, rather than a threat or a taunt.
3. Reid recognizes this as the direct address the previous summons only gestured toward, now made final and specific.
4. He checks the address against the case boards and confirms it matches nowhere the investigation has already searched.
5. He decides again to go alone, treating this as his to answer rather than something to report.
6. The audience understands this is the Curator closing the last distance between design and execution.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mia catches Reid as he's about to leave, sensing immediately this isn't an ordinary case call.
2. She tells him plainly that if he walks out the door for this, there's nothing left for him to come back to.
3. Reid tries to start an explanation, but Mia cuts past it, saying she's past needing to understand, only needing to know if he'll choose it anyway.
4. He doesn't deny where he's going or soften what it might cost, which is its own answer to her.
5. Mia doesn't cry or escalate; she simply waits, already knowing what his silence means.
6. The audience sees Reid choose the mill over the marriage in the moment it matters most, without a single word confirming it.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Joy Chen stands before reporters on the courthouse steps, ready to lay out what she's learned about evidence that predated Daniel's plea.
2. She states clearly that someone in the department sat on information that could have cleared her brother months ago.
3. She doesn't yet name Reid specifically, still believing the failure is institutional rather than personal.
4. Reporters press her on names and consequences, and she promises the hearing will provide both.
5. She feels the weight of months of being disbelieved starting, finally, to turn in her favor.
6. The audience feels the frame-up thread reaching its public reckoning in parallel with Reid's private one.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Hollis stands before an oversight panel, asked directly why Daniel Chen was held so long on evidence this thin.
2. He gives careful, procedural answers, avoiding any admission of how much latitude he gave Reid to keep pursuing his own case instead.
3. A panel member references the courthouse story breaking that morning, tightening the pressure in the room.
4. Hollis realizes the account he's giving won't survive contact with what Park's inquiry and the press are about to reveal together.
5. He hides, even here, exactly how deliberately he let Reid's doubts about Daniel go unacted on.
6. The audience sees Hollis's controlled account start to crack under pressure he can't yet fully contain.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Park finds Reid before he can leave for the address and lays out what she's pieced together: the summons, the pattern, the toll of the season.
2. She tells him she knows he means to go alone and states flatly that she isn't letting that happen.
3. Reid tries the same deflection that's worked all season, but Park has already decided it won't work again.
4. She frames it not as backup for the case but as refusing to let him do this one thing by himself.
5. Reid, worn past the point of arguing, doesn't agree aloud but doesn't stop her from coming either.
6. The audience sees the first crack in Reid's isolation, arriving too late to change what's about to happen at the mill.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Reid arrives at the Old Textile Mill to find the Curator's tableaux fully assembled, arranged in a sequence that reads as complete rather than ongoing.
2. At the center of the arrangement sits one conspicuously empty space, clearly built to hold a final piece.
3. Reid studies the empty space and feels, without yet naming it, that it's shaped around him specifically.
4. He notices details in the tableaux he recognizes from his own private choices this season, not just the murders.
5. Park, arriving separately, takes in the same scene and reads the same wrongness in what's missing from it.
6. The audience recognizes the gallery as finished exactly the way Scene 2 of the prior episode foretold — and understands the empty space is meant for Reid himself.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Park, working the mill's outer rooms rather than the gallery itself, finds a hidden study no one else has located.
2. Inside, she finds a journal clearly left in a place and manner meant to be discovered at exactly this moment.
3. She begins reading and immediately recognizes entries referencing Reid's career and home life predating the first murder.
4. She realizes this study was never meant to be hidden from investigators forever — only until this specific night.
5. She keeps reading rather than immediately calling out to Reid, needing to understand what she's holding first.
6. The audience learns the journal exists as the Curator's own instrument for exposing his design, running in parallel to the confrontation at the mill.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. The Curator steps into view before Reid, unarmed and unhurried, and speaks to him without the taunting distance of every prior letter.
2. He tells Reid, in essence, that he was never the hunter in this story — that he was chosen and shaped from the very start.
3. He confirms he studied Reid's case history, his marriage, his temperament, long before the first cipher was ever sent.
4. He frames the frame-up of Daniel Chen as one deliberate lesson among several, designed to teach Reid that instinct could outrun procedure.
5. Reid tries to hold onto the idea that he's still the one in control here, even as the Curator's calm suggests otherwise.
6. The audience hears, at last spoken aloud, the truth the season has been building toward: Reid as instrument, not investigator.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. The Curator moves toward the empty space in the gallery, positioning himself exactly where the final piece was always meant to stand.
2. He makes clear, without begging or resisting, that he expects Reid to be the one to finish this, here, without a warrant or backup.
3. Reid has a clear moment where arrest is still possible, and recognizes it as such even as it passes him by.
4. He crosses the line the entire season was quietly walking him toward, believing even now that the choice is entirely his own.
5. In the instant after, Reid feels a version of victory that has nothing solid underneath it.
6. The audience witnesses the season's true design complete itself exactly as the Curator intended, with Reid as its final, willing brushstroke.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Park sits with the journal in the hidden study and turns to its final, most explicit pages.
2. She reads aloud, alone, the passage naming Reid as the necessary hand meant to complete the Curator's work.
3. She realizes in real time that everything she's documented about Reid this season was never separate from this design — it was the design working as intended.
4. She understands the frame-up of Daniel Chen wasn't incidental cruelty but a calculated step in shaping Reid specifically.
5. She feels the ground shift under her own certainty about what she thought she'd caught him doing.
6. The audience watches the season's meaning reorganize itself entirely around Reid's name, just as the confrontation elsewhere reaches its end.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Back at the precinct, the journal's contents are laid out for the record, corroborating what Park found at the mill.
2. The entry naming Daniel Chen as "a page turned to buy time" is confirmed against the physical evidence: the planted stub, the shift-log discrepancy, the timing tied to the news profile.
3. Investigators reconcile the timeline, confirming Daniel's only real connection to the murders was his night-shift route, nothing more.
4. Hollis is shown the confirmation and can no longer frame Daniel's detention as a defensible judgment call.
5. No one in the room yet grasps the full second layer — that this frame-up was aimed at shaping Reid, not just at making an arrest.
6. The audience sees the frame-up thread's central lie collapse completely, beyond any further doubt or spin.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Outside the courthouse, word reaches Joy Chen that the journal confirms everything she's argued for months.
2. She's told Daniel is being formally cleared, the charges against him ended entirely.
3. She receives this vindication with relief that has no room left in it for triumph, given everything the fight already cost her and Daniel.
4. She still doesn't know the frame-up was aimed at Reid personally rather than department carelessness, only that the truth finally, undeniably, came out.
5. Reporters push for a statement, and she gives only enough to confirm the clearance, nothing more.
6. The audience sees the frame-up thread close on the exact vindication Scene 5 of the prior episode set in motion, arriving through means Joy never anticipated.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Hollis, back at the precinct, absorbs the combined weight of the oversight hearing, the journal, and what happened at the mill.
2. He recognizes that every quiet accommodation he made for Reid across the season fed directly into what the Curator was engineering.
3. He understands his own account to oversight won't hold once the journal's contents become fully known.
4. He faces, for the first time without deflection, that protecting Reid wasn't a neutral kindness but a cost that landed on Daniel Chen and now on the department itself.
5. He doesn't yet decide what he'll do about his own position, only that the easy version of this story is no longer available to him.
6. The audience sees accountability finally reach the one figure who spent all season managing it away from Reid.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Park and Reid sit together in the aftermath, neither yet speaking directly about what happened in the empty space at the mill.
2. Reid gives an account of the confrontation angled closer to self-defense than what actually occurred, testing whether it will hold with her.
3. Park, having read the journal, doesn't challenge him outright, but her silence makes clear she knows more than his version accounts for.
4. Neither says aloud that she now understands he was cultivated rather than pursued, though it sits plainly between them.
5. Reid senses, without full confirmation, that whatever trust remained between them has changed shape permanently.
6. The audience watches Reid's habitual concealment meet a version of Park who now holds the one account that could unmake it completely.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Reid returns to the apartment to find it exactly as Mia left it: quiet, half-emptied, no verdict waiting for him.
2. He tries to speak about what happened at the mill, downplaying how close he came to losing control there.
3. Mia doesn't ask for details, having already decided this isn't about facts she's missing but a decision she's already made.
4. Reid still believes there's time left to repair things now that "the case" is finally over, not registering that her deadline passed weeks ago.
5. Neither delivers a final word on the marriage; they simply face each other with nothing left to investigate but themselves.
6. The audience closes the season understanding Reid solved the case that was never his to solve, and lost, without yet seeing it, the one thing that was.
