--- SCENE 1 ---
1. A lab technician delivers a report to Reid's desk, and he reads it alone before anyone else sees it.
2. The report conclusively places Daniel Chen away from the scene at the time of the killing.
3. Reid understands immediately that this single document would end the case against Daniel.
4. He sets it aside rather than routing it to Hollis or the prosecutor as protocol requires.
5. He tells himself he'll deal with it shortly, once he has a clearer head.
6. The audience recognizes this as the exact moment the frame-up could have ended and didn't.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Alone with the report, Reid weighs what filing it now would cost the Curator investigation.
2. He reasons that Daniel is safe enough for a few more days inside, so nothing irreversible happens by waiting.
3. He decides not to file it yet, treating the delay as a technicality rather than a choice with a victim.
4. He tells himself this is temporary, that he can correct it the moment the Curator is caught.
5. He locks the report away rather than destroying it, so he can still believe he hasn't fully crossed a line.
6. The audience understands he has just made the compromise the Curator has been waiting years to see him make.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. At the mill, investigators find a new tableau the Curator has left, arranged with his usual care.
2. Embedded in its details is an implication that whoever left it already knows a choice was just made.
3. Reid studies it and feels, without being able to prove it, that it's responding directly to something private.
4. He can't explain how anyone could know what happened in his office only hours earlier.
5. He says nothing about this unsettling read to Park or Hollis, keeping it inside as he has before.
6. The audience alone understands the tableau is exact confirmation the Curator has been shaping this decision all along.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Daniel Chen's bail hearing takes place, and the judge denies release given the current evidence on record.
2. Joy Chen watches from the gallery as the ruling closes another door for her brother.
3. She sees the system moving against him with a confidence Reid privately knows is unearned.
4. She doesn't yet know a report exists that would have changed the outcome entirely.
5. She leaves resolved to fight harder through legal channels, unaware the fight is already rigged against her.
6. The audience feels the weight of what Reid's silence has directly cost, scene by scene.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mia asks Reid to account for where he was on a specific missed evening.
2. Reid gives an answer about being at the precinct, vague on specifics he'd normally recall easily.
3. Mia notices the smoothness of it, the way it avoids detail rather than supplies it.
4. She doesn't have proof yet, but she registers the shape of a lie before she can name its content.
5. She lets the conversation end without pressing further, filing the moment away instead.
6. The audience already knows the true answer: he was pressuring an informant that night, not at the precinct.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Park reviews the case's evidence log as part of routine follow-up on the mill discovery.
2. She notices an entry whose official timestamp doesn't match when she remembers it actually being logged.
3. She checks it twice, assuming her own error before trusting what she's seeing.
4. She can't reconcile the discrepancy with anything she was told about the evidence's handling.
5. She decides to keep looking into it quietly before raising it with anyone, including Reid.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first crack in the concealment Reid has built around the case.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Hollis meets with Reid and Park, relaying pressure from the mayor's office to close the Chen case swiftly.
2. He frames a fast plea deal as the outcome that serves everyone, including Daniel.
3. Reid listens without objection, offering nothing about the report sitting in his desk.
4. Park watches Reid's silence and notes it, though she doesn't yet connect it to anything specific.
5. Hollis leaves the meeting believing he's pushing toward resolution, not toward an injustice.
6. The audience feels the gap between what Hollis believes he's closing and what Reid knows he's protecting.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Joy visits Daniel in custody and finds him changed, hollowed out by fear and exhaustion.
2. He tells her he's ready to take whatever deal makes the pressure and the danger stop.
3. She argues against it, insisting the truth is still worth fighting for.
4. Privately, she's no longer certain continuing the fight is safe for him, though she doesn't say so.
5. She leaves without changing his mind, her own resolve quietly cracking beneath her public one.
6. The audience feels how close the case is to the report's absence sealing an innocent man's fate.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Reid meets an informant in a location off any official record.
2. He applies pressure that goes beyond legally sanctioned tactics, isolating and threatening consequences he can't actually promise.
3. The informant, rattled, gives up information that meaningfully advances the Curator case.
4. Reid takes the information without documenting how it was obtained.
5. He tells himself the ends justify a method he wouldn't defend if asked directly.
6. The audience recognizes this as the same evening he lied to Mia about, the informant standing in for the truth he withheld from her.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. In the private, ordered room, the Curator selects a small new object to add to his collection.
2. He considers its placement carefully, adjusting it until it sits exactly where he intends.
3. He regards the completed arrangement with quiet satisfaction rather than urgency.
4. Nothing in his manner suggests he's improvising or reacting to anything unexpected.
5. The scene offers no explanation of what the object represents or where it points next.
6. The audience senses the collection nearing a shape whose purpose only the Curator currently understands.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Park corners Reid and asks directly about a report she now suspects is missing or delayed.
2. Reid meets her question with a calm, immediate denial that nothing is outstanding.
3. He states it plainly enough that Park has no visible seam to push against.
4. Internally, Reid registers the lie as necessary, not as the line he'd have called it a year ago.
5. Park accepts the answer outwardly but doesn't discard her suspicion.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first time Reid has lied to Park's face rather than merely omitting.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Mia, searching for something unrelated, finds a record — a receipt, a message, a location trace — placing Reid somewhere other than where he told her.
2. It doesn't reveal the informant or the case, only that his account of that evening was false.
3. She confronts him with what she's found, and he's caught without a ready explanation.
4. The fight that follows is sharper than their usual arguments, Mia making clear this isn't a one-off she'll absorb quietly.
5. Reid deflects toward the case's demands without addressing the lie itself.
6. Mia doesn't reveal the attorney meeting even now, but privately moves closer to deciding it's necessary.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Daniel Chen, worn down by custody and fear, formally accepts a guilty plea in front of counsel.
2. Joy objects in the moment, urging him to hold out, but he goes through with it anyway.
3. The plea is entered on record, closing the case exactly as Hollis and the mayor's office wanted.
4. Daniel's decision is shaped by conditions and fear, not by any actual guilt.
5. Joy leaves believing the department's neglect broke him, still unaware a report existed that could have stopped this.
6. The audience feels the exact cost of Reid's earlier silence land, now irreversible.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. Reid finally submits the exculpatory report, moved by the plea's finality to act.
2. He learns from the prosecutor's office that Daniel's guilty plea is already signed and binding.
3. He's told the report, however conclusive, can't simply undo a plea entered in good standing.
4. Reid absorbs that his belief in a reversible delay was wrong from the moment he made it.
5. He doesn't yet tell Park or Hollis the full truth of when the report was ready or why it waited.
6. The audience sees his wrongly-held belief in a "not yet" collapse into a permanent, unrecoverable fact.

--- SCENE 15 ---
1. Investigators decode the final layer of tonight's clue from the Curator.
2. Its structure implies foreknowledge of exactly the choice Reid made about the report, down to its timing.
3. Reid reads it and feels a chill he can't yet fully articulate or justify to himself.
4. He starts to suspect the case has been shaping his decisions rather than merely testing him.
5. He still keeps this suspicion from Park and Hollis, extending his isolation further.
6. The audience alone understands the Curator anticipated this exact compromise as the season's real design coming to completion.
