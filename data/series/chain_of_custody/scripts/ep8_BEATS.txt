--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Mei Lin enters her override code into Interrogation Room 3's panel, and the door seals with a soft mechanical finality behind her.
2. The corridor camera feed outside the room dies to static in the exact same instant, though no one in the bullpen is watching that monitor right now.
3. Kai Yang reaches the corridor seconds later, log printout in hand, and finds the door reads locked from an internal override, not a standard lock.
4. He tries his own credentials against the panel and gets rejected, realizing this lockout was deliberately set to exclude even a fellow detective.
5. He does not yet know the same code also disabled the rear exit's alarm; he only registers that Mei Lin has cut herself off from everyone, including him.
6. The audience understands he is now locked out of the exact truth he was one hallway away from confirming.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Inside the sealed room, Voss registers the door's finality with quiet satisfaction he keeps entirely off his face.
2. Mei Lin mistakes the sudden quiet for privacy earned, not privacy engineered, and steadies herself to ask what she came to ask.
3. Voss watches her composure with the patience of someone who has already rehearsed every version of this conversation in his head.
4. He offers her a small, disarming stillness, letting her believe the room's silence is on her side.
5. The audience recognizes this calm as the same calibrated warmth from the previous episode, now deployed at the exact moment it was built for.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Zoe Han's story goes live, the ledger figures and the pattern of cancelled harbor raids now public under her byline.
2. Anna Wu reads it beside her, seeing Daniel's scrawled harbor-company note finally validated in print, though Daniel's name appears nowhere in the piece.
3. Zoe still withholds her source's identity in the published version, per her earlier promise, even as the story implicates the precinct's own leadership by inference.
4. Neither woman yet states Sun's name outright in what they know, but the raid pattern alone points hard enough that readers will ask the question for them.
5. The audience sees the public thread and the private thread inside Interrogation Room 3 now running on the same clock, unbeknownst to either side.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Sun sees the story break on his phone before anyone briefs him officially, and understands immediately that denial is no longer an available strategy.
2. He tries to reach someone above him to control the precinct's public response, but the calls go unanswered or deflected.
3. He realizes his earlier private decision to intervene physically is now colliding with a public crisis he also has to manage, and he cannot do both.
4. He still believes, wrongly, that his own exposure can be separated from Mei Lin's — even as the same story ties the harbor network to a case he buried with her.
5. The audience watches his control fracture in two directions at once, neither of which he can actually contain.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei Lin asks Voss, as plainly as she has ever allowed herself, exactly where Daniel Wu is being held.
2. She frames it as the deal they agreed to — her time and risk in exchange for one real answer.
3. She hides, even from herself in this moment, how far outside procedure this question and this room have already taken her.
4. Voss lets a long pause sit before answering anything, watching her need for the answer sharpen with every second of silence.
5. The audience sees this pause as Voss testing exactly how much more pressure her guilt can absorb before he gives her anything at all.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Zoe's finance-office source calls her in a panic now that the story is live and the raid pattern is drawing attention she didn't expect.
2. The source demands reassurance that nothing in the published figures can be traced back to her specific access logs.
3. Zoe reassures her while privately unsure the promise will hold if Sun or IA moves to identify precinct leaks aggressively.
4. The source hints she may recant or refuse further cooperation if pressure increases, threatening the story's next phase before it can even begin.
5. Anna, listening nearby, realizes for the first time how fragile the whole chain tracing back to Daniel actually is.
6. The audience sees the public story now as exposed and vulnerable as the private one behind the locked door.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Kai Yang decides against waiting for backup, choosing to force the room's override himself rather than escalate through channels that might reach Sun first.
2. He tells the two uniformed officers with him only that there's a safety concern behind the door, withholding his real fear that he's protecting Mei Lin more than enforcing procedure.
3. He begins working the panel's manual override sequence, aware it will take real time even once started.
4. He still has no idea forcing this override will do anything to the rear exit's alarm status — his focus is entirely on the door in front of him.
5. The audience, already knowing what that circuit controls, understands he's about to trigger something far larger than the door he's watching.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Voss finally answers Mei Lin's question, but with a location vague enough to demand another meeting, another concession, another piece of her control.
2. Mei Lin recognizes, for a flicker of a moment, that she's being managed rather than answered — then lets the need for Daniel override that instinct.
3. Voss reframes her disappointment as her own impatience, subtly making her feel she asked the question wrong rather than that he answered it wrong.
4. He privately notes her acceptance of this reframe as the last confirmation he needed that she'll comply with whatever comes next.
5. The audience watches Voss convert her most desperate moment yet into the final increment of control he's been building toward all along.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Sun arrives near the wing housing Interrogation Room 3, still intending to intervene before Zoe's story can be traced back further to the old case number.
2. He is stopped short by the sight of Kai Yang and two officers already working to force the very door he needs to reach.
3. He now has two active fires — the public ledger story and this sudden breach — with no way to address either without exposing his connection to both.
4. He hesitates in the corridor, unseen for the moment, trying to decide which crisis to walk toward first.
5. The audience sees him paralyzed exactly when decisive action would matter most, undone by the same secrecy that got him here.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. The override sequence Kai Yang is forcing causes the corridor's camera feed to flicker briefly back to life before dying again.
2. In that flicker, the feed catches half a second of the rear emergency exit door's status light shifting from disabled to active.
3. No one monitoring in real time is present to see it, but the system logs the change permanently this time.
4. Kai Yang, focused on the interrogation room panel, misses the flicker entirely and keeps working.
5. The audience alone registers this as the first hard evidence — soon to be discoverable — that the room's override does more than lock a door.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. The override sequence completes, and Interrogation Room 3's door releases with a heavy mechanical give.
2. Kai Yang pushes it open, officers behind him, adrenaline overtaking any plan he'd rehearsed for this moment.
3. Sun, still in the corridor, hears the door break open before he can reach it himself and understands his last chance to intervene privately is gone.
4. Mei Lin turns toward the sudden light and noise, the vague location Voss just gave her still unconfirmed and unrecorded.
5. Voss's expression shifts, just briefly, into something closer to satisfaction than alarm, though he lets his face catch up to fear a beat later.
6. The audience sees three separate reckonings — Kai Yang's, Sun's, and Voss's private triumph — all landing in the same second.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Kai Yang finds Mei Lin alone with Voss, no camera record, no lawyer, no backup — exactly what his evidence had warned him to expect, and worse.
2. He says nothing yet about the falsified logs, focused instead on getting her out of the room before anyone else arrives.
3. Sun appears in the doorway moments later, and his presence beside the newly broken door raises a question among the officers he can't immediately answer.
4. Mei Lin realizes, seeing Kai Yang's face, that whatever she believed she still controlled about this situation is already gone.
5. Voss stays entirely calm, letting the chaos around him do exactly what he predicted it would.
6. The audience understands this is not the rescue Kai Yang believes it to be, but the exact scene Voss has been engineering toward for months.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. In the confusion following the door's breach, a young officer mentions offhand that the rear exit alarm shows a status change with no matching authorization on record.
2. Sun overhears this and recognizes, with cold certainty, that the trail now leads directly back to the override circuit tied to his own name and Mei Lin's.
3. Elsewhere, word reaches the precinct that one of the three previously cancelled harbor raids is being quietly reopened for review in light of Zoe's published story.
4. Sun realizes the raid that was never supposed to happen again is about to happen after all, on a timeline he no longer controls.
5. Kai Yang, still focused on Mei Lin, has not yet connected the exit alarm anomaly to anything beyond a technical glitch.
6. The audience closes the episode understanding that both threads — the locked room and the ledger — have now converged into a single unraveling no one character can contain alone.
