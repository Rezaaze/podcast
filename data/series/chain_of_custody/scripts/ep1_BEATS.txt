--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Anna Wu walks the length of Daniel's apartment, touching his things without moving them, as if disturbing them would make his absence final.
2. She marks the wall calendar or her phone with a private count of hours since he vanished, already well past a full day.
3. She rehearses a call to the police in her head, then doesn't make it, certain she'll be told nothing again.
4. A detail lands wrong to her only now — Daniel had been distracted and secretive in the weeks before, something she brushed off at the time.
5. The audience sees her fear directly, though later she'll only let Mei Lin see her anger.
6. She decides she can't just wait anymore and resolves to go to the precinct herself.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Mei Lin and Kai Yang go over a tip that places someone near the harbor docks on the night Daniel disappeared.
2. Kai Yang reads off the detail: a security camera partially obscured by rain caught a man matching Jonas Voss at the same location.
3. Mei Lin cross-references it with phone tower data already confirming Daniel was in that area that night.
4. Neither of them yet knows why Voss was there, only that his presence and Daniel's overlap in time and place.
5. Mei Lin feels the case shift from a missing-persons file to something with a name attached, and pushes for Voss to be brought in.
6. Kai Yang flags that the footage alone won't be enough to hold him long, only enough to ask questions.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Patrol officers bring Jonas Voss in from the rain at the harbor, and he goes without resistance or protest.
2. He is calm in a way that reads as confidence rather than innocence, offering no reaction to being detained.
3. He gives only minimal, procedural answers to the officers' first questions — name, no comment beyond that.
4. Internally, Voss is unbothered because he knows the camera only caught him partially, not clearly enough to charge him.
5. The audience senses his composure is deliberate, though the officers read it as either innocence or practiced cool.
6. He is walked into the building past where Mei Lin will eventually see him for the first time.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Captain Sun is informed that Jonas Voss has been brought in on the Daniel Wu case.
2. For one unguarded second, his composure breaks — a flicker of alarm before he catches himself.
3. He recovers quickly, asking flat procedural questions to cover the reaction, as if he'd shown nothing at all.
4. The audience registers the slip even if the officer informing him does not.
5. Internally, Sun is thinking of the favors and channels that connect Voss's name to people he doesn't want examined.
6. He tells himself distance and non-involvement will keep him safe, though the reaction already contradicts that.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei Lin sits down with Voss for a first formal interview, expecting to rattle something loose.
2. She lays out what they have — the docks, the camera, the timing with Daniel's disappearance.
3. Voss answers everything with calm non-answers, giving nothing that could be used or contradicted later.
4. Mei Lin believes procedure will wear him down eventually, misjudging how prepared and unbothered he actually is.
5. Internally, Voss recognizes this as exactly the kind of pressure he expected and is not troubled by it.
6. The interview ends with no admission, no lead, and no leverage — only the clock continuing to run.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Anna Wu arrives at the precinct and finds Mei Lin in the bullpen, demanding to know what's actually happening.
2. Mei Lin can only confirm that someone is in custody, unable to share details of an active interrogation.
3. Anna reads the vagueness as evasion or indifference, not as Mei Lin protecting the process.
4. She lets only anger show in front of Mei Lin, burying how frightened she really is that the case will go cold.
5. Mei Lin, unaware of the full weight of Anna's fear, responds with procedural reassurance that lands as hollow.
6. The exchange ends with neither woman getting what she needed, and Mei Lin now carrying visible pressure from both directions.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Anna returns alone to Daniel's apartment, searching more deliberately this time instead of just sitting with his things.
2. She finds a sign — something hidden or out of place, like a note, a changed lock, or an object suggesting Daniel had been afraid before he vanished.
3. The discovery reframes his disappearance in her mind: not a random misfortune, but something he saw coming.
4. She has no way yet to connect this fear to the docks, to Voss, or to what he witnessed — the audience is ahead of her here.
5. Her resolve hardens; she decides she can no longer wait quietly on the police and starts thinking about going outside the department for help.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Mei Lin reviews the case timeline and realizes the forty-eight-hour window to formally charge Voss is nearly up.
2. She has nothing solid enough to hold him — no confession, no clear identification, no physical evidence.
3. She understands that if she follows procedure and releases him on schedule, Daniel's only lead walks out the door.
4. She privately doubts Captain Sun will fight to extend or escalate the case, suspecting he wants it handled quietly.
5. The pressure of the clock starts pushing her thinking toward something outside standard procedure, though she hasn't named it yet even to herself.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Kai Yang goes through Voss's priors paperwork as routine background work on the case.
2. He notices a date inconsistency in the file — something that doesn't line up with the rest of the record.
3. He assumes it's a clerical error, filing the discrepancy away as a minor oddity rather than a warning sign.
4. The audience is left holding more suspicion than Kai Yang is, aware this could point to something altered inside the department itself.
5. He mentions it in passing to Mei Lin, who is too focused on the closing clock to give it full attention.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Journalist Zoe Han picks up a whisper through her contacts that the missing man's sister may be ready to go to the press.
2. She recognizes the potential of the story — a missing man, a suspect in custody, a department that won't confirm anything.
3. She starts working her sources to confirm the tip before approaching Anna directly.
4. Neither Zoe nor Anna yet knows this story, once public, will shorten the department's timeline far more than anyone inside it has planned for.
5. Zoe decides to move quickly, sensing the story won't stay quiet for long.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Captain Sun steps away to a private space to make a call about the Voss case, away from the bullpen.
2. He keeps his voice low and chooses his words carefully, clearly not wanting to be overheard.
3. The call touches on the channels and favors tied to Voss's name, though the specifics stay deliberately vague to the audience.
4. Sun's unease from earlier resurfaces here, sharper now that the case is moving faster than he anticipated.
5. He ends the call still committed to staying uninvolved, not yet recognizing that his inaction is already letting the situation slip past his control.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. With the forty-eight-hour clock nearly expired, Mei Lin starts quietly arranging to get time alone with Voss.
2. She finds a reason to have the assigned watch officer step away or reassigned, framing it as routine.
3. She tells herself this is temporary and justified, a last option rather than a real breach of procedure.
4. She has no idea that an unsupervised, unrecorded room is precisely the condition Voss has been hoping to be given.
5. Voss, watching her maneuver, recognizes what she's setting up and lets it happen without doing anything to stop it.
6. The audience understands what neither of them can fully see yet: Mei Lin believes this is her own risk alone, while Voss has been quietly steering toward exactly this.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mei Lin dismisses the watch officer at the door, giving a calm, procedural-sounding reason.
2. She disables the room's recording herself, telling herself it's only to get Voss talking off the record.
3. She steps back inside and closes the door, now alone with Voss with no witness and no paper trail.
4. Voss watches the door click shut with quiet, contained satisfaction, though he shows nothing on the surface.
5. Elsewhere, Kai Yang notices Mei Lin has gone dark on the radio for longer than protocol allows, assuming she's just pushing boundaries again as before.
6. The audience is left alone with the two of them, aware of how deliberately this moment was built and how little anyone outside that room understands what's about to happen.
