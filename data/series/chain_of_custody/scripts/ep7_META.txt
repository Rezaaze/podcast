She thinks the board finally belongs to her. Cold coffee, quiet victories, a name pulled up from old water — Mei Lin reads every sign as proof she's ahead, while Voss says almost nothing and moves everything. In this episode of Chain of Custody, half a mile of silence becomes its own kind of confession, an old case number resurfaces exactly when it shouldn't, and Kai Yang keeps walking a line that leads straight back to where she's been hiding. Sun watches, saying less than he knows. A bargaining chip changes hands without anyone naming it as one. What builds here isn't a confrontation — it's a register quietly filling with logs, a door someone is already walking toward before the room realizes it's open. Rank, restraint, and timing collide in small, precise moments that only look small until they don't. This is the long game, told in the pauses between what people choose not to say.
