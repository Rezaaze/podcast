--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Mei Lin checks the sign-in log and finds no record of her extended time alone with Voss.
2. She realizes the erased record means nothing exists to corroborate the lock-in except her own word.
3. She tells herself this gives her leverage — she alone can decide when and how this comes to light.
4. She doesn't yet grasp that being the only witness also makes her the only one who can be blamed.
5. The audience recognizes her sense of control as the opposite of the truth: she's now completely exposed.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Voss brings up the name Lily Okafor, watching Mei Lin's composure crack slightly.
2. He recounts fragments of the case — enough detail to unsettle her, though it's secondhand gossip from a former officer, not personal knowledge.
3. Mei Lin tries to deflect, insisting the case was closed and reviewed properly.
4. Voss presses on the word "reviewed," sensing there's more beneath it.
5. The audience knows Voss's knowledge is thinner than he's letting on, but the effect on Mei Lin is real regardless.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Worn down by exhaustion and Voss's pressure, Mei Lin admits there was a tip she missed on the Okafor case.
2. She confesses she requested early sign-off on the internal review specifically to keep that missed tip from ever being formally logged.
3. She admits, for the first time to anyone, that Lily's death might have been preventable if she hadn't been so overextended.
4. Voss listens without judgment, letting his calm read as sympathy rather than calculation.
5. Mei Lin feels something like relief, mistaking his silence for trust earned rather than leverage gained.
6. The audience understands no one at the precinct knows what she just admitted — this confession exists only in this room.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Kai Yang locates an older backup of the interrogation room's digital logs that Sun never thought to alter.
2. He compares timestamps and finds a fragment proving the log was tampered with the same night Mei Lin went dark.
3. He now has physical proof, not just suspicion, that something was deliberately hidden.
4. He hesitates over taking it to Sun, remembering how quickly Sun dismissed his earlier concerns.
5. He decides to keep building his private timeline before showing anyone what he's found.
6. The audience sees him closing in on exactly the cover-up Sun committed, even though Kai Yang still doesn't know why.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Anna Wu arrives at the Blue Lantern, following up on a name she found scrawled in Daniel's notes before he disappeared.
2. She orders a drink and studies the room, unsure who might recognize the name or react to it.
3. She asks the bartender careful, indirect questions, trying not to spook anyone into silence.
4. She gets nothing concrete, only a few uneasy glances that tell her she's asking about someone real.
5. She decides to stay a while longer, sensing she's closer to something than the precinct has let her believe.
6. The audience feels her persistence succeeding where official channels have failed.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Anna overhears two men at a nearby table discussing a shipment problem and a name she recognizes from Daniel's notes.
2. One of them names an associate of Voss's, connecting him directly to the docks issue Daniel had flagged.
3. Anna keeps still, pretending not to listen, memorizing every detail she can.
4. She now has a direct link between Daniel's disappearance and someone in Voss's orbit — something no one at the precinct has given her.
5. She realizes she can't just call this in anonymously again; she needs someone who will actually act on it.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first hard, provable connection between Daniel and Voss's network.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Zoe Han and Anna Wu meet in person, building on their earlier phone call.
2. Anna shares the name she overheard at the Blue Lantern and its tie to Daniel's disappearance.
3. Zoe recognizes the name from her own financial trail, though she doesn't yet say so directly.
4. Zoe asks careful follow-up questions, still withholding her working theory to protect it from leaking back to Sun.
5. Anna, desperate for progress, tells Zoe more than she probably should about what Daniel photographed at the docks.
6. The audience sees the two threads — the missing man and the captain's ledger — visibly converging at this table, even though neither woman says the connection out loud yet.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Voss no longer treats Mei Lin as a captor to be managed but as someone he now has real leverage over.
2. He references her confession about Lily Okafor obliquely, just enough to remind her he holds it.
3. He reframes their arrangement, suggesting future cooperation should feel more like an exchange between equals.
4. He never states outright that he intends to use both her confession and the illegal detention itself to secure his release.
5. Mei Lin senses the shift in his tone but can't yet name what's changed.
6. The audience understands Voss is quietly repositioning the entire situation to serve his exit strategy.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Sun learns, through a quiet word from a colleague, that Kai Yang has been pulling old digital backups.
2. Internally rattled, he keeps his expression neutral in front of the rest of the precinct.
3. He starts quietly steering conversations and access away from Kai Yang without explaining why.
4. He tells himself he can still contain this internally if he moves carefully enough.
5. He has no idea Kai Yang has already found a tampered log fragment, not just a hunch.
6. The audience sees Sun's containment strategy failing in real time, one step behind the truth.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Alone after leaving Voss, Mei Lin replays exactly what she admitted to him.
2. She realizes she gave him not just a fact but the one thing she's spent years protecting — her own culpability in Lily's death.
3. She understands there is no way to take the confession back now that it's spoken.
4. The warmth she mistook for trust starts to look, in hindsight, like patience — like he was waiting for exactly this.
5. She doesn't yet know how he intends to use it, only that he now can.
6. The audience feels her dawning dread land exactly where the case file says it should: she gave away far more than she meant to.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Sun meets the same Voss associate from before, this time away from the docks, in a rushed, tense exchange.
2. The associate tells him Anna Wu has been asking too many specific questions, and it's making people nervous.
3. Sun promises to look into containing Anna's inquiries, though he has no real plan yet.
4. He tells no one at the precinct about this meeting or what it concerns, keeping it fully separate from his official duties.
5. He still believes his bigger problem is managing Kai Yang internally, unaware Anna and Zoe have already started comparing notes.
6. The audience sees Sun's blind spot widening — he's watching the wrong threat.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Voss offers Mei Lin a specific, verifiable detail about where Daniel might be — more concrete than anything he's given before.
2. He frames it as a gift, proof of good faith rather than another delay tactic.
3. Mei Lin can tell, this time, that the information is real, not a stall — and it changes how she weighs everything else he's said.
4. She takes it eagerly, believing this brings her closer to finding Daniel without any further cost to herself.
5. Voss deliberately withholds the actual current location, giving just enough to keep her compliant and coming back.
6. The audience recognizes this as another step deepening her dependence on him, exactly as he intends.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Anna Wu, armed with the name from the Blue Lantern and what Zoe shared, decides she can't wait for the precinct to come to her.
2. She resolves to go in person and demand answers from whoever is actually in charge, not another desk officer.
3. She doesn't know Sun himself is the one containing her brother's case, only that no one has taken her seriously enough.
4. She steels herself for a confrontation she knows might not go well.
5. The audience, knowing what Anna doesn't — that she's about to walk straight toward the man covering up her brother's disappearance — feels the tension of her decision sharply.
