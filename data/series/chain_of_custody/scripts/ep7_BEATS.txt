--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Kai Yang sits alone with a cold coffee, cross-referencing Mei Lin's check-in logs against the precinct's automated entry timestamps.
2. He notices the handwritten log times are suspiciously "clean" compared to the system's usual irregular entries, as if copied rather than lived.
3. He realizes someone has been backfilling these entries after the fact, not writing them in real time.
4. He stops short of naming Mei Lin directly even in his own head, unwilling to fully accept what the pattern implies.
5. He decides to pull the raw system logs next, needing harder proof before he trusts his own suspicion.
6. The audience sees him move from suspicion to near-certainty that the falsification is deliberate and ongoing.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Voss studies Mei Lin's hesitation from the prior exchange and privately reads it as a temporary setback, not a real obstacle.
2. He recalculates his approach, deciding patience now will cost him less than pressure would.
3. He mentally reviews how close he already is to what he actually wants, treating her resistance as a delay, not a defeat.
4. He conceals this calculation completely, letting his outward manner stay warm and unhurried with her.
5. The audience understands Voss already considers this a winning position, regardless of how Mei Lin feels about it.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Zoe Han shares a fragment of her financial trail with Anna Wu, tracing discretionary funds back to a harbor logistics company.
2. Anna recognizes the company's name from one of Daniel's scrawled notes, confirming the link she suspected.
3. Zoe digs further and surfaces an old case reference tied to the same harbor operation, years before Daniel's disappearance.
4. Neither woman yet knows the reference belongs to the Delgado case or that Mei Lin worked it personally.
5. Anna urges Zoe to trace the old case number further, sensing it might explain more than just Daniel's disappearance.
6. The audience recognizes this as the first external thread reaching toward Mei Lin's buried past, though the women don't know it yet.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Sun corners Kai Yang under the pretext of a routine check-in on his caseload.
2. He uses his rank pointedly, reminding Kai Yang that off-book inquiries reflect on the whole precinct's credibility right now.
3. Kai Yang reads the pressure as unusually specific and wonders what exactly Sun is trying to steer him away from.
4. Sun leaves the conversation believing he's reasserted control, misjudging how much suspicion the pressure itself has created.
5. The audience sees Sun's authority working against him, sharpening Kai Yang's instincts instead of dulling them.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Mei Lin walks the empty corridor outside Interrogation Room 3, the half-mile stretch to the nearest occupied desk utterly silent at this hour.
2. She notices, almost idly, how easily the rear emergency exit door could go unnoticed by anyone this far from the bullpen.
3. She doesn't yet connect this observation to anything beyond convenience for her own secrecy.
4. She feels a brief, unexamined unease at how alone she truly is in this wing, then pushes it aside.
5. The audience recognizes the exact geography Voss has been counting on, seen now through Mei Lin's own unsuspecting eyes.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Alone, Mei Lin pulls up the sealed case number from the Delgado file for the first time in eight years.
2. She stares at her own old notes, confronting the forensic lead she once chose to bury under Sun's direction.
3. She feels the full weight of what she's hidden from everyone, sharpened now by what Voss told her about his network's role.
4. She closes the file without acting on it, telling herself the timing still isn't right to reckon with it.
5. The audience sees her carrying two secrets now instead of one, and neither has any safe place to go.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Sun rehearses, alone, what he would say if Kai Yang or Zoe ever asked him directly about the Delgado case.
2. He decides silence remains safer than any explanation he could construct, even a partial one.
3. He weighs telling Mei Lin he suspects she's realized the connection, then decides against warning her at all.
4. He convinces himself she still doesn't know his role was ever more than administrative back then, wrongly certain his secret is still fully his own.
5. The audience already knows this belief is false, deepening the dramatic irony of Sun's next moves.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Voss returns to Mei Lin with his dangerous request reframed as a smaller, more reasonable first step.
2. He references the Delgado case obliquely, reminding her what she owes to finishing what she started, without saying Daniel's name.
3. Mei Lin recognizes he's using her own guilt as the bargaining chip, though she still underestimates how deliberately.
4. She agrees to consider the smaller step, believing she still retains control over how far this goes.
5. Voss notes this concession as further proof his timeline is holding, not as any real trust earned.
6. The audience sees Voss converting old guilt into new leverage in real time.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Zoe Han returns to the Blue Lantern's old guest register, searching specifically for any name tied to the harbor case number Anna found.
2. She matches a name in the register to an alias linked, eight years ago, to Voss's network.
3. Anna realizes this predates Daniel's disappearance entirely, meaning the pattern used against her brother had already been used once before.
4. Zoe still withholds her fullest suspicion about Sun's personal involvement, wanting one more piece of proof first.
5. The audience sees the register confirm the network's history without either woman yet knowing Delgado's name or Mei Lin's role in it.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Kai Yang finally accesses the raw system log Mei Lin's handwritten entries were meant to match.
2. He finds a gap that lines up exactly with the interrogation room's own access records for the lock-in period.
3. He realizes the falsified check-ins were deliberately timed to cover her continuous presence in that specific room.
4. He still has no idea the room's override circuit does anything beyond the door and camera, missing its link to the rear exit.
5. He decides this evidence is now serious enough that he can't sit on it much longer, even to protect her.
6. The audience sees him one discovery away from the full truth, closer than anyone in the precinct realizes.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Word reaches Sun that Zoe has been asking questions at the Blue Lantern about an old register entry.
2. He understands, with rising panic, that the Delgado case's edges are now being touched from an entirely new direction.
3. He abandons his earlier plan of simple denial, deciding instead to physically intervene before the register or the case number can be traced further.
4. He tells no one at the precinct what he intends to do, keeping the decision entirely private.
5. The audience senses Sun is now improvising under panic rather than following any real strategy.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Voss and Mei Lin share what feels, to her, like an easy, almost ordinary exchange, the tension of his last request seemingly eased.
2. She lets herself relax slightly, mistaking his patience for the relationship finally stabilizing on her terms.
3. Voss uses the calm deliberately, reinforcing her trust just enough to secure her agreement to the smaller step from before.
4. He privately marks this as the last piece he needs before moving to what he actually wants.
5. The audience recognizes this warmth as the final setup before Voss's true endgame activates.
6. Nothing about the override or the exit is mentioned aloud, though the audience now understands exactly what this calm precedes.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mei Lin, following through on her agreement, prepares to re-enter her override code into the room's panel once more.
2. She still believes this is only about extending time to extract more of Daniel's location from Voss.
3. Voss watches her approach the panel with quiet, controlled anticipation he doesn't let reach his face.
4. Elsewhere, Sun moves through the precinct with his own unspoken intent, unknowingly converging on the same wing.
5. Kai Yang, log evidence in hand, starts walking toward Interrogation Room 3 to confront what he now suspects.
6. The audience sees all three threads — Mei Lin's code, Sun's panic, Kai Yang's discovery — heading toward the same door at once.
