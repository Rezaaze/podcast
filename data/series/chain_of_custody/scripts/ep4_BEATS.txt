--- SCENE 1 ---
1. Kai Yang notices Mei Lin's desk untouched and her check-in log blank for over a day.
2. He raises it with Captain Sun, expecting concern or at least procedure.
3. Sun waves it off, calling it typical for a detective deep in a hard case.
4. Kai Yang senses the dismissal is too quick but can't yet name why.
5. The audience knows Sun is deflecting to protect the illegal detention he's already covering for, not out of genuine unconcern.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Sun sits alone at a terminal and opens the interrogation room's digital log.
2. He deletes the gap where no sign-in exists for Voss's holding, backdating a plausible entry.
3. He tells himself this is containment, not falsification, to keep functioning.
4. He checks twice that no one is watching him do it.
5. The audience sees the cover-up as a deliberate, physical act, not the passive omission Sun frames it as to himself.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Voss casually lists two or three precinct names known for minor corruption, watching Mei Lin's reactions.
2. He's gauging which names make her flinch and which don't register at all.
3. Mei Lin keeps her face neutral, unwilling to confirm any suspicion she already holds about Sun.
4. Voss notes her restraint as its own kind of answer.
5. The audience understands he's mapping how much she already knows before he risks naming Sun directly.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Kai Yang asks Sun to authorize a formal welfare check on Mei Lin.
2. Sun cites procedure and staffing as reasons to wait, promising to "handle it personally."
3. Kai Yang points out this isn't like her, pushing harder than he normally would.
4. Sun's patience thins just enough for Kai Yang to notice, though he can't place the cause.
5. The audience recognizes Sun has no intention of ever authorizing the check.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. Anna Wu calls the precinct asking for any update on Daniel's case.
2. She's transferred to a desk officer who has nothing new to tell her.
3. She mentions, again, that Daniel said he'd "found something wrong" at the docks, hoping it finally lands with someone.
4. The officer logs it as routine, not realizing its relevance to an active internal crisis two floors below.
5. The audience feels the gap between what Anna is trying to surface and how little the department is actually looking.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Voss tells Mei Lin plainly that the department protects itself first and her never.
2. He points to Sun's stalling and the department's silence on Daniel as proof the system already failed her.
3. He urges her to trust only what she can verify herself, not procedure or her captain.
4. Mei Lin doesn't argue back this time, and her silence is its own admission.
5. The audience sees her resistance to his worldview weakening, exactly as Voss intends.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Zoe Han meets a nervous, lower-level precinct employee at the Blue Lantern.
2. He confirms a discretionary fund line has been quietly padded for years, though he doesn't know by whom.
3. He asks to remain anonymous, clearly afraid of retaliation.
4. Zoe Han promises to protect his identity, meaning it, but also aware she's now one step closer to Sun.
5. The audience recognizes this source as another thread tightening around the captain without his knowledge.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. Sun meets a Voss associate at the harbor, away from cameras and colleagues.
2. He's told a reporter has been asking questions near the docks.
3. Sun instructs the associate to keep the operation quiet a little longer, promising the situation is "handled."
4. He doesn't mention Mei Lin's illegal detention of Voss, keeping that leverage separate even from his own allies.
5. The audience sees Sun juggling two cover-ups he can no longer fully separate.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Mei Lin presses Voss directly: how does he know what he claims about Sun.
2. Voss offers one small, specific, verifiable detail — enough to prove access, not enough to prove depth.
3. He deliberately withholds how thin his actual knowledge is, letting her assume more.
4. Mei Lin's certainty about Sun's corruption hardens based on this single confirmed fragment.
5. The audience knows, per the case file, that Voss is inflating a narrow truth into something that feels total.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Kai Yang, still quietly investigating on his own, pulls the interrogation room's digital access log.
2. He notices a suspicious gap in the timestamps around the night Mei Lin went dark.
3. He doesn't yet connect it to Sun, only to Mei Lin's unexplained absence.
4. He starts a private timeline, unwilling to bring it to anyone until it's clearer.
5. The audience sees him closing in on the exact cover-up Sun just committed in Scene 2.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Voss shifts his tone, framing his next offer as cooperation rather than coercion.
2. He proposes that if she gets him out cleanly, he'll help her find Daniel and expose the rot in the department together.
3. He never uses the word "partnership," but the implication is unmistakable.
4. Mei Lin recognizes the manipulation even as it works on her.
5. The audience understands, per the case file, this is Voss maneuvering toward the illegality itself becoming his exit.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Zoe Han calls Anna Wu, having noticed the docks keep surfacing in both their separate inquiries.
2. She asks careful, general questions about Daniel without revealing what she's really investigating.
3. Anna Wu, eager for any lead, shares more than she normally would with a stranger.
4. Neither realizes yet how directly their two threads intersect through Sun and the harbor.
5. The audience sees the convergence the season has been building toward finally beginning.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Alone again, Sun decides the log edit isn't enough and resolves to erase every trace the lock-in happened at all.
2. He begins drafting a plan to alter physical sign-in sheets and backup records, not just the digital log.
3. He justifies it to himself as protecting the department, not himself.
4. He doesn't yet know Kai Yang has already found the gap he's trying to erase.
5. The audience understands Sun is about to destroy evidence that someone has already started tracing.
