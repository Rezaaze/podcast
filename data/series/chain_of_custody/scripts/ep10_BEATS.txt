--- SCENE 1 ---
1. By mid-morning the bullpen is quietly buzzing that Mei Lin hasn't logged in, badged in, or answered radio calls for over a day.
2. Sun intercepts the chatter before it reaches anyone above him, telling a duty officer she's on an approved off-book assignment.
3. He hides that he has no idea where she actually is or what condition she's in.
4. Kai Yang, cornered by the same rumors, gives vague cover for her without knowing if he's protecting her or enabling something worse.
5. Sun privately calculates how many more hours he can contain this before it becomes an official missing-officer report.
6. The audience senses Sun is now managing two simultaneous cover-ups with the same currency: time he doesn't have.

--- SCENE 2 ---
1. Voss, unhurried, tells Mei Lin that today is the day everything about the room resolves, one way or another.
2. He states plainly that he knows about the destroyed witness statement from eight years ago, naming it without ever revealing his source.
3. Mei Lin hides how deeply this lands, refusing to give him the satisfaction of visible shock.
4. Voss frames the knowledge not as a threat but as proof they understand each other completely.
5. She realizes, watching his calm, that he has always known this would be his last real leverage over her.
6. The audience understands Voss is spending his best secret now because he expects to need nothing from her after today.

--- SCENE 3 ---
1. Mei Lin hands Kai Yang the container number fragment, still withholding exactly how she obtained it.
2. Kai Yang accepts it at face value, choosing not to press the question he's been avoiding since the interrogation room.
3. He assembles a raid team fast, citing the urgency of the reopened harbor case rather than her involvement.
4. He hides from the team that the tip originates from an unauthorized source inside their own building.
5. The audience recognizes he is protecting Mei Lin's information the same way he protected her body days earlier, without addressing the danger underneath.

--- SCENE 4 ---
1. Anna receives a call telling her only to come to the precinct immediately, with no explanation given.
2. She sits in the lobby's harsh fluorescent light, replaying months of silence and bracing for the worst possible version of news.
3. She hides her fear behind stillness, refusing to let the desk officers see her fall apart before she knows anything.
4. She has no idea a raid is already underway based on evidence she helped surface without realizing its full weight.
5. The audience knows she is one door away from an answer she has spent the season dreading and hoping for in equal measure.

--- SCENE 5 ---
1. An Internal Affairs liaison enters Sun's office without warning, carrying a folder built from Zoe Han's published figures.
2. Sun's practiced composure falters for a beat before he recovers it, aware denial is no longer a viable first move.
3. The liaison lays out preliminary findings connecting years of redirected shipments to Sun's decisions.
4. Sun hides how much more the ledger could still expose if the investigation goes one layer deeper.
5. The audience sees the six-year arrangement finally meeting daylight, even as its origin above Sun stays untouched.

--- SCENE 6 ---
1. Voss offers Mei Lin a final transaction: his continued calm cooperation through processing in exchange for her permanent silence on how she got the container number.
2. He frames it as protection for her, though it equally protects the illegal method he helped provoke.
3. Mei Lin recognizes the offer as another version of the same trap the whole lock-in was built from.
4. She hides her hesitation, needing him compliant through transfer more than she needs to refuse him on principle.
5. The audience sees her agree to something that will outlast Voss's custody, tying her to him even after he's gone.

--- SCENE 7 ---
1. Kai Yang's team cuts through the rusted container's seal at Dock 14, the metal groaning open after weeks shut.
2. Daniel Wu is found alive inside, disoriented and flinching from the first daylight he's seen in a season.
3. The team's relief is immediate, but no one yet questions why this container, only newly relocated to, was left so easy to find.
4. Kai Yang radios confirmation back to the precinct, unaware the empty first container from the previous search and this one are connected by the same warning Voss enabled.
5. The audience understands this rescue succeeds only because Voss's earlier stall tactic ran out of time, not because his network's caution failed.

--- SCENE 8 ---
1. At the Blue Lantern, a transport officer meets a contact who does not work for Sun, despite the arrangement's appearance.
2. He receives quiet instructions for Voss's transfer route and a folded envelope of cash, sealing his role in advance.
3. He hides from everyone at the precinct that this assignment came through favors older and higher than Sun's authority.
4. The audience realizes Sun's compromised network is being used by someone even Sun doesn't fully see or control.

--- SCENE 9 ---
1. Mei Lin fills out the paperwork formally closing the illegal detention, choosing the version of events that ends the exposure fastest.
2. She frames the filing to herself as finally taking responsibility, not noticing it also finalizes Voss's clean path to transfer.
3. She hides the full thirty-plus hours from the official record, rounding the timeline down to something survivable.
4. Signing it, she understands too late that ending the lock-in on paper is the exact condition Voss needed to move.
5. The audience watches her act of closure become the last piece Voss's plan required.

--- SCENE 10 ---
1. Voss is walked out of holding for what appears to be routine transfer processing.
2. The compromised transport officer manages the handoff between two doors, two signatures, and one unwatched gap.
3. In that gap, Voss is simply gone, with no alarm raised until the paperwork trail catches up minutes later.
4. No one on the transfer team yet realizes the missing custody record from the lock-in makes the disappearance nearly impossible to timeline.
5. The audience sees the entire season's engineered gap finally cash out in real time.

--- SCENE 11 ---
1. Anna is brought into a hospital room and finds Daniel alive, thinner and changed but unmistakably her brother.
2. She breaks down in relief, the months of assumed death collapsing into this one contradicting moment.
3. Daniel, still disoriented, begins offering fragments of what he saw at the docks before he vanished.
4. Neither of them yet grasps that his testimony directly implicates the same network now unraveling around Sun and Voss.
5. The audience understands Daniel's fragments are about to become the missing piece connecting every thread still open.

--- SCENE 12 ---
1. Sun is confronted with Zoe Han's full documentation and the transport officer's name now tied to Voss's escape.
2. He offers no real defense, recognizing the evidence has already closed every angle he might have argued.
3. He is suspended pending a formal internal investigation, his authority stripped in the same conversation.
4. He hides, even now, the identity of who originally recruited him into the network years ago.
5. The audience sees Sun fall exactly as far as the evidence reaches, and no further.

--- SCENE 13 ---
1. Mei Lin sits across from Kai Yang, not Voss, for the first time all season choosing honesty over control.
2. She tells him the truth about the destroyed witness statement from eight years ago before Internal Affairs can present it as an accusation.
3. Kai Yang's understanding of her reshapes in real time, the secrecy of the lock-in suddenly reframed as one piece of a much older pattern.
4. She hides nothing this time, including how directly her old silence connects to the network that took Daniel.
5. The audience watches her surrender the one secret she could have kept, on her own terms instead of someone else's.

--- SCENE 14 ---
1. At the harbor at dawn, with Voss's trail already cold, the narrator surveys what the season leaves behind.
2. Anna has her brother back, though the men responsible for taking him remain scattered and unnamed.
3. Mei Lin has her honesty, but not her badge, and not the officer's trust she spent the season risking everything to keep.
4. Sun's network is exposed at his level only, the debt to whoever built it above him left deliberately unpaid.
5. Voss is gone, his calm having outlasted everyone who tried to hold him.
6. The audience is left understanding that the season's true cost was never the case being solved, but what solving it required each of them to give up.
