TITEL:
Walter Freeman — Ten Minutes, One Ice Pick, and a Silenced Mind

BESCHREIBUNG:
He called it mercy. He carried it in a leather bag, drove it across America in a station wagon he nicknamed the lobotomobile, and delivered it through the eye socket with a mallet and an ice pick. Walter Freeman believed he could cure madness in the time it took to boil an egg — and thousands of desperate families believed him too. In this episode of Anatomy of the Damned: Physicians Who Butchered to Heal, we trace Freeman's transformation from ambitious neurologist to traveling evangelist of the transorbital lobotomy, a man so convinced of his own righteousness that he operated on children, on housewives, on a president's sister, often without anesthesia beyond a jolt of electricity. We follow the fear that made a nation desperate enough to hand him their loved ones, the theater he built around a ten-minute procedure, and the silence that settled over homes long after the ice pick was withdrawn. This is a story about conviction, and what it costs the ones who cannot refuse it.
